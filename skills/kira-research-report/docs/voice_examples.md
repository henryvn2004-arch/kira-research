# voice_examples.md — 10 worked examples from the R0152 baseline

Each entry is a passage extracted **verbatim** from `references/sample_R0152_baseline.html`, the gold-standard report, paired with a one-line analysis of why it works. Use these as voice anchors when generating new content.

`prompts/voice_guide.md` carries the rules. This document carries the proof.

## 1. Executive-summary subhead (page exec_summary_p1)

> A **USD 24 bn construction backdrop** meets accelerating non-asbestos substitution, a renewed property cycle, and a consolidating supply base. Long-form opportunity outweighs short-term cyclical noise.

**Why it works:** Two sentences. First names the backdrop number with one bold phrase, then lists three vectors. Second sentence is a *thesis claim* — long-form > short-term — that the rest of the page argues. No filler, no warm-up.

## 2. Executive-summary narrative — "Market context"

> Indonesia's construction sector contributed **9.84% of GDP in Q1 2025**, anchoring a roofing, exterior and insulation opportunity sized at **USD 1.8–2.2 billion**. After 7.0% real growth in 2024, value-add softened to 3.6% in H1 2025 as IKN financing eased. The 2026–2031 horizon stays structurally constructive: a **9.9–11 million housing backlog**, the Prabowo administration's **3 Million Houses Program**, and extended 100% VAT exemption form the demand floor.

**Why it works:** Dense with anchored numbers (4 quantitative claims in 60 words), but each is positioned in service of one argument: "construction is the anchor, near-term softness is cyclical, long-term floor is policy-set." Inline bolds are used only for the structural numbers, not decoration. The phrase "form the demand floor" is the kind of muscular consultant verb that earns its place.

## 3. Executive-summary narrative — "Why now: three convergent forces"

> BI's H2 2024 rate cut from 6.25% to 6.00% lifted KPR mortgage transactions **+58% QoQ in Q4 2024**. The March 2024 Supreme Court mandate on asbestos warning labels compresses a long-promised substitution — Indonesia still imports **~150,000 tons of asbestos annually**, roofing 13% of households. Fiber cement HHI nearly doubled from 4,171 (2017) to **8,737 (2023)**, widening the gap between scale incumbents and the long tail.

**Why it works:** Three sentences, three convergent forces. Each sentence states a discrete force, anchored to a precise number. The structure mirrors the heading promise ("three convergent forces"). Note the verb choices: *compresses*, *widening*. These do work.

## 4. Implication card — "01 · SUBSTITUTION"

> Indonesia imports **~150,000 tons of asbestos annually** with no full ban yet. Supreme Court warning-label mandate (Mar 2024) plus the 2035 Asbestos-Free target compresses substitution into 5–7 years. Etex KALSI/ETER and listed Impact Pratama (IMPC) best positioned.
>
> *Anchor: 13% ID households on asbestos*

**Why it works:** Card budget = 360 chars. This lands at ~330. Pattern: state the structural reality → state the regulatory catalyst → identify the named winners. Final line is the **anchor** — a single irrefutable data point that justifies the whole card. The verb "compresses" recurs — the analyst's metaphor for "regulation forces a timeline that operators don't get to negotiate".

## 5. Implication card — "02 · CHANNEL"

> Mitra10 (**48+ Superstores**), Ace Hardware (USD 515M revenue), Depo Bangunan captured **~20–25% share** versus <10% a decade ago. B2B e-commerce grows **15.9% CAGR**; 6M annual online transactions reroute long-tail demand.
>
> *Anchor: USD 4.5 bn DIY/hardware 2023*

**Why it works:** Names companies by name + scale signal + share evolution + adjacent channel growth. Five distinct data points in two sentences, each anchored. "Reroute long-tail demand" is more loaded than "grow" — it implies winners and losers.

---

## 6. Implication card — "03 · CONSOLIDATION"

> Fiber cement HHI **moved from 4,171 (2017) to 8,737 (2023)**, crossing the highly concentrated threshold. M&A and informal exit of sub-scale converters accelerates. Etex, SCG (Siam Indo), Impack likely capture **>60% volume** by 2028.
>
> *Anchor: HHI +109% in 6 years*

**Why it works:** Names the technical mechanism (HHI threshold) without explaining it (the reader is expected to know — consulting register). Predicts the endpoint ("3 winners take >60%") with a date stamp. Anchor reframes the same fact in % terms — gives the reader two grips on the same number.

## 7. Competitive-landscape subhead

> The HHI for Indonesia fiber cement crossed the **highly concentrated threshold (HHI >8,000)** in 2023, having spent 2017 well within the moderately concentrated band. Asbestos compliance costs and two-plant scale economics favour 3–4 incumbents.

**Why it works:** Past-perfect construction ("having spent 2017 well within…") implies the reader is observing a transition — not a snapshot. "Favour 3–4 incumbents" predicts the endpoint of consolidation without overclaiming. Two sentences carry both the metric and the structural reason behind it.

---

## 8. Comp-side narrative — "Consolidation accelerating"

> Sub-scale local converters reliant on asbestos formulations are giving way to **two-plant national-coverage incumbents** with European, Thai or Belgian backing.

**Why it works:** ONE sentence, no fluff. Names the loser ("sub-scale local converters reliant on asbestos formulations") with enough specificity that the reader sees the exit. Names the winner phenotype ("two-plant national-coverage with European/Thai/Belgian backing") with enough specificity that the reader sees the consolidator. "Are giving way to" — passive but accurate; the structural force isn't agency, it's economics.

## 9. Dark divider thesis — Section 14 (AI Impact)

> Indonesia's AI-in-construction market reached **USD 2.3 billion in 2024**, marking a generational shift in how roofing, exterior and insulation players forecast demand, optimize pricing, and influence specifier decisions. PUPR's BIM Roadmap (2017–2024) has completed; the 2026–2031 period is about translation.

**Why it works:** Divider theses are the most concentrated voice opportunity in the entire report — 5 lines max, sets up the next chapter's argument. This one anchors with a number, lists the three operating areas AI touches (forecast, pricing, specifier influence), then frames the chapter's window: "period is about translation". That phrase carries the whole next section.

## 10. AI use case body — "03 · PRICING"

> Real-time engines arbitrate between modern trade (Mitra10, Ace), traditional *toko bangunan*, and B2B e-commerce (Ralali, Tokopedia). Captures **15.9% B2B e-commerce CAGR upside** without channel cannibalization.
>
> *Channel mix in flux through 2028*

**Why it works:** Names the AI's job ("arbitrate between") with the channels as parenthetical concrete instances. Italicizes the local Indonesian phrase *toko bangunan* — the reader trusts the analyst more for using the term operators use. Quantifies the upside with a named anchor (CAGR). Final tag line ("Channel mix in flux through 2028") gives a horizon, signaling this isn't a stable answer — the reader should plan for re-optimization.

## How to use this file

- **Before drafting any narrative slot**, scan this list for the structural pattern closest to what you're producing (exec subhead → entry 1; implication card → entries 4-6; AI use case → entry 10).
- **Copy the rhythm, not the words.** The R0152 sample is about Indonesian construction — your topic isn't. Replicate the sentence structure, the verb energy, the anchor placement.
- **Numbers per claim density.** R0152 averages 1 quantified claim per 12-18 words in body text. That's the target.
- **Bolding rule visible across all 10 entries:** ≤2 bolded phrases per paragraph, and they're always *structural numbers* or *named entities* — never adjectives.
