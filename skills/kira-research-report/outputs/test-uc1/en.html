Market context

Indonesia's construction sector contributed 9.84% of GDP in Q1 2025 [secondary], anchoring a roofing, exterior and insulation opportunity sized at USD 1.8–2.2 billion [estimate]. After 7.0% real growth in 2024, value-add softened to 3.6% in H1 2025 as IKN financing eased. The 2026–2031 horizon stays structurally constructive: a 9.9–11 million housing backlog [secondary], the Prabowo administration's 3 Million Houses Program, and extended 100% VAT exemption form the demand floor.

Why now: three convergent forces

BI's H2 2024 rate cut from 6.25% to 6.00% lifted KPR mortgage transactions +58% QoQ in Q4 2024 [secondary]. The March 2024 Supreme Court mandate on asbestos warning labels compresses a long-promised substitution — Indonesia still imports ~150,000 tons of asbestos annually [secondary], roofing 13% of households. Fiber cement HHI nearly doubled from 4,171 (2017) to 8,737 (2023) [secondary], widening the gap between scale incumbents and the long tail.

Five strategic implications for market participants

Five forces materially reshape competitive dynamics through 2031. Each is anchored to documented market data and KIRA's synthesis from two decades of building-materials studies across Southeast Asia.

01 · SUBSTITUTION
Non-asbestos transition unlocks share gain
Indonesia imports ~150,000 tons of asbestos annually with no full ban yet [secondary]. The March 2024 Supreme Court warning-label mandate plus the 2035 Asbestos-Free target compresses substitution into 5–7 years. Etex KALSI/ETER and listed Impack Pratama (IMPC) are best positioned.
Anchor: 13% ID households on asbestos
02 · CHANNEL
Modern trade reorders distribution economics
Mitra10 (48+ Superstores), Ace Hardware (USD 515M revenue), and Depo Bangunan captured ~20–25% share versus <10% a decade ago [estimate]. B2B e-commerce grows 15.9% CAGR; 6M annual online transactions reroute long-tail demand.
Anchor: USD 4.5 bn DIY/hardware 2023
03 · CONSOLIDATION
HHI doubling drives 3–4 winner outcome
Fiber cement HHI moved from 4,171 (2017) to 8,737 (2023) [secondary], crossing the highly concentrated threshold. M&A and informal exit of sub-scale converters accelerates. Etex, SCG (Siam Indo), Impack likely capture >60% volume by 2028.
Anchor: HHI +109% in 6 years
04 · AI/DIGITAL
AI procurement reshapes spec influence
Indonesia AI-in-construction reached USD 2.3 billion in 2024 [secondary]. PUPR's BIM Roadmap completed Phase 4 in 2024. AI spec recommendations, demand forecasting and channel pricing shift bargaining power from sales reps to data-enabled procurement.
Anchor: USD 2.3 bn AI/construction ID
05 · SUSTAINABILITY
Green-building spec mandates favour certified
GBCI Greenship plus government BGH (PWPH Reg 21/2021) create dual-track green incentives. 60+ buildings certified by July 2024 [secondary]. Cement decarbonisation mandate (30% emissions cut by 2030) trickles into specifier-led material substitution.
Anchor: 60+ GBCI certified

Business overview

The reference manufacturer of fiber cement building boards in Indonesia for over 50 years. Established 1971 as a JV between Eternit Belgium and PT Semen Gresik; began operations 1973 selling corrugated fiber cement roofing and flat boards. Renamed from PT Eternit Gresik to PT Etex Building Performance Indonesia as part of the group's 2018 brand consolidation.

Brand architecture

Two anchor brands. KALSI covers flat fiber cement boards (ceilings, partitions, cladding, list-plank, façades, flooring). ETER covers corrugated sheet roofing (Eter6, Eter11, Eter14). Both ranges are 100% asbestos-free, with Etex Indonesia the local pioneer in asbestos-free fiber cement technology.

Strategic positioning

KALSI sits within Etex's global commercial portfolio alongside CEDRAL, DURLOCK, EQUITONE, ETERNIT, PROMAT and SINIAT. Parent operates 118 production sites across 45 countries, €3 billion+ turnover. The Belgian group brings R&D depth and technology transfer beyond local-competitor reach.

Strengths & watchlist

  • Heritage: 50+ years of entrenched distributor and specifier relationships
  • Scale: Two-plant national logistics economics (Gresik + Karawang)
  • Asbestos-free pioneer — best positioned for the 2035 transition
  • Watch: SCG cross-sell pressure; Impack innovation in twin-wall

AI in Indonesian construction: USD 2.3 bn market, accelerating

Indonesia AI-in-construction sized at USD 2.3 billion (2024) [secondary] reflects a generational re-platforming. Global construction design software on a 7.7% CAGR to USD 15.4 billion by 2030 [secondary], with emerging markets — Indonesia, India, Vietnam — as the fastest adopters.

Market size & velocity

Indonesia AI in construction sized at USD 2.3 bn (2024) [secondary], anchored by predictive analytics, BIM and machine learning across the project lifecycle. Concentrated in large-tier developers and MNC contractors; now extending into distributor workflows.

Government catalyst

PUPR's BIM Roadmap (4 phases, 2017–2024) codified by BALITBANG PU Decree 32/KPTS/KL/2017. Concluded 2024 with mandatory BIM on government infrastructure projects; private-sector uptake growing.

Regional benchmark

Singapore's BCA Construction AI Mandate (2023) sets the regional standard. Indonesia lags 2–3 years but compresses fast given RPJMN 2024–2029 (5% GDP target, 200+ National Strategic Projects).

Six AI applications reshaping roofing, exterior & insulation operators

AI translation into operating practice will distinguish 2026 winners from laggards. Use cases below span specification influence through channel execution, with deployment patterns observable in Indonesia or the closest regional benchmarks today.

01 · DEMAND
Distributor demand forecasting
ML models combine project pipeline (PUPR, IKN), weather seasonality, FX and toko bangunan order velocity. Cuts stockouts and improves working capital. Highest adoption among modern-trade distributors with B2B SKU breadth.
Observable: Mitra10, Adimas Isolasitama
02 · SPEC
BIM-integrated spec recommendations
AI plug-ins for Revit and ArchiCAD surface relevant material specs at the architect's decision moment. Pushes branded fiber cement, insulation and façade systems into BIM model objects with traceable specs.
Benchmark: Singapore BCA workflow
03 · PRICING
Channel pricing optimisation
Real-time engines arbitrate between modern trade (Mitra10, Ace), traditional toko bangunan, and B2B e-commerce (Ralali, Tokopedia). Captures 15.9% B2B e-commerce CAGR upside without channel cannibalisation.
Channel mix in flux through 2028
04 · QUALITY
Drone + AI installation QC
Commercial and high-rise roofing benefits from drone visual inspection paired with computer-vision defect models. Reduces warranty claims and reinforces premium-brand positioning for asbestos-free formats.
Pilot: IKN projects, Jakarta high-rise
05 · WASTE
Material waste optimisation
AI-driven cutting optimisation on fiber cement and insulation rolls reduces wastage 12–15% on documented deployments [estimate]. Aligns with the cement industry's 30% decarbonisation mandate by 2030.
Tie-in: GBCI material credits
06 · SEARCH
AEO & conversational visibility
As specifiers shift from Google to AI assistants, brand visibility in answer engines becomes table stakes. Structured content, FAQ-rich pages and FAQPage schema gain disproportionate spec influence in the discovery layer.
