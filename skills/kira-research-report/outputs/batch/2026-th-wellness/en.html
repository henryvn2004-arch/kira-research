This view is built from two evidence streams: primary inputs — field channel checks, analyst synthesis, and operator reads across Thai hospitals and wellness operators — and secondary anchors — statistical authorities, listed-company filings, and triangulated estimates. Diversification is structural

The pivot is not a slogan. Wellness-tourism spend grew 36.4% in a single year to USD 14 bn [GWI 2025], wellness real estate 22.9% and spas 18% — each outpacing the cosmetic-led core. The Tourism Authority is steering a 2026 target of THB 125 bn in health-tourism receipts from roughly 580,000 medical tourists [TAT 2026], explicitly courting longevity and preventive demand rather than discount volume.

The value buyer is arriving

Operator filings confirm the mix shift. Bumrungrad drew 66.8% of revenue from international patients in Q3 2025 [Bumrungrad Q3 2025], led by Middle East, Myanmar and Bangladesh growth; BDMS grew international receipts 11% in FY2024 with Qatar up 56% [BDMS AR 2024]. High-acuity and preventive packages now anchor the economics that cosmetic price-competition cannot.

Section 02 · Market Sizing
007 / 016

Two metrics, one pivot: economy size and tourism receipts

Thailand's wellness story reads on two scales — the broad USD 42.7 bn wellness economy [GWI 2025] and the narrower health-tourism receipt pool the government actively steers. Both point the same way: toward higher-value spend per visitor.

The wellness economy

The wellness economy reached USD 42.7 bn in 2024, up 10.1% and ranking Thailand 24th globally and 9th in Asia-Pacific [GWI 2025]. Wellness tourism — USD 14 bn — was the fastest-growing limb at 36.4%, ahead of wellness real estate at 22.9% and spas at 18%.

Health-tourism receipts

The Ministry counts 92,813 operators and ~THB 670 bn in 2025 health-tourism receipts, of which direct-service providers number 28,203 with a THB 220 bn market [MoTS 2025]. The Tourism Authority targets THB 125 bn from the medical-tourist segment specifically in 2026 [TAT 2026].

Why the gap matters

The wide receipt pool captures spas, fitness and food; the narrow medical target captures the high-acuity buyer. Diversification is the deliberate effort to grow the second faster than the first.

Section 03 · The Segment Shift
008 / 016

The mix moves from the scalpel to the long game

Cosmetic and dental procedures built Thailand's reputation and still anchor volume — but the marginal growth dollar is migrating to preventive, longevity and traditional-medicine segments that command higher spend and repeat visits.

The maturing core

Cosmetic surgery remains the headline draw and a meaningful share of procedure volume, but it competes on price against Korea, Turkey and Malaysia. Our reads put cosmetic and aesthetic work at roughly a quarter of medical-tourism value in 2026 [Kira estimates] — still large, no longer the growth story.

The new growth limbs

Preventive check-ups, oncology and longevity programs are where operators report the fastest receipt growth. BDMS lifted wellness revenue ~12% to THB 15 bn and targets THB 20 bn [BDMS AR 2024]; wellness real estate grew 22.9% and spas 18% [GWI 2025].

Traditional medicine as a wedge

Thai herbal and traditional medicine is being elevated from spa add-on to a clinically integrated "New S-Curve" segment under national strategy [DTAM 2026], giving Thailand a differentiator rivals cannot easily copy.

Section 04 · Demand Diversification
009 / 016

Spreading the source-market base toward higher spend

Diversification is happening on the demand side too. Operators are deliberately broadening beyond any single source country — courting Middle East high-net-worth, CLMV neighbours and Western longevity buyers to stabilise utilization and lift spend.

Middle East at the high end

GCC patients now sit at the top of the revenue table. Bumrungrad's Middle East volumes rose ~21% and Qatar led BDMS international growth at +56% YoY [BDMS AR 2024]. The Tourism Authority is explicitly courting Middle Eastern high-net-worth travellers toward the THB 125 bn 2026 goal [TAT 2026].

CLMV provides the base load

Myanmar, Cambodia, Lao PDR and Vietnam supply steady cross-border volume — Bumrungrad's Myanmar revenue grew ~21% and Bangladesh ~25-31% [Bumrungrad Q3 2025] — though single-country softness (recent Cambodian volumes) shows why no operator can over-index.

The Western longevity buyer

Europe and North America are the emerging prize for longevity retreats and preventive programs, where length of stay and spend per head are highest. This is the segment the LTR visa and wellness real estate are designed to convert into repeat, long-stay demand.

Section 06 · Policy & Regulation
012 / 016

Policy is engineering long-stay, repeat demand

Thailand's diversification is state-sponsored. Visa reform, investment incentives and a national traditional-medicine strategy are designed to convert procedure tourism into residency, recurring care and exportable wellness products.

The LTR residency lever

A January 2025 cabinet revision eased the 10-year Long-Term Resident visa, removing the income floor for wealthy applicants and offering a 17% flat tax to skilled residents [BOI 2025]. Medical and wellness is a named target industry, turning long-stay patients into residents.

Investment incentives

The Board of Investment frames wellness and rehabilitation tourism inside its priority list, with a one-stop visa-and-work-permit center to ease operator and talent inflows [BOI 2025]. Capital is being steered toward preventive and longevity infrastructure.

Traditional medicine strategy

The Department of Thai Traditional & Alternative Medicine has set a "New S-Curve" herbal strategy targeting regional traditional-medicine leadership by 2027 [DTAM 2026] — converting Thai healing heritage into clinical programs and exportable products.

Section 08 · Forecast & Outlook
015 / 016

Receipts compound as the mix turns to value

Our base case sees the wellness economy compounding toward USD 70 bn by 2030 [Kira estimates], with the value-led segments — preventive, longevity, traditional medicine — growing several points faster than the cosmetic core.

Base case

Anchored to the 10.1% 2024 print [GWI 2025] and the TAT THB 125 bn medical target [TAT 2026], we model the wellness economy rising from ~USD 47 bn in 2025 to ~USD 70 bn by 2030 [Kira estimates], a high-single-digit to low-double-digit CAGR as value mix lifts spend per visitor.

What tilts the upside

Faster GCC and Western longevity conversion, LTR-driven long-stay residency, and clinical traditional-medicine exports. BDMS's THB 20 bn wellness ambition signals where private capital expects the curve [BDMS AR 2024].

What caps it

Near-term private-hospital margin pressure, single-source-country policy shocks, and regional capability catch-up from Singapore, Malaysia and India keep the bear case live.
