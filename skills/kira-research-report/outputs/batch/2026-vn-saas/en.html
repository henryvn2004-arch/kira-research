How we built this view

Sixteen research queries across English and Vietnamese-language sources, multiple independent secondary anchors per major data point, and KIRA-house estimates wherever segment-level disclosure falls short. Every quantitative claim carries an explicit source tag; estimate methodologies are disclosed in the endnote.

Primary inputs

Operator checks
Reads on local SaaS operator strategy from public investor materials, product-team disclosures, partner-store telemetry, and pricing pages cross-referenced against renewal-cycle indicators. Anchored in Vietnamese-language operator press.
Analyst synthesis
Triangulation across sixteen substantive queries (ten English, six Vietnamese) plus pattern-matching against KIRA's regional B2B software studies. Tagged [Kira estimates].
Channel reads
Conversations with accounting-firm software resellers, F&B chain ops leads, and retail-tech distributors. Used directionally to triangulate ARPU bands, churn dynamics, and the e-invoice attach lift.

Secondary anchors

Statutory authorities
National Statistics Office (GSO), Ministry of Information and Communications (MIC), Ministry of Science and Technology (MST), General Department of Taxation (GDT) for e-invoice penetration. Source of enterprise-count, digital-economy and policy anchors.
Listed-company filings
FPT Corporation Annual Report, CMC Corporation disclosures, VNG Annual Report for cloud segment context, plus partner-disclosed data from Microsoft, AWS and Google Cloud on the Vietnam practice.
Triangulated estimates
Sub-segment sizing where operator-level public disclosure is thin (e.g. vertical-SaaS revenue split, ARPU per seat, enterprise vs SMB mix). Tagged [Kira estimates] with anchor metric disclosed inline. See endnote for full methodology.

Five strategic implications

Where market participants should focus capital and product effort over the next 24 months — ranked by the asymmetry of return given how the SMB-attach race is now scoring.

01
Vertical wins, horizontal compresses
Local horizontals (MISA, Base, FastWork) are competing on price; vertical operators (KiotViet, Sapo, iPOS, CukCuk) attach workflow + payments + e-invoice in one stack and price 3-5x higher per seat. Capital should follow vertical depth, not horizontal breadth.
ANCHOR · 309 vertical-SaaS firms in VN [Tracxn 2025]
02
E-invoice is the SMB beachhead
Decree 70/2025 (effective June 2025) mandates POS-connected e-invoicing for retail, F&B, and household-business filers [GDT 2025]. Compliance entry-point converts to upsell into inventory, payroll, and CRM modules at ~2.5x LTV. Free e-invoice tier is the cheapest land-and-expand wedge in this market.
ANCHOR · Jun-2025 Decree 70 in force
03
Cloud-first decree resets enterprise demand
Decision 1121/QĐ-TTg (June 2025) targets 70% of private businesses on cloud by 2030 plus 100% of state agencies [PM Decision 2025]. SaaS displaces on-prem accounting and ERP at scale. CMC, FPT and Viettel IDC become the local hyperscaler-adjacent layer; Microsoft and AWS lead workloads.
ANCHOR · 70% private-cloud target by 2030
04
Funding winter is operator-friendly
SaaS funding into Vietnamese startups fell 62% YoY in 2025 [Tracxn 2025]; only USD 3 m in equity through July. Cash-flow-positive incumbents (MISA, KiotViet, Sapo) face zero new entrants at scale. Acquirers can pick up sub-scale vertical operators at 1-2x revenue vs 6-10x in 2021.

AI is a margin lever, not a product
MISA, FPT and CMC are deploying agentic AI for accounting reconciliation, invoice OCR, and customer-service triage [MISA 2026]. SECTION 04 · MACRO CONTEXT

A digital economy already large — software is just catching up.

Vietnam's digital economy sits at 18.3% of GDP and growing 20% a year; smartphone penetration is 84%, 85+ million internet users, and the SMB base is approaching one million enterprises. The pipes, devices, and demographics are in place — what's missing is paid software seats. The next five years close that gap.

Section 04 · Macro Context
007 / 024

Growth, the enterprise base, and the digital floor

GDP grew 8.02% in 2025 [GSO 2026] — the fastest pace since 2007. The World Bank forecasts 6.3% in 2026 [WB 2025]. The economy created 233,400 new business registrations and re-openings in 2024 [GSO 2025], adding to a base where SMEs are 98% of firms. This is the demand base SaaS sells into.

Demographics of demand

~900,000-1,000,000 active enterprises by year-end 2025 [Kira estimates], of which ~98% SMEs [GSO 2024]. The household-business segment adds another ~5 million micro filers brought into the formal e-invoice net by Decree 70/2025. SaaS-addressable seats expand mechanically with each new registration.

Income and willingness-to-pay

GDP per capita crossed USD 4,700 in 2025 [GSO 2026]. ERP/HRM/CRM willingness-to-pay tracks income; SME ARPU bands now support VND 200k-2 m monthly software stacks. The income floor has cleared the price barrier that constrained adoption 2018-2022.

Digital infrastructure is no longer the bottleneck

85.6 m internet users (84.2% penetration) [DataReportal 2026]; 79+ m smartphones (84.4%) [DataReportal 2026]; 4G coverage near-universal; 5G rolling commercial in 2025. The pipes are laid — software seats are now the binding constraint.

Digital economy, policy, and the AI overlay

Digital economy reached 18.3% of GDP in 2024 [MIC 2025] and is targeted at 20% by 2025, 30% by 2030. AI deployment hit 170,000 firms (18% of enterprises) [MST 2025] — up 39% YoY. Government policy is the most consistent demand driver in the market.

Policy program SMEdx is the channel

MPI's SME digital transformation program (2021-2025) has trained 14,200 enterprises and deployed 32,000 advisor-hours to 400 firms by end-2024 [MPI 2025]. SMEdx and OneSME portals act as the SaaS marketplace where MISA, Base, FastWork, KiotViet bid for free-tier enrollment turning paid.

National AI ambition — with practical adoption

Vietnam's AI market hit USD 0.75 bn in 2024 [B-Company 2025] heading to USD 2.81 bn by 2033 (15% CAGR). 47,000 new AI-deploying enterprises added in 2024 [MST 2025]. IT (31%) and finance (22%) lead enterprise AI; SaaS vendors are the practical distribution layer.

Cloud-first & data residency reset

Decision 1121/QĐ-TTg (Jun 2025) commits to 100% state-agency cloud + 70% private cloud by 2030 [PM Decision 2025]. Law 91/2025 on Personal Data Protection (effective Jan 2026) [Hogan Lovells 2026] codifies the 2023 PDPD — data localization rules push local hosting and create defensive moats for VN-domiciled SaaS.

Section 05 · Sector Overview
010 / 024

Market size & growth: Vietnam SaaS

Multiple definitions in play. The narrow pure-SaaS revenue pool (operator topline) sizes USD 240 m in 2025 [Kira estimates] growing to USD 660 m by 2031 [Kira estimates]. The wider enterprise-software market reads USD 278 m in 2025 to USD 414 m by 2030 [Statista 2026]. Both numbers are right; they're measuring different things.

Three concentric envelopes

Innermost: pure SaaS subscription revenue — USD 240 m (2025) [Kira estimates]. Middle: enterprise-software bookings including on-prem licenses — USD 278 m [Statista 2026]. Outermost: cloud computing including IaaS — USD 3.5-4.0 bn [techsci 2025], where SaaS is ~45% share by deployment value. We anchor analysis on the innermost ring.

Growth distribution is uneven

Statista projects 24-25% CAGR on the public-cloud SaaS view to USD 697 m by 2029 [Statista 2026]; vendor-source views (TheReportCubes, MarknTel) cluster at 12-17% on a wider base. Our pool view: ~18% blended CAGR 2025-2031 [Kira estimates], heavier in the first three years as e-invoice compliance forces adoption.

The funding chill is incumbent-positive

2025 SaaS equity into Vietnamese startups: USD 3 m, down 62% YoY [Tracxn 2025]. New entrants starved; cash-flow-positive incumbents (MISA, KiotViet, Sapo) lock in compounding. Acquirers face the most favorable pricing window since 2018.

Section 05 · Sector Overview (cont.)
011 / 024

Where the revenue actually sits

Three product buckets — horizontal back-office, vertical operating system, and enterprise ERP — carry the pool. Vertical is the smallest absolute share but the highest growth and the deepest moat per seat.

Horizontal back-office (~45% of pool)

Accounting, HRM, payroll, basic CRM. MISA, Base, FastWork, Fast Accounting, Bravo. SME ARPU bands VND 300k-1.5 m/mo [Kira estimates]. The most price-competitive layer; e-invoice add-ons (MeInvoice) are the cheap acquisition wedge. Approaching commoditization on accounting.

Vertical operating systems (~30% & rising)

POS + inventory + e-invoice + payments + delivery, bundled per vertical. KiotViet (retail), Sapo (omnichannel), iPOS / CukCuk / Speed POS (F&B), pharmacy / clinic operators. ARPU 3-5x the horizontal layer [Kira estimates]; churn lower because workflow runs on the software.

Enterprise ERP & large-account SaaS (~25%)

SAP, Oracle, Microsoft Dynamics, NetSuite, Bravo's large-account ERP, FPT integration. Sells to the ~2% of firms employing 200+. ARPU 10-50x SMB; longer sales cycle; cloud-first decree is the demand catalyst pulling on-prem buyers to subscription.

Why vertical economics beat horizontal

The accounting bucket has 5+ credible operators (MISA, Fast, Bravo, AccNet, Misa SME) competing on price. Vertical operators (KiotViet, Sapo) bundle POS hardware + payments + e-invoice + delivery + supplier portal. Switching cost is the operational workflow, not the software. Net revenue retention 110-125% [Kira estimates] vs ~95-105% horizontal.

SMB churn and the household-business floor

Household-business churn runs 25-35% annual [Kira estimates] — the segment shuts down a lot. Operators offset via aggressive top-of-funnel (free e-invoice tier) and rapid onboarding. The mandate floor is the lifeline: once a merchant must file e-invoices, even churned customers come back.

Enterprise: longer cycle, deeper moat

SAP S/4HANA Cloud rollouts at Vingroup, Vinamilk, Masan run 9-18 months. Once live, switching costs are prohibitive. Local integrators (FPT, CMC) take 30-50% of the deal value [Kira estimates] — integration is where Vietnamese firms add the margin.

Direct self-serve dominates SMB acquisition

~60-70% of SMB seats acquired direct-online [Kira estimates] — landing page + free e-invoice tier + paid module upsell. Operators with the best onboarding-friction profile (single-form sign-up, instant invoice issuance) win the channel.

Accountants and auditors are the trusted reseller

Vietnamese SMB owners delegate software choice to their accounting firm 60-80% of the time [Kira estimates]. MISA, Fast and Bravo win here through deep auditor channel. Vertical operators have weaker accountant penetration but offset with operational urgency.

SMEdx and OneSME are the policy channel

MPI's SMEdx portal lists certified SaaS solutions at subsidized terms. Free 6-month tiers + government grant co-pay. Conversion to paid runs 25-40% [Kira estimates] — high enough to make the channel CAC-positive even at zero gross margin in year one.

Section 09 · Regulatory Landscape
020 / 024

Three regulatory tailwinds, one compliance overhead

Policy is the most reliable demand driver. E-invoice mandate (2022, tightened June 2025), cloud-first decree (June 2025), and the Personal Data Protection Law (effective Jan 2026) collectively force SaaS adoption AND create defensive moats for VN-domiciled operators.

E-invoice: from compliance to platform

Decree 123/2020 made e-invoicing mandatory from 1 July 2022 [GDT 2022]. Decree 70/2025 (effective 1 June 2025) extends to POS-connected invoicing for retail / F&B / household business [GDT 2025]. XML format, GDT real-time clearance, 10-year archival. MISA, KiotViet, Sapo, MeInvoice, Viettel-Invoice, VNPT-Invoice all certified.

Cloud-first decree pushes ERP migration

Decision 1121/QĐ-TTg (3 June 2025) [PM Decision 2025] sets cloud adoption targets: 100% state agencies and 70% private firms by 2030. Local SaaS operators (FPT, CMC, Viettel IDC) become the preferred hosting layer; foreign hyperscalers (AWS, Microsoft Azure, Google Cloud) lead workload but route through local data centers.

PDPL replaces PDPD — raised compliance bar

Law 91/2025/QH15 (effective 1 January 2026) elevates the 2023 Personal Data Protection Decree to statutory law [Hogan Lovells 2026]. Cross-border data transfer assessments, consent management, breach notification, and data localization for sensitive categories. SaaS vendors with local data residency win defensive advantage.

Section 10 · AI in vertical software
021 / 024

AI lands as a margin lever before it lands as a product

Vietnam's AI market hit USD 0.75 bn in 2024 [B-Company 2025] with 170,000 firms (~18% of enterprises) deploying AI [MST 2025]. For SaaS operators the near-term economics sit in opex compression, OCR, and seat attach — not standalone AI products. The bookkeeping copilot is the most defensible early use case.

Adoption already underway

47,000 new enterprises adopted AI in 2024 (+39% YoY) [MST 2025]. IT (31%), finance (22%), education (17%) lead by sector. MISA, FPT, CMC and Viettel are the local AI distribution layer; AWS, Microsoft and Google route enterprise workloads.

Where the ROI is

OCR for invoice + receipt classification (~70% time savings); automated reconciliation; customer-service triage; F&B menu / inventory optimization. Bookkeeping copilots are the standout: MISA's agentic AI ships Q1-2026 [MISA 2026] targeting the 250k+ SMB accounting base.

Talent is the binding constraint

Top barrier per MST: shortage of high-quality AI talent [MST 2025]. FPT, Viettel, VNG and CMC compete for the same ~5,000-10,000 specialist pool. Salary inflation 30-50% YoY for senior ML engineers [Kira estimates]. Mid-tier SaaS operators rely on partnerships with FPT AI Factory or cloud-hosted foundation models.

Infrastructure improving locally

FPT operates AI Factories in Vietnam and Japan; CMC partnered with Nvidia in 2024; Viettel runs a national cloud data center program. Foundation-model API access via AWS, Azure, Google Cloud is the dominant route — data residency rules under PDPL push sensitive workloads to local stack from 2026.

SMB AI willingness-to-pay is low

SMB ARPU bands (VND 200k-1.5 m/mo) constrain how much AI can be priced into a seat. Operators are bundling AI rather than line-item upselling. Sustained-usage drop-off after the trial period remains the most-cited barrier in operator interviews [Kira estimates].

Section 11 · Forecast 2026-2031
023 / 024

Five-year outlook: base, upside, downside

Base case: SaaS revenue pool reaches USD 660 m by 2031 at ~18% blended CAGR [Kira estimates]. Upside (cloud-first overachieves + vertical attaches lift): USD 820 m. Downside (funding chill extends, macro slowdown, SMB churn elevated): USD 510 m. Anchoring driver in all cases is enterprise count and the e-invoice mandate.

Base — USD 660 m by 2031 (18% CAGR)

Assumes enterprise count compounds at 5-6% to 1.3 m firms; SME ERP penetration rises from 14% to 28%; vertical-attach revenue per merchant grows 8-10% yearly; AI-bundled tiers deliver ~5-10% ARPU lift by 2028. Most likely scenario.

Upside — USD 820 m (22% CAGR)

Cloud-first decree hits 70% private adoption by 2028 vs 2030 target; enterprise ERP migration accelerates; AI bookkeeping copilot reaches 30% of MISA's installed base by 2028. Triggers: a successful FDI semiconductor cluster pulling SaaS demand.

Downside — USD 510 m (13% CAGR)

Macro shock (interest-rate spike or FDI pullback), SMB churn rises to 30%+, AI hype cycle dampens spend. Operators consolidate via M&A. Local triad still grows but slope flattens.
