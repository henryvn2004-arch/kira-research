A market past its trough

Singapore's green, social, sustainability and sustainability-linked (GSSSL) issuance rebounded to S$13.3 bn in 2024, up roughly 80% [MAS 2025], and outstanding sustainable bonds reached US$32.6 bn by end-2025 [ADB 2025]. The republic holds more than half of ASEAN's labelled bond and loan market [MAS 2025]. The recovery is structural — anchored by a sovereign programme and a deepening private pipeline — not a one-year cyclical bounce.

From arranging to transition

The decisive shift into 2027 is qualitative. With the Singapore-Asia Taxonomy operational and a credible transition category, plus FAST-P targeting US$5 bn of blended capital [MAS 2025], the market's centre of gravity moves from financing the unambiguously green toward financing the hard-to-abate. That is where ASEAN's US$1.5 trn-by-2030 gap [Bain-Temasek 2024] actually sits.

Five strategic implications for market participants

The 2027 window rewards those who position for transition finance and taxonomy alignment early, rather than waiting for the green-only market to re-inflate.

01
Position for transition, not green-only
The next leg of issuance is hard-to-abate. With the Singapore-Asia Taxonomy's transition category live, arrangers and issuers should build transition-instrument capability now, ahead of the demand inflection.
ANCHOR: US$1.5 trn SEA gap by 2030 [Bain-Temasek 2024]
02
Align frameworks to the taxonomy stack
Singapore's updated Green Bond Framework now maps to the Singapore-Asia Taxonomy [Allen & Gledhill 2025]. Issuers retaining legacy frameworks risk pricing penalties and slower book-building by 2027.
ANCHOR: 8 focus sectors in SAT [MAS 2023]
03
Tap concessional and blended capital
FAST-P targets US$5 bn of mobilised capital [MAS 2025]; GIP reached a US$510 m first close [MAS 2025]. Sponsors of marginally bankable projects should structure for blended layers early.
ANCHOR: US$500 m SG concessional match [MAS 2024]
04
Price the rising carbon signal
The carbon tax steps to S$45/tCO2e in 2026-2027 [NCCS 2025], strengthening transition-project economics. Model the carbon cost into financing cases now, not at refinancing.
ANCHOR: S$50-80 /tCO2e by 2030 [NCCS 2025]
05
Use the grant runway while it lasts
MAS has extended bond and loan grant schemes to 31 Dec 2028 [MAS 2025], offsetting external-review cost. First movers lock in subsidised structuring before the window closes.
ANCHOR: S$125k per loan offset [MAS 2025]

Section 05 · Market Sizing
006 / 014

Labelled-debt issuance: recovery with regional dominance

Singapore intermediates the majority of ASEAN's labelled bonds and loans. The 2024 rebound restores momentum after a soft 2022-2023, with the stock compounding into 2027.

Annual issuance rebounded sharply

GSSSL issuance reached S$13.3 bn in 2024, up about 80% year on year [MAS 2025], though still shy of the S$14.4 bn 2021 peak [MAS 2025]. Green instruments regained dominance over social and sustainability-linked formats within the mix.

The stock keeps compounding

Outstanding sustainable bonds hit US$32.6 bn by end-2025, a 25.6% rise [ADB 2025], equal to 4.3% of emerging East Asia's total [ADB 2025]. Private-sector issuance grew 29.7% to US$3.1 bn in 2025 [ADB 2025].

A regional concentration point

Singapore accounts for more than half of ASEAN's labelled bond and loan market [MAS 2025]. We see outstanding stock reaching US$45 bn by 2027 [Kira estimates], driven by sovereign supply and a recovering corporate pipeline.

Section 05 · Issuance Composition
007 / 014

Composition: public anchor, private flywheel

The outstanding stock splits almost evenly between public and private issuers — but each plays a different role. The sovereign sets the curve; corporates supply the growth.

A balanced issuer base

Public-sector bonds were 52.8% of outstanding stock at end-2025, entirely green, while private issuers held 47.2% [ADB 2025]. The sovereign and statutory boards anchor a green yield curve that private issuers price against.

The sovereign programme

The public sector plans up to S$35 bn of green bonds by 2030 [MOF 2025]. FY2024 allocated a further S$2.8 bn of proceeds to the Jurong Region Line and Cross Island Line [MOF 2025], deepening secondary liquidity.

Private momentum widening

Corporate issuance is broadening beyond financials into real assets — data centres among them, with a S$650 m green bond in 2025 [Equinix 2025]. Private issuance rose 29.7% in 2025 [ADB 2025].

Section 06 · Taxonomy Stack
008 / 014

Two taxonomies, one transition logic

The Singapore-Asia Taxonomy and ASEAN Taxonomy converge on a traffic-light approach. Both treat transition as a first-class category — the structural advantage for a hub financing hard-to-abate Asia.

Singapore-Asia Taxonomy

Launched December 2023 by the Green Finance Industry Taskforce convened by MAS, the SAT sets green and transition thresholds across eight focus sectors [MAS 2023] — energy, real estate, transport, agriculture, industrial, ICT, waste and carbon capture.

ASEAN Taxonomy V4

The ASEAN Taxonomy Board completed Version 4 in November 2025, closing all six focus and three enabling sectors of the Plus Standard [ASEAN 2025]. Its two-tier Foundation and Plus Standard design lets members adopt at differing readiness.

Transition is the differentiator

Both frameworks embed remedial transition measures with time-bound thresholds [ASEAN 2025]. SAT practical guidance issued July 2025 [SSFA 2025] makes the transition label financeable, not just definitional.
