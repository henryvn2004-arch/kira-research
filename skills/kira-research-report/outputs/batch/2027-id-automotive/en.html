Two clocks running at once

The headline market contracted to 803,687 units in 2025, down 7.2% [Gaikindo 2025], pressured by high rates and a squeezed middle class. Underneath, the powertrain mix moved fast: EV penetration reached 15.2% of passenger sales in Q2 2025 [ICCT 2025], and Chinese brands took over 75% of that pool [IEA 2026]. Five strategic implications for market participants

The 2027 contest is decided less by product than by localization pace, financing reach, and after-sales footprint. Below, the moves that matter for incumbents, entrants, and the supply base.

01
Localize before perks lapse
CBU incentives ended in 2025; only BEVs at TKDN ≥40% keep the 1% VAT from 2026. Entrants reliant on imports face a price step-up; pull local assembly forward to hold the sticker.
Anchor: TKDN 40%→60% by 2027-29 [DJP 2025]
02
Defend on hybrids, not ICE
Incumbents should treat HEV as the bridge, not diesel. The 3% PPnBM relief on hybrids preserves price competitiveness while charging anxiety caps near-term BEV conversion.
Anchor: 54% of buyers cite charging gaps [Astute 2025]
03
Compete on the monthly payment
Loans fund 83% of financing; banks hold ~50%. The buyer's mental price is the EMI. Captive-finance attach and tenor extension move more units than sticker cuts.
Anchor: Auto finance USD 42 bn 2024 [Astute 2025]
04
Treat service network as a moat
Incumbents' decades-deep authorized networks are the hardest asset to replicate. Entrants must fund service density ahead of volume or cede resale confidence.
Anchor: ~90% installed base Japanese [Nation 2025]
05
Pace the nickel-to-cell bet
Policy ties demand support to domestic nickel content. Supply-base participants should sequence cell and pack capacity to the TKDN ramp, not to spot EV demand.
Anchor: ~USD 9.25 bn Chinese capex [AMS 2025]

Section 04 · Macro Context
007 / 023

Macro indicators driving demand

A 5%+ economy with contained inflation is supportive on paper; the binding constraints for vehicle demand are the policy rate and household purchasing power, both of which ease only gradually into 2027.

Growth steady, not accelerating

GDP grew 5.01% over the first three quarters of 2025 [BI 2026], with the central bank guiding 5.33-5.4% for 2026 [BI 2026]. That is a demand backdrop consistent with mid-single-digit market recovery, not a V-shaped rebound.

Rates and rupiah cap the cycle

The policy rate sat at 4.75% in early 2026 [BI 2026] and the rupiah is guided near 16,430/USD [BCA Outlook 2025]. Both feed directly into auto demand: financing cost sets the EMI, and a soft rupiah lifts the landed cost of imported CBU and components.

Inflation gives room to ease

Headline inflation is projected at 2.62% for 2026, inside the 2.5%±1% corridor [BI 2026]. Contained prices leave space for gradual rate cuts — the single largest swing factor for the 2027 financed-purchase base.

Section 04 · Macro Context
008 / 023

Demographics and the demand floor

Indonesia remains Southeast Asia's largest car market, with a structural demand floor set by household formation, urbanization, and a still-low motorization rate — the multi-year case beneath the soft 2025 cycle.

First-time buyers are the floor

A young, urbanizing population continues to form households faster than the installed base replaces. This first-time-buyer cohort is the durable-demand floor; it is deferred by high EMIs, not erased [Kira estimates].

Credit growth is the lever

Bank Indonesia set a 8-12% credit-growth target for 2026 [BI 2026]. Auto demand is credit-led; the pace of loan expansion, more than headline GDP, governs how quickly the 2027 base recovers toward its trend.

Motorization headroom

Car penetration per capita trails regional peers, leaving structural headroom as incomes cross financing thresholds. The replacement-plus-first-buyer mix keeps the long-run trajectory upward even through a soft credit cycle [Kira estimates].

Section 05 · Sector Overview
010 / 023

Market size & growth: Indonesia automotive

Wholesale units fell to 803,687 in 2025 [Gaikindo 2025]; the passenger-car value pool sits near USD 15 bn and is set to reach ~USD 24 bn by 2030 as mix premiumizes even where unit growth is modest.

Units down, value resilient

Volume contracted 7.2% in 2025 to 803,687 units [Gaikindo 2025], yet the value pool held near USD 15.2 bn (2024) [AMR 2025]. The durable story is units-flat, ASP-up — premiumization and EV mix lift value even when the unit line is soft.

Recovery underway in 2026

The industry targets 850,000 units for 2026, +5.8% [Gaikindo 2026]; first-four-month sales were already up 12.5% to 289,787 units [Gaikindo 2026]. We read 2027 as the first clean year above the pre-shock run-rate.

Value compounding to 2030

The passenger pool is guided to ~USD 23.6 bn by 2030 at ~7.6% CAGR [AMR 2025]. The EV slice alone reached USD 3.8 bn in 2025 [PSM 2025] — the fastest-compounding component of the value base.

Section 05 · Sector Overview
011 / 023

Segment economics: ICE core, hybrid bridge, EV edge

The mix splits three ways. The ICE/LCGC core still drives volume, hybrids are the defended bridge, and the EV pool — though ~15% of sales — carries the value and narrative growth.

ICE and LCGC: the volume core

Affordable MPVs and low-cost green cars — Avanza, Sigra, Brio, the Kijang line — remain the volume engine [Gaikindo 2025]. Margins are thin and price-led; this is the base the incumbents defend on cost and service, not features.

Hybrid: the defended bridge

HEVs replaced diesel as the practical step-up [Nation 2025], aided by a 3% PPnBM relief [DJP 2025]. For incumbents the hybrid line protects ASP and buys time against full electrification while charging gaps persist.

EV: small base, fast slope

BEV+PHEV reached 15.2% of passenger sales in Q2 2025 [ICCT 2025]; PHEVs grew over 1,300% quarter-on-quarter off a tiny base [ICCT 2025]. High value-per-unit makes this the disproportionate driver of the value pool.

Channel & distribution: financing is the demand multiplier

The purchase decision runs through credit, not cash. With loans funding 83% of financing and banks holding ~50%, the captive-finance attach rate and the service-network footprint decide who converts intent into units.

Credit is the lever

Auto financing reached USD 41.56 bn in 2024 [Astute 2025]; loans account for 83% of volume and banks for nearly half [Astute 2025]. The buyer's mental price is the EMI — tenor and rate move more units than headline discounts.

Network sets resale confidence

The incumbents' authorized service-and-parts network is the channel moat. It anchors total cost of ownership and resale value — the hardest asset for entrants to replicate, and a P&L driver in its own right [Kira estimates].

Charging gates conversion

54% of potential buyers cite Indonesia's uneven charging network as a deterrent [Astute 2025]. Until coverage deepens, BEV demand stays urban-concentrated and hybrids hold the bridge.

Section 08 · Regulatory & Policy
019 / 023

Regulatory & policy: the local-content gate

Policy has pivoted from adoption subsidies to a localization gate. CBU import perks ended in 2025; from 2026 only locally assembled BEVs at TKDN ≥40% keep the 1% VAT — and the bar climbs to 60% by 2027-29.

From import perks to local content

Import-duty, luxury-tax and reduced-VAT relief on CBU EVs expired 31 December 2025 [DJP 2025]. The signal is a deliberate shift from demand pull toward building a domestic production ecosystem.

The 2026+ TKDN regime

From 2026, locally assembled BEVs at TKDN ≥40% retain the 1% VAT rate; the threshold rises to 60% in 2027-29 and 80% from 2030 [DJP 2025]. A new PPN-DTP facility covers ~200,000 EV units from June 2026 to support domestic nickel use [Babel 2026].

Hybrids keep relief

HEVs retain a 3% PPnBM relief under the low-carbon program [DJP 2025] — the policy backbone of the incumbents' hybrid bridge while charging coverage catches up.

Automotive is the largest segment of Indonesia's smart-factory build-out and a priority under the national 4.0 agenda. The near-term return is operational — downtime, quality, and channel inventory — not consumer-facing novelty.

Factory floor leads

Budgets are committing

Dealer network next

Demand sensing across the dealer network is the channel-side prize — reducing inventory days for incumbents with hundreds of outlets, and accelerating entrants' thin networks [Kira estimates].

5-year outlook & forecast

Base case

Total units recover to ~850k in 2026 [Gaikindo 2026] and ~905k in 2027, with EV share near 19% [Kira estimates]. Value compounds faster than units as mix premiumizes toward HEV and BEV.
