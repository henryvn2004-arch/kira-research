Thailand's data center market — a 2027 landing test

A ~USD 1.9 bn market in 2025 sits beneath a pipeline that drew over THB 728 bn of BOI applications, yet only ~150–350 MW is live. Firm renewable power, not demand, sets the pace at which EEC campuses energize.

The setup

Thailand's data center market reached roughly USD 1.9 bn in 2025, on a path toward USD 4.9 bn by 2031 at a ~17% CAGR [DC Portfolio 2025]. Live IT capacity sat near 350 MW exiting 2024 [Bloomberg 2025]. The ambition dwarfs that base: BOI logged 36 data-center applications worth THB 728 bn in 2025 [BOI 2025], with ByteDance alone committing up to USD 8.8 bn across three provinces [DCD 2025].

The constraint

The bottleneck is physical and increasingly green. Hyperscalers tie siting to clean power, so the unlock is the 2,000 MW Direct PPA pilot and the Utility Green Tariff [ERC 2025]. The PDP 2024–2037 plans 73 GW of renewables [PDP 2024], but campus energization tracks grid-connection windows and PPA rules — both of which slipped into review in late 2025.

Section 03 · Executive Summary

Five strategic implications for market participants

Five forces govern who captures Thailand's data center build-out through 2027. Each is anchored to documented data and KIRA synthesis.

01 · POWER
Clean power is the binding constraint
The PDP 2024–2037 funds 73 GW of renewables toward a 51% RE share by 2037 [PDP 2024]. But campuses energize on grid-connection windows; the 2,000 MW Direct PPA pilot is the mechanism, and its criteria slipped into review. Power readiness, not demand, sets the 2027 ceiling.
Anchor: 2,000 MW PPA pool
02 · THE EEC
Rayong and Chonburi anchor the build
In 2025 BOI filings, Rayong took 33% and Chonburi 32% of data-center investment, Samut Prakan 12% [BOI 2025]. The EEC pairs 15-year tax holidays and duty-free imports with cable-landing access, cutting project cost by up to 20% [DCD 2025].
Anchor: 65% EEC share
03 · FOREIGN CAPITAL
Hyperscalers shift to self-build
AWS commits USD 5 bn through 2037; Google is building a Chonburi campus; ByteDance pledged up to USD 8.8 bn [DCD 2025]. Hyperscale and self-built capacity now exceed 80% of demand, displacing pure colocation [DC Portfolio 2025].
Anchor: USD 5 bn AWS
04 · POLICY
Cloud-First builds a demand floor
The “Go Cloud First” policy, effective Oct 2025, mandates cloud priority for government IT [DEPA 2025]; PDPA enforcement and a digital economy targeting 30% of GDP by 2027 anchor structural demand [NESDC 2025] independent of the AI cycle.
Anchor: 30% GDP target
05 · GREEN PPA
PPA design decides who lands
The Utility Green Tariff launched UGT1 in Jan 2025 with ~2,000 GWh of hydropower-backed supply [UGT 2025]; UGT2 and Direct PPA criteria were paused for restudy [ERC 2025]. Carbon-target tenants commit only where clean supply is contractible.
Anchor: 2,000 GWh UGT1

Section 04 · Market Sizing
007 / 017

Capacity roughly triples, revenue more than doubles by 2031

Two clocks run at different speeds. Installed capacity climbs from ~350 MW toward ~1 GW by 2027 and beyond; market value compounds as AI-grade racks and hyperscale colocation lift revenue per MW.

Capacity curve

Live IT capacity was about 350 MW exiting 2024, with industry power scaling toward ~1 GW by 2027 [Bloomberg 2025]. One dataset models capacity rising from ~0.77 GW in 2025 to ~2.93 GW by 2030, a ~30% CAGR [DC Portfolio 2025] — the ceiling, not the base case, given grid limits.

Revenue curve

Market value stood near USD 1.9 bn in 2025, on track for USD 4.9 bn by 2031 (~17% CAGR) [DC Portfolio 2025]. Krungsri Research takes a more cautious near-term read of 7.5–8.5% annual growth over 2025–2027 [Krungsri 2025].

Footprint

The colocation market spans 39 existing and 27 upcoming facilities across 34 operators [DC Portfolio 2025]. Bangkok holds the live base, but Rayong, Chonburi and Bangkok account for over 45% of upcoming power capacity [DC Portfolio 2025].

Section 04 · Demand Drivers
008 / 017

Three demand engines: AI compute, cloud regions, and Cloud-First policy

Demand is the structurally easy side. An AI-investment wave, hyperscaler cloud-region landings, and a Cloud-First government mandate each pull capacity into the EEC — and they reinforce each other.

AI compute

AI-ready infrastructure already accounts for about 28% of Thailand's data center capacity [Bloomberg 2025]. Hyperscalers and local operators are deploying GPU clusters for sovereign and enterprise AI; these racks draw far more power per cabinet, amplifying the grid question on page 010 and pulling siting toward firm clean power.

Cloud regions

AWS committed USD 5 bn through 2037 for a Thailand region projected to add USD 10 bn to GDP; Google is building in Chonburi; Microsoft named Thailand for a regional cloud build [DCD 2025]. Hyperscale and self-built demand now exceeds 80% of the total, shifting the market away from pure colocation [DC Portfolio 2025].

Cloud-First & localization

The “Go Cloud First” policy (Oct 2025) mandates cloud priority for government IT [DEPA 2025], and PDPA enforcement plus the 30%-of-GDP digital-economy target convert policy into durable demand independent of the AI cycle [NESDC 2025].

Section 05 · Power & the Grid
010 / 017

Power supply is the gate, clean power is the lock

The PDP 2024–2037 commits to a large generation build, but the binding question for EEC campuses is where and when firm, clean load reaches the meter — and on what contract.

The plan

The PDP 2024–2037 lifts total demand from ~51 GW in 2024 to ~112 GW by 2037, with renewables rising to a 51% fuel share — about 73 GW of RE capacity, over half of it solar [PDP 2024]. Gas and coal fall from ~80% toward 48% across the plan.

The constraint

Headline generation is not the issue; firm clean supply contractible to a single campus is. EGAT supplies ~44% of generation and anchors the grid [PDP 2024]. The current retail tariff sits near THB 4.15 per unit, and a guaranteed-offtake rule of THB 4.5 mn per MW has been floated to deter speculative filings [BOI 2025].

The clean-power fix

Sustainability is now a campus-design input: tenants with carbon targets pair load with the Utility Green Tariff and Direct PPA rather than vanilla grid supply [ERC 2025]. Clean siting, not just raw megawatts, increasingly decides which hyperscale tenants commit.

Section 05 · The Operational Gap
011 / 017

The gap between announced and energized

The pipeline reads in gigawatts of intent; the meter reads in hundreds of megawatts of delivery. Closing that gap by 2027 is a power-and-PPA problem, not a demand one.

What is live

National live IT capacity was about 350 MW exiting 2024 [Bloomberg 2025], with Bangkok carrier-neutral colocation near 105–113 MW [DC Portfolio 2025]. The base is real but thin against the announced pipeline.

What is announced

The EEC pipeline is large. True IDC plans a THB 77 bn EEC mega-campus (223 MW IT load) [BOI 2025]; a Gulf–Singtel–AIS JV (GSA) reserves ~120 MW in Rayong [BOI 2025]; Bridge Data Centres is approved for 134 MW and Skyline (DAMAC) for 200 MW [DCD 2025]. BOI logged 36 applications worth THB 728 bn in 2025 [BOI 2025].

What lands by 2027

Most flagship campuses target 2026–2028 phased energization, tied to grid windows and PPA approval. [Kira estimates] put realized 2027 live capacity near 0.9–1.0 GW — a large step up, but a fraction of the headline pipeline.

Section 05 · PPA Structures & Rules
012 / 017

Three routes to clean power; data rules set the demand floor

Two enablers complete the constraint picture: a renewable-procurement menu — Direct PPA, UGT, on-site — that decides who can land carbon-target tenants, and a data-policy regime that guarantees baseline local demand.

Direct PPA via Third-Party Access

In June 2024 the NEPC approved a pilot letting data centers procure up to 2,000 MW of renewables directly from generators over the national grid via Third-Party Access [ERC 2025]. The ERC issued draft criteria in Oct 2025, but the energy policy committee ordered a restudy of both Direct PPA and UGT2 rules — the key 2026 catalyst to watch.

Utility Green Tariff

UGT1 launched in Jan 2025 with about 2,000 GWh/year of EGAT hydropower-backed supply plus RECs, sold without specifying source; UGT2 (source-specified) was designed for buyers needing additionality [UGT 2025]. Over 46 data-center projects have signalled green-power demand [ERC 2025].

Data policy

Thailand imposes no blanket foreign-data localization, but PDPA (in force since 2022) and the Oct-2025 Cloud-First mandate channel public-sector and regulated data onshore [DEPA 2025] — anchoring colocation demand beneath the hyperscale layer.

Methodology

Where a figure carries [Kira estimates], KIRA analysts derived it from a blend of disclosed inputs — BOI filings, regulator rulings, the national power plan — using the method disclosed in the relevant section.

Capacity figures reconcile published market datasets against operator and BOI disclosure; the live-versus-pipeline gap and the 2027 scenario fan are KIRA constructions. Approximately 25% of quantitative claims are KIRA-derived; the remainder are externally cited.
