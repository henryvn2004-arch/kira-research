How we built this view

This view is built from two evidence streams: primary inputs — dealer-floor walks, smelter-operator reads, and analyst synthesis — and secondary anchors — statistical authorities, listed-company filings, and triangulated estimates. Every quantitative claim carries an explicit source tag.

Primary inputs

Dealer-floor checks

Industrial-park reads

Analyst synthesis

Secondary anchors

Statistical authorities
BPS (Badan Pusat Statistik), Bank Indonesia, Ministry of Energy and Mineral Resources (ESDM), Ministry of Industry (Kemenperin), Ministry of Finance — cited inline by named source.
Listed-company filings

Triangulated estimates
Where no single source covers a figure, we combine 2-3 inputs and disclose the method. Tagged [Kira estimates]. Aim for 20-30% of total quantitative claims to keep the analyst-house voice without over-extending.

Nickel-led integration

Indonesia produced 2.2 Mt of mined nickel in 2024 [ESDM 2025], holding 59% of global supply and 42% of reserves. HPAL capacity targeted for end-2026 — Merdeka MBMA's 90 kt high-purity line [MBMA 2025], Vale-Ford-Huayou Pomalaa USD 4.5 bn project [Vale IR 2025], and BASF-Eramet 67 kt nickel-cobalt refinery [Eramet 2025] — converts Class 2 laterite into battery-grade Class 1 sulfate for the first time at scale.

Cell capacity unlocks the TKDN gate

The Hyundai-LG Karawang plant (10 GWh, operational 2024) [Hyundai PR 2024] and CATL-IBC Karawang (6.9 GWh, mid-2026 start) [CATL PR 2025] together deliver enough domestic cell volume to clear the 40% TKDN threshold for 2026-built BEVs. The 60% step-up in 2027 pulls OEMs toward NMC cells over LFP imports.

Section 03 · Strategic Implications
005 / 029

Five strategic implications for the next 18 months

Market participants should treat the mid-2026 to end-2027 window as the structural decision point — not the 2030 net-zero target. TKDN sourcing decisions, cell-platform choices and dealer-finance footprints made now lock in incentive eligibility through the end of the decade.

IMPLICATION · 01
Treat the nickel-NMC path as the policy-favoured bet
The June 2026 PPN DTP scheme grants 100% VAT relief to nickel-based NMC cells and only 40% to non-nickel chemistries [Kemenkeu 2026]. LFP imports keep volume cost down but lose the incentive arbitrage; the 1,400 bps tax wedge widens NMC's effective price advantage from late 2026.
100% vs 40% VAT relief
IMPLICATION · 02
Lock in Karawang or Subang assembly before the 60% TKDN step-up
The TKDN gate climbs from 40% (2022-2026) to 60% (2027-2029) to 80% (2030+) [Perpres 79/2023]. OEMs without an Indonesian cell-pack relationship by Q3 2026 lose incentive eligibility from January 2027. BYD's Subang plant launch [BYD 2025] and Hyundai's Karawang anchor are the reference cases.
60% TKDN by 2027
IMPLICATION · 03
Anchor 2W strategy on conversion + entry-price OEMs, not premium
Electric 2W penetration sits at ~1% of the 6.3m annual two-wheeler market [AISI 2025] despite a target of 12m units by 2030 [Kemenperin 2024]. The Rp 7m purchase / Rp 10m conversion subsidy is paused as of Q1 2026 [Jakarta Post 2025]; redesigned scheme expected to launch alongside the June 2026 PPN DTP package.
12m 2W target 2030
IMPLICATION · 04
Plan upstream around HPAL completion timing
Three HPAL projects target end-2026 commissioning: Merdeka MBMA 90 kt [MBMA 2025], Vale-Ford-Huayou USD 4.5 bn [Vale IR 2025], BASF-Eramet 67 kt [Eramet 2025]. Pomalaa and Morowali timing slips by 1-2 quarters from current guidance per analyst reads [Kira estimates]. Off-take agreements signed pre-completion command 8-15% premium.
USD 8.6 bn HPAL capex
IMPLICATION · 05
Treat dealer finance as the demand multiplier
BYD captured 39.7% of BEV wholesale Jan-May 2025 [Databoks 2025] on the back of aggressive PPN-bundled financing through Astra-affiliated dealers. Credit-attach rate on retail BEV sales is the volume multiplier — OEMs without a captive or partnered finance line lose share to BYD-style zero-down structures.

Macro indicators driving EV demand

Indonesia's 280m-person economy expands at 5%-plus through the forecast period [IMF WEO 2026], anchoring household formation and middle-class motorization. Urban demand pull is one engine; downstream commodity industrialization is the other — Jakarta and Surabaya absorb retail BEV adoption while Morowali and Karawang absorb capex.

Growth pace

Real GDP expanded 5.0% YoY in 2025 [BPS 2026]. The IMF April 2026 WEO sets 2026 growth at 5.1% and 2027 at 5.0% [IMF WEO 2026]; the government's RPJMN 2025-2029 target is 5.8%, anchored on the nickel-downstream multiplier [Bappenas 2025]. EV-relevant subsegments (passenger vehicles, household durables) typically run 1.2-1.4x headline GDP growth [Kira estimates].

Population & urbanization

The mid-2025 estimate puts population at 281.6m [BPS 2026]; the urban share sits at 58.6% (165m people) [BPS 2026]. Middle-class households cluster in the Jabodetabek, Surabaya-Gresik, Bandung and Medan corridors. Vehicle ownership stands at 95 cars per 1,000 people [Gaikindo 2026] — well below Thailand's 280 — giving room for a first-time-buyer cohort to shift directly into BEV.

Inflation & rates

CPI inflation tracked 2.6% in 2025 [BPS 2026], inside Bank Indonesia's 2.5±1% target. The rate backdrop keeps consumer-credit pricing competitive — BYD's 0% DP financing through Astra Credit Companies [Databoks 2025] sits on that base.

Section 04 · Macro Context
008 / 029

Policy timeline: from Perpres 55 to the 2030 TKDN 80%

Indonesia's EV transition runs on a clear policy ladder anchored by Perpres 55/2019 and its 2023 revision (Perpres 79) [Sekneg 2023]. The next two rungs — the June 2026 nickel-tilted PPN DTP and the 60% TKDN step-up in Jan 2027 — drive the demand window.

Anchor framework

Perpres 79/2023 reset the BEV regime [Sekneg 2023]: TKDN floor stays at 40% through 2026, climbs to 60% in 2027-2029, and 80% from 2030. PMK 38/2023 cut VAT on locally-assembled BEVs from 11% to 1% [Kemenkeu 2023]; Perpres 79 added a luxury-tax (PPnBM) exemption for CBU imports through 2025. The CBU import window closed 31 December 2025 [Jakarta Post 2025].

June 2026 nickel-tilted incentive

From June 2026, PPN DTP shifts to a chemistry-tilted scheme: 100% VAT relief for nickel-based NMC BEVs, 40% for non-nickel (LFP-import) BEVs [Kemenkeu 2026]. Allocation: 200k units across 4W and 2W [Indonesia Business Post 2026]. The policy explicitly favours the domestic nickel-NMC chain over imported LFP cells.

Subsidy & tariff

Electric motorcycle purchase subsidy (Rp 7m new / Rp 10m conversion) was paused January 2025 [Jakarta Post 2025]; relaunch expected alongside the June 2026 PPN DTP. Luxury-tax exemption on BEV passenger cars remains in force through 2027 [Tilleke 2025]. Import duty on CKD components zero-rated for OEMs meeting the 40% TKDN floor.

Market size & growth: Indonesia EV 2024-2030

The total EV value pool runs from USD 1.8 bn in 2025 to USD 5.5 bn by 2030, a ~25% CAGR [Kira estimates]. Four-wheelers contribute majority dollar value through the forecast; battery cells deliver the deepest downstream margin pool.

Total EV market

USD 1.8 bn in 2025 → USD 2.4 bn in 2026 → USD 5.5 bn by 2030 at a 25% CAGR [Kira estimates anchored on Gaikindo 2026 + IndexBox 2025]. A parallel third-party read places the EV battery market specifically at USD 900m in 2025 rising to USD 2.0 bn by 2032 at 12% CAGR [PSMR 2025]. We adopt the higher vehicle-plus-cell view as the planning case.

Four-wheeler EV value pool

The 4W BEV pool runs from ~USD 1.6 bn in 2025 to ~USD 4.6 bn by 2030 [Kira estimates anchored on Gaikindo + ICCT]. 2025 retail BEV sales were 114,413 units (+103.6% YoY) [Gaikindo 2026]; BEV penetration reached 10% of total passenger-car sales [ICCT 2025], up from less than 1% in 2023.

Two-wheeler EV value pool

Electric 2W is the long tail — ~1% of the 6.3m annual 2W market in 2025 [AISI 2025], targeting 12m units cumulative by 2030 [Kemenperin 2024]. Value pool runs from ~USD 0.2 bn in 2025 to ~USD 0.9 bn by 2030 [Kira estimates], gated on subsidy redesign and the entry-OEM pipeline.

Section 05 · Sector Sizing · Nickel
011 / 029

The nickel-to-cell value chain: where the margin sits

Indonesia mines 59% of global nickel [ESDM 2025] but historically captured only the smelting margin. The 2026-2027 HPAL completions move USD 12-15 bn of incremental annual value-chain revenue onshore [Kira estimates] — first to MHP and nickel sulfate, then to pCAM and cell packs.

Mining + smelting (upstream)

2024 mined nickel output of 2.2 Mt [ESDM 2025] feeds 51 operating smelters, of which 44 are RKEF (ferronickel/NPI) and 7 are HPAL (MHP/sulfate) [ESDM 2025]. Class 1 battery-grade share rises from ~5% of output in 2024 to ~20% by 2027 [Kira estimates] as HPAL capacity comes online.

Precursor + cathode (midstream)

pCAM (precursor cathode active material) is the value-multiplier step — Morowali's integrated complex hosts the first commercial-scale pCAM lines [Kemenperin 2025]. The Gotion-ANEM HPAL project adds 120 kt/y MHP capacity [Kontan 2025]. Margin runs ~2x of smelter-gate ferronickel.

Cell assembly + pack (downstream)

Hyundai-LG Karawang 10 GWh operational since July 2024 [Hyundai PR 2024]; CATL-IBC Karawang 6.9 GWh targeted for mid-2026 [CATL PR 2025]; second-phase Hyundai-LG +20 GWh in planning [LGES 2025]. Combined ~32 GWh by 2027F [Kira estimates] — enough for ~500k BEV equivalents.

Section 06 · Segment Economics
012 / 029

Segment economics: where the unit margin lives

The unit-margin geography differs sharply by segment. Cell assembly delivers 12-18% gross margin; smelter ferronickel runs 4-8% [Kira estimates]. BEV OEMs operate on 8-12% blended pack-plus-vehicle margin under the 2026 TKDN regime.

4W BEV unit economics

Average retail BEV ASP in 2025 was Rp 425m (~USD 26k) [Gaikindo 2026], pulled up by BYD Atto 3 / Seal and Hyundai Ioniq 5 mix. Battery cost per kWh fell from USD 149 in 2024 to a projected USD 80 by 2026 [Kemenperin 2026] as domestic cells displace imports; pack-level cost is 28-32% of vehicle BOM at current chemistry.

2W BEV unit economics

Mass-tier electric 2W retails at Rp 18-32m (~USD 1.1-2.0k) [Kira estimates from dealer floor]. Margin is gated by battery cost — a 1.5 kWh pack runs USD 120-180 at current chemistry. The Rp 7m subsidy lifts unit margin from 4% to ~14% [Kira estimates]; without it, the entry-OEM unit economics collapse.

Cell assembly margin

Hyundai-LG Karawang reports run-rate gross margin of 15-18% on the 10 GWh line at full ramp [LGES 2025]. CATL-IBC margin guidance not disclosed; we model 12-16% steady-state on similar ASEAN benchmarks [Kira estimates]. Margin compresses if Indonesian nickel sulfate input premium exceeds 8% vs Chinese feed.

Strategic position

BYD entered Indonesia in early 2024 with the Atto 3, Dolphin and Seal — three models spanning Rp 425-1,200m. By Jan-May 2025, the brand captured 39.7% of BEV wholesale volume [Databoks 2025] and ranked 6th in total auto sales [Jakarta Globe 2025]. Captive Blade LFP cells and vertically-integrated mining footprint (lithium, battery materials) create a cost stack incumbents can't match without 3-4 years lead.

Local assembly play

USD 1.3 bn Subang plant ramps mid-2026 with 150k units/year capacity at full run [BYD 2025]. Local content target 40% in 2026, 60% by 2027 matching the TKDN ladder. Pack assembly localized; cell import from BYD China for the first 18 months, then evaluated against domestic NMC alternatives under the June 2026 PPN DTP framework.

Strengths

  • Captive Blade LFP cells deliver pack-level cost ~30% below open-market alternatives
  • Astra-affiliated dealer pipeline via partnership — 150 outlets by end-2025, target 250 by 2027
  • Three-model lineup spans Rp 425-1,200m — covers entry-premium gap competitors can't address
  • Aggressive 0% DP financing through Astra Credit Companies sets the floor for retail demand

Watch-outs

  • LFP chemistry loses the 100% PPN DTP tier from June 2026 — only 40% relief vs nickel-NMC's 100% [Kemenkeu 2026]
  • Subang plant timing slip would push TKDN 2027 60% step-up exposure
  • Indonesian government scrutiny on Chinese-import dependence sharpening through 2026

Section 08 · Demand drivers

Demand pull factors: three engines, three frictions

Retail BEV demand runs on three engines — incentive arbitrage, fuel-price spread, urban-pollution narrative — and three frictions — charging-density gap, residual-value uncertainty, and outer-island infrastructure thinness. The June 2026 PPN DTP reset adds a 14-pp price wedge for nickel-NMC vehicles [Kemenkeu 2026].

Incentive arbitrage

The 1% VAT on locally-assembled BEVs [Kemenkeu 2023] vs 11% on ICE delivers a Rp 40-65m spread at the Rp 400-650m segment. From June 2026, nickel-NMC BEVs add 100% PPN DTP relief vs 40% for LFP — another Rp 20-35m gap at retail [Kira estimates].

Fuel-price spread

Pertalite RON-90 at Rp 10,000/litre [Pertamina 2025] vs PLN home tariff at Rp 1,500/kWh [PLN 2025] yields a per-km cost of Rp 850 for ICE 4W vs Rp 280 for BEV — a 3x running-cost spread that pays back the BEV premium in 4-5 years at 15k km/year [Kira estimates].

Frictions on the demand curve

Charging-density gap remains the dominant friction outside Greater Jakarta and Surabaya. Residual-value uncertainty hangs over BYD and Chery in particular — neither has 5+ years of resale data in-market. Outer-island infrastructure (Sumatra, Kalimantan, Sulawesi) lags Java by 18-24 months [PLN 2026].

Section 09 · Regulatory landscape
022 / 029

Regulatory & policy landscape

The Indonesian EV regulatory stack rests on Perpres 79/2023, layered with PMK 38/2023 VAT relief, the June 2026 PPN DTP reset, and the TKDN ladder. The compliance burden is concentrated on local-content sourcing, not safety or emissions standards.

Anchor regulations

Perpres 55/2019 first set the BEV acceleration framework; Perpres 79/2023 reset incentive structure and TKDN ladder [Sekneg 2023]. PMK 38/2023 cut VAT on locally-assembled BEVs from 11% to 1% [Kemenkeu 2023]. PMK 8/2024 added customs-duty relief on imported CKD components meeting TKDN floor.

TKDN ladder

The local-content ladder gates incentive eligibility: 40% (2022-2026), 60% (2027-2029), 80% (2030+) [Sekneg 2023]. Kemenperin issued enforcement notices in August 2025 reminding BYD and peers to lock in compliance plans [Kompas 2025]. Penalties for shortfall: loss of PPN DTP eligibility, not import bans.

June 2026 chemistry-tilt

The PPN DTP scheme from June 2026 grants 100% VAT relief for nickel-NMC, 40% for LFP and other non-nickel [Kemenkeu 2026, Indonesia Business Post 2026]. Annual allocation: 200k units across 4W and 2W. This is the first chemistry-tilted incentive in ASEAN — explicitly designed to monetize Indonesia's nickel position.

Section 11 · 5-Year Outlook

5-year outlook: base, bull, bear to 2030

Base case: BEV penetration reaches ~22% of passenger-car sales by 2030 [Kira estimates], on the back of nickel-NMC incentive arbitrage + falling cell cost. Bull adds another 8-10 pp on subsidy redesign + Hyundai/Wuling sub-Rp 400m models. Bear cuts 6-8 pp on TKDN enforcement slip or HPAL delays.

Base case (P50)

2025 → 2030: BEV sales 114k → 365k units, value pool USD 1.8 bn → USD 5.5 bn at 25% CAGR [Kira estimates]. BEV penetration of new PC sales reaches 22%. Nickel value-chain GMV expands USD 7.4 bn → USD 16.5 bn at 17% CAGR. Cell capacity reaches ~50 GWh installed; ~38 GWh utilized.

Bull case (P80) — +30%

Subsidy redesign for 2W electric launches Q3 2026 with redesigned Rp 7m + conversion bonus. Hyundai or Wuling launches a Rp 350-380m B-segment BEV by H2 2027. BYD ramps Subang to 150k units/yr by 2028. BEV penetration of 4W new sales pushes 30%+ by 2030; nickel value chain clears USD 20 bn.

Bear case (P20) — -22%

TKDN enforcement slips into 2028 under industry lobbying; PPN DTP allocation cut from 200k to 100k units in 2027; HPAL commissioning at Pomalaa + Morowali slips 12-18 months. BEV penetration stalls at 14% of 4W new sales; nickel margin pool grows but value capture stays in mining, not midstream.
