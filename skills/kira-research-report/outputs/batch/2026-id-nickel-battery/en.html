Indonesia's nickel-battery chain — 2026 localization gate

Indonesia already holds the upstream. The 2026 contest is downstream: who converts MHP into cells under a tightening local-content regime. The 18 months from mid-2026 to end-2027 — bounded by the nickel-tilted PPN DTP incentive (June 2026) and the 60% TKDN step-up (Jan 2027) — decide which operators lock the gate and which stay stranded at intermediate margin.

Upstream won, downstream contested

Indonesia mined 2.2 Mt of nickel in 2024 [ESDM 2025], roughly 59% of global supply, and lifted MHP output to ~482 kt in 2025 [Argus 2026]. Raw ore fetches USD 30-50/t while battery-grade sulfate clears USD 15,000-20,000/t — a 300-400x value multiplier [Jakarta Post 2026]. But Indonesian-owned firms still hold only ~13% of refining capacity [CREA 2025]; the contested value sits in precursor, cathode and cells.

The Korean JV split the chain

LG Energy Solution exited the USD 9.8 bn Grand Package in April 2025 [Petromindo 2025], replaced by a Huayou-EVE consortium [Jakarta Globe 2025]. Yet the Hyundai-LGES HLI Green Power cell plant — 10 GWh, operational since 2024 [Hyundai PR 2024] — stays. The Korean play narrowed from full-chain integration to cell assembly; Chinese partners absorbed the upstream-to-precursor risk.

Section 03 · Strategic Implications
005 / 028

Five strategic implications for the next 18 months

Market participants should treat mid-2026 to end-2027 as the structural decision window — not the 2030 capacity target. Off-take contracts, cell-platform choices and TKDN-component pathways set now lock in incentive eligibility and margin position through the end of the decade.

IMPLICATION · 01
Read the chain as a margin ladder, not a volume story
Ore-to-sulfate multiplies value 300-400x [Jakarta Post 2026], but Indonesian-owned firms hold only ~13% of refining capacity [CREA 2025]. Stranding at MHP forfeits the precursor and cathode margin where the policy is now pointed.
300-400x ore→sulfate value
IMPLICATION · 02
The nickel-NMC tilt is now written into the tax code
The June 2026 PPN DTP scheme grants 100% VAT relief to nickel-NMC EVs and only 40% to non-nickel chemistries [Kemenkeu 2026]. The 60-point wedge engineers demand toward locally-sourced nickel cells over imported LFP.
100% vs 40% VAT relief
IMPLICATION · 03
Lock a domestic cell relationship before the 60% TKDN step-up
TKDN climbs from 40% (2026) to 60% (2027-29) to 80% (2030) [Perpres 79/2023]. OEMs without an Indonesian cell-pack tie by Q3 2026 lose eligibility from January 2027. HLI Green Power and IBC-CATL are the reference anchors.
60% TKDN by 2027
IMPLICATION · 04
Price in partner turnover as a structural, not one-off, risk
LG's exit from the USD 9.8 bn Grand Package [Petromindo 2025] handed the upstream-to-precursor leg to a Huayou-EVE consortium [Jakarta Globe 2025]. Non-Chinese off-take diversification is unproven — IBC courted alternatives through 2025 with no confirmed deal [CSIS 2025].
USD 9.8 bn package re-cut
IMPLICATION · 05
Treat the 2026 supply squeeze as a margin reset, not noise
The 2026 RKAB ore quota was cut ~28% to 270 Mt wmt [Mysteel 2026]; royalties shift to a 14-19% progressive band [ORF 2025]. HPAL break-even near USD 18,000/t [Discovery 2026] squeezes sub-scale operators — integration and captive power decide who survives.

Section 04 · Macro Context
007 / 028

Macro indicators behind the downstream push

Indonesia's ~280m-person economy grows at 5%-plus through the forecast period [IMF WEO 2026], but the battery story runs on a policy engine, not consumer demand. Hilirisasi — the ban-and-build downstreaming doctrine — is the macro variable that matters, converting raw ore advantage into manufactured-value ambition.

Growth pace

Real GDP grew ~5.0% across the first nine months of 2025 [World Bank 2025]; the government holds a 5.4% target for 2026 [Business Indonesia 2025], anchored on the nickel-downstream multiplier in the RPJMN 2025-2029. Battery-chain capex is a leading contributor to the investment line.

Inflation & rates

CPI tracked ~1.9% in 2025 and is projected near 3.1% in 2026 as the rupiah feeds through [OECD 2025]. Bank Indonesia held its 7-day reverse repo at 4.75% [OECD 2025], keeping project-finance pricing workable for the USD 15 bn-plus battery-chain pipeline announced 2023-25 [IEA 2026].

The hilirisasi current

Nickel export value rose from USD 5 bn (2015) to USD 33 bn (2023) — a 600%-plus jump under the raw-ore export ban [East Asia Forum 2026]. The doctrine now pushes past metal into cells; the February 2025 FX-retention rule keeps mineral export proceeds onshore for a full year [S&P 2025].

Section 04 · Macro Context
008 / 028

The 2025-26 policy tightening repriced the cost map

A cluster of resource-nationalism moves in 2025-26 reset upstream economics. Quota cuts, progressive royalties, an FX-retention rule and a mooted low-grade export tax together pull capital toward integrated, downstream-anchored operators — and away from standalone ore and intermediate plays.

Quota & price

The 2026 RKAB ore quota was set at 270 Mt wmt, down ~28% from 375 Mt in 2025 and below the ~345 Mt the market expected [Mysteel 2026]. The tightening lifted reference prices and narrowed the surplus that had capped LME nickel through 2025 [ING 2026].

Royalty & tax

From 2025 the flat 10% ore royalty shifts to a progressive 14-19% band keyed to benchmark prices [ORF 2025]. The government is also weighing an export duty on nickel products below 70% nickel content [ANTARA 2025] — a direct nudge to push past ferronickel and NPI into battery-grade chemistry.

Capital controls

The February 2025 rule mandates that natural-resource export earnings stay in domestic banks for 12 months [S&P 2025]. The combined effect is a higher onshore cost of carry that favours vertically integrated balance sheets [Kira estimates].

Section 05 · Sector Sizing
009 / 028

Where the chain is built — and where it is not

Indonesia ran 40-plus nickel smelters by 2025 [Discovery 2026] and lifted MHP past 482 kt [Argus 2026], but cell output is a rounding error against the 140 GWh 2030 target [Kemenperin 2024]. The volume gap between intermediate and cell is the sizing story.

Upstream at scale

Over 40 RKEF and HPAL smelters were operating by 2025, drawing USD 6 bn-plus of processing investment in 2023-25 [Discovery 2026]. MHP output reached ~482 kt nickel-equivalent in 2025 and is guided to nearly double to ~862 kt in 2026 as new HPAL trains commission [Argus 2026].

Midstream thickening

By mid-2025, nine facilities processed ore into battery-grade nickel and cobalt sulfate — four operational, three building, two in feasibility [The Diplomat 2025]. Battery-materials nickel consumption rose ~18% YoY in 2025 [PS Market 2025], the first sign of domestic sulfate pull.

Downstream nascent

Operational cell capacity was ~10 GWh (HLI Green Power) entering 2026 [Hyundai PR 2024], rising toward ~17 GWh as IBC-CATL ramps mid-year [Kontan 2025]. That is ~12% of the 140 GWh 2030 ambition [Kira estimates] — the chain's narrowest rung.

Section 05 · Sector Sizing
010 / 028

MHP output is doubling into a price downcycle

The intermediate-product surge is the most concrete number in the chain. MHP output nearly doubles in 2026 even as the global nickel surplus persists [ING 2026] — a volume-up, price-down setup that rewards the lowest-cost integrated operators and squeezes everyone else.

The supply wave

MHP nickel-equivalent output rose ~50% to ~482 kt in 2025 [Argus 2026] and is guided to ~862 kt in 2026 as Morowali, Weda Bay and Obi HPAL trains commission [Argus 2026]. Indonesia held four operational HPAL plants by mid-2025, three more building [Argus 2026].

The price counterweight

The global nickel surplus was ~212 kt in 2025, widening toward ~288 kt in 2026 [ING 2026]. HPAL break-even sits near USD 18,000/t [Discovery 2026]; operators whose realizations fall below it face the run-at-a-loss-or-curtail choice — the 2026 quota cut is partly a price-defence move.

Why integration wins

Players integrated from ore through cathode precursor capture higher value-added margin than standalone MHP sellers reliant on external refining [PS Market 2025]. JVs increasingly push into precursor and CAM specifically to escape a mooted export tax on sub-70%-nickel products [PS Market 2025].

Section 06 · Value-Chain Economics
011 / 028

The margin ladder from ore to cathode

Each step up the chain multiplies realized value and concentrates margin in fewer hands. The policy design is explicit: tax the bottom rungs, incentivize the top ones — so the economics of where an operator sits now determine its forward returns more than ore tonnage does.

The multiplier

Raw laterite ore clears USD 30-50/t; refined battery-grade nickel sulfate commands USD 15,000-20,000/t — a 300-400x value multiplication [Jakarta Post 2026]. MHP sits in between as a traded intermediate, priced at a payable-percentage discount to LME nickel.

Who captures it

Chinese firms control ~61% of refining capacity and most announced gigafactory volume [The Diplomat 2025]; Indonesian-owned firms hold ~13% of refining, mostly low-value [East Asia Forum 2026]. The state's IBC vehicle and listed miners are racing to climb into precursor and CAM before the value locks in.

The squeeze case

With HPAL break-even near USD 18,000/t [Discovery 2026] and a persistent surplus capping price [ING 2026], integrated operators with captive power and downstream off-take absorb the cycle; standalone MHP sellers do not [Kira estimates]. Captive-coal economics are themselves a forward carbon-cost risk for export buyers.

Section 06 · Value-Chain Economics
012 / 028

Korean JV economics after the Grand Package re-cut

The single most consequential 2025 event was LG Energy Solution's exit from the full-chain Grand Package. The Korean position did not collapse — it retreated to the highest-certainty rung, leaving the upstream-to-precursor risk to Chinese partners and the state.

What LG walked away from

The USD 9.8 bn Grand Package, agreed December 2020, spanned mining through precursor, cathode and cells [Petromindo 2025]. LG cited investment and market conditions — trade tension and softening EV demand — in its April 2025 exit [The Diplomat 2025]. The precursor/CAM legs carried the most commodity-price and ramp risk.

What stayed Korean

HLI Green Power — the Hyundai-LGES-IBC cell JV at Karawang — keeps running: 10 GWh, USD 1.1 bn, operational since April 2024, expandable toward 20-30 GWh [Hyundai PR 2024]. Cells are the rung with contracted OEM demand and TKDN pull; LG retained that and shed the merchant-priced upstream.

Who absorbed the upstream

A Huayou-EVE-DBL consortium (HYD Investment) replaced LG across the precursor JVs, at a re-cut ~USD 5-6 bn scope [Jakarta Globe 2025] [Kontan 2025]. The lesson for operators: Korean capital now prices cell-stage certainty over vertical-integration optionality [Kira estimates].

Position

The IBC-CATL cell plant is the centrepiece of Indonesia's "integrated EV battery ecosystem", groundbroken by President Prabowo in June 2025 alongside Antam and IBC [IBP 2025]. Phase 1 targets 6.9 GWh with commercial operation around July 2026 [Kontan 2025], expandable to 15 GWh [Listrik Indonesia 2026].

Ecosystem play

The broader CATL-linked program spans the full nickel-to-cell chain at a stated EUR 5.5 bn group scope [The Diplomat 2025]. The Karawang ecosystem is officially valued at up to ~USD 6 bn with ~10,000 jobs, and government modelling cites a wider economic impact toward USD 48 bn [Listrik Indonesia 2026].

Why it matters

  • State coordination anchor — IBC pools MIND ID, Antam, PLN and Pertamina, giving the JV captive feedstock and power optionality [IBP 2025].
  • TKDN enabler — domestic cells from Karawang help OEMs clear the 40% (2026) and 60% (2027) local-content gates [Perpres 79/2023].
  • Chinese-underwritten risk — CATL absorbs cell-tech and ramp risk; the model mirrors China's chain dominance [The Diplomat 2025].

Watch items

  • Ramp slippage from the mid-2026 COD guidance [Kira estimates].
  • Off-take diversification beyond Chinese-linked precursor supply [CSIS 2025].

Position

HLI Green Power opened Southeast Asia's first integrated cell plant in Karawang in 2024 — 10 GWh on a 330,000 m² site, enough for over 150,000 EVs [Hyundai PR 2024]. It supplies Hyundai's locally-assembled Ioniq line, the original proof case that domestic cells can clear the TKDN gate [Tempo 2024].

The economics that stayed

When LG exited the broader USD 9.8 bn Grand Package in April 2025 [Petromindo 2025], the HLI cell JV was retained — and LG reaffirmed its Indonesia battery commitment specifically at the cell stage [Korea Times 2025]. This is the rung with contracted OEM demand and TKDN pull, not merchant commodity exposure.

Why it matters

  • Reference JV economics — 50/50 Hyundai-LGES structure, IBC partnership, expandable to 20-30 GWh [Hyundai PR 2024].

  • Phase-2 expansion timing under softer regional EV demand [The Diplomat 2025].

Position

The squeeze in numbers

Antam's downstream ladder

Antam's PT Feni Haltim is building a ~USD 6 bn downstream program: an 88 kt/yr ferronickel smelter (2027), a 55 kt/yr MHP hydrometallurgical line (2028), and a 30 kt/yr NCM cathode plant [IBP 2025]. Most sits in North Maluku, with the cell tie at Karawang via IBC. This is the clearest state attempt to climb from ore into cathode.

PT Vale's HPAL anchor

PT Vale Indonesia (INCO) anchors the Pomalaa and Sorowako HPAL projects with global partners, converting Class 2 laterite into battery-grade Class 1 chemistry [The Diplomat 2025]. Vale is one of four listed nickel miners alongside Antam, MBMA and Harita [IEEFA 2025].

Why it matters

  • State value-capture bid — Antam's CAM plant is the policy goal made concrete: keep cathode margin onshore and Indonesian-owned [IBP 2025].
  • HPAL backbone — Vale's projects are core to the 2026 battery-grade sulfate ramp [The Diplomat 2025].
  • Timing risk — North Maluku build-out runs 2027-28; slippage of 1-2 quarters is a live base-case risk [Kira estimates].

Watch items

  • Captive-power carbon intensity vs EU/US buyer requirements [Ember 2025].
  • Whether 13% Indonesian-owned refining share rises materially [East Asia Forum 2026].

Section 08 · Off-take Channels
018 / 028

Two off-take channels, two risk profiles

Indonesia plans to export roughly one-third of future cell output [IBC Bulletin 2025], but the bulk of value still flows as intermediate and refined nickel to global cathode makers. The export leg is where geopolitics — IRA rules, EU carbon caps, Chinese ownership — bites hardest.

Captive cell demand

The domestic channel is policy-pulled: TKDN forces OEMs assembling in Indonesia to source local cells, and the nickel-tilted PPN DTP steers buyers toward NMC EVs [Kemenkeu 2026]. HLI Green Power and IBC-CATL are the two qualifying cell suppliers entering 2026.

Export sulfate & precursor

The 2030 plan targets ~1m t/yr of battery-grade nickel with roughly one-third of cell output exported [IBC Bulletin 2025]. Demand is real — IEA sees battery use rising to 25-30% of global nickel by 2030 from ~10% [IEA 2026] — but routing it to Western buyers is constrained.

The geopolitics tax

Heavy Chinese ownership complicates US IRA-compliant supply, and EU battery-passport plus carbon-footprint caps pressure captive-coal smelting [IEA 2026]. IBC courted non-Chinese off-take partners through 2025 with no confirmed deal [CSIS 2025] — the central unresolved risk for the export channel.

Section 09 · Localization & Policy
019 / 028

The downstream localization regime, decoded

Three instruments do the work: the TKDN local-content staircase, the nickel-tilted PPN DTP tax break, and the upstream royalty/export squeeze. Together they engineer a closed loop — mine here, refine here, build cells here, buy nickel EVs here.

TKDN staircase

Under Perpres 79/2023, EV local content must reach 40% by 2026, 60% in 2027-2029, and 80% from 2030 [Perpres 79/2023]. Permenperin 35/2025 sets the TKDN formula at 75% direct materials, 10% direct labour, 15% factory overhead [Kemenperin 2025] — a weighting that rewards local cell and component sourcing.

The nickel tax tilt

The June 2026 PPN DTP scheme grants 100% VAT relief to nickel-NMC EVs versus 40% for non-nickel chemistries [Kemenkeu 2026], explicitly to drive nickel hilirisasi. The package targets 200,000 vehicles (100k cars, 100k two-wheelers at ~Rp 5m each); pure BEVs only, no hybrids [ANTARA 2026].

Upstream squeeze

Royalties move to a 14-19% progressive band [ORF 2025], a sub-70%-nickel export duty is under study [ANTARA 2025], and the 2026 ore quota was cut ~28% [Mysteel 2026]. The upstream is taxed to push capital downstream; the downstream is subsidized to pull it. Note: the June 2026 incentive launch faced a ~1-month delay as of late May 2026 [Kompas 2026].

Section 10 · AI Impact
020 / 028

AI in the nickel-battery chain: sizing the efficiency lever

AI here is a margin tool, not a market in itself. With HPAL break-even near USD 18,000/t and a persistent surplus, a 2-4% efficiency gain on cost or yield is the difference between curtailing and running [Kira estimates]. Adoption is early and concentrated in the largest integrated operators.

Where it lands first

The clearest near-term value is in HPAL and smelter process control — reagent dosing, leach optimization, and energy management — plus predictive maintenance on high-capex equipment [MDPI 2025]. Asia-Pacific is an emerging growth region for smelter-optimization AI [Dataintelo 2025].

Cell-line yield

On the cell side, digital-twin and AI frameworks target manufacturing yield, defect detection, and quality control across electrode and assembly steps [Springer 2025]. For new plants like IBC-CATL and HLI, first-pass yield directly drives cost per kWh [Kira estimates].

Adoption reality

Indonesia-specific HPAL deployments are nascent — the technology base exists but in-country roll-out trails China and Australia [Dataintelo 2025]. Scale and capital concentrate adoption in the few integrated majors, widening the cost gap versus sub-scale operators [Kira estimates].

Section 11 · Forecast
022 / 028

Cell-capacity outlook to 2030

The 140 GWh 2030 target is aspirational; the realistic path is a steep but partial ramp. Our base case reaches ~85 GWh by 2030 — well above today's ~17 GWh but short of target, gated by demand softness, partner turnover and the surplus cycle.

Base case

From ~17 GWh in 2026 (HLI 10 + IBC-CATL 6.9), capacity climbs as CATL phases expand to 15 GWh, HLI adds Phase 2, and North Maluku cell lines reach ~8 GWh by mid-2026 scaling to 20 GWh by 2027 [The Diplomat 2025]. Base case ~85 GWh by 2030 [Kira estimates] — ~60% of the 140 GWh target.

Bull case

If the PPN DTP demand pull holds, non-Chinese off-take deals materialize, and HYD-consortium precursor commissions on schedule, capacity could approach ~120 GWh by 2030 [Kira estimates]. This needs the surplus to clear and EV demand to re-accelerate regionally.

Bear case

Prolonged surplus, IRA/EU market-access friction on Chinese-owned supply, and incentive delays (the June 2026 package already slipped ~1 month [Kompas 2026]) cap the ramp near ~55 GWh by 2030 [Kira estimates] — cells built but under-utilized.

Key sensitivities for the build-out

Four variables swing the outcome between base and tails. Two are demand-side (incentive durability, export market access) and two are supply-side (price cycle, partner execution) — and they correlate, which is why the bear case clusters rather than averages out.

SENSITIVITY · 01
Incentive durability
The PPN DTP nickel tilt is the demand engine. A ~1-month delay already hit the June 2026 launch [Kompas 2026]; a longer stall or scope cut would pull the base case toward bear.
100% NMC VAT relief
SENSITIVITY · 02
Export market access
IRA-compliant US routing and EU carbon caps gate the export third of output [IEA 2026]. No confirmed non-Chinese off-take deal as of end-2025 [CSIS 2025] is the swing risk for the bull case.
~1/3 output for export
SENSITIVITY · 03
Nickel price & surplus
A ~288 kt 2026 surplus [ING 2026] keeps price near HPAL break-even [Discovery 2026]. Sustained sub-break-even pricing forces curtailment and starves the chain of reinvestment capital.
~288kt 2026 surplus
SENSITIVITY · 04
Partner execution
The HYD consortium must deliver the precursor legs LG vacated [Jakarta Globe 2025]. 2026 groundbreaking targets leave little schedule buffer [ANTARA 2025]; slippage cascades to cell feedstock.
USD 5-6bn re-cut scope

Carbon & ESG gate
Captive-coal smelting raises EU carbon-cap exposure [Ember 2025]. Renewable-powered processing is a forward cost and a market-access condition, not optional, for Western off-take.
