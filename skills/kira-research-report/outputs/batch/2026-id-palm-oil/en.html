Market context

Indonesia produced 56.6 million tonnes of CPO and palm kernel oil in 2025 [GAPKI 2026], up 7.2% on 2024, and shipped 32.3 Mt of palm products for USD 35.9 billion (Rp 590 trillion) [GAPKI 2026], a record value on firm prices. Yet GAPKI guides only 1–2% output growth for 2026 [GAPKI 2026] as plantations age and El Nino risk lingers. The center of gravity shifts from volume to value capture.

Why now: three convergent forces

First, the EU deforestation rule (EUDR) was deferred to 30 December 2026 for large operators [WRI 2026], a reprieve that converts traceability spend into a license-to-trade for EU-bound flows. Second, the biodiesel mandate steps from B40 toward B50, slated for 1 July 2026 [ESDM 2026], which could absorb ~19 Mt of CPO and divert volume from export. Third, a state-backed downstream push channels IDR 44.1 trillion of CPO and oleochemical investment [Bappenas 2025] into refined and derivative value.

Five strategic implications for market participants

Five forces materially reshape competitive dynamics through 2031. Each is anchored to documented market data and KIRA's synthesis from soft-commodity studies across Southeast Asia.

01 · TRACEABILITY
The deferred EU rule is a margin moat, not a reprieve
EUDR now applies from 30 December 2026 for large operators [WRI 2026]. With ~43% of palm land held by smallholders [GAPKI EUDR 2025] and over 60% lacking formal title, integrators with plot-level traceability hold EU access; aggregator-pooled supply faces a USD 25–60/t compliance drag [Kira estimates].
Anchor: ~43% smallholder land share
02 · BIODIESEL
B50 redirects volume from export to the tank
The mandate steps to B50 from 1 July 2026 [ESDM 2026], which could absorb ~19 Mt of CPO — roughly 36% of output [ESDM 2026]. KIRA reads a structural drag of 2–3 Mt off exportable volume [Kira estimates], tightening the spot market and supporting domestic refiner margins.
Anchor: ~19 Mt CPO drawn by B50
03 · DOWNSTREAM
Value migrates from crude to derivatives
A state-backed pivot channels IDR 44.1 trillion into CPO & oleochemical capacity [Bappenas 2025], with a USD 4.4 bn government target to 2040. Asia-Pacific oleochemicals cleared USD 11.0 billion in 2024 [Oleochem Asia 2025]; refined-and-derivative exporters keep the levy gap that crude shippers surrender.
Anchor: IDR 44.1 tn downstream investment
04 · LEVY
The 10% CPO levy steers the chain downstream
The CPO export levy rose to 10% from 7.5% in May 2025 [BPDPKS 2025], while downstream products sit at 4.5%. The spread funds the Rp 46–47 trillion B40 subsidy [BPDPKS 2025] and deliberately penalises raw exports. Total chain burden reached ~USD 221/t before the hike.
Anchor: 10% vs 4.5% CPO/downstream levy
05 · YIELD
Ageing acreage rewards replanting and yield-tech
Smallholders hold ~7 Mha of ~16 Mha total [GAPKI 2025], but 2024 replanting reached only 38,247 ha against a 70,000 ha target [BPDPKS 2025]. The subsidy rose to IDR 60 m/ha from September 2024 [BPDPKS 2025]. Yield recovery, not new land, is the only sustainable supply lever.
Anchor: 38.2k ha 2024 replanting

Business overview

Indonesia's reference integrated palm operator. Wilmar spans plantation, milling, refining, branded consumer oils and a leading oleochemical franchise. The group's refining and oleochemical capacity anchors the downstream value tier [Kira estimates] that the export-levy spread now rewards, with the CPO levy at 10% versus 4.5% on downstream products [BPDPKS 2025].

Channel architecture

Multi-port export logistics plus domestic refined and consumer-oil distribution. Forward supply spans biodiesel feedstock for the B40 program, refined olein for the cooking-oil DMO, and oleochemical exports to detergent, cosmetic and pharmaceutical buyers across Asia and Europe.

Strategic positioning

Best-positioned incumbent for the EUDR transition. Traced-supply programs and mill-level mapping position the group to keep EU access when the rule applies from 30 December 2026 [WRI 2026], capturing the USD 25–60/t compliance premium [Kira estimates] over pooled-supply traders. Oleochemical expansion converts levy-penalised crude into derivative value.

Strengths & watchlist

  • Integration: Plantation-to-oleochemical under one roof
  • EUDR readiness: Traced supply, mill-level mapping
  • Downstream: Leading refining and oleochemical scale
  • Watch: Smallholder-sourced volume exposes traceability gaps; levy and DMO compress crude margins

AI in Indonesian palm: from satellite plot to process control

The EU deforestation rule forces plot-level geolocation and deforestation-risk assessment on EU-bound supply [WRI 2026]. KIRA reads this as the decisive pull for AI adoption across mapping, yield forecasting and refinery process control, layered onto a state digital-agriculture agenda.

The traceability catalyst

With EUDR live from 30 December 2026 [WRI 2026] and only ~2.2% of smallholder plots carrying e-STDB registration [GAPKI EUDR 2025], satellite remote sensing plus GPS surveys are the only scalable route to compliance for the ~43% of land smallholders hold.

Government catalyst

The ISPO standard now extends to the downstream sector [Palm Oil Mag 2026], pushing mills and refiners toward digital traceability records. State downstream investment of IDR 44.1 trillion [Bappenas 2025] funds process-control automation in refining and oleochemicals.

Where value concentrates

KIRA reads the largest near-term AI returns in integrated operators that can amortise mapping and analytics across plantation, mill and refinery — the same groups the levy and EUDR already favour.

Six AI applications reshaping Indonesian palm operators

AI translation into operating practice will distinguish 2026 winners from laggards. Use cases below span plot-level traceability through oleochemical process control, with deployment patterns observable in Indonesia or the closest regional benchmarks today.

01 · TRACEABILITY
EUDR plot-level due diligence stack
Satellite remote sensing combined with smallholder GPS surveys generates the plot-level geo-data the EU rule requires. Cuts compliance cost per tonne for integrated operators; pooled-supply traders face a traceability gap of USD 25–60/t [Kira estimates].
Observable: Wilmar, Apical traced supply
02 · LAND
Deforestation-risk & legality screening
ML models flag post-2020 forest clearing against the EUDR cut-off using time-series satellite imagery. Critical given >31,000 ha of palm-linked forest loss in 2025 [Mongabay 2026]; integrators screen supply sheds before contracting.
Benchmark: independent monitoring platforms
03 · YIELD
Plot-level yield forecasting
ML models combine NDVI, rainfall and historical FFB yield to forecast output months ahead. Adopted first by operators with directly-contracted estates; sharpens replanting prioritisation given the 38,247 ha 2024 replanting shortfall [BPDPKS 2025].
Tie-in: PSR replanting targeting
04 · MILL
FFB quality & computer-vision grading
Vision models grade fresh-fruit-bunch ripeness and oil-extraction-rate potential at the mill ramp. Reduces losses and standardises grower payments; smallholder-linked mills adopt to defend FFB supply under tighter sustainability rules.
Pilot: Sumatra ramp-line vision systems
05 · PROCESS
Refinery & oleochemical process control
ML-tuned process control optimises refining and fatty-acid / fatty-alcohol yields. As IDR 44.1 trillion flows into downstream capacity [Bappenas 2025], automation lifts derivative margins that the levy spread already favours.
Tie-in: Dumai, Gresik oleochemical lines
06 · BIODIESEL
Feedstock allocation & price arbitrage
Engines arbitrate CPO between B40/B50 feedstock, refined export and the cooking-oil DMO against the 10% levy [BPDPKS 2025]. As B50 draws ~19 Mt of CPO [ESDM 2026], allocation optimisation becomes a core trading-desk margin lever.
