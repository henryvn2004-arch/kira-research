How we built this view

This view is built from two evidence streams: primary inputs — distributor and store checks, importer conversations, and analyst synthesis — and secondary anchors — Vietnamese statistical authorities, listed-company filings, and trade-association data. Every quantitative claim carries an explicit source tag.

Primary inputs

→ Channel checks
Modern-trade store walks, general-trade grocer reads, and importer conversations across Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City — surfaces real shelf, real price, real velocity on imported versus domestic SKUs.
→ Analyst synthesis
KIRA in-house triangulation across customs data, retail panels, and disclosed financials. Tagged inline as [Kira estimates].
→ Operator reads
Conversations with category buyers and distributors closer to the demand signal than headline statistics, especially on premium-formula velocity.

Secondary anchors

→ Statistical authorities
GSO national accounts, Vietnam Customs import data, and Vietnam Dairy Association market bulletins — cited inline by named source.
→ Listed-company filings
Annual reports and investor disclosures of Vinamilk, Nutifood, and peers — cited inline by company and period.
→ Triangulated estimates
Where no single source covers a figure, we combine two to three inputs and disclose the method. Tagged [Kira estimates].

Value shift over volume

Per-capita consumption sits near 29 litres a year [VDA 2025], well below Thailand's 35 and Singapore's 45 — a structural runway. But the near-term story is value: domestic revenue grew only ~1% in 2025 while imports surged 36% [Customs 2025], pulling growth into premium formula and fortified lines [Kira estimates].

Imports fund premium, locals hold volume

Domestic raw milk meets only ~40% of processing demand [VDA 2025]; the gap is filled by powder from New Zealand, the US and Australia. Foreign brands own the premium formula tier while Vinamilk and TH hold the liquid-milk base — a two-speed structure that defines the opportunity.

Section 04 · Macro Context
007 / 024

Macro indicators driving dairy demand

Vietnam crossed the upper-middle-income threshold in 2025, and rising urban incomes pull premium dairy demand even as headline growth cools in 2026.

Income and urbanization

GDP grew 8.0% in 2025, the strongest since 2011, with GDP per capita near USD 5,026 [GSO 2025]. Urban monthly income per person reached VND 6.9 mn, up 10.1% [GSO 2025]. Real earnings are forecast to grow ~4.8% a year to 2029, lifting purchasing power 13% over 2019 [World Bank 2025].

A cooler but solid 2026

Growth is forecast to moderate to 6.8% in 2026 [World Bank 2025] with CPI held near 3.3% [GSO 2025]. A softer top line compresses, not erases, the dairy demand pull — premiumization continues to run ahead of volume.

The consumption gap

At ~29 litres per capita [VDA 2025], Vietnam trails Thailand (35) and Singapore (45); the government targets 40 litres by 2030 [MARD Strategy 2025]. The gap is the multi-year volume floor beneath a value-led near term.

Section 05 · Sector Sizing
009 / 024

Market size and growth

The category reached an estimated USD 6.2 bn in 2025 [Kira estimates], compounding near 8-9% — but 2025 revenue mix tells the real story: imports up 36%, domestic up ~1%.

Size and trajectory

KIRA sizes the 2025 market at USD 6.2 bn [Kira estimates], triangulating VDA volume data, customs values, and processor revenue. Industry-wide revenue exceeded VND 133,000 bn in 2025, up ~1% on 2024 [VDA 2025]. Forward CAGR to 2030 lands near 8-9% [Kira estimates].

Volume-to-value pivot

2025 was the inflection: value growth outran volume. Domestic processor revenue was near-flat while powder consumption rose 33.9% YoY by value [VDA 2025], driven by premium imported formula rather than litres of liquid milk.

Export upside

Dairy exports reached USD 390.9 mn in 2025, up 20.6% [Customs 2025] — Vinamilk-led shipments to Taiwan, Korea, Japan and the US add a second growth vector beyond domestic demand.

Section 06 · Segment Economics
010 / 024

Segment economics: where the margin sits

Liquid milk carries the volume; milk powder and formula carry the import value; yogurt is the fastest local-value grower. Margins climb with provenance.

Liquid milk — the base

UHT and fresh milk are the largest value pool at ~42% [Kira estimates], dominated by Vinamilk and TH. Growth is slow and volume-led; this is the consolidation, not the premium, battleground.

Powder and formula — the import tier

Milk powder is ~26% of value [Kira estimates] and the locus of premiumization: imported formula posted 33.9% value growth [VDA 2025], with Abbott, Nestlé, FrieslandCampina and Mead Johnson leading the premium shelf.

Yogurt and emerging lines

Yogurt (~14%) is the fastest-growing local-value segment [Kira estimates], with high-protein and drinking formats driving frequency. Plant-based and cheese remain small but post the strongest growth rates off a low base [Kira estimates].

Section 08 · Demand & Channels
018 / 024

Channel and distribution structure

General trade still moves base volume, but modern trade and e-commerce are taking value share — and they are the route imported premium brands use to reach urban shoppers.

A shifting channel mix

Family grocers remain the largest channel by value but are in structural decline [Kira estimates]. Modern trade — super, hyper and convenience — and e-commerce now carry the premium and niche SKUs (cheese, formula, fortified) urban shoppers seek.

E-commerce as a primary channel

In tier-2/3 cities, e-commerce adoption rose from ~25% in 2019 to ~55% in 2025 [Metric 2025], and is now a primary channel for formula. On Shopee's liquid-milk category, Vinamilk leads with 53.6% and TH 16.6% [Metric 2025].

The incumbents' depth

Vinamilk fields ~200 exclusive distributors, 6,000+ GT points, 690 modern outlets and 8 e-commerce channels [Kira estimates] — the distribution moat entrants must out-flank by leading in modern trade and online, not general trade.

Section 09 · Regulatory Landscape
019 / 024

Regulatory and policy landscape

Policy pushes two ways at once: a self-sufficiency strategy that favours upstream investment, and tighter safety oversight after the 2025 counterfeit-powder scandal. EVFTA keeps lowering import barriers.

The 2030-2045 dairy strategy

Decision 309/QD-TTg targets 60-65% domestic raw-milk self-sufficiency by 2030 and 80-85% by 2045, with the herd rising to 1.3-1.5 mn cows and 12-14% annual sector growth [MARD Strategy 2025]. It rewards players who invest upstream — exactly Nutifood's and TH's play.

Safety and standards

QCVN technical standards govern liquid, powdered and infant products; Decree 100/2014 bans advertising breast-milk substitutes under 24 months [ChemLinked 2025]. After the 2025 fake-powder bust, the Ministry of Health opened a review of four infant-formula QCVN regulations [ChemLinked 2025].

Trade liberalization

EVFTA phases out dairy tariffs over ten years and recognizes EU geographical indications, narrowing the landed-cost gap for European premium imports — structurally favourable to FrieslandCampina and peers [Kira estimates].

Section 10 · AI Impact
021 / 024

AI in Vietnam dairy: from pilot to margin lever

AI's near-term payoff in dairy is operational: sharper demand forecasting and lower cold-chain waste, plus precision herd management on the integrated farms incumbents are building.

Where the value is

The single highest-impact use is demand forecasting — machine-learning models on seasonal sales, weather and promotions cut inventory mismatch and waste, which matters acutely for short-shelf-life premium lines [Kira estimates].

Smart farms

Vietnam's AI-in-agriculture market is small but compounding — USD 8.7 mn in 2024 to USD 43.0 mn by 2033, a 19.4% CAGR [Industry data 2025]. Vinamilk and TH already run automated Global GAP herds [Kira estimates].

Adoption reality

Deployment leads at scaled, vertically integrated operators; the ~28,700 smallholder farming households adopt far more slowly, gated by capital and data [Kira estimates].

Section 11 · Five-Year Outlook
022 / 024

Five-year outlook and scenarios

Base case: the category compounds near 9% to roughly USD 9.8 bn by 2030 [Kira estimates], with value growth running ahead of volume as premiumization and imports lead.

Base case (~9% CAGR)

KIRA projects the market to reach ~USD 9.8 bn by 2030 [Kira estimates], anchored to per-capita convergence toward the 40-litre 2030 target [MARD Strategy 2025] and a steady premium-mix shift.

What moves it

Upside: faster premium-formula penetration and export wins. Downside: raw-milk cost inflation, FX on imported powder, and trust shocks like the 2025 scandal that depress the very premium tier driving value [Kira estimates].
