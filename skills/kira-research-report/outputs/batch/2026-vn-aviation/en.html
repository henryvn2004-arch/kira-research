How we built this view

This view is built from two evidence streams: primary inputs — analyst synthesis, operator reads, and channel checks with carriers, airport concessionaires and travel-trade contacts — and secondary anchors — the civil aviation authority and statistical releases, listed-company filings, and triangulated estimates. Every quantitative claim carries an explicit source tag.

Primary inputs

Analyst synthesis. KIRA in-house triangulation across multiple disclosed inputs — carrier filings, regulator releases, fleet and order data, and airport disclosures. Tagged inline as [Kira estimates].

Operator & trade reads. Conversations with carrier and airport operators, ground-handlers and travel-trade contacts closer to the load-factor and concession signal than headline data.

Field channel checks. Route, fare and terminal-retail walk-throughs — surfacing real seat supply, real fare floors, and real spend per departing passenger.

Secondary anchors

Authority & statistical releases. The Civil Aviation Authority of Vietnam, the national tourism administration, and customs cargo data — cited inline by named source.

Listed-company filings. Annual reports, investor presentations and order disclosures of named carriers and the airport operator — cited inline by company and period.

Triangulated estimates. Where no single source covers a figure, we combine two to three inputs and disclose the method. Tagged [Kira estimates].

Demand is real, supply is the bottleneck

The market carried 83.5m passengers in 2025, up 10.7% and the highest on record, with international up 12.6% to 46.6m on a tourism surge to 21.5m arrivals [CAAV 2025]. The 2026 target is ~95m, up 13.6% [CAAV 2025]. The constraint is no longer demand but seats, slots and aircraft — which is exactly why fleet orders and terminal capacity now decide share.

Two games, two P&Ls

Carriers fight a capacity war on thin yields — Vietjet net profit reached VND 2.1tn in 2025 [Vietjet FY2025] against ACV's VND 12.1tn [ACV 2025]. The airport operator out-earns the airlines; the next margin pool is non-aeronautical spend per passenger, and Long Thanh's commercial launch in late 2026 is the lever [Gov VN 2026].

Section 04 · Executive summary
005 / 017

Five strategic implications for market participants

Where the next 24 months of value accrues across Vietnam's carriers and airports — and what stakeholders should sequence now.
01 · FLEET
Win the seat race with secured deliveries
Vietjet's 250+ narrowbody backlog plus 40 A330neo widebodies [Airbus 2025] convert order slots into future share. With aircraft scarce industry-wide, delivery certainty — not list-price discount — is the strategic asset. Carriers without firm slots cede growth.
Anchor: 250+ narrowbody backlog
02 · SLOTS
Treat Long Thanh capacity as the prize
Tan Son Nhat T3 lifted HCMC capacity to ~50m [Airport-Tech 2025], and Long Thanh adds 25m more from late 2026 [Gov VN 2026]. Slot access at the southern hubs is the binding constraint on the capacity war — incumbents hold the advantage.
Anchor: 25m Long Thanh phase 1
03 · GROUND
Shift the airport P&L to non-aero
Non-aeronautical revenue is ~20% of ACV's mix [Kira estimates], below the global ~40% norm [ACI 2025]. Retail, F&B, duty-free and the Long Thanh free-trade zone are the margin upside — spend per departing passenger is the metric to manage.
Anchor: ~20% non-aero share
04 · CAPITAL
Position for the 49% ownership window
A draft decree lifts the foreign airline cap from 34% to 49% [TheInvestor 2026], matching Thailand and Indonesia. Restructuring carriers — Bamboo, Vietravel — gain a recapitalization path; AirAsia's Vietravel approach is the template.
Anchor: 49% proposed cap
05 · OUTLOOK
Underwrite the international leg
International drove 2025 growth (+12.6%) on China and Korea recovery [CAAV 2025]; the 2026 tourism target is 25m arrivals [VNAT 2026]. Carriers tilting capacity to international routes capture the richer yield and the structural tailwind.
Anchor: 46.6m intl pax 2025

Domestic plateaued near 37m as carriers tilted seats abroad; international did the heavy lifting. The 2026 story is an international-led market running into a hard aircraft-and-slot ceiling.

International carries the growth

International passengers rose 12.6% to 46.6m in 2025 while domestic grew a softer 8.4% to ~36.9m [CAAV 2025]. Carriers redeployed narrowbodies onto higher-yield international routes — Vietjet alone runs 109 international vs 45 domestic [Vietjet FY2025].

CONSTRAINT

The growth machine

Vietjet posted FY2025 consolidated revenue of VND 82.1tn and net profit of VND 2.1tn, up 51% and 120% of plan [Vietjet FY2025]. It took 22 aircraft in 2025, its largest single-year intake, spread across the Vietnam fleet, Vietjet Thailand and Con Dao COMAC operations [AeroTime 2025].

Order book as strategy

At the 2025 Paris Air Show it signed for 100 A321neo (plus 50 options) and lifted its A330neo order to 40 — the largest globally for the type [Airbus 2025]. Backlog past 250 narrowbodies turns delivery slots into committed future capacity in a supply-short market.

Strategic posture

  • International tilt — 109 of 154 routes are international; India is the fastest-growing corridor at 78 weekly flights [Vietjet FY2025].
  • Widebody entry — A330neo opens longer-haul and higher-density leisure routes beyond narrowbody range.
  • Multi-market platform — Vietjet Thailand and wet-lease capacity flex seats into peak demand.
  • Foreign capital — the 49% cap window supports its stated plan to attract overseas investors [vietbao 2026].

The anchor recovers

Vietnam Airlines returned to profit in 2025 and set a 2026 plan of 176,600 flights and 29m+ passengers, up 13% [Vietnam Airlines 2026]. It targets double-digit growth and is building fuel-price scenarios to defend margin against international volatility [VietnamPlus 2026].

The premium franchise

As flag carrier it holds the premium-yield and corporate-travel segment, with SkyTeam connectivity and slot-protected positions at Noi Bai and Tan Son Nhat. Pacific Airlines and Vasco extend the group into low-cost and feeder niches without diluting the core brand.

Strategic posture

  • Yield over volume — competes on network, schedule and product rather than the fare floor that defines the LCC contest.
  • Group restructuring — Pacific Airlines returned leased aircraft and resumed on three jets leased from the parent [VIR 2025].
  • Long Thanh shift — positioned to migrate long-haul to Long Thanh as the southern hub opens [Aviation Week 2026].
  • Capital constraint — state-majority ownership limits the foreign-capital flexibility its private rivals are courting.

The new-entrant wave tests a hardened duopoly

A leisure-backed start-up and a foreign-capital approach reopen a market that looked closed. Slot scarcity at the major hubs, not licensing, is what decides whether the newcomers scale.
ENTRANT 01
Sun PhuQuoc Airways
Sun Group's carrier launched commercial flights on 1 Nov 2025 and went international on 29 Mar 2026 to Taipei and Seoul [TravelandTour 2026]. It plans 100 Airbus and Boeing jets over five years, including 40 Dreamliners.
EDGE: PHU QUOC TOURISM + RESORT ECOSYSTEM
ENTRANT 02
Vietravel × AirAsia
AirAsia is in final talks for a minority stake in Vietravel Airlines [TravelandTour 2026], its long-sought Vietnam entry. AirAsia's low-cost playbook could turn a ~3%-share niche carrier into a credible third force.
EDGE: REGIONAL LCC SCALE + BRAND
ENTRANT 03
Bamboo Airways reset
Post-restructuring, Bamboo rebuilds around 11 high-capacity domestic routes and a fleet target of ~18, licensed toward ~30 [Bamboo 2025]. Recovery is real but scale and balance sheet remain the constraint.
EDGE: REBUILT NETWORK, THIN CAPITAL
BARRIER 01
Slot scarcity
Securing slots at Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City is the gating constraint for any newcomer [TravelandTour 2026]. Incumbents hold the grandfathered positions; Long Thanh is the only material new slot pool.
GATE: SOUTHERN-HUB SLOT ACCESS
BARRIER 02
Aircraft availability
A global delivery backlog means even funded entrants wait for lift; Sun PhuQuoc bridged with ex-American A330s [SimpleFlying 2025]. Order-book position is a years-long head start for incumbents.
GATE: DELIVERY SLOTS

Regulation and the foreign-ownership inflection

A draft decree to raise the foreign airline cap from 34% to 49% is the policy lever of 2026. It recapitalizes restructuring carriers and invites regional LCC entry while preserving domestic control at 51%+.

Section 13 · AI, digital & outlook
016 / 017

Digital rails, AI and the five-year outlook

Biometric boarding and AI-driven operations are moving from pilot to scale, while the demand path points toward a market roughly doubling pre-pandemic volume by 2030. Supply, not demand, sets the slope.

Digital identity at the gate

Biometric check-in via the VNeID system rolled out at Tan Son Nhat and Noi Bai, expanding to Can Tho and 10 more airports in phase two [VietnamPlus 2025]. ACV is building an ACV ID system to automate security and identity checks [VietnamPlus 2025].

AI in operations and cargo

AI is being applied to passenger support, ground-operations efficiency and air-cargo security screening [investvietnam 2025]. With e-commerce now ~20% of global air cargo, predictive cargo-flow analytics is the near-term operational unlock.

Green flight path

Sustainable aviation fuel, low-emission aircraft and green airport infrastructure anchor the sector's climate commitments [vietnam-briefing 2025] — a cost and compliance vector into 2030.
