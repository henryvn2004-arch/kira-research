How we built this view

This view draws on two evidence streams: primary inputs — developer site visits, off-taker conversations, and EPC procurement data — and secondary anchors — EVN disclosures, MOIT policy circulars, and project-level grid-interconnection records. Every quantitative claim carries an explicit source tag.

Primary inputs

Developer and off-taker reads
Conversations with rooftop solar developers operating under the new DPPA/commercial PPA framework, industrial park energy managers across Binh Duong, Dong Nai, and Long An, and FDI manufacturers assessing on-site solar as part of RE100 commitments — surfaces real contracting structures, PPA pricing ranges, and off-taker appetite.
EPC & equipment procurement
Module import data, inverter procurement trends, and EPC cost benchmarks from installers across the 100 kWp–5 MWp rooftop segment and the utility-scale pipeline with signed grid-interconnection agreements.
Analyst synthesis
KIRA in-house triangulation across EVN annual capacity tables, MOIT Power Development Plan VIII installation schedules, and grid-congestion notifications. Tagged inline as [Kira estimates].

Secondary anchors

Statistical and regulatory authorities
EVN (Tập đoàn Điện lực Việt Nam) capacity + dispatch tables, MOIT (Bộ Công Thương) Power Development Plan VIII (PDP8) and revision memos, NLDC (National Load Dispatch Centre) curtailment bulletins, Ministry of Finance investment incentive schedules.
Project-level disclosures
Signed PPA filings, grid-code compliance certificates, and interconnection queue data from EVN regional subsidiaries (EVNCPC, EVNNPC, EVNSPC). FDI solar project approvals from MPI (Bộ Kế hoạch và Đầu tư) provincial investment registers.
Triangulated estimates
Where no single source spans the figure (curtailment rates by corridor, PPA pricing distributions, rooftop penetration by industrial segment), we combine 2-3 inputs and disclose the method. Tagged [Kira estimates].

Vietnam solar 2027 — rooftop PPA unlocked, grid is the binding constraint

Vietnam's installed solar base reached ~20.7 GWp by end-2025 [EVN 2025], covering roughly 25% of total generation capacity. The Decree 135/2024 commercial DPPA framework activated rooftop PPA contracting for the first time at scale. The binding constraint for 2027-30 is not solar demand — it is transmission capacity from the south-central solar belt to load centres.

The PPA pivot is structural

Decree 135/2024/NĐ-CP enabled commercial-scale direct power purchase agreements between rooftop solar operators and industrial off-takers, effective November 2024 [MOIT 2024]. For the first time, a factory or industrial park tenant can contract multi-year solar supply at a negotiated tariff without routing through EVN's retail price schedule. The first signed DPPAs are priced at VND 1,450-1,700/kWh — a 15-25% discount to the industrial retail tariff [Kira estimates from developer conversations].

Grid is the ceiling, not demand

Vietnam's south-central solar corridor — Ninh Thuan, Binh Thuan, Khanh Hoa — generated more solar power than the 500kV backhaul to Hanoi and HCMC could absorb in 2024-25 [NLDC 2025]. Curtailment rates peaked at ~25% in Q2 2025 [NLDC 2025]. EVN's 500kV third-circuit extension (Đà Nẵng to Pleiku) and the 220kV reinforcements in the south are scheduled for 2026-27 completion, but grid timing, not project demand, decides when the next GW of utility-scale capacity becomes economically viable.

Section 03 · Strategic Implications
005 / 016

Five strategic implications for the next 24 months

Market participants should treat 2025-27 as the rooftop DPPA land-grab window, not the utility-scale build cycle. The constraint is grid, not demand; operators who move first on commercial PPA structures capture the off-taker relationships that persist through the decade.

IMPLICATION · 01
Rooftop DPPA is the lowest-friction growth channel
Decree 135/2024 removes the need to route through EVN's purchase schedule for on-site and near-site commercial solar [MOIT 2024]. Projects below 1 MWp can be contracted and commissioned within 6-9 months [Kira estimates] — far faster than utility-scale GIA queues. The 2026-27 window is decisive: off-takers signing 10-15 year DPPAs now lock in supply at current module prices (~USD 0.11-0.13/Wp) before any rebound.
6-9 months DPPA timeline <1 MWp
IMPLICATION · 02
Sequence utility-scale builds around grid completion dates
Three 500kV and eight 220kV projects in EVN's 2026-27 grid investment plan will unlock ~8-10 GW of stranded south-central solar capacity [EVN Grid 2025]. Utility-scale developers with signed grid-interconnection agreements should stage commercial operation dates to the specific line completion — commissioning into a curtailment corridor destroys IRR.
~8-10 GW transmission-constrained
IMPLICATION · 03
Anchor pricing on RE100 off-takers, not FiT dependence
Vietnam's feed-in tariff (FiT) era closed for new registrations in 2020-21 [EVN 2021]. The replacement competitive auction mechanism (AUCM) is operational but oversubscribed. RE100 industrial tenants — Samsung, Intel, LG, Nike supply chains — offer multi-year DPPAs at 80-90% of retail tariff [Kira estimates from off-taker conversations], a stable offtake base independent of government pricing cycles.
80-90% retail-parity DPPA pricing
IMPLICATION · 04
Use storage to monetise curtailment — the next margin lever
Battery storage co-located with utility-scale solar in curtailment zones can shift generation from peak-cut hours to evening peak, converting curtailed output into dispatchable power. PDP8 targets 300 MW of battery storage by 2030 [PDP8 2023]; project economics become viable below USD 200/kWh BESS at current curtailment rates — a threshold reached in 2025 [Kira estimates on BloombergNEF BESS pricing].
300 MW PDP8 storage target
IMPLICATION · 05
Northern solar is underpenetrated — the next geography
Vietnam's north has <5% of installed solar versus ~70% of industrial load [EVN 2025, Kira estimates]. Irradiance is lower (GHI ~4.5 kWh/m²/day vs south's 5.2-5.5) but the load-proximity advantage eliminates transmission risk. Decree 135/2024 structures favour north-of-load-centre C&I solar as a lower-risk alternative to south-centric utility scale.

Section 04 · Policy & Regulation
007 / 016

PDP8 sets 73.4 GWp by 2030 — DPPA unlocks the merchant layer

Power Development Plan VIII (Decision 500/QĐ-TTg, May 2023) set Vietnam's first legally binding solar installation target: 73.4 GWp by 2030 and 168-189 GWp by 2050 [PDP8 2023]. Decree 135/2024 activated the direct commercial PPA channel that makes reaching those targets without state-financed EVN procurement financially feasible.

PDP8 solar targets

PDP8 disaggregates the 73.4 GWp target: ~50 GWp utility-scale ground-mounted (mainly south-central and the Mekong delta) and ~23 GWp rooftop solar (commercial, industrial, and residential) [PDP8 2023]. Vietnam's base case as of end-2025 sits at 20.7 GWp installed — roughly 28% of the 2030 target with five years remaining [EVN 2025]. The gap is real but arithmetically achievable if transmission investment tracks.

Decree 135/2024 — the commercial PPA pivot

Decree 135/2024/NĐ-CP (November 2024) enables direct power purchase agreements between renewable energy generators and corporate off-takers, without requiring EVN as intermediary [MOIT 2024]. Eligible projects include rooftop solar (any capacity), ground-mounted solar below specified thresholds, and co-located battery storage. The decree resolves a 5-year legal limbo where RE100-committed FDI manufacturers could not legally contract private solar supply.

AUCM mechanism for utility-scale

The competitive auction mechanism (AUCM) governs grid-connected utility-scale solar above the DPPA threshold. Two pilot auction rounds (2025-26) allocated ~3.2 GW at clearing prices between VND 1,644-1,900/kWh [MOIT 2025] — roughly 7-8 US¢/kWh, competitive with regional peers but contingent on grid-interconnection agreement (GIA) availability. GIA issuance is the bottleneck: EVN's queue exceeds available transmission slots by ~2:1 [Kira estimates from EVN grid planning docs].

Section 05 · Grid Bottleneck
009 / 016

Transmission constraints: curtailment geography and the GIA queue

The structural mismatch is geographic: ~70% of Vietnam's installed solar is in the south-central provinces (Ninh Thuan, Binh Thuan, Khanh Hoa, Phu Yen) which represent less than 20% of national electricity demand [EVN 2025, Kira estimates]. Every MWp added to these provinces without new 500kV transmission faces curtailment risk until the grid catches up.

Curtailment by corridor

NLDC's 2024-25 dispatch data shows peak curtailment of 23-27% in Ninh Thuan during dry-season solar peaks (Q1-Q2) [NLDC 2025]. Binh Thuan curtailment tracked 15-20% in the same period. By contrast, north-central and Mekong delta projects report near-zero curtailment — grid topology there still has absorption headroom. The differential creates a clear geographic arbitrage in project IRR calculation.

500kV backhaul — the relief timeline

EVN's grid investment program includes three critical interventions: the 500kV North-South circuit-3 extension (Da Nang–Pleiku–Duc Hoa, scheduled 2026Q4), a 220kV reinforcement ring across south-central provinces, and a new HVDC interconnect study for the post-2028 period [EVN Grid 2025]. If EVN's own schedule holds, south-central curtailment falls to under 10% by late 2027 [Kira estimates]. EVN's grid project schedule has historically run 6-18 months late against announced dates [Kira estimates from historical completion analysis].

GIA queue dynamics

Grid-interconnection agreement (GIA) requests submitted to EVN regional subsidiaries exceed available 2026-27 transmission slots by an estimated 2:1 ratio [Kira estimates from EVN grid planning documents]. Projects without a signed GIA cannot participate in AUCM bidding, cannot access EVN procurement, and cannot contract DPPAs that route power through the grid. The queue effectively rations new utility-scale entries and focuses near-term growth on the rooftop and near-site solar segment.

Section 06 · Rooftop PPA Market
011 / 016

Commercial & industrial PPA: structure, pricing, and the RE100 pipeline

Vietnam's C&I rooftop solar market is entering its second phase: from self-consumption incentive installs under the old net-metering regime to multi-year commercial DPPAs under Decree 135/2024. The shift is structural — it brings professional project finance, institutional off-takers, and bankable contract structures to a segment previously dependent on government feed-in tariffs.

Off-taker profile: RE100 as the demand anchor

The primary driver of C&I solar demand through 2027 is not electricity cost savings — it is supply chain sustainability requirements. Samsung (SDC & SEV), Intel, LG Electronics, Nike and Apple contract manufacturers have committed to 100% renewable electricity under RE100 or equivalent corporate pledges [RE100 2025]. These commitments extend to Vietnam's manufacturing tiers; landlords of industrial parks serving these tenants face direct RE procurement obligations. The addressable rooftop surface area across Binh Duong, Dong Nai, Hanoi electronics clusters and Long An logistics parks represents ~4-5 GWp of technically feasible rooftop capacity [Kira estimates].

DPPA pricing mechanics

Under Decree 135/2024, the DPPA price is bilaterally negotiated between generator and off-taker, subject to a floor (EVN avoided-cost reference) and ceiling (EVN retail tariff minus transmission wheeling charges) [MOIT 2024]. In practice, first-generation contracts are pricing at VND 1,450-1,700/kWh (~6.0-7.0 US¢/kWh) [Kira estimates from developer conversations] against an industrial retail tariff of ~VND 1,900-2,200/kWh for comparable consumption profiles [EVN 2025]. The 15-25% discount provides a compelling business case without requiring government subsidy.

Contract structure and bankability

Standard DPPA structures in Vietnam follow international PPA conventions: 10-15 year term, take-or-pay floor at 85-90% of contracted volume, quarterly indexed price adjustments linked to a consumer price index component [Kira estimates from project documentation]. Project lenders (ADB, IFC, DFI-aligned local banks) are accepting Decree 135 PPAs as bankable off-take for solar project financing at debt:equity ratios of 70:30.

Section 05 · Capacity & Forecast
012 / 016

Installed base, pipeline, and the 2027-2030 trajectory

Vietnam's solar installed base adds roughly 4-5 GWp per year under the current regulatory structure — a pace that achieves PDP8's 2030 target only if transmission investment and GIA issuance keep pace. Our base case reaches ~35 GWp by end-2027, well below PDP8's implied run-rate but reflecting grid-constrained reality.

The 2025-2027 addition window

Vietnam added ~1.9 GWp of new solar in 2025 [EVN 2025], the lowest annual addition since the post-FiT reset in 2022. The drop reflects the transition from guaranteed FiT to AUCM contracting — developers waited for Decree 135 clarity and AUCM Round 1 results before committing new capacity. 2026-27 additions should rebound to 5-7 GWp per annum [Kira estimates] as AUCM Round 2 capacity comes online and DPPA-structured rooftop projects clear permitting. The northern grid geography absorbs the incremental rooftop additions without curtailment risk.

Rooftop vs utility-scale split

The 2026-27 capacity addition is expected to skew ~60% rooftop / 40% utility-scale versus the historical ~80:20 utility-dominant mix [Kira estimates]. The shift reflects: (a) DPPA framework enabling fast-cycle C&I rooftop contracting, (b) GIA queue constraining new utility-scale additions in high-irradiance zones, and (c) northern load-proximate sites increasingly preferred over south-central high-irradiance but transmission-constrained locations.

Module cost trajectory

Mono-PERC modules landed Vietnam at ~USD 0.11-0.13/Wp ex-port in Q1 2026 [Kira estimates from EPC procurement conversations], down from USD 0.22/Wp in 2023. TOPCon bifacial modules command a ~10% premium but deliver 5-7% higher energy yield in Vietnam's irradiance profile. EPC all-in cost for rooftop installs sits at USD 0.60-0.75/Wp [Kira estimates] — a 30% reduction from 2022 — making rooftop DPPA economically viable without any subsidy or government offtake guarantee.

Competitive dynamics

The top-5 developers collectively hold ~8.6 GWp — roughly 41% of total installed capacity [Kira estimates]. The remaining ~12 GWp is distributed across 200+ smaller operators, many of whom built under the FiT window and lack the institutional capacity to navigate AUCM or structure DPPAs.

The DPPA era creates a new competitive axis: project finance capability and off-taker relationship depth matter more than pure megawatt volume. FDI developers from Singapore (Sembcorp, Amverton), Japan (Marubeni, JERA), and South Korea (SK E&S, Korea Electric Power) are entering as DPPA-structured project owners — a structural shift from FiT-era domestic dominance.

Section 08 · 5-Year Outlook
015 / 016

Base / bull / bear to 2030: grid timing decides which scenario plays

All three scenarios agree on one variable: rooftop C&I solar adds 3-5 GWp per annum regardless of the utility-scale trajectory [Kira estimates]. The divergence is entirely in utility-scale — and utility-scale divergence is entirely driven by whether EVN's 500kV and 220kV grid expansion runs on schedule, 18 months late, or hits a procurement/financing disruption.

Base case: ~50 GWp by 2030

EVN's 500kV North-South circuit-3 completes Q4 2026 — 6 months late vs original target. South-central curtailment falls to ~10% by mid-2027, unlocking a ~4 GWp tranche of stranded utility-scale capacity. Annual additions run 7-9 GWp in 2027-29 before tailing off as 2030 PDP8 targets are approached. Rooftop C&I adds ~3.5 GWp per year steadily from 2026 [Kira estimates]. Total installed at end-2030: ~50 GWp in the base case — 68% of PDP8's 73.4 GWp target, with the gap concentrated in slow-moving ground-mounted utility-scale in constrained corridors.

Bull case: ~65 GWp by 2030

EVN transmission runs on the revised 2026 schedule; AUCM Round 3+ clears efficiently; DPPA contracts accelerate rooftop C&I to 5+ GWp/year by 2028 [Kira estimates]. First utility-scale BESS co-location projects commission in 2027, improving dispatch economics and grid absorption. Battery storage unlocks an additional ~2 GWp of solar capacity that was previously uneconomic in curtailment zones. Total installed at end-2030: ~65 GWp.

Bear case: ~38 GWp by 2030

EVN grid investment delays compound — 18-24 months late on circuit-3 and 220kV reinforcement. South-central curtailment persists at 20%+ through 2028, freezing utility-scale additions. AUCM Round 3 clearing is delayed by regulatory review of Round 1-2 disputes [Kira estimates from developer conversations on appeal risk]. Total installed at end-2030: ~38 GWp — a 48% shortfall versus PDP8 targets. RE100 and national net-zero commitments require accelerated recovery post-2030.

Methodology endnote

This report synthesises primary research (developer conversations, off-taker interviews, EPC procurement data) with secondary anchors (government statistical sources, EVN/MOIT regulatory disclosures, project-level filings). All quantitative claims carry explicit inline source tags.

Research approach

Installed capacity and pipeline data are triangulated from EVN annual capacity reports, MOIT project approval registries, and NLDC dispatch bulletins. Where the three differ, we apply a conservative reconciliation — discounting announced project capacities that have not received grid-interconnection agreements (GIA) by 50% for inclusion in the pipeline estimate.

Curtailment estimates are derived from NLDC quarterly bulletins supplemented by developer conversations on specific site-level dispatch data. Peak-period (Q1-Q2) curtailment rates are used to represent the worst-case constraint rather than annual averages, which mask dry-season grid stress.

DPPA pricing estimates are based on conversations with developers involved in first-generation Decree 135/2024 contracts and cross-referenced against EVN's published industrial retail tariff schedule. Individual contract terms are commercially sensitive; ranges reflect the distribution of known-signed contracts.

Forward estimates

Scenario capacity forecasts (base / bull / bear) are KIRA analytical estimates. They are not drawn from EVN planning models or MOIT official projections, which tend to reflect policy ambition rather than grid-constrained delivery probability.
