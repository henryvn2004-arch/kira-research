How we built this view

This view is built from two evidence streams: primary inputs — corridor site walks, developer and agency reads, and analyst synthesis — and secondary anchors — statistical authorities, listed-developer filings, and broker market data. Every quantitative claim carries an explicit source tag.

Primary inputs

Corridor & project site reads
Station-area walks along the MRT North–South line and Phase 2A alignment, plus township checks in BSD, Serpong and Bekasi — surfaces real asking prices, TOD launch density, and absorption pace ahead of new-line commissioning.
Developer & agency reads
Conversations with listed-developer investor-relations teams, marketing-sales desks, and brokerage research — surfaces presales conversion, recurring-income mix, and the general-trade buyer's mortgage sensitivity.
Analyst synthesis
KIRA in-house triangulation across BPS national accounts, the Bank Indonesia residential-property survey, and listed-developer filings. Tagged inline as [Kira estimates].

Secondary anchors

Statistical authorities
BPS (Badan Pusat Statistik), Bank Indonesia (BI residential-property price and sales survey), the Ministry of Finance (PPN DTP rules), and the Nusantara Capital Authority (OIKN) — cited inline by named source.
Listed-developer filings

Broker & triangulated estimates
Brokerage market reads anchor supply, vacancy and corridor pricing; where no single source covers a figure, we combine 2-3 inputs and disclose the method. Tagged [Kira estimates], targeted at 20-30% of claims.

Section 04 · Macro Context

Macro indicators driving property demand

Indonesia's 281m-person economy expands at 5%-plus through the forecast period [BPS 2026], with household consumption the dominant engine. The growth floor is firm; the cost of mortgage credit is the variable that decides whether the residential recovery broadens through 2027.

Growth pace

Real GDP grew 5.11% in 2025, narrowly missing the 5.2% target [Jakarta Globe 2026]; Q4 2025 accelerated to 5.39% YoY [BPS 2026]. Bank Indonesia holds a 2026 growth band of 4.9-5.7% [BI 2026]. Property demand tracks the consumption and credit cycle more tightly than headline GDP, so the rate path matters more than the growth print.

Rates & the rupiah

BI raised the benchmark 50bps to 5.25% in May 2026 — its first hike since April 2024 — to defend a rupiah near a multi-decade low [BI 2026]. The move follows ~150bps of cuts from September 2024 [PintarSaham 2026]. Higher policy rates lift KPR mortgage costs, the single most-cited barrier to residential purchase after construction-cost inflation [BI SHPR 2026].

Inflation & sentiment

CPI inflation eased to 2.42% YoY in April 2026, inside BI's 2.5±1% corridor [BI 2026]. Contained inflation supports real incomes, but Moody's cut the sovereign outlook to negative in February 2026 [Global Property Guide 2026], a reminder that fiscal and currency risk frame the rate path into 2027.

Section 04 · Macro Context
008 / 025

Policy timeline: the housing-demand support ladder

Indonesia's property demand runs on a clear policy ladder anchored by the 3 Million Houses Program and the government-borne VAT incentive [Observer ID 2025]. The decisive rung is the extension of the 100% VAT waiver into 2026-2027, which lowers the effective entry price for the mid-market buyer.

The backlog mandate

Indonesia's housing backlog reached ~15 million units by April 2025 [Property Outlook 2025], the structural rationale for the flagship 3 Million Houses Program [Observer ID 2025]. The 2026 state budget targets ~770,000 supported units [VOI 2026]. The backlog guarantees a multi-year demand floor even through a cyclical sales trough.

The VAT-borne-by-government lever

Under PMK 90/2025, the government bears 100% of VAT on landed houses and apartments for the price portion up to IDR 2bn, on units priced up to IDR 5bn, running January-December 2026 [MoF PMK 90/2025]. The waiver was extended into 2027 [Jakarta Globe 2025] and covers eligible foreign buyers. It is the primary demand trigger for sub-IDR 5bn stock.

Foreign access & Nusantara incentives

Foreigners may own apartments via SHMSRS subject to minimum-price thresholds (e.g. IDR 2bn in Bali) and immigration status [ICLG 2025]; HGB build-rights run to 80 years [Permitindo 2025]. Nusantara layers in extended HGB tenor and tax holidays to draw private capital [Business Indonesia 2025], a parallel incentive track to the mainland VAT waiver.

Section 05 · Sector Sizing

Market size & growth: Indonesia residential 2024-2030

The residential market stands at ~USD 47.9bn in 2025 and grows at a ~4.7% CAGR to ~USD 51.8bn by 2030 [Property Outlook 2025]. The headline trajectory is steady; the near-term shape is a 2026 sales trough followed by a supply-scarcity-driven price recovery into 2027 [BI SHPR 2026, Kira estimates].

Total residential market

The market is sized at ~USD 47.9bn in 2025, reaching ~USD 51.8bn by 2030 at a ~4.7% CAGR [Property Outlook 2025]. Value growth is led by price, not unit volume, as the supply pipeline thins and incentive-supported demand concentrates in the mid-market. Q1 2026 marked the cyclical low, with sales down 25.67% YoY [BI SHPR 2026].

The supply-crunch mechanic

Jakarta new apartment completions fall to ~1,095 units in 2026 and ~434 in 2027 [Brokerage 2026] — a near-vertical drop from prior-cycle peaks. Around 3,200 units are scheduled to hand over across 2026-2027, ~70% in South Jakarta [Property Outlook 2025]. Recovering demand meeting stalled supply lifts occupancy, rents and secondary prices from mid-2026.

The growth shape

Forecasts cluster at 1-3% national price growth over the coming year, with 5-8% in infrastructure-linked and prime locations [Property Outlook 2025]. The 2026-2027 swing factor is the mortgage-rate path after the May 2026 BI hike [BI 2026], weighed against the VAT incentive and the supply gap.

Section 06 · Segment Economics
010 / 025

Segment economics: where the margin and the resilience live

Returns differ sharply by segment. Landed township sales carry the volume and the margin; recurring-income assets carry the resilience [Kira estimates]. The Nusantara segment is high-optionality but policy-dependent; the apartment segment is supply-constrained and set to reprice.

Landed & townships (volume core)

Landed housing in master-planned townships is the volume and margin core for the listed majors. BSDE's residential segment drove ~44% of presales, led by BSD City and Kota Wisata Cibubur [BSDE 2025]; Summarecon Serpong alone delivered IDR 2.48tn of marketing sales, up ~45% YoY [SMRA 2025]. Township land banks bought cheaply decades ago underpin development margin.

Apartments (supply-constrained)

The apartment segment is where the supply crunch bites: new Jakarta completions fall to ~434 units in 2027 [Brokerage 2026], even as rents rose ~4% YoY into early 2026 against ~40% vacancy that still caps pricing power [Brokerage 2026]. Transit-linked stock is the exception — TOD units command a 15-26% premium near new stations [Kira estimates].

Recurring income & Nusantara

Malls, offices and hospitality smoothed the presales downturn — Pakuwon's recurring-income tilt protected margin through the cycle [Kira estimates]. The Nusantara segment is the optionality layer: ~IDR 225tn committed but only ~USD 4bn realized [Linos 2026], with private superblocks (Pakuwon, Vasanta) breaking ground from March 2026 [Konstruksi Media 2026].

The MRT North–South line has reset land economics along its alignment. Units within walking distance of stations have appreciated 15-26% since station announcements [Kira estimates], and the Phase 2A extension to Kota opens a new TOD frontier through Central Jakarta from 2027 [MRT Jakarta 2026].

The TOD premium is structural

Transit-oriented developments near MRT and LRT stations show the strongest appreciation as buyers price in connectivity [Property Outlook 2025]; the corridor uplift sits at 15-26% post-announcement [Kira estimates]. The highest concentration of new South Jakarta apartments clusters around TB Simatupang and the MRT line [Property Outlook 2025]. The premium accrues before commissioning, so entitlement timing matters more than completion.

Phase 2A opens the next frontier

Phase 2A extends the line north from Bundaran HI through Thamrin-Monas, Harmoni, Glodok and Kota — seven new stations [MRT Jakarta 2026]. The Bundaran HI–Harmoni segment targets commissioning from mid-2027; Harmoni–Kota follows in 2029 [MRT Jakarta 2026]. Central Jakarta's heritage core becomes a new TOD zone, with buildings along the corridor connecting directly to stations.

The East-West line as the long game

Phase 3, the Cikarang-Balaraja East-West line, extends transit-led demand far beyond the current core [MRT Jakarta 2026]. The first sub-phase (Medan Satria-Kembangan) opens land-value capture across western and eastern Greater Jakarta over the next cycle [Kira estimates], a multi-year pipeline of corridor land plays.

Section 08 · Demand & Corridors

Nusantara demand: real commitments, policy-dependent pace

The capital relocation enters Phase II in 2026, shifting from basic infrastructure to a functioning political and digital city [Kompas IKN 2026]. Private capital is now breaking ground — Pakuwon, Vasanta and Korean investors start construction in 2026 — but realized investment trails commitments and state funding has been cut sharply.

The commitment-realization gap

The Nusantara Authority reports ~IDR 225tn ($13.8bn) in commitments from 42 companies, but only ~$4bn is realized [Linos 2026]. State funding fell ~85% — from $2bn in 2024 to ~$300m for 2026 [Linos 2026]. Pure-private commitments stand at ~IDR 66.5tn, with a further ~IDR 158.7tn under public-private (KPBU) schemes [Kompas IKN 2026]. The signal is genuine but the realization pace is policy-dependent.

Private superblocks break ground

Private hospitality and commercial projects begin construction from March 2026 [Konstruksi Media 2026]. Pakuwon, Vasanta and Jambuluwuk together commit ~IDR 5.3tn [Konstruksi Media 2026]; a Korean investor starts an IDR 1.15tn apartment-and-hotel project in Q4 2026 [Kompas IKN 2026]. 2026 marks the move from public to private development — the credibility milestone the market watched for.

Housing supply & population pull

Civil-servant housing advances — TNI/Polri towers hit ~93.5% completion, with ASN tower packages added for an expected 1,200-3,000 new residents in 2026 [PUPR 2026, Radar Bontang 2026]. The forward demand is the staged relocation of ministries, a captive tenant base that anchors early commercial and residential absorption [Kira estimates].

Section 09 · Regulatory & Policy
018 / 025

Regulatory landscape: incentives, foreign access, IKN tenor

Policy is net-supportive of demand. The government-borne VAT waiver and the 3 Million Houses Program pull the mid-market; relaxed foreign-ownership rules and extended Nusantara land tenor widen the buyer pool. The binding constraints are permitting friction and construction-cost inflation, not demand-side rules.

Demand-side incentives

PMK 90/2025 grants 100% government-borne VAT on the price portion up to IDR 2bn, for units priced up to IDR 5bn, through 2026 [MoF PMK 90/2025], extended into 2027 [Jakarta Globe 2025]. The 2026 budget supports ~770,000 units under the 3 Million Houses Program [VOI 2026]. Together these lower the effective entry price for the recovering mid-market buyer.

Foreign ownership & structures

Foreigners may own apartments via SHMSRS subject to minimum-price thresholds (e.g. IDR 2bn in Bali) and Second-Home immigration permits [ICLG 2025]. HGB build-rights run an initial 30 years, extendable to 80 [Permitindo 2025]; PT PMA structures allow corporate ownership [DDA 2025]. The eligible-foreigner VAT waiver and Jakarta minimum-value bands frame the addressable expat and investor pool.

Friction & the IKN track

Developers cite construction-cost inflation (~21%), permitting/bureaucracy (~18%) and mortgage rates (~16%) as the top barriers [BI SHPR 2026]. Nusantara runs a parallel incentive regime — extended HGB tenor and tax holidays to draw private capital [Business Indonesia 2025] — but execution depends on the program's contested funding path [Linos 2026].

Adoption sits between the pilot and integration stages on our maturity curve. Proptech platforms have moved from listings to AI valuation and consumer credit intelligence [Ken Research 2025], and developers are beginning to fold AI into pricing, lead scoring and design — the levers that matter most in a high-rate, low-supply cycle.

Where adoption stands

Why it matters now

In a supply-constrained, high-rate market, AI's value is sharper underwriting and faster sales conversion [Kira estimates]. Section 11 · 5-Year Outlook
021 / 025

5-year outlook & forecast: a supply-led recovery

Our base case sees the residential market reaching ~USD 51.8bn by 2030 at a ~4.7% CAGR [Property Outlook 2025, Kira estimates], with the near-term shape defined by a 2026 trough and a supply-scarcity recovery from 2027. The rate path and the rupiah are the decisive swing factors.

Base case — supply-led turn

The market troughs in 2026 on the 25.67% Q1 sales contraction [BI SHPR 2026], then recovers as the apartment supply gap (down to ~434 units in 2027) collides with VAT-supported demand [Brokerage 2026]. National prices grow 1-3%, with 5-8% along transit corridors [Property Outlook 2025]. ~4.7% value CAGR to 2030 [Property Outlook 2025].

Bull & bear

Bull: BI pivots back to cuts, the rupiah stabilizes, and the supply gap drives a sharper corridor repricing — 6-7% value CAGR [Kira estimates]. Bear: persistent rupiah weakness forces tighter policy, Moody's negative outlook bites, and Nusantara stalls — sub-3% CAGR with the recovery deferred to 2028 [Kira estimates].

Key sensitivities

The decisive variable is the mortgage-rate path after the May 2026 hike [BI 2026]; second is rupiah stability, which frames both policy and foreign appetite [Global Property Guide 2026]. Construction-cost inflation (~21%) and Nusantara's funding pace are the supply-side wildcards [BI SHPR 2026, Linos 2026].
