Thailand's modern trade market — 2027 inflection

Convenience is now the load-bearing format of Thai retail, and one operator owns it: 7-Eleven runs 15,945 stores and 70.6% of convenience value [CP All 2025][Krungsri 2025]. The 2027 question is no longer who leads — it is whether growth comes from new stores, or from basket, format and data as the density curve flattens.

Market context

Thai modern trade is worth around THB 2.5 tn, inside a total retail market that grew just 3.0% in 2025 — the slowest in four years [SCB EIC 2025][KResearch 2025]. Convenience is the exception: a THB 638 bn segment growing 5.3% [Krungsri 2025], more than 90% of the country's ~20,000 modern-trade outlets [USDA 2024]. 7-Eleven's 15,945 stores [CP All 2025] make Thailand the third-largest 7-Eleven country on earth.

Why now: three convergent forces

First, demand is capped by debt — household debt sits at 86.8% of GDP, the highest in ASEAN [BOT 2025], and private consumption is slowing [IMF 2025]. Second, challenger capital has arrived: CJ More targets 2,000 stores and the Saha–Lawson venture 1,000 [Bangkok Post 2025][Nation 2025]. Third, the fight is shifting to O2O and food service — 7-Eleven's online channels already run at 11% of sales [CP All 2024].

Five strategic implications for market participants

Five forces materially reshape competitive dynamics through 2032. Each is anchored to documented market data and KIRA's synthesis from modern-trade studies across Southeast Asia.

01 · DENSITY
The density curve is flattening — store maths tighten
With ~20,000 convenience outlets over ~66 million people [USDA 2024], Thailand is among the region's densest markets. At +700 stores a year [CP All 2025], incremental sites increasingly cannibalise. Same-store sales turned negative in Q2 2025 even as profit rose [CP All Q2 2025] — the growth model is shifting.
Anchor: ~20,000 convenience outlets
02 · CONCENTRATION
A near-monopoly the state now watches
7-Eleven holds 70.6% of convenience value [Krungsri 2025] and the top three chains 95.3% [LH Bank 2025], all under a handful of conglomerates. The Trade Competition Act B.E. 2560 governs this ground [TCCT 2024]; every merger or exclusivity deal now carries antitrust weight.
Anchor: 95.3% top-3 value share
03 · CHALLENGER
Conglomerate capital enters the format war
CJ More targets 2,000 stores by 2026, 3,000 by 2030 [Bangkok Post 2025]; the Saha–Lawson venture aims for 1,000 in five years [Nation 2025]; Central rebranded FamilyMart to Tops Daily [CRC 2024]. New entrants are not startups — they are rival tycoons with balance sheets.
Anchor: 2,000+1,000 challenger store targets
04 · BASKET
Value migrates to food service and O2O
With an average ticket near THB 85 [CP All 2024], growth comes from ready-to-eat, fresh food and delivery, not footfall. O2O already runs at 11% of 7-Eleven sales [CP All 2024]; 7Delivery promises 30-minute drop as quick commerce compounds double-digit [Kira estimates].
Anchor: 11% O2O share of sales
05 · DEMOGRAPHICS
A complete aged society resets the shelf
Thailand became a complete aged society in 2025, with over-60s above 20% and births below deaths for a fifth year [NESDC 2025][DOP 2025]. A shrinking working-age base caps volume; health, personal care and single-serve mixes carry the value story instead.
Anchor: >20% population over 60

Business overview

CP All has operated 7-Eleven in Thailand since its first store in 1989 and today runs the country's convenience category with 15,945 stores — roughly 70.6% of convenience value [CP All 2025][Krungsri 2025]. Revenue reached THB 1.02 tn in 2025, up 3.5%, with net profit of THB 28.2 bn [CP All 2025]. The estate splits ~51% company-owned, ~43% store-business-partner and ~6% sub-area [CP All 2024].

Margin & basket

Gross margin rose to 22.6% in 2024 from 21.9%, and net profit jumped 37.1% to THB 25.3 bn [CP All 2024], led by a mix shift into higher-margin ready-to-eat and food service. Average ticket sits near THB 85, and O2O channels — 7Delivery and All Online — already account for 11% of sales [CP All 2024].

Strategic positioning

Best-positioned for a density-plateau market. With annual capex of THB 12-13.6 bn and ~700 new stores a year [CP All 2025], the group is pivoting spend toward store refresh, food service and its 20 m+ ALL Member data engine rather than raw count — a shift underlined by negative same-store sales but rising profit in Q2 2025 [CP All Q2 2025].

Strengths & watchlist

  • Density: more stores than all rivals combined, several times over
  • Supply chain: owned distribution and CP food ecosystem
  • Data: 20 m+ ALL Member closed loop, O2O at 11% of sales
  • Watch: antitrust scrutiny under the Trade Competition Act [TCCT 2024]; challenger capex from CJ More and Lawson [Bangkok Post 2025]; consumption capped by household debt [BOT 2025]

Section 10 · AI Impact on Modern Trade
020 / 116

AI in Thai convenience: from loyalty data to catchment assortment

With the density curve flattening, growth must come from basket and turns, not new sites. KIRA reads AI's decisive pull in Thai convenience across loyalty personalisation, 200-metre-catchment assortment and O2O search — the levers that lift ticket and margin without a single new store.

The plateau catalyst

Same-store sales turned negative in Q2 2025 even as profit rose [CP All Q2 2025]. When footfall stops compounding, the return on demand sensing, planogram optimisation and personalised offers rises sharply — the assortment, not the site count, becomes the growth engine.

The data catalyst

A 20 m+ ALL Member closed loop [CP All 2025] gives the leader one of Southeast Asia's largest first-party retail datasets. CP All has committed to 60% of staff using AI by 2028 and runs partner AI programmes with Google, AWS and state agencies [Brand Inside 2025][Marketing Oops 2025].

Where value concentrates

KIRA reads the largest near-term returns in operators that pair loyalty scale with owned supply chains — the same groups that already lead on density, widening the data gap to sub-scale challengers.

Six AI applications reshaping Thai convenience operators

AI translation into operating practice will distinguish 2027 winners from laggards. Use cases below span loyalty personalisation through distribution optimisation, with deployment patterns observable at Thai operators or the closest regional benchmarks today.

01 · LOYALTY
Personalised offers on the ALL Member loop
A 20 m+ member base [CP All 2025] lets ML target coupons, birthday rewards and member pricing to individual baskets. The lever raises frequency and ticket where footfall has stopped growing — the core defence of a density-saturated network.
Rail: ALL Member app, redesigned 2024
02 · ASSORTMENT
Catchment-level planogram & demand sensing
AI planograms tailor SKU mix to the 200-metre catchment demographics of each store [Kira estimates], maximising turns on a fixed shelf. Store-week-SKU demand sensing cuts out-of-stocks and waste on short-life ready-to-eat lines.
Benchmark: regional MT demand-sensing
03 · O2O
AI product search & 30-minute fulfilment
Gosoft's goSearch AI powers product search on 7Delivery, learning from prior queries to lift conversion [Gosoft 2024]. With O2O at 11% of sales [CP All 2024], search relevance and basket-building directly move the digital P&L.
Deployed: 7-Eleven TH app, 7Delivery
04 · FOOD SERVICE
Fresh-food forecasting & waste control
Ready-to-eat and fresh drove the margin lift to 22.6% gross in 2024 [CP All 2024], but carry spoilage risk. Demand forecasting on short-shelf-life SKUs trims markdowns and shrink — the margin defence as the basket premiumises.
Tie-in: CP owned food supply chain
05 · WORKFORCE
Store-ops copilots & AI upskilling
CP All targets 60% of staff using AI by 2028 [Brand Inside 2025], with Creative AI Camp partners including Google, AWS and state agencies [Marketing Oops 2025]. Copilots for ordering, rostering and reporting lift per-store labour productivity.
Programme: Creative AI Camp, year 8
06 · LOGISTICS
Distribution & last-mile optimisation
Replenishing ~16,000 stores is a routing problem at national scale. ML on distribution-centre allocation and delivery routing, plus robot and rider pilots, compresses cost per drop as O2O volume climbs toward ~20% of sales [Kira estimates].
Pilot: automated delivery trials
