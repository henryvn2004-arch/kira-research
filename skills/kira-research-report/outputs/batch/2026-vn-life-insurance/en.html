Contraction bottoms, not breaks

Gross written premium fell to ~VND 146 trn in 2025, down ~1.3%, the third straight annual decline after −12% in 2023 and ~−6% in 2024 [GlobalData 2025]. But first-half 2025 premium grew ~2.4% year on year to VND 72.1 trn [MoF Insurance 2025]. The market has stopped falling — and growth returns at ~3.8% in 2026 [GlobalData 2025].

The cause was distribution, not demand

The downturn traced to a mis-selling crisis and the regulatory overhaul of bancassurance, where new-business premium collapsed ~44% in 2023 [AM Best 2024]. With penetration at ~2.3% of GDP versus an ASEAN ~3.35% and a 3.5% national target [Kira estimates], the underlying need is intact; the channels that sell into it must be rebuilt.

01 · DISTRIBUTION
Rebuild bancassurance on advice, not tying
With the 60-day loan-linked sales ban and the tying prohibition in force [Circular 67/2023], the old credit-attached model is closed. Re-engineer the bank channel around needs-based advice and protection products, or cede the recovering volume to agency.

Treat agent quality as the moat

Pivot the book to protection
Investment-linked share fell from ~70% to ~66.9% as term life rose to ~8.2% in 2025 [MoF Insurance 2025]. Decree 46 caps ILP cover to death and disability [Decree 46/2023]. Lead with health and term, ride riders.

Section 04 · Market size, premium and contraction
007 / 017

A USD 6 bn market that has stopped falling

Gross written premium settled near VND 146 trn in 2025 after a three-year slide; the first-half recovery and a forecast turn point to a shallow, distribution-led rebound.

Scale and trajectory

Life GWP reached ~VND 146.1 trn — about USD 6.0 bn — in 2025, down ~1.3% and a third consecutive decline [GlobalData 2025]. The book peaked above VND 160 trn in 2022 before the mis-selling crisis cut volumes back toward 2017 levels [Milliman 2025].

The half-year inflection

First-half 2025 premium rose ~2.4% to VND 72.1 trn, with benefit payouts up sharply as the in-force book matured [MoF Insurance 2025]. The turn is real but shallow — GlobalData models ~3.8% growth in 2026 after a near-flat 2025 [GlobalData 2025].

The penetration floor

At ~2.3% of GDP, penetration sits below the ASEAN ~3.35% average and well under the 3.5% national 2030 target [Kira estimates]. The structural wedge — first-time insured adults — keeps the long-run pull intact.

Section 06 · The bancassurance overhaul
010 / 017

From golden goose to re-regulated channel

Bancassurance carried half of new business at its peak, then fell hardest in the crisis. New rules close the loan-tied model and force a rebuild around advice and protection.

The rise and the crash

Bank-distributed premium climbed from ~21% of new life business in 2018 to ~50% in 2023 before mis-selling scandals broke trust; new-business premium through banks fell ~44% in 2023 [AM Best 2024]. By 2025 the channel sat far below its peak, with several banks reporting insurance fee income sharply down [Kira estimates].

The rule reset

Circular 67/2023 bars credit institutions from selling or arranging insurance within 60 days before or after loan disbursement [Circular 67/2023]. The 2024 Law on Credit Institutions, effective 1 July 2024, prohibits tying non-mandatory insurance to banking services [LOCI 2024]. The credit-attached sale is closed.

The shape of the rebuild

Early-2025 data shows a tentative banca recovery on cleaner, advice-led sales [Fili 2025]. Banks are re-engaging on improved terms — but the model now competes on protection value, not loan leverage.

Section 07 · Six forces reshaping distribution
011 / 017

Six forces reshaping how life cover is sold

The distribution reset runs through regulation, product rules and channel economics at once.
01 · RULE
The 60-day loan rule
Circular 67 bans insurance sales within 60 days before or after loan disbursement [Circular 67/2023], severing the credit-attached channel that drove the 2018-23 boom.
Observable: loan-tied banca closed
02 · RULE
The tying prohibition
The 2024 Law on Credit Institutions bars tying non-mandatory insurance to banking services [LOCI 2024]. Banks may advise and sell, but not coerce — resetting incentives toward suitability.
Observable: effective Jul 2024
03 · PRODUCT
ILP cover narrowed
Decree 46 limits investment-linked policies to death and total permanent disability [Decree 46/2023]; extras move to standalone riders, simplifying the sale and the illustration.
Observable: Art. 102, from Jul 2025
04 · PORTFOLIO
Product withdrawals
Carriers pulled non-compliant lines: AIA discontinued 16 products; Chubb Life and others withdrew ILP and hybrid plans from July 2025 [Kira estimates]. The shelf is being cleaned.
Observable: AIA −16 product lines
05 · CHANNEL
Agency back to #2
As banca contracted, agency returned to the second channel at ~12.7% of new premium in H1 2025 [MoF Insurance 2025] — but on fewer, better-trained advisers.
Observable: ~12.7% NBP share
06 · CHANNEL
Digital and e-insurance
Direct and e-insurance journeys deepen as the shift from banca to digital accelerates into 2026 [GlobalData 2025]. Hybrid digital-agent models lower cost-to-serve on protection.
Observable: e-insurance scaling

Section 09 · Agency mix and quality
013 / 017

Fewer agents, more professionals: the quality rebuild

Agency headcount is falling while the channel reclaims share — the rebuild trades volume for competence and persistency.

The headcount reset

Active-agent numbers have declined and new recruitment has grown harder as carriers tighten quality gates [Vietnam.vn 2025]. By mid-2025, 100% of life insurers had reviewed and corrected agency operations under Decree 46 [MoF Insurance 2025]. Quality is now the recruiting filter, not the slogan.

MDRT as the benchmark

The industry increasingly measures professionalism by MDRT membership — agents who lead teams and anchor persistency [Vietnam.vn 2025]. Carriers are building structured competency frameworks and certification rather than chasing raw adviser counts.

The share signal

Agency rebounded to the #2 channel at ~12.7% of new premium in H1 2025 [MoF Insurance 2025], reclaiming ground from banca even as the agent base shrank — evidence that productivity per adviser is rising.

Section 12 · Regulatory and product frame
015 / 017

The regulatory frame: a cleaner market by design

A 2022-25 sequence of law, decree and circular reset distribution conduct and product design — the finance ministry's insurance authority holds the rule on each.

The new statutory base

The Insurance Business Law (No. 08/2022/QH15) took effect 1 January 2023, strengthening conduct, capital and licensing [IBL 2022]. Decree 46/2023 and Circular 67/2023 then guided implementation, raising charter-capital floors and tightening bancassurance conditions [Decree 46/2023].

The distribution clamp

Circular 67's 60-day loan rule and the 2024 Law on Credit Institutions' tying ban closed the credit-attached channel [LOCI 2024]. Insurers had to comply with the new agency conditions by 1 July 2024 [Decree 46/2023].

The product reset

From July 2025, ILP cover narrowed to death and disability under Decree 46 Article 102 [Decree 46/2023], pushing carriers to re-shelf around term, health and rider-based protection — a cleaner, more comparable product set.
