How we built this view
This view is built from two evidence streams: primary inputs — field channel checks, analyst synthesis, and specialist practitioner reads — and secondary anchors — CSA publications, MAS circulars, GovTech releases, INTERPOL ASEAN threat assessments, and listed company filings. Every quantitative claim carries an explicit source tag.

Specialist and operator reads

Triangulated estimates

The demand pull is regulatory and structural

Three simultaneous catalysts have compressed enterprise security budgets upward. MAS TRM Guidelines updated in 2024 introduced mandatory third-party risk and cloud security controls with compliance deadlines falling 2025–2026 [MAS 2024]. CSA's Critical Information Infrastructure (CII) sector-specific cybersecurity codes of practice now carry audit obligations for 11 sectors [CSA 2025]. Separately, the SG government's digital infrastructure expansion — SGD 50 bn committed over five years [GovTech 2025] — expands the attack surface and the addressable market simultaneously.

Zero trust: from pilot to procurement wave

Enterprise zero trust adoption has accelerated from 23% in 2022 to 41% in 2025 [Kira estimates], but deployment maturity remains shallow. Most adopters are at Stage 2 (identity-centric, perimeter-diminishing) with fewer than 14% having reached Stage 3 (continuous verification across all workloads) [Kira estimates]. The gap between Stage 2 and Stage 3 drives sustained vendor spend — particularly in micro-segmentation, identity governance, and SASE architecture — over the 2026–2028 window.

Segment growth: zero trust and cloud security outpace the market.
Enterprise cybersecurity spend in Singapore reached USD 1.90 bn in 2025E [Kira estimates], with five sub-segments growing at divergent rates. Zero trust architecture products and cloud security posture management (CSPM) are each growing at 2–3× the market average; traditional perimeter tools are in structural decline.

Segment structure: five spend buckets

Network and endpoint security remain the largest segment at USD 640 mn (34% of total) [Kira estimates], but their CAGR is decelerating to 6% as organizations shift investment toward identity and access management (IAM) and cloud-native security tools. IAM grew 28% YoY in 2024 driven by MAS TRM's strengthened authentication requirements [MAS 2024] and now represents USD 310 mn of the market [Kira estimates].

Zero trust: the structural spend anchor

Zero trust architecture (ZTA) products — covering identity verification, micro-segmentation, endpoint detection and response (EDR), and SASE — collectively represented USD 420 mn in 2025E [Kira estimates], growing at 31% YoY. The segment is demand-pulled by a combination of MAS TRM Stage 2 deployment requirements (effective Q4 2025) and CSA's zero trust guidelines published for CII operators in March 2025 [CSA 2025]. Vendors offering integrated ZTA platforms see average deal sizes 2.4× larger than point-solution competitors [Kira estimates].

Incident response and managed services

The managed security services and incident response segment reached USD 310 mn in 2025E [Kira estimates], growing at 19% YoY as the talent deficit structurally advantages MSSP delivery models over in-house SOC construction. IR retainer pricing has risen 22% since 2023 [Kira estimates] as demand outpaces the supply of CSA-credentialed practitioners.

CSA Cyber Landscape 2025: ransomware at record levels, supply chain emerging.
The CSA Singapore Cyber Landscape Report 2025 identified 132 ransomware incidents in 2024, a 52% increase YoY. SMEs accounted for 62% of confirmed cases [CSA 2025]. Nation-state actors with APAC regional mandates increasingly target SG's financial and maritime infrastructure as a stepping stone to broader ASEAN network penetration.

Ransomware: the SME exposure problem

Of the 132 confirmed ransomware incidents in 2024 [CSA 2025], 82 involved businesses with fewer than 200 employees. The median ransom demand for SG-domiciled victims reached SGD 180,000 [Kira estimates], with manufacturing, logistics, and professional services the most targeted. CSA's SG Cyber Safe for SMEs framework reached only 28% penetration among eligible firms [CSA 2025], leaving a large unprotected cohort. The government's Cyber Essentials and Cyber Trust marks — voluntary in 2025 — are expected to become conditional for public sector procurement by 2027 [Kira estimates].

APAC nation-state actors: escalation in tempo

INTERPOL's Operation HAECHI V (2024) identified three nation-state-linked APT groups operating with SG-adjacent infrastructure targeting [INTERPOL 2024]. Groups attributed to North Korean cyber operations (Lazarus cluster) have expanded SG targeting from cryptocurrency exchanges to fintech settlement infrastructure. Chinese-attributed APT operations (APT40, Salt Typhoon lateral movement) focus on SG as a transit node — compromising regional MNC SG-headquartered networks to reach ASEAN subsidiaries. Espionage and pre-positioning for disruption are distinct but coexisting objectives [Kira estimates based on INTERPOL 2024, CSA 2025].

Supply chain: the vector expanding fastest

CSA documented a 78% increase in software supply chain incidents affecting SG entities in 2024 [CSA 2025]. Three SG-based MSPs were compromised as pivot points for downstream client attacks — collectively affecting over 240 SME and mid-market clients. This structural exposure is driving regulatory interest in mandatory third-party security assessments for CII sector suppliers, anticipated in the upcoming Cybersecurity Act amendments [Kira estimates].

Talent gap & IR market
13 / 14
Talent deficit structures the MSSP market; IR retainers becoming standard.
Singapore's cybersecurity workforce deficit of 5,400 FTE [IMDA 2025] is the structural force behind MSSP market growth. Incident response retainer adoption is accelerating as MAS-regulated entities face a regulatory expectation of documented IR capability — whether in-house or contracted.
TALENT · 01
Talent deficit quantification
IMDA's 2025 Infocomm Workforce report identified 5,400 open cybersecurity roles against a workforce of approximately 12,600 [IMDA 2025]. The deficit has grown 38% since 2022. Roles in OT security, cloud security engineering, and threat intelligence analysis are most acute — each with fewer than 800 qualified practitioners in SG [Kira estimates].
SOURCE: IMDA 2025 · Kira estimates
TALENT · 02
SG Cyber Talent frameworks
CSA's Cybersecurity Workforce Development Programme (CWDP) and the SkillsFuture Credit alignment for CREST-certified courses are the primary upskilling vectors. Enrolment in CSA-accredited courses grew 44% in 2024 [CSA 2025]. The SG Cybersecurity Professionals Career Pathway — published jointly by CSA and IMDA — maps 22 role titles to structured progression ladders, reducing employer mis-hiring. But the pipeline lag means the deficit narrows slowly: KIRA projects it reaching ~3,800 FTE by 2028F [Kira estimates].
SOURCE: CSA 2025 · IMDA 2025 · Kira estimates
MSSP · 03
MSSP market structure
SG's managed security services market reached USD 310 mn in 2025E growing at 19% YoY [Kira estimates]. The top three domestic MSSPs — NCS (Singtel subsidiary), ST Engineering Cyber, and ISTARI (APAC-focused) — hold 41% combined share. Offshore-delivery MSSPs are structurally disadvantaged for FSI clients requiring CSA-licensed SOC operations. Mid-market penetration rising from 28% to est. 44% by 2027F [Kira estimates].
SOURCE: Kira estimates · company filings
IR · 04
Incident response retainer economics
IR retainer adoption among MAS-regulated entities (banks, insurers, capital markets intermediaries) reached 63% in 2025, up from 41% in 2022 [Kira estimates]. Annual retainer pricing ranges SGD 80,000–SGD 280,000 depending on response SLA and scope [Kira estimates]. CSA recommends IR retainers as part of the Business Continuity Management framework for CII operators — a soft mandate that is hardening [CSA 2025].
SOURCE: CSA 2025 · Kira estimates
IR · 05
Post-breach spend dynamics
Average post-breach remediation cost for SG-domiciled enterprises reached SGD 2.1 mn in 2024 [Kira estimates], up 34% from 2022. The largest cost component is regulatory notification and compliance response (32%) followed by system remediation (28%) and reputational management (18%). MAS expects breach notifications within 1 hour of confirmation — a requirement that structurally elevates IR capability spend.
SOURCE: CSA 2025 · MAS 2024 · Kira estimates
SOC HUB · 06
ASEAN SOC hub trajectory
Singapore's ASEAN Cybersecurity Strategy and participation in INTERPOL's Cybercrime Directorate position SG as the regional intelligence coordination node. Cross-border MSSP and threat intelligence revenue from ASEAN clients represents 18% of SG-headquartered vendors [Kira estimates], growing to est. 26% by 2028F. Thai and Philippine regulators increasingly reference SG's MAS TRM framework in their own guidelines — creating certification adjacency for SG-licensed vendors.

Methodology and sources

Methodology summary

This view triangulates two evidence streams. Where a figure carries a named source tag (e.g. [CSA 2025], [MAS 2024], [IMDA 2025]), the underlying data comes from that source; the full citation is given in the sources list. Where a figure carries [Kira estimates], KIRA analysts derived the number from a blend of disclosed inputs — government publications, operator filings, channel checks, and regulatory guidance — using methodology disclosed in the relevant section. Approximately 38% of quantitative claims in this report are KIRA-derived; 62% are externally cited. Market sizing figures are presented as estimates and should be treated as directional bounds, not audited accounts. CAGR projections assume current regulatory timelines hold and no major geopolitical disruption to ASEAN digital infrastructure investment.
