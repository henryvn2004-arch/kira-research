Indonesia healthcare — 2026 inflection

A USD 19.5 bn hospital market meeting the largest single-payer reset in a decade: BPJS Kesehatan's KRIS standard-class rollout, Rp 9.56 tn payer deficit, INA-CBGs migrating to iDRG, and a still-fragmented private sector where the top 10-15 chains hold only ~10% of private beds.

The payer is squeezing; the system absorbs it

BPJS Kesehatan's Rp 9.56 tn deficit in 2024 reflects rebound utilisation, not pricing failure — claims hit Rp 174.9 tn against Rp 165.73 tn premiums [Bisnis 2025]. KRIS (Perpres 59/2024) standardises inpatient facilities and primes a single-tariff move; the INA-CBGs to iDRG transition will reprice case-mix on severity-weighted logic [Kompas 2025]. Hospitals that ran on class-1 cross-subsidy must rebuild margin elsewhere.

Private chains are still 10% of private beds

1,787 private hospitals (63.5% of the national 2,820 stock) hold an estimated 180,000 beds [Databoks 2025, Kira estimates]; the top 10-15 chains operate ~10% of that pool. CR4 sits below 40 nationally [JAKI 2024]. Djarum's Rp 1.04 tn Hermina entry [Suara 2025] and Quadria's Hermina stake [DealStreetAsia 2025] signal the consolidation thesis is now backed by patient capital.

Five strategic implications for operators

Where market participants should focus capital and product effort over the next 24 months — ranked by the asymmetry of return given how KRIS, iDRG and PE capital are now scoring.

01
Stage the KRIS conversion
Class-1 differentiation revenue will compress. Reconfigure bed mix toward day-care, ambulatory and high-acuity where private willingness-to-pay still holds — not toward defending a vanishing premium tier. KRIS criteria require 12 facility upgrades [Perpres 59/2024]; capex schedule and tariff timing must match.

02
Reprice for iDRG severity logic
INA-CBGs to iDRG migration adds severity and clinical-complexity weighting. Tariff variance of 46-75% on common procedures [JFSP 2025] becomes either the upside or the squeeze, depending on coding capability. Build the clinical-documentation function as a profit lever, not back-office.

Be the acquirer or be acquired
Djarum and Quadria are now active in Hermina. TPG owns Columbia Asia. AdMedika is in play. Sub-scale independents face capital-cost disadvantage on KRIS retrofits and iDRG coding investment — price-discovery for a sale will be most generous in the next 12-18 months.

Section 05 · Macro indicators
007 / 016

Macro indicators driving healthcare demand

Indonesia's macro mix — 5.0-5.2% GDP, 2.4% CPI, 4.75% BI policy rate [IMF 2026, BI 2026] — gives healthcare planners stable nominal growth. The constraints are not macro: they are health spend at 2.9% of GDP, 0.20 doctors per 1,000, and ~2 million patients still leaving the country for care annually.

Coverage breadth, financing depth still shallow

JKN reached 280 m members by July 2025 — 98% of the 286.7 m population [BPJS 2025] — making it one of the world's largest single-payer systems. But coverage is wider than financing: total health expenditure is Rp 639.9 tn (2.9% of GDP) in 2024 [WHO 2026], well below SEA peers' 4-6% band and OECD's 9%.

Workforce is the binding constraint

The system carries 1.05 m health workers (3.84 per 1,000), short of WHO's 4.45 threshold for 80% UHC [medRxiv 2024]. Doctors are the thinnest layer: 0.20 per 1,000 vs WHO's 0.5 [WHO 2024]. PP 28/2024 opening pathway for foreign medical personnel is partly a workforce response.

Outbound leakage at USD 9 bn

~2 m Indonesians sought treatment abroad in 2022, with ~1 m to Malaysia, ~750k to Singapore [JakPost 2023]. Estimated economic leakage USD 9.2-11.5 bn annually [Arab News 2025]. The Sanur medical-tourism SEZ (Jun 2025) is the first onshore retention play at scale.

Section 06 · Market sizing & segments
008 / 016

Market sizing & segments

The hospital-services pool is USD 19.5 bn in 2025 [KenResearch 2025], inside a broader healthcare-providers definition reaching USD 49 bn. Segment growth is uneven: digital health and HIS run double-digit CAGR; the inpatient base grows in line with nominal GDP plus a JKN-utilisation kicker.

Hospital base vs digital adjacents

Core hospital services USD 19.5 bn (2025) [KenResearch 2025] grows ~8% nominal to ~USD 30 bn by 2030 [Kira estimates anchored on Ken + Statista]. Health insurance penetration outside JKN is USD 1.77 bn (2026) [Market Report Analytics]; connected healthcare USD 1.13 bn (2026) → 3.81 bn (2031) at 27.4% CAGR [Kira estimates] — the fastest-moving adjacency.

Why the JKN pool doesn't translate to revenue

JKN claims paid 2024: Rp 174.9 tn (~USD 11.0 bn) [Bisnis 2025]. Of which ~60-70% lands in hospitals [Kira estimates from INA-CBGs flow], suggesting BPJS underwrites ~USD 7-8 bn of hospital revenue. Out-of-pocket fills the gap: 31.5% of total health spend [WHO 2024], concentrated on premium inpatient, dental, and specialists.

Sub-segment growth ranking

Healthcare Information Systems USD 1.06 bn (2023) → 3.10 bn (2030) at 16.5% CAGR [Grand View 2024]; health check-up USD 656 m (2024) → 1.15 bn (2030) at 9.9% [Grand View 2024]; CRO USD 1.26 bn (2023) → 2.20 bn (2030) at 8.3% [Grand View 2024]. The provider-services core is the slowest of the cohort but the largest pool by ~10x.

SILOAM — THE PREMIUM SCALE PLAY

9M25 revenue Rp 9.43 tn (+3.3% YoY) [SILO 9M2025]; net profit Rp 797 bn (+19% YoY). FY24 underlying EBITDA margin 29.2%. JCI accreditation at flagship sites supports private-pay tilt; premium positioning leaves Siloam less exposed to the KRIS class-1 compression than mid-market chains.

MITRA KELUARGA — HIGH-MARGIN DENSITY

FY24 revenue Rp 4.87 tn (+14.3% YoY); net income Rp 1.15 tn (+25.1% YoY) [MIKA FY2024]. ~25 hospitals concentrated in Jabodetabek + Surabaya. Highest profitability in the listed cohort — payor mix tilted to corporate-insured + cash-pay segments insulates from BPJS deficit squeeze.

HERMINA — BPJS-FIRST, NOW DJARUM-BACKED

52 hospitals in 63 cities, 17 provinces — the broadest geographical footprint of any private chain [HEAL FY2024]. ~70% BPJS revenue mix [Quadria 2025]. Djarum's Dwimuria vehicle bought ~Rp 1.04 tn at Rp 1,875/share in Jun 2025 [Suara 2025]; Quadria has been on the cap table since 2017.

THE THREE-MODEL CONVERGENCE

  • Premium (SILO): defend tertiary mix, capture medical-tourism repatriation via Bali SEZ
  • High-margin density (MIKA): add adjacent catchments, deepen corporate-insured book
  • BPJS-anchored breadth (HEAL): scale into Tier-2 / Tier-3 cities while iDRG coding is still maturing
  • All three converge on day-care, ambulatory, and clinical-AI as the next-leg growth wedge

AI imaging triage

5-year outlook & scenarios

Base case carries the hospital market from USD 19.5 bn (2025) to ~USD 30 bn by 2030 [Kira estimates]. The variance is set by three dials: KRIS execution speed, iDRG calibration, and how much medical-tourism leakage Indonesia recaptures.

BASE CASE
USD 30bn (2030)
~8% nominal CAGR. KRIS rollout on schedule by 2027; iDRG fully phased 2028. Top-10 chain share rises from ~10% to ~16% of private beds via tuck-in M&A. Medical-tourism leakage holds at USD 8-10 bn. BPJS deficit narrows but doesn't close.
UPSIDE
USD 34bn (2030)
~10% CAGR. Foreign-hospital entrants execute Bali SEZ tertiary + JKT/SGP-comparable specialty; USD 3-4 bn outflow repatriated. iDRG transparency unlocks margin for accurate coders; chain consolidation reaches ~20% top-10 share. Quadria + Djarum-style capital continues at >USD 500 m/yr.
DOWNSIDE
USD 26bn (2030)
~6% CAGR. KRIS rollout slips past 2027; BPJS deficit widens, claim-holds re-tighten; rural hospital fragility deepens. PE entry slows on macro shock; foreign-hospital permission stalls on local-association lobby. ~3-5% of small private hospitals exit or downsize by 2028.

What moves Indonesia between scenarios

KRIS execution speed — full single-tariff implementation 2027 vs 2029 changes ~USD 2 bn of 2030 outcome.

iDRG calibration — if severity weights are set conservatively, listed chains absorb 100-200 bps margin uplift; aggressive parity costs the system the same.

Outflow recapture — every USD 1 bn of medical-tourism repatriation is ~3% of the 2030 hospital market — large lever, modest realised so far.

Foreign-hospital entrants — if Mt. Elizabeth / Raffles / Bumrungrad-tier operators enter Bali SEZ by 2027, premium ASP firms and Siloam/Mayapada either partner or lose tertiary share.

HOW WE TAG

Where a figure carries a named-source tag (e.g. [BPJS 2025], [SILO 9M2025], [Perpres 59/2024]), the underlying data comes from that source — the full citation appears in the source list to the right and in each page's source-key footer. ESTIMATE METHODOLOGIES DISCLOSED

Top-10 chain bed share (~10%): Sum of disclosed beds across Hermina (6,200), Siloam (~4,900), MIKA (~3,250), Mayapada (~1,400), Primaya/Awal Bros (~1,150), plus inferred ~5-6 next-tier chains, divided by Databoks-implied private bed pool of ~180,000.
