Thailand's palm oil market — 2027 inflection

Thailand is the world's third-largest palm producer at just ~4.7% of global output [USDA 2025] — a domestic, smallholder crop whose economics are set by two levers the state controls: the biodiesel mandate and the farm-gate price floor. The 2027 window resets both.

Market context

Thailand produced 3.27 Mt of crude palm oil in 2024 from 6.34 million rai of harvested oil palm [OAE 2025], with roughly 72% consumed domestically and only ~2% exported [USDA 2025]. Unlike Malaysia and Indonesia, this is an inward-facing crop: biodiesel absorbed 1.05 Mt of CPO and refined food oil 1.44 Mt in 2024 [Krungsri 2025]. Price and demand are policy-made, not export-made.

Why now: three convergent forces

First, the AEDP 2024 caps biodiesel demand at 900 million litres by 2037 [DEDE 2024], scrapping B10 and leaving B7 and B20 — a structural demand ceiling for the sector's largest CPO buyer. Second, the B100 price subsidy is set to end around 2027 [Advanced Biofuels 2026], removing the cushion under farm-gate FFB. Third, EUDR from 30 December 2026 [SGS Thailand 2026] exposes a supply chain that is ~85% smallholder and thinly traced [RSPO 2025].

Five strategic implications for market participants

Five forces materially reshape competitive dynamics through 2032. Each is anchored to documented market data and KIRA's synthesis from soft-commodity studies across Southeast Asia.

01 · MANDATE
The biodiesel ceiling is now the binding constraint
AEDP 2024 caps biodiesel at 900 ML by 2037, down from a 1,570 ML draft [DEDE 2024], with B10 scrapped and B7 the base blend. Biodiesel absorbs ~1.05 Mt of CPO [Krungsri 2025]; a flat-to-shrinking mandate removes the sector's demand escalator.
Anchor: 900 ML 2037 biodiesel cap
02 · YIELD
Yield — not new land — is the only supply lever
OER sits at 17.7% against a reform-plan target of 22-23% [OAE 2025], and FFB yield near 2,935 kg/rai lags the 3,500 kg/rai goal. Closing even half the gap adds more CPO than a decade of area growth. Smallholder agronomy is the prize.
Anchor: 17.7% vs 22-23% extraction gap
03 · SUBSIDY
The end of the B100 cushion resets farm-gate risk
The B100 price subsidy is set to end around 2027 [Advanced Biofuels 2026], and poor stock management has cost the state ~120 bn baht over six years [Nation 2026]. Without the cushion, FFB near THB 7-8/kg [Krungsri 2025] becomes far more price-volatile.
Anchor: ~2027 B100 subsidy sunset
04 · TRACEABILITY
EUDR turns a smallholder chain into a compliance test
EUDR applies from 30 December 2026 for large operators [SGS Thailand 2026]. With ~85% of supply from smallholders [RSPO 2025] who sell through collectors, plot-level traceability breaks where it matters most. Export access hinges on fixing it.
Anchor: ~85% smallholder supply share
05 · VALUE
Move up the chain: oleochemicals and specialty oils
With biodiesel capped and exports thin (~2% of output [USDA 2025]), value migrates to oleochemicals — detergents, soap, cosmetics — and niche products like red palm oil. Downstream demand grows 2.5-3.5% a year [Krungsri 2025] as the energy leg plateaus.
Anchor: ~2% of output exported

Business overview

A pioneer of Thailand's oil palm industry since its first plantations in 1969 and today the country's leading producer of crude palm oil and elite oil-palm seed [Univanich AR 2022]. The group runs five palm-oil crushing factories and two palm-kernel plants across Krabi, Phang-nga and Phatthalung, processing over 1.3 million tonnes of fresh fruit bunches to yield ~260,000 tonnes of CPO in 2021 [Univanich AR 2022].

Grower architecture

Buys fresh fruit bunches from thousands of southern smallholders alongside its own estates, and breeds high-yielding seed that lifts grower productivity — a direct lever on the national OER and FFB-yield gap. Delivered Thailand's first export of Certified Sustainable Palm Oil [Univanich AR 2022].

Strategic positioning

Best-positioned for the yield-and-traceability agenda. Its seed franchise and grower network make it the natural anchor for closing the OER gap from 17.7% toward the 22-23% reform target [OAE 2025], while early CSPO experience gives it a head start on EUDR-grade traceability from 30 December 2026 [SGS Thailand 2026].

Strengths & watchlist

  • Seed genetics: elite high-OER planting material
  • Integration: grower + five crushers + kernel plants
  • Certification: first Thai CSPO exporter, EUDR-ready base
  • Watch: biodiesel demand cap under AEDP 2024 [DEDE 2024]; FFB price volatility as the B100 subsidy sunsets ~2027 [Advanced Biofuels 2026]

AI in Thai palm: from satellite plot to extraction-rate control

The EU deforestation rule forces plot-level geolocation on EU-bound supply [SGS Thailand 2026], and the OER gap makes agronomic analytics a direct margin lever. KIRA reads these as the decisive pull for AI adoption across smallholder mapping, yield forecasting and mill process control.

The traceability catalyst

With EUDR live from 30 December 2026 [SGS Thailand 2026] and ~85% of supply from smallholders selling through collectors [RSPO 2025], satellite remote sensing and digital plot registration are the only scalable route to deforestation-free proof — RSPO's Prisma platform is one such rail.

Yield-gap catalyst

OER at 17.7% against a 22-23% target [OAE 2025] and FFB yield near 2,935 kg/rai vs a 3,500 goal make agronomic analytics — NDVI, rainfall, tree-age — a direct margin lever on the country's largest crop by area.

Where value concentrates

KIRA reads the largest near-term AI returns in integrated crushers with seed and grower networks that amortise mapping and analytics across the plot-to-mill chain — the same groups the traceability rules already favour.

Six AI applications reshaping Thai palm operators

AI translation into operating practice will distinguish 2027 winners from laggards. Use cases below span smallholder plot registration through mill extraction-rate control, with deployment patterns observable in Thailand or the closest regional benchmarks today.

01 · TRACEABILITY
EUDR plot-level due diligence stack
Satellite remote sensing plus smallholder GPS plot registration generates the plot-level geo-data the EU rule requires from 30 December 2026 [SGS Thailand 2026]. Integrated crushers amortise the cost; loose-FFB buyers using collectors carry the traceability gap.
Rail: RSPO Prisma, group schemes
02 · LAND
Deforestation-risk & legality screening
ML models flag post-2020 clearing against the EUDR cut-off using Sentinel time-series imagery, proven for detecting smallholder plots and plantation age in Thailand [Nature 2021]. Buyers screen southern supply sheds before contracting EU-bound volume.
Benchmark: Copernicus Sentinel monitoring
03 · YIELD
Plot-level yield & replant targeting
ML on NDVI, rainfall and tree-age forecasts FFB output and prioritises replanting — acute given yield near 2,935 kg/rai vs a 3,500 target [OAE 2025] and water-limited yields below half of potential in drier zones [ScienceDirect 2016].
Tie-in: smallholder replant prioritisation
04 · MILL
FFB ripeness & extraction-rate grading
Vision models grade fresh-fruit-bunch ripeness and OER potential at the mill ramp — the direct lever on the 17.7% vs 22-23% extraction gap [OAE 2025]. Standardises grower payments and defends FFB supply against neighbouring crushers.
Pilot: southern-belt ramp-line vision
05 · INPUTS
Fertiliser & agronomy advisory
Advisory engines correct the 40-90% of recommended nutrient rates and counterfeit-seed problems that cap smallholder yield [ScienceDirect 2016]. Seed breeders like Univanich pair genetics with digital agronomy to lift grower OER at scale.
Tie-in: seed + agronomy bundles
06 · FEEDSTOCK
Feedstock allocation & price arbitrage
Engines arbitrate CPO between biodiesel (B100), refined food oil and thin exports as the AEDP mandate caps biodiesel [DEDE 2024] and the oil-fund price gap shifts. Allocation optimisation becomes a core margin lever as the B100 subsidy sunsets ~2027.
