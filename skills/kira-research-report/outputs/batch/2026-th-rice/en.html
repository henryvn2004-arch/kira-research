Market context

Thailand shipped 7.9 million tonnes of rice in 2025 [TREA 2026], beating its 7.5 Mt target and earning 148.2 billion baht (USD 4.5 billion) [TREA 2026]. Yet the country has slipped from second to third among world exporters, behind India and Vietnam [Platts 2026], and the Commerce Ministry guides only 7.0 Mt for 2026 [MOC 2026]. The centre of gravity shifts from volume to defending the premium.

Why now: three convergent forces

First, India lifted all export curbs and targets a record 30 Mt for 2026 [Platts 2026], pulling Thai 5% white-rice FOB toward ~USD 340/t [Platts 2026]. Second, the baht's strength priced Thai grain ~USD 40/t above Indian [Nation 2026] just as buyers turned origin-flexible. Third, IRRI-bred and Vietnamese aromatics — ST25 reached ~USD 1,200/t [Vietnam News 2025] — now contest the fragrant niche Hom Mali long owned.

Five strategic implications for market participants

Five forces materially reshape competitive dynamics through 2031. Each is anchored to documented market data and KIRA's synthesis from soft-commodity studies across Southeast Asia.

01 · PREMIUM
Hom Mali defends a narrowing, not a fixed, moat
Thai Hom Mali held ~USD 1,100–1,171/t FOB [TBS 2026], a USD 100–250/t premium over standard jasmine [TBS 2026]. But Vietnam's ST25 reached ~USD 1,200/t [Vietnam News 2025] and took the world-best-rice title Thailand won five years running. GI provenance, not aroma alone, is the durable defence.
Anchor: ~1,171/t Hom Mali FOB
02 · YIELD GAP
The lowest yield in ASEAN caps cost competitiveness
Thai paddy yields ~2.9 t/ha (370–600 kg/rai) [Nation 2026], against Vietnam's ~800 kg/rai. Production cost runs 7,200–7,500 baht/t versus Vietnam ~6,000 and India ~5,000 [Nation 2026]. Without a yield step-change, Thailand cannot win on price — only on quality.
Anchor: ~2.9 t/ha Thai paddy yield
03 · INDIA
India's surplus resets the global price floor
India lifted all curbs, targets 30 Mt of exports and earns ~USD 10.7 bn a year [Platts 2026]. A record 556.4 Mt global crop [FAO 2026] pushes Thai 5% white toward ~USD 340/t FOB [Platts 2026]. Thai white and parboiled volume is most exposed; the premium tier least.
Anchor: 30 Mt India 2026 target
04 · IRRI VARIETY
Bred aromatics contest the fragrant niche
Foreign breeders have built aromatic varieties with Hom Mali-like cooking traits but higher yield and pest resistance [FFTC 2026]. IRRI lines and Vietnamese ST series compress Thailand's quality lead; the Rice Department's own release pipeline is the only structural answer.
Anchor: photoperiod-bound Hom Mali, 1 crop/yr
05 · SUBSIDY TRAP
Populist support delays the productivity fix
The 2025 support paid 1,000 baht/rai, capped at 10,000/household, to 5.49 m households [MOC 2025]. Fourteen years of pledging and guarantees cost 1.2–1.3 trillion baht [Nation 2026] yet discouraged technology upgrade. Reform of support toward yield-tech is the lever.
Anchor: 5.49 m farming households

Business overview

A reference Thai rice exporter spanning Hom Mali jasmine, white rice and parboiled grades, with export capacity reaching ~2 million tonnes a year [TREA 2026]. The group has twice taken the Prime Minister's Export Award as a leading exporter, anchoring its position at the top of a market where total 2025 shipments reached 7.9 Mt [TREA 2026].

Channel architecture

Multi-market export distribution spanning government-to-government tenders, private bulk contracts, and branded retail jasmine. Forward supply covers the premium Hom Mali tier from the Northeast belt and the white-and-parboiled bulk flows from the Central Plain, with logistics through the Bangkok and Laem Chabang gates.

Strategic positioning

Best-positioned incumbent to defend the premium as India compresses bulk prices. Scale across grades lets the group cross-subsidise low-margin white-rice volume with branded Hom Mali [Kira estimates], holding buyer relationships while origin competition intensifies. The strategic question is how fast to invest in traceable GI provenance versus chase thin bulk margins.

Strengths & watchlist

  • Scale: ~2 Mt/yr capacity across the full grade range
  • Brand: Award-recognised exporter equity in 40+ markets
  • Premium: GI Hom Mali sourcing from the Isan belt
  • Watch: Baht strength and Indian undercutting compress white-rice margins; ST25 contests the aromatic tier

AI in Thai rice: from drone-mapped paddy to grain grading

The lowest yield in ASEAN forces productivity gains the subsidy cycle has not delivered [Nation 2026]. KIRA reads precision agriculture as the decisive pull for AI adoption across yield mapping, climate-smart cultivation and export quality control, layered onto a fast-growing agricultural-drone base.

The productivity catalyst

With Thai paddy yields stuck near 2.9 t/ha [Nation 2026] and a cost base above every regional rival, satellite and drone analytics are the only scalable route to closing the gap without new land — the structural constraint Hom Mali's single annual photoperiod-bound crop imposes.

Government & institutional catalyst

Thailand now hosts 200,000+ agricultural drones and 60,000+ registered operators [Chiang Rai 2026], growing 20%+ a year, with the Department targeting 5,000 more certified operators by 2027 [Chiang Rai 2026]. IRRI, the Rice Department and Kasetsart ran drone remote-sensing workshops in early 2026 [IRRI 2026].

Where value concentrates

KIRA reads the largest near-term AI returns in integrated exporter-miller groups that can amortise mapping, grading and traceability across contracted estates, mills and the export gate.

Six AI applications reshaping Thai rice operators

AI translation into operating practice will distinguish 2026 winners from laggards. Use cases below span plot-level yield mapping through export grain grading, with deployment patterns observable in Thailand or the closest regional benchmarks today.

01 · YIELD
Drone & satellite yield mapping
NDVI imagery from the 200,000+ drone base combined with rainfall and soil data forecasts paddy output plot by plot. Targets the yield gap at source — Thailand's ~2.9 t/ha versus Vietnam's higher base [Nation 2026] — sharpening replanting and input decisions.
Observable: Rungsit-plain precision platform
02 · CLIMATE
Low-emission, climate-smart cultivation
Drone-based remote sensing supports alternate-wetting-and-drying and methane-reduction protocols. The IRRI – Rice Department – Kasetsart 2026 workshops [IRRI 2026] target low-emission rice that protects EU and Japanese buyer access as carbon scrutiny rises.
Benchmark: IRRI low-emission rice program
03 · VARIETY
Breeding-pipeline acceleration
ML-assisted phenotyping speeds the Rice Department's release of higher-yield aromatics to answer IRRI and Vietnamese lines. Critical given Hom Mali's photoperiod-bound single annual crop [FFTC 2026] caps the productivity ceiling of the flagship variety.
Tie-in: Rice Department release pipeline
04 · GRADING
Computer-vision grain grading
Vision models grade milled-rice purity, broken-grain ratio and aroma-varietal authenticity at the mill and export gate. Defends the GI Hom Mali premium of USD 100–250/t [TBS 2026] against adulteration and protects buyer trust.
Pilot: exporter QC lines
05 · TRACE
GI provenance & traceability
Geo-tagged source-to-mill records authenticate Thung Kula Ronghai-origin Hom Mali, the basis of its 10–20% GI price premium [TBS 2026]. As aroma alone ceases to differentiate, verifiable origin becomes the durable moat.
Tie-in: GI registration enforcement
06 · PRICE
Export price & FX arbitrage
Engines arbitrate grade allocation and timing against Indian and Vietnamese FOB and the strong baht. With Thai 5% white pulled toward ~USD 340/t [Platts 2026], allocation and hedging optimisation becomes a core exporter margin lever.
