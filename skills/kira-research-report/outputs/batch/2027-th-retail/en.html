Section 01

Executive summary

Thailand's retail sector enters 2027 in a state of structural bifurcation. The market is expanding — total retail value is forecast to reach THB 7.1 trillion by 2030 from THB 5.4 trillion in 2025, a five-year CAGR of 5.7% Kira estimates — yet the distribution of that growth is radically uneven. Convenience stores are absorbing share from hypermarkets. E-commerce, energised by social commerce and quick delivery, is rewriting the consumer journey. And tourist receipts, once a structural tailwind, delivered a surprise correction in 2025 that forces operators to rethink the visitor-capture playbook.

Three structural forces are reshaping Thailand retail through 2027: (1) the continued dominance and density expansion of CP ALL's 7-Eleven franchise — now approaching 16,000 outlets and evolving into a daily-life services hub; (2) the accelerating shift of consumer spend online, with Thailand's e-commerce GMV surging to an estimated USD 35.5 bn in 2025 at more than 50% year-on-year growth Retail Association of Thailand 2025; and (3) the format compression of large-format hypermarkets, where Big C registered a 4.1% revenue decline in Q1 2026 partly attributable to over 100 small-format store closures Inside Retail Asia 2026.

The central analytical finding of this report: Thailand retail is not a single market in 2027 — it is three parallel contests running at different speeds. Convenience store operators compete on density and service bundling. E-commerce platforms compete on fulfilment speed and live-commerce engagement. Hypermarket operators compete, defensively, on omnichannel penetration and mixed-use property anchoring. Strategies calibrated for only one of these contests will underperform structurally.

Section 02

Market size and growth trajectory

Thailand's retail market is among Southeast Asia's most mature, underpinned by a 70-million consumer base, a broad middle class, and Bangkok's status as a regional luxury and tourist consumption node. Our analysts estimate the total addressable market reached approximately USD 148.7 bn in 2025 (THB 5.4 trillion at contemporaneous exchange rates) and will expand to USD 162–168 bn by 2027, implying a three-year CAGR in the 5–6% range Kira estimates. The 2030 ceiling from government and industry projections sits at THB 7.1 trillion TDRI 2026.

Private consumption accounts for roughly 52% of Thai GDP, giving retail a structural tailwind from wage growth and urbanisation. However, the headline growth rate masks profound format divergence: modern trade formats grew at 4–7% while traditional trade (fresh markets, mom-and-pop grocers) contracted approximately 2–3% annually over 2022–2025 as the convenience format eroded its last advantage — proximity. Thailand's middle class urbanisation rate continues its decade-long upward drift, adding an estimated 1.5 million urban consumers per year who strongly prefer modern-format retail NESDC 2025.

External headwinds are real. Consumer confidence remained below the 2019 pre-pandemic baseline through most of 2025, constrained by household debt running above 90% of GDP and a modest baht depreciation that raised import-cost pressures for electronics and FMCG categories. The Bank of Thailand's single policy rate cut in 2025 provided limited stimulus impulse. Our 2027 base case assumes gradual household deleveraging and a resumption of 5%+ wage growth in the Bangkok metro corridor, supporting mid-single-digit retail volume expansion.

Growth anchors to 2027

Three demand drivers underpin the 5–6% base-case CAGR: urbanisation adding modern-format consumers, a post-COVID normalisation of discretionary categories (apparel, beauty, electronics), and the progressive formalisation of rural retail through convenience store expansion into second and third-tier provinces.

The downside scenario (3.5% CAGR) materialises if household debt deleveraging suppresses discretionary spending and tourism fails to rebound above 34 million arrivals in 2026 Kira estimates. The upside scenario (7% CAGR) requires a sustained commodity tailwind and faster wage growth in manufacturing corridors.

Structural note: Thailand's retail formalisation rate — the share of total consumption captured by modern trade versus informal channels — crossed 55% in 2024 and is forecast to reach 62% by 2027 Kira estimates, implying that even flat total consumption generates modern-trade volume growth through channel migration.

Section 03

Format shift: the convenience store imperative

The most consequential structural dynamic in Thailand retail is the accelerating share transfer from hypermarkets and supermarkets to convenience stores. Convenience stores held 38.8% of total modern-trade retail value in 2025, the largest single format share and a full 6 percentage points above their 2018 level Krungsri Research 2025. Hypermarkets, conversely, have seen their combined share compress as consumer trip frequency fragments across smaller-basket, proximity-led purchase occasions.

The CP ALL thesis — that the convenience store can absorb functions previously split across a pharmacy, ATM, bill-payment counter, fresh-food stall, and parcel locker — has proven structurally correct. CP ALL's 7-Eleven network reached approximately 15,400–16,000 outlets by 2025, the second-largest 7-Eleven market globally after Japan CP ALL Annual Report 2025. The July 2024 commitment to open 700 additional stores with a THB 13 billion (USD 380 million) investment signals continued confidence in suburban and rural white-space penetration. At current trajectory, the network will comfortably exceed 17,000 outlets by end-2027 Kira estimates.

Why the convenience model is structurally superior in Thailand's context

Three reinforcing factors explain convenience store outperformance. First, Thailand's urban density profile — particularly in Bangkok and secondary cities like Chiang Mai, Khon Kaen, and Phuket — creates ideal foot-traffic economics for 150–300 sqm stores sited at transit nodes and residential ingress points. Second, CP ALL's service bundling (7-Delivery now accounting for approximately 11% of store sales CP ALL Investor Presentation 2025) converts a commodity trip into a multi-service touchpoint that builds basket frequency rather than just basket size. Third, food service — approximately 70% of Thai 7-Eleven sales — insulates the format from e-commerce substitution in ways that non-food hypermarket categories cannot.

The hypermarket operators face a structural squeeze that is more than cyclical. Big C's Q1 2026 retail sales declined 4.1% year-on-year to THB 24.4 bn, with same-store sales down 3.3% Inside Retail Asia 2026. The closure of over 100 small-format Big C stores signals a strategic contraction rather than a tactical pruning. Lotus's, with a stronger omnichannel posture (22% of sales through digital channels in Q1 2026, targeting 25% by year-end), appears better positioned to defend against convenience and e-commerce substitution simultaneously Lotus's Investor Disclosure 2026.

Section 04

E-commerce and omnichannel acceleration

Thailand's e-commerce market recorded one of the most dramatic single-year GMV expansions in Southeast Asia, with total online retail estimated at approximately THB 1.1 trillion (USD 35.5 bn) in 2025, up from THB 970 bn in 2024, representing more than 50% year-on-year growth driven by the maturation of social commerce and live-selling formats Retail Association of Thailand 2025. The market is on a trajectory to reach THB 1.6 trillion by 2027 Kira estimates, implying continued double-digit CAGRs even as the base expands.

The platform hierarchy has reconfigured materially since 2022. Shopee leads with an estimated >50% GMV share across Southeast Asia including Thailand. TikTok Shop has emerged as the structurally disruptive challenger, capturing younger urban consumers through short-video discovery and live-selling sessions that compress the path from awareness to purchase into minutes. Lazada, while still the third major platform, has ceded ground in the mass-market segment and is repositioning toward premium and cross-border categories. Meta Commerce (Instagram, Facebook Shops) retains relevance in the fashion and beauty verticals where social proof and community are high-conversion levers.

The omnichannel imperative: from aspiration to execution

Thailand's modern trade operators are moving beyond omnichannel rhetoric to measurable integration. Lotus's achieved 22% omnichannel sales penetration in Q1 2026 Lotus's Investor Disclosure 2026; CP ALL's 7-Delivery contributed ~11% of 7-Eleven store revenue through the 7App, offering order-on-app for over 15,000 SKUs with delivery from the nearest outlet CP ALL Investor Presentation 2025. Central Retail's omnichannel model — integrating The 1 loyalty programme across Robinson, Big C, and its digital channels — creates a single consumer identity across format and channel.

The quick-commerce segment is emerging as a distinct competitive arena, with LINE MAN Wongnai, Grab Mart, and 7-Eleven's own delivery network collectively targeting a market projected to reach USD 630 million by 2029, growing at a 10.3% CAGR from USD 385 million in 2024 GlobalNewswire 2026. This segment is notable because it converts convenience store inventory into on-demand delivery, reinforcing rather than cannibalising CP ALL's physical network advantage.

A critical watch variable: Thailand's Commerce Ministry has explicitly mandated omnichannel and data-led transformation as a sector priority through 2027, signalling potential regulatory pressure on pure-play e-commerce platforms regarding data sharing, logistics standards, and SME seller support MARKETECH APAC 2025. Operators that can demonstrate integrated customer data practices will face lower regulatory friction in this environment.

Social commerce: the most structurally disruptive channel

Thailand's social commerce market reached an estimated USD 5.2 bn in 2025, up 18.6% year-on-year, with a projected 10.2% CAGR through 2030 Retail Association of Thailand 2025. The mode shift is qualitative, not just quantitative: live-selling sessions on TikTok Live and Shopee Live create urgency and community that static product pages cannot replicate. Categories with high sensory engagement — food and beverage, cosmetics, fashion — are the primary beneficiaries, channelling share away from mid-tier physical mall operators whose anchor tenant economics are built on exactly these verticals. The structural read: social commerce is not a channel adding to total retail; it is a channel compressing the impulse-to-purchase window in ways that advantage digitally native brands over distributed physical operators.

Section 05

Competitive landscape and key players

Thailand retail is dominated by a small number of highly capitalised conglomerates. The top three corporate groups — CP Group (via CP ALL and Makro), Central Group, and TCC Group (Berli Jucker / Big C) — collectively control an estimated 60–65% of modern-trade retail value Kira estimates. This concentration is structurally stable: capital barriers (network density, loyalty ecosystems, supply-chain integration) deter new full-scale entrants, while e-commerce disruption primarily reallocates share within the value chain rather than reducing conglomerate revenues proportionally, as they are increasingly investing in digital channels themselves.

Section 06

Tourist spending: structural headwind, selective tailwind

Thailand welcomed 32.9 million international tourists in 2025, a 7.2% decline from 35.55 million in 2024 and the first annual contraction outside the COVID period TAT 2025. International tourism revenue correspondingly dropped to THB 1.53 trillion (USD 45 bn) from THB 1.67 trillion in 2024 Bank of Thailand 2025. For retail operators in Bangkok's Central Business District, Sukhumvit corridor, and major resort destinations (Phuket, Koh Samui, Pattaya), this represents a meaningful demand shortfall concentrated precisely in the highest-margin categories: luxury goods, cosmetics, electronics, and souvenir-adjacent FMCG.

The structural analysis is more nuanced than the headline decline suggests. Visitor mix shifted decisively toward long-haul, higher-spending travellers — Europeans, Americans, and Gulf markets — whose average trip duration extended to 14–21 days and whose daily retail spend per trip is estimated at THB 65,000–80,000 TAT Forward Planning 2026. The shortfall was concentrated in volume-based short-haul Chinese tourism, which remains below its pre-2020 levels as outbound travel patterns reconfigured. The implication for retail operators is asymmetric: airport duty-free and luxury mall concessionaires benefited from the high-value mix shift, while mass-market souvenir and pharmacy operators relying on Chinese bus-tour traffic underperformed.

2026–2027 forecast: TAT is targeting 36.7 million arrivals in 2026 — above the 2024 peak — generating THB 2.8 trillion in total tourism revenue. The revised target incorporates higher per-trip spending assumptions from long-haul markets and a partial Chinese tourism recovery. Retail operators calibrating inventory for 2026–2027 tourist seasons should model a bifurcated visitor profile rather than a single "recovery" scenario. TAT 2026

Duty-free and airport retail: the structural insulation

King Power, Thailand's duty-free monopoly, operates across all major international airports and benefits structurally from the long-haul mix shift. Long-haul travellers — who are the dominant growth segment — disproportionately use Suvarnabhumi Airport (Bangkok's main international hub) and purchase in King Power stores on both arrival and departure. Even at 32.9 million total arrivals, the average-spend uplift from a traveller mix weighted toward Europeans and Americans means King Power's revenue per passenger is likely to have increased in 2025 despite the volume decline Kira estimates. This format isolation from the headline tourist count is a structural advantage that pure-play general retailers do not share.

Section 07

Strategic implications for market participants

Position
Accelerate service bundling in convenience assets
Convenience stores holding 38.8% of modern-trade value have room to expand further by deepening non-retail revenue per outlet: financial services, healthcare, logistics pick-up, and prepared-food delivery. The 7-Delivery model (11% of store revenue) demonstrates the viable economics. Operators should benchmark service depth per sq metre, not just merchandise density. Kira estimates
Reposition
Hypermarket operators: omnichannel or asset monetisation
Large-format players face a binary strategic choice: achieve ≥25% omnichannel penetration (Lotus's target) within 24 months, or pursue property income optimisation by anchoring community malls around the hypermarket footprint. Big C's Q1 2026 results suggest the pure-volume model is no longer sustainable. The decision point is now, not at the next planning cycle.
Invest
Live-commerce and social commerce infrastructure
Thailand's USD 5.2 bn social commerce market is growing at 18.6% annually. Brands without live-commerce capability (dedicated studio, trained host talent, real-time inventory integration) will structurally cede impulse categories to digitally native competitors. Investment payback is rapid in beauty, FMCG, and fashion; builds a defensible discovery-to-purchase flywheel. Retail Assoc. Thailand 2025
Hedge
Segment tourism exposure by visitor origin
Retailers with Bangkok CBD and resort-destination exposure should re-weight inventory and staffing toward long-haul visitor preferences (premium brands, cosmetics, health supplements) rather than mass-market Chinese tourism SKUs. The 2025 volume decline at high per-spend is more profitable than the 2024 volume peak at lower spend-per-head for luxury-adjacent categories.
Anticipate
Regulatory pressure on data and logistics in e-commerce
Thailand's Commerce Ministry mandate on omnichannel and data-led transformation is a precursor to regulation. Cross-border e-commerce duty enforcement and platform data-sharing requirements are likely before 2027. Operators building first-party data assets and compliant logistics infrastructure today will face lower adjustment costs than those relying on third-party platform data at scale.
Build
Rural and second-tier province networks while white space remains
7-Eleven's 700-store expansion targets rural and suburban corridors where modern retail penetration is still below 40%. For competitors, the window for establishing second-mover positions in second and third-tier provinces (Korat, Udon Thani, Hat Yai) is narrowing. The convenience format's capex per outlet (THB 18–22 million including fit-out) is accessible even for mid-tier regional operators. Kira estimates

Methodological note

Market size figures in this report represent total retail value across all channels (modern trade, traditional trade, e-commerce), expressed in nominal USD at contemporaneous THB/USD exchange rates. CAGR calculations use 2024 as the base year for three-year forecasts to 2027. Omnichannel penetration rates reflect share of total company revenue attributed to digital channel initiation prior to or during purchase, per operator disclosure methodology — definitions are not fully standardised across operators and should be treated as directional. Format share data reflects modern-trade value share (traditional trade excluded) unless otherwise stated. Tourist spending data reflects official TAT and Bank of Thailand definitions: total international tourism receipts inclusive of accommodation, F&B, and retail. The report does not constitute investment advice. All forecasts carry material uncertainty; the range of plausible outcomes is wider than any single point estimate.
