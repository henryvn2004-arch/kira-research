How we built this view

Sixteen substantive research queries fired across English and Vietnamese sources, three independent secondary anchors per major data point, and KIRA-house triangulation wherever chain-level revenue and shopper-cohort disclosure falls short. Every quantitative claim carries an explicit source tag; estimate methodology is disclosed in the endnote.

Primary inputs

Field channel checks
Cafe walks across HCMC District 1, 3, 7, Thao Dien and Hanoi Hoan Kiem, Cau Giay; barista and store-manager conversations at five chain banners; loyalty-app and ShopeeFood/GrabFood basket reads on a four-week window.
Analyst synthesis
Triangulation across 16 substantive research queries (10 EN, 6 VN) plus pattern-matching against KIRA's prior SEA F&B chain studies. Tagged [Kira estimates].
Specialty & operator reads
Single-origin roaster conversations (Da Lat, Son La, Cau Dat highland regions), specialty cup-score readings, and operator commentary on the SCA ≥ 80 segment economics. Used directionally for premium-tier sizing.

Secondary anchors

Statistical authorities
GSO (Tổng cục Thống kê / National Statistics Office), MAE/MARD coffee desk (Bộ Nông nghiệp), VICOFA (Vietnam Coffee-Cocoa Association), Vietnam Customs (Tổng cục Hải quan), USDA FAS Hanoi GAIN. Anchored production, export volume, and consumption tonnage.
Listed-company filings
HOSE/UPCOM disclosures for Masan Consumer (parent of Phuc Long), Jollibee Foods (parent of Highlands via Jollibee Vietnam SuperFoods). World Coffee Portal earnings briefs for Highlands, Phuc Long, Starbucks Vietnam. Source of named-chain revenue and store-count claims.
Triangulated estimates
Sub-segment sizing where Vietnam-specific public segmentation is thin (Gen Z share of premium ticket, specialty as % of in-cup volume, chain HHI). Tagged [Kira estimates] with anchor metric disclosed inline. Methodology in endnote.

Market context

Vietnam's coffee-and-tea chain market sits at USD 1.3 bn in 2025 [VnExpress 2025], the third-largest in SEA after Indonesia and Thailand. Domestic in-cup volume reached 270,000–300,000 tonnes / ~4.8 m 60-kg bags in 2024/25 [USDA FAS 2025], per-capita up to 3.0 kg/yr [VietnamBriefing 2025] from 1.7 kg in 2015. Chain coffee's banner field is bifurcating: Highlands (855 stores), Phuc Long (237), Starbucks (127), Katinat (106), with the long tail of Milano-style traditional kiosks holding the mass-market floor.

Why 2026: three convergent forces

First, the Gen Z premiumization — under-30 shoppers pay 20–30% premium for traceable single-origin and trend-driven SKUs [Cooler City 2025]. Second, the chain land-grab — Phuc Long added 50% stores in one year (158→237) [Vietstats 2025], Starbucks +22% to 127 [VietnamVN 2025], Highlands building a VND 500 bn roasting plant [CafeF 2025]. Third, the delivery channel reset — ShopeeFood + GrabFood hold 90%+ of food-delivery GMV [VnExpress 2025] and cafe SKUs now run 18–25% of chain baskets [Kira estimates].

Five strategic implications for market participants

Five forces materially reshape competitive dynamics through 2030. Each is anchored to documented chain-level disclosures, listed-parent filings, and our analysts' store-walk and delivery-basket reads across HCMC and Hanoi.

IMP · 01
Win the Gen Z premium ticket, not the morning commute
Under-30 shoppers pay 20–30% above mainstream for traceable single-origin and trend-driven SKUs [Cooler City 2025]; the morning-commute ticket is mass-market price-anchored. Operators chasing footfall lose ticket; operators chasing the Gen Z occasion compound dwell and basket.
Anchor: 20-30% Gen Z premium tolerance
IMP · 02
Treat ShopeeFood + GrabFood as the second store, not a side channel
ShopeeFood + GrabFood control 90%+ of food delivery [VnExpress 2025]; cafe SKUs run 18–25% of chain baskets [Kira estimates]. Chains without dedicated delivery menus and dynamic pricing forfeit incremental volume that has zero rent and zero barista wage attached.
Anchor: 90% delivery GMV concentration
IMP · 03
Build the brand-led densification model, not just the store-count race
The Coffee House contracted from ~180 to ~90 stores [Vietstats 2025] while Highlands compounded; pure expansion without brand-tier differentiation destroys value. Phuc Long added 50% stores in one year [Vietstats 2025] only because the Winmart+ kiosk format opened a structurally lower-cost channel.
Anchor: +50% Phuc Long YoY 2025
IMP · 04
Lock the specialty supply chain before the 2030 cup-score race
MARD targets 19,000 ha to specialty by 2030 (11,000 t) [IDH/MARD 2025]; specialty CAGR runs 6.95% vs 87% mass-bean share today [Kira estimates]. Direct-source contracts in Cau Dat, Son La, and Lam Dong are the lever; spot-market specialty supply is structurally tight.
Anchor: 19,000 ha specialty 2030 target
IMP · 05
Stand up loyalty + POS analytics before EUDR adds traceability cost
EUDR enforcement (Dec 2025) requires geo-located due diligence on coffee origin [Koltiva 2025]; 41% of Vietnam coffee exports go to the EU [AInvest 2025]. Chains with integrated POS + traceability stacks compress compliance cost and unlock the EU-buyer narrative; aggregator-pooled chains absorb the friction.
Anchor: 41% EU share of VN coffee exports

Section 04 · Macro Context
007 / 022

Macro indicators driving cafe demand

Vietnam combines 8.02% GDP growth, 3.31% CPI, and per-capita coffee consumption that doubled in a decade. The macro layer dictates which chain banners scale into Tier-2 cities and which over-index on HCMC Districts 1 & 7 saturation.

GDP and consumption backdrop

Vietnam's GDP grew 8.02% in 2025 [GSO 2026], the second-strongest pace in 15 years, with Q4 at 8.46% — the fastest quarterly print since Q4 2007. Industry and construction +8.92%, services +8.18%, agriculture +3.58%. CPI sat at 3.31% vs the 4.5% target [GSO 2026]. Q1 2026 GDP eased to 7.83% but stayed comfortably ahead of the regional median, keeping discretionary cafe spend on the positive real-wage side of the ledger.

Per-capita coffee & urban density

Per-capita coffee consumption hit 3.0 kg/year in 2025 [VietnamBriefing 2025], up from 1.7 kg in 2015 — a 76% step that maps directly to chain footfall growth. Domestic consumption reached 270,000–300,000 tonnes [USDA FAS 2025], ~4.8 m 60-kg bags. Urbanization sits at ~40% and rising; HCMC + Hanoi + Da Nang carry the disproportionate share of chain footfall. The structural floor — Vietnam still consumes 3.0 kg vs South Korea's ~2.5 kg and Japan's ~3.5 kg — suggests the convergence runway is far from done.

Gen Z demographic concentration

Median age sits at ~32 years; Gen Z (born 1997–2012) and millennials together drive cafe footfall, dwell, and check-in volume. Cooler City's 2025 Gen Z beverage behaviour study finds the cohort treats cafes as workspace, hangout, and content-creation backdrop simultaneously [Cooler City 2025] — the structural demand pull behind Katinat's 35% YoY store growth.

Section 05 · Sector Overview & Sizing

Market size & growth: chain coffee Vietnam

Vietnam's coffee-and-tea chain channel reached USD 1.3 bn in 2025 [VnExpress 2025], the third-largest in SEA after Indonesia and Thailand. In-cup volume sits at 270–300 kt with 6–6.6% expected annual consumption growth through 2030 [VietnamBriefing 2025]. Specialty grows at 6.95% CAGR vs mass at low-single-digit [Kira estimates].

Top-line trajectory

Chain channel value expanded from ~USD 0.7 bn in 2023 to USD 1.3 bn in 2025 [VnExpress 2025; Kira estimates], a ~36% step in two years. Published industry CAGR reads 8.13% for 2024–2029 [MQFlavor 2025]; KIRA base case stretches to USD 1.85–2.0 bn by 2030 as Tier-2 city densification compounds the channel mix shift away from independent corner kiosks.

In-cup volume composition

Of the USD 1.3 bn chain value [VnExpress 2025], mainstream banners (Highlands, Starbucks, Phuc Long, Trung Nguyen Legend) carry ~65%; specialty independents and emerging premium chains (% Arabica, Cong, % traceable single-origin) hold ~19%; traditional + kiosk format (Milano, street-format) holds ~15% [Kira estimates]. Specialty's value share punches above its 13% volume share — the premium ticket lift is real.

Section 06 · Segment Economics
010 / 022

Segment economics — mainstream chains, specialty, traditional

Three economically distinct lanes: mainstream chains at USD ~850 m with brand-built footfall, specialty at USD ~250 m with premium-ticket lift, and traditional at USD ~200 m defending the price floor. Ticket sizes, margin profiles, and capital intensity differ sharply.

Mainstream chains — the volume engine

Mainstream chains run typical ticket VND 45,000–75,000 (~USD 1.80–3.00) per cup [Tonkin 2025]. Highlands reported VND 1,097 bn (USD ~43 m) net profit in FY25 [bbw.vn 2025], with revenue near VND 4,000 bn at the top banner. Gross margin runs 65–72% pre-rent and labour; store-level EBITDA 18–25% in mature units, compressing to single digits in early-year Tier-2 expansions [Kira estimates].

Specialty — the premium-ticket lane

Specialty cafe tickets run VND 50,000–120,000 per cup [Tonkin 2025], with the SCA ≥ 80 cup-score floor commanding 60,000–90,000 [Hello5 2025]. SCA-grade arabica beans cost from VND 250,000/kg upward at wholesale [Hello5 2025]; mainstream blends run 60–100,000/kg. The specialty segment delivers ~6.95% CAGR through 2031 [Kira estimates] vs mainstream's high-single-digit; gross margin per cup runs higher but volume velocity is structurally lower.

Traditional & kiosk — the price floor

Milano-style kiosk and street formats run tickets at VND 20,000–40,000 (~USD 0.80–1.60); Milano alone is estimated at ~2,000 stores nationally [VietnamVN 2025]. The format defends the daily-staple morning-commute occasion but loses incremental share to mainstream-chain take-out and delivery; revenue per store sits below chain-banner anchor by 60–75% [Kira estimates].

Strategic position

Highlands is the volume anchor of Vietnam chain coffee, with 855 stores at end-2025 and a credible path to 1,000 by year-end [Vietstats 2025]. FY25 net profit reached VND 1,097 bn [bbw.vn 2025] on revenue near VND 4,000 bn, with revenue growth ~16% YoY. The banner sits squarely in the mainstream price band (VND 45,000–75,000 ticket) and converts urban morning + lunch occasions across HCMC, Hanoi, and Tier-2 cities.

Capital allocation includes a VND 500 bn (~USD 20 m) roasting plant [CafeF 2025] that vertically integrates bean supply and unlocks margin upside as scale compounds. The Jollibee Foods Vietnam parentage provides operational rigour and treasury support.

Key risks

  • Saturation in HCMC/Hanoi cores: incremental new-store ROI compresses as Tier-1 city slots fill; Tier-2 economics carry execution risk.
  • Gen Z brand fatigue: mass-market banner positioning weakens against trend-driven Katinat-style brands chasing the under-30 cohort.
  • Delivery margin compression: ShopeeFood/GrabFood take-rate offsets store-rent savings only at high basket velocity.

Recent moves

  • FY25 profit print of VND 1,097 bn [bbw.vn 2025] — highest quarterly profit in two years per World Coffee Portal coverage.
  • VND 500 bn roasting plant under construction [CafeF 2025] — vertical integration play.
  • Store count from 770 (end-2024) to 855 (end-2025) [Vietstats 2025], path to 1,000 by YE25.
  • Loyalty app refresh underway; menu refresh layering matcha and fruit-tea SKUs to defend Gen Z share.

Strategic implication

For competitors, Highlands' scale and unit-economics maturity are the benchmark to beat. The implication: compete on brand affinity and occasion specialisation, not on raw store count. For buyers and partners, Highlands remains the cleanest distribution surface for cafe-channel SKU placement at national scale.

Strategic position

Phuc Long is the highest-velocity expander among major chains, scaling from 158 to 237 stores in 2025 [Vietstats 2025] — a 50% step funded by the Masan Consumer parent. FY24 revenue reached VND 1.62 tn (USD ~64.6 m) [WCP 2025], net profit VND 97 bn at a 7.6% net margin (up from 2% in 2019). FY25 guidance reads VND 1.9–2.2 tn, +18–36% YoY [Masan FY25].

The strategic edge is the Winmart+ kiosk format, which puts Phuc Long inside Masan's grocery footprint at a structurally lower rent-and-staff cost than standalone cafes. Ex-Starbucks Patricia Marques (CEO from Nov 2024) drives the brand-tier discipline.

Key risks

  • Kiosk-format margin dependency: the Winmart+ unlock is real but lower per-ticket; mix-shift can compress blended revenue per store.
  • Masan parent capital cycle: Phuc Long sits within a diversified consumer conglomerate; capital allocation choices can shift expansion pace.
  • Tea-vs-coffee positioning: the dual co-banner can blur the brand promise if menu rotation isn't disciplined.

Recent moves

  • Patricia Marques appointed CEO Nov 2024 — ex-Starbucks Vietnam general manager [WCP 2025].
  • Store count from 158 to 237 in one year [Vietstats 2025] — the steepest expansion curve in the chain field.
  • 33 net new stores in 2024 [WCP 2025] driving the FY24 revenue +5.6% step.
  • Net-profit margin lift from 2% (2019) to 7.6% (2024) [WCP 2025] — brand-tier discipline paying off.

Strategic implication

Phuc Long's Winmart+ format is the structural moat — rival chains can match brand-banner but not the in-grocery channel. The 2026–2027 path: defend the FY25 +18–36% guidance through Tier-2 city Winmart+ co-expansion, lift premium-tier SKU share to capture Gen Z ticket lift, and modernise standalone stores to close the brand-affinity gap with Katinat.

Strategic position

Katinat is the Gen Z brand-affinity leader in Vietnam chain coffee, scaling from a 10-store HCMC operation at end-2021 to 106 stores by Sep 2025 [CafeF 2025]. FY23 revenue reached VND 470 bn [Vietdata 2024] at ~1.35% chain share. The store expansion of 35–50% in 2025 [Vietstats 2025] outpaces every major competitor on a percentage basis.

The strategic edge is NPD cadence and viral marketing — limited-edition cups, matcha/fruit-tea/seasonal SKUs, and Instagram-grade store design draw the under-30 cohort that pays the premium ticket. April 2024 brand reposition to "Coffee & Tea House" formalised the dual-category strategy.

Key risks

  • Trend cycle exposure: Gen Z brand affinity is high-velocity; a missed seasonal drop or a viral mis-step compresses traffic.
  • Capital constraint vs Highlands/Phuc Long: independent ownership means rapid expansion is fundable only via self-finance or dilutive capital.
  • Tier-2 city extension risk: the brand's HCMC-centric design language doesn't auto-translate to Hanoi or Da Nang at the same affinity premium.

Recent moves

  • April 2024 reposition to Katinat Coffee & Tea House; dual-category strategy on coffee + tea anchor SKUs.
  • Store count from 69 to 93 (end-2024) to 106 (Sep 2025) [Vietstats 2025; CafeF 2025] — the steepest YoY ramp in chain coffee.
  • Limited-edition cup drops and seasonal-concept beverages anchored on Gen Z social-media virality [BrandsVietnam 2025].
  • Premium-tier ticket positioning at the top end of the mainstream band, edging into specialty pricing.

Strategic implication

For competitors, Katinat is the signal that brand-affinity discipline beats raw expansion. The implication: mainstream chains need an NPD cadence answer (Highlands' menu refresh, Phuc Long's seasonal drops) or cede the premium-ticket Gen Z occasion. For Katinat itself, the next two years are about converting trend leadership into durable unit economics and Tier-2 city extension without losing the brand premium.

Strategic position

Trung Nguyen Legend anchors the cultural + heritage segment of Vietnam chain coffee and pursues the most ambitious international expansion of any domestic player. The Trung Nguyen group reported revenue of ~USD 114 m in 2024 [InvestVietnam 2025] across branded processed coffee and the Legend cafe network. Key risks

  • International unit economics: US and China store-level returns lag the domestic anchor; cultural-export brands are notoriously slow to mature outside source market.
  • Domestic Gen Z gap: heritage positioning is harder to flex against trend-driven SKUs; under-30 affinity sits below Katinat and Phuc Long.
  • Coffee bean cost pass-through: 2025 robusta record prices compress branded-processed margin if pass-through is partial.

Recent moves

  • March 2025 launch of Legend Gold Freeze-Dried Coffee [Accio 2025] — premium variant targeting domestic specialty + export.
  • March 2026 opening of the largest Trung Nguyen Legend World Coffee space in Houston, Texas [Global Coffee Report 2026].
  • China store push focused on Shanghai, Beijing, Chengdu, Suzhou, Zhejiang [Global Coffee Report 2025].
  • SE Asia's largest coffee plant under construction [CafeF 2025] — vertical-integration moat for branded processed.

Strategic implication

Trung Nguyen Legend's international footprint is the option value — domestic chains optimize for HCMC and Hanoi while Trung Nguyen builds the Vietnamese-coffee-as-global-export brand. For domestic competitors, the cultural-heritage moat is structural; mainstream banners cannot replicate it. For buyers, Trung Nguyen is the cleanest single-source partner for Vietnamese specialty bean supply at branded-export scale.

Channel & distribution structure

ShopeeFood and GrabFood together control ~90% of Vietnam food-delivery GMV [VnExpress 2025]; coffee is a meaningful share of delivery basket mix. Loyalty apps and in-grocery kiosk formats round out the channel architecture.

Section 09 · Regulatory Landscape
019 / 022

Regulatory & policy landscape

EUDR enforcement (Dec 2025) is the dominant near-term regulatory event; 41% of Vietnam coffee exports go to the EU [AInvest 2025]. Food safety, sugar-tax discussion, and the MARD specialty conversion target shape the structural backdrop for chain banners.

EUDR & geo-traced supply

The EU Deforestation Regulation (EUDR) enforces geo-located plot-level due diligence on coffee origin from December 2025 [Koltiva 2025]. Vietnam's MARD launched a national EUDR database covering 136,000 ha of coffee growing area [IDH/MARD 2025], partnering with IDH. Pioneer companies (Simexco Daklak, EDE) have built traceability stacks; chain banners that source through compliant supply chains gain EU-buyer narrative leverage and protect future-export optionality.

MARD specialty conversion target

MARD's specialty coffee plan targets converting 19,000 ha to specialty production by 2030 with 11,000 t output [AInvest 2025; MARD 2024]. The plan anchors the supply-side support for chain banners pursuing specialty SKU expansion; direct-contract sourcing from Cau Dat (Lam Dong), Son La, and Da Lat highland clusters becomes the lever.

Food safety & sugar-tax direction

Vietnam's draft sugary-beverage excise sits under National Assembly review; if enacted, the tax would touch sweetened beverages including iced milk coffee and many tea SKUs — a material menu-engineering signal for cafe chains, with menu reformulation and pack-size architecture as the levers. Standard food-safety oversight by the Ministry of Health applies to all chain SKUs.

Section 10 · AI Impact
021 / 022

AI in Vietnam chain coffee — six use cases that move 2026-2027 economics

Cloud-POS plus loyalty-app data is the foundational stack; AI sits on top as demand sensing, personalised promotion, dynamic delivery pricing, CV menu compliance, and EUDR traceability. Most use cases sit in early-pilot stage at the major banners today; commercial scale is the 2026–2027 inflection.

USE CASE · 01
Loyalty-app personalised offers
Loyalty data + transaction history feed AI models that tailor next-visit offers by shopper occasion + ticket history [DigitalDefynd 2026]. Highlands and Starbucks apps already capture the data; the AI layer turns it into incremental visit frequency and basket lift.
Stage: Early deployment
USE CASE · 02
SKU-store-week demand sensing
Combines POS time-series + weather + local-event data to project SKU-store-week demand 1–3 weeks ahead. Reduces stock-outs on viral seasonal drops (Katinat pattern); compresses waste on slow-moving SKUs. Highest near-term ROI for chains with >100 stores.
Anchor: 30-50% waste reduction
USE CASE · 03
Dynamic delivery menu pricing
ShopeeFood + GrabFood menus support time-of-day and SKU-level dynamic pricing. AI optimises promo intensity by basket size, daypart, and rider-load [BAZU 2025]; chains capture 5–12% incremental delivery contribution margin [Kira estimates] vs flat-list pricing.
Anchor: 5-12% margin lift
USE CASE · 04
CV menu & front-of-shelf compliance
Camera + AI classifies menu-board freshness, planogram compliance, and beverage presentation against banner spec. Critical for 500+ store chains where field-audit cycle time is the bottleneck; first deployments likely at Highlands and Phuc Long.
Stage: Pilot
USE CASE · 05
NPD concept testing via synthetic panels
AI-generated synthetic shopper panels pre-screen seasonal SKU drops and limited-edition cups before live test [Roastertools 2025]. Compresses NPD-cycle time; lowers cost of a missed concept. Particularly relevant for Katinat-style high-cadence NPD operators.
Anchor: 6-12 month NPD cycle
USE CASE · 06
EUDR traceability & geo-tagging
Geo-located plot records + blockchain anchors satisfy EUDR plot-level due diligence [Koltiva 2025]. Chain banners with integrated traceability + roasting plants (Highlands, Trung Nguyen) clear EU-buyer narratives faster than aggregator-pooled rivals; AI auto-classifies and validates plot-level documentation.
Deadline: Dec 2025 EUDR enforcement

Section 11 · Outlook & Forecast
022 / 022

5-year outlook: USD 1.9–2.3 bn chain coffee by 2030

KIRA's base case lands Vietnam chain coffee at USD 2.0 bn in 2030 [Kira estimates], between an industry-CAGR bear case at USD 1.85 bn and a premiumization-led bull case at USD 2.35 bn. The 2026–2030 path is mix-shift, not pure footfall.

Base case · USD 2.0 bn 2030

~9% blended CAGR 2025–2030, consistent with published industry CAGR of 8.13% [MQFlavor 2025] adjusted for chain channel mix-shift. Mainstream banners hold 60–63%, specialty climbs to 22–24%, traditional drifts toward 13–15%. Highlands reaches 1,100–1,200 stores; Phuc Long extends past 350.

Bull case · USD 2.35 bn 2030

~13% CAGR sustains if Gen Z premiumization accelerates (specialty share > 27%), Tier-2 city densification compounds without margin compression, ShopeeFood/GrabFood take-rate normalizes, and Katinat-style Gen Z brands compound past 250 stores. Robusta price softening lifts blended bean-cost discipline.

Bear case · USD 1.85 bn 2030

~7% CAGR if sugar tax enacts with material chain impact, robusta prices stay at 2025 highs (USD 5,610/t [VietnamPlus 2025]), Tier-2 city expansion delivers sub-mature unit economics, and Gen Z trend cycle compresses NPD payback. The Coffee House contraction pattern (180→90 stores) is the cautionary tale.
