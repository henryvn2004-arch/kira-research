Vietnam's aquaculture market — 2027 inflection

A USD 11.3 bn seafood franchise is rotating its export book toward China, absorbing a US tariff overhang on shrimp, and grinding through a structural feed-cost reset. The 2027–2031 window separates integrated processors from share donors.

Market context

Vietnam shipped USD 11.3 bn of seafood in 2025 [VASEP 2026], a record high, with shrimp at USD 4.6 bn (+19%) and pangasius at USD 2.2 bn (+8%) [VASEP 2026]. Aquaculture output reached 6.12 million tonnes [GSO 2026], up 5.1% on 2024, with shrimp at 1.38 mt (+6.1%) and farmed fish at 4.04 mt (+4.9%). The expansion arrives despite a Mekong Delta salinity event that ran sharper than the multi-year norm through Feb–Apr 2025 [MARD Hydromet 2025].

Why 2027: three convergent forces

First, the China rotation — shrimp shipments to China + Hong Kong reached USD 1.3 bn (+55%, 28.3% share) [VASEP 2026]; pangasius to China hit USD 560 m (+2%) [VietfishMag 2025]. Second, the US tariff overhang — DOC POR19 raised the cash-deposit rate to 25.46% for mandatory respondents [DOC POR19 2026], even as POR20 preliminary rates landed at 6.76–10.76% [DOC POR20 2025]. Third, the feed-cost reset — soybean imports climb to 2.8 mt in 2025/26 [USDA FAS 2025], with feed at 50–60% of pond cost [Hoan Duong 2025].

Five strategic implications for market participants

Five forces materially reshape competitive dynamics through 2031. Each is anchored to documented export data, listed-processor disclosures, and our analysts' pond-and-mill channel checks across the Mekong Delta.

IMP · 01
Treat China as the structural offset to the US tariff overhang
China + Hong Kong absorbed USD 1.3 bn of Vietnam shrimp in 2025 [VASEP 2026], up 55%, and pangasius to China held USD 560 m at +2% [VietfishMag 2025]. With DOC POR19 cash-deposit at 25.46% [DOC POR19 2026], processors over-indexed to the US lose unit margin; rebalancing toward China and Japan recovers it.
Anchor: +55% China/HK shrimp value growth 2025
IMP · 02
Hedge feed cost as the durable margin lever, not a quarterly tactic
Soybean meal is 35–45% of shrimp feed [Kira estimates] and feed is 50–60% of pond cost [Hoan Duong 2025]. Vietnam soybean imports rise to 2.8 mt in 2025/26 (+300 kt) [USDA FAS 2025]; locking forward-supply Q1 windows compresses worst-case unit cost by an estimated USD 0.20–0.35/kg green-weight [Kira estimates].
Anchor: 50–60% feed share of pond cost
IMP · 03
Push value-added processed share above the 50% threshold
EU buyers absorbed ~USD 434 m Vietnam shrimp in 9M 2025 [VASEP 2026], +21% YoY, with value-added processed at over 50% of EU shipments [VietfishMag 2025]. EVFTA duty-free access vs Ecuador's 12–20% MFN [HungHau 2025] is the lever; processors below 40% VA share leave EU margin on the table.
Anchor: +21% shrimp exports to EU 9M 2025
IMP · 04
Make Vinh Hoan-style US AD exemption a strategic asset, not a side note
Vietnam-US bilateral pangasius deal (Jan 2025) carved Vinh Hoan out of US AD on filets [VnExpress 2025]; six other processors gained exemption [VnExpress 2025]. With US absorbing 15% of pangasius and Brazil surging +45% to USD 128 m [VietfishMag 2025], exempted processors hold an irreplaceable channel.
Anchor: 0% Vinh Hoan US AD rate 2025
IMP · 05
Stand up AIoT pond monitoring before next disease cycle
AIoT systems cut shrimp early-mortality losses materially in pilot ponds [VietfishMag 2025]. With Mekong salinity intrusion sharper than normal in Feb–Apr 2025 [MARD Hydromet 2025], processors integrating real-time DO/pH/salinity telemetry with AI feed dosing compress disease loss windows from days to hours.
Anchor: 95% ops automatable [ShrimpTech 2025]

Section 04 · Macro Context
007 / 022

Macro indicators driving aquaculture demand & cost

Vietnam combines 8% GDP growth, a 2.8 Mt soybean import bill, and a Mekong salinity event sharper than the multi-year norm. The macro layer dictates which processors compound margin and which trade volume for unit dollars.

GDP and consumption backdrop

Vietnam's GDP grew 8.02% in 2025 [GSO 2026], the second-strongest pace in 15 years, with Q4 at 8.46%. Industry and construction +9.73%, services +8.82%; CPI sat at 3.31% versus a 4.5% target. The favourable macro keeps domestic seafood demand firm and amplifies discretionary purchasing in tier-1 China and Japan — Vietnam's two biggest growth markets in 2025.

Feed cost & soybean import dependence

Vietnam will import 2.8 mt of soybeans in 2025/26 [USDA FAS 2025], 300 kt more than the prior year; soybean meal demand for feed grows 400 kt to 7.6 mt [USDA FAS 2025]. Soybean meal is 35–45% of shrimp feed formulation [Kira estimates]; feed is 50–60% of pond cost [Hoan Duong 2025]. A 10% benchmark soy move translates to a 2–3% farm-gate cost swing — the dominant 2027 margin lever.

Mekong salinity step-change

The Feb–Apr 2025 dry-season salinity intrusion was sharper than the multi-year mean though softer than 2015/16 and 2019/20 [MARD Hydromet 2025]; the peak Feb 24–Mar 4 window pushed brackish lines deeper into Long An, Tien Giang, Ben Tre, Tra Vinh, Soc Trang, Bac Lieu, Ca Mau. The Mekong Delta carries 95% of pangasius and 70–80% of shrimp output [APN 2024]; even a softer-than-peak salinity year is now structural to the planning calendar.

Section 05 · Sector Overview & Sizing
009 / 022

Market size & growth: aquaculture Vietnam

Aquaculture output reached 6.12 million tonnes in 2025 [GSO 2026] — shrimp 1.38 mt (+6.1%), farmed fish 4.04 mt (+4.9%), other 0.70 mt (+4.4%). Export value at USD 11.3 bn [VASEP 2026] is a record; the MARD 2030 target stretches to 7.0 mt and ~USD 12 bn.

Top-line trajectory

Aquaculture output expanded from ~4.74 mt in 2020 to 6.12 mt in 2025 [GSO 2026] — a 29% step over five years, or roughly 5.2% CAGR. The MARD strategy targets 7.0 mt aquaculture and 9.8 mt total seafood by 2030 [MARD Strategy 2021]; the 2026 guidance is 6.25 mt farmed and 10 mt total [GSO 2026]. KIRA's read: 6.55–6.70 mt farmed by 2027 is achievable absent a heavy salinity year.

Export value composition

Of the USD 11.3 bn 2025 export book [VASEP 2026], shrimp at USD 4.6 bn carries 41% and pangasius at USD 2.2 bn carries 19%; the residual ~USD 4.5 bn comes from tuna (USD ~1.0 bn), squid/octopus, molluscs, marine cage species, and processed mixes [Kira estimates based on VASEP 2026]. The 2026 industry-VASEP target is USD 11.5 bn [VietnamPlus 2026]; KIRA reads USD 12.0–12.4 bn by 2027 as the base case.

Section 06 · Segment Economics
010 / 022

Segment economics — shrimp, pangasius, marine cage

Three economically distinct lanes: white-leg shrimp at USD 3.0 bn with 50%+ value-added, pangasius at USD 2.2 bn rotating from US to CPTPP, and marine cage on a MARD-targeted USD 1.8–2.0 bn build-out by 2030. Margin pools and capital intensity differ sharply.

White-leg shrimp — the volume engine

White-leg shrimp shipped USD 2.98 bn in 2025, 64.6% of shrimp value, +9.6% YoY [VASEP 2026]; black tiger held USD 452.9 m (+1.2%). Value-added processed product exceeds 50% of EU-bound shipments [VietfishMag 2025] — that's the unit-margin lever vs Ecuador's commodity raw shipments [HungHau 2025]. Pond gross margin runs 15–22% in normal feed-cost years, compressing to single digits when soy benchmark spikes [Kira estimates].

Pangasius — the channel rotator

Pangasius reached ~USD 2.2 bn 2025, +8% [VietfishMag 2025] with China at USD 560 m (27%, +2%), US at 15% (−3.3%), CPTPP at USD 271.4 m (+35%, 17%), Brazil at USD 128 m (+45%, 8%) [VietfishMag 2025]. The 2026 industry view targets USD 2.3 bn [VietnamPlus 2026]. Processed-filet conversion at Vinh Hoan and Nam Viet runs higher than industry average; that's the margin gate.

Marine cage — the policy-pulled long bet

MARD's Decision 1664/QD-TTg targets 280,000 ha and 850,000 tonnes marine cage by 2025, growing to 300,000 ha / 1.45 mt / USD 1.8–2.0 bn by 2030 [MARD Marine 2021]. Today's output is below the 2025 line; the gap is real-but-narrowable with cage-tech investment. Sea bass, cobia, lobster, oyster and grouper carry the segment; Quang Ninh, Khanh Hoa, Ca Mau, Kien Giang are the priority provincial clusters.

Strategic position

Vinh Hoan is the sole Vietnamese pangasius processor exempted from the US anti-dumping order [VnExpress 2025], a moat that delivered 9M24 revenue of VND 9.33 tn (+22%) [VnBusiness 2024] even as the broader peer set rotated away from US shipments. Q3 2024 stand-alone revenue hit VND 3.28 tn (+21.5%) with profit at VND 320 bn (+68%) [BaoDauTu 2024].

2025 brokered guidance reads VND 13.7 tn revenue and VND 1.58 tn net income [VCBS 2025] — a +29% profit step from 2024. The platform also includes collagen and gelatin downstream, lifting per-tonne realisation above commodity filet baselines.

Key risks

  • US AD exemption review: annual administrative reviews can revisit eligibility; legal & lobbying continuity is the recurring cost.
  • China filet pricing: at 27% of pangasius exports, China buyer concentration drives top-line beta.
  • Mekong salinity: pond catchment in An Giang and Dong Thap exposes upstream supply to the 2025-onwards salinity intrusion regime.

Recent moves

  • Vietnam-US bilateral signed 17 Jan 2025, formalising AD exemption on filet exports [VnExpress 2025].
  • Six additional pangasius processors gained narrow US exemption alongside Vinh Hoan [VnExpress 2025], modestly diluting the exclusivity premium but keeping VHC as benchmark.
  • Collagen & gelatin capacity expansion — downstream value-add layer that lifts blended realised price.
  • Sustainability positioning — ASC certification cited as the EU-buyer prerequisite for Q4 2025 contracts [VietfishMag 2025].

Strategic implication

For competitors, Vinh Hoan's US-exempt status is structurally non-replicable in the short term. The implication: compete on channel diversification (CPTPP, Brazil) and on value-added mix, not on US filet share. For buyers, VHC remains the cleanest single-source US-market entry point until DOC's next administrative review concludes.

Strategic position

Minh Phu is the volume anchor of Vietnam shrimp, integrated from Ca Mau pond clusters through processing and into the US and Japan markets [Investing 2025]. The company posted a record FY24 loss of VND 235 bn [Tuoitre 2025] despite revenue growth, reflecting feed-cost compression and the US AD cash-deposit drag.

Q4 2024 alone delivered a VND 101 bn loss [BaoDauTu 2025]. The DOC POR19 final result raised mandatory respondent cash deposits to 25.46% [DOC POR19 2026]; the POR20 preliminary rate landed at 6.76–10.76% [DOC POR20 2025]. The two-year stretch defines the company's 2027 margin recovery path.

Key risks

  • US AD overhang: mandatory respondent cash deposit posture pressures US-bound margin until POR20/21 finalises.
  • Feed cost: soybean meal at 35–45% of feed [Kira estimates] leaves single-digit pond margins in adverse soy-benchmark years.
  • Disease cycle: EHP and AHPND outbreak risk in Mekong Delta clusters compounds in salinity-stress years.

Recent moves

  • Surpassed Sao Ta in Japan-bound shrimp volume in early 2025 [VASEP 2025], capturing Japan's incremental yen-strength demand.
  • Highest profit in 7 quarters reported in late 2025 [TapChiCongThuong 2025] — sharp swing from the FY24 loss base, driven by recovery in domestic financial income and US-channel volume.
  • Plans to push value-added processed share further to offset commodity-shrimp price beta — the strategic narrative for FY26.

Strategic implication

Minh Phu's scale is unmatched — but the US AD posture and feed-cost exposure mean unit margin is structurally below Sao Ta and Vinh Hoan. The 2026–2027 path requires: rotation toward Japan and China, value-add share lift, and a defensive POR21 strategy. For sourcing buyers, MPC remains the volume floor for large-tonnage shrimp contracts.

Strategic position

Sao Ta is the value-added margin specialist of the Vietnamese shrimp set, with H1 2024 gross profit at VND 87.4 bn (+31% YoY) on VND 842 bn revenue (+10%) [ThitruongTaichinh 2024]. The +31% gross-profit growth on +10% revenue indicates structural unit-margin expansion — the inverse of Minh Phu's FY24 trajectory.

The PAN Group parent provides agri-portfolio capital and cross-business synergy (the group also owns Vinaseed, NSC, BBC). Strategic axis: Japan and EU value-added shrimp products, where pre-cooked, breaded, and processed packs command 25–40% per-tonne premium over raw frozen [Kira estimates].

Key risks

  • Smaller scale: ~5x below Minh Phu in volume; cost-per-tonne floor is structurally higher on the raw-frozen line.
  • Japanese-buyer concentration: Japan rotation amplifies FX and channel-specific demand beta.
  • EHP / AHPND outbreak exposure: Soc Trang and Bac Lieu cluster exposure to disease cycles.

Recent moves

  • Continued per-tonne margin expansion through value-added pack mix — H1 2024 gross margin rose to 10.4% from 8.7% [ThitruongTaichinh 2024].
  • Lost Japan-volume crown to Minh Phu in early 2025 [VASEP 2025] — competitive pressure on the highest-margin export lane.
  • Continued PAN Group capital support for plant modernisation and ASC certification expansion.

Strategic implication

Sao Ta's structurally higher unit margin makes it the cleanest peer-set bellwether for value-added strategy execution. For buyers, FMC remains the premium-pack supplier of choice for Japan and EU specialty channels. The Japan-volume loss to MPC underscores that value-added share alone is not a permanent moat — scale-plus-value-added (the Vinh Hoan formula) is the durable model.

Strategic position

Vietnam's aquafeed tier is moderately consolidated and foreign-anchored [Kira estimates]. Top five — Skretting (Nutreco), Cargill, De Heus, Charoen Pokphand, ADM — account for the majority of branded-feed revenue [Kira estimates]. Market sized at USD 2.92 bn 2026, growing 8.3% CAGR to USD 4.36 bn 2031 [Kira estimates].

De Heus opened a new shrimp-feed mill in Co Chien Industrial Zone (Vinh Long) in March 2025 [De Heus PR 2025], signalling foreign-capital commitment to Vietnam's pond density. De Heus, Skretting, and CP cluster mills near Mekong Delta hubs for just-in-time delivery economics. Cargill's premix plant supplies functional additives for antibiotic-free shrimp programmes.

Key risks

  • Soy benchmark beta: 35-45% soybean meal exposure makes branded feed P&L correlated to global oilseed markets.
  • Domestic feed substitution policy: any future Vietnamese mandate on local feed-input share would compress branded-feed margin.
  • Pond consolidation: if pond owners scale and integrate feed milling, foreign-branded share could compress.

Recent moves

  • De Heus Co Chien (Vinh Long) shrimp-feed mill commissioning March 2025 — flagship Mekong Delta capacity addition [De Heus PR 2025].
  • Skretting Vietnam continued R&D-led premium-feed positioning, particularly antibiotic-free and high-feed-conversion lines [Kira estimates].
  • Cargill premix plant supplies functional additives tailored for ASC-aligned production [Kira estimates].
  • Charoen Pokphand (CP) deepens vertical integration with farmer-financing programmes — locking demand at the pond.

Strategic implication

For Vietnamese processors, the feed-tier consolidation means negotiating power on input cost is asymmetric; only the largest integrated processors (Minh Phu, Vinh Hoan) can credibly bargain. For new aquafeed entrants, the market is large and growing but capacity gap is closing — differentiation must come from specialty additive science, not commodity feed.

Section 08 · Demand & Channels

Channel & destination structure

Shrimp's 2025 destination split: China/HK 28%, US 17%, Japan 12%, EU ~10%; pangasius's: China 27%, US 15%, CPTPP 17%, Brazil 8% [VASEP 2026, VietfishMag 2025]. The channel-mix rotation toward China and CPTPP is the dominant trade-pattern signal for 2027.

China & Hong Kong — the structural pull

Shrimp to China/HK USD 1.3 bn (+55% YoY), 28.3% share [VASEP 2026]; pangasius to China USD 560 m (+2%, 27% share) [VietfishMag 2025]. The proximity, customs simplicity, and the rapid Chinese seafood-consumption recovery underwrite this lane. China's relative weight in Vietnam's 2025 book lifted to a multi-year high — the structural pull is durable, not cyclical.

US — the AD/CVD-compressed lane

US shrimp at USD 796 m (+5.4%, 17.2%) [VASEP 2026]; pangasius at 15% (−3.3%). The DOC POR19 final cash-deposit at 25.46% [DOC POR19 2026] and CVD investigation overhang have compressed Vietnamese processor competitiveness vs Ecuador and India. Vinh Hoan's pangasius AD exemption is the cleanest US play; most processors will accept lower US volume share through 2027.

EU & CPTPP & Brazil — the diversifier lanes

EU shrimp at USD 434 m in 9M 2025 (+21%), with September alone +48% [VASEP 2026]; Vietnam ranks third behind Ecuador (160 kt) and India (37 kt) at 35 kt for 8M 2025 [VietfishMag 2025]. EVFTA's duty-free access and Vietnam's 50%+ value-added share [VietfishMag 2025] are the moats. CPTPP pangasius +35% to USD 271 m and Brazil +45% to USD 128 m [VietfishMag 2025] open clean lanes outside the US AD overhang.

Section 09 · Regulatory Landscape
019 / 022

Regulatory & policy landscape — AD/CVD, IUU yellow card, EUDR

Three regulatory regimes anchor the 2027 risk surface: US DOC AD/CVD reviews on shrimp, the EU's ongoing IUU yellow card on Vietnam, and EUDR/CSDDD due-diligence requirements on traceable raw material. Each is a license-to-trade test, not a one-off.

US AD/CVD — the ongoing rate cycle

DOC POR19 final (Feb 2026) lowered the Vietnam-wide AD rate from 35.29% to 4.58% [DOC POR19 2026], but raised mandatory respondent (STAPIMEX, Thong Thuan) cash deposit to 25.46% after export-subsidy offset [DOC POR19 2026]. POR20 preliminary (June 2025) landed at 6.76% and 10.76% for the two mandatory respondents [DOC POR20 2025]. Final POR20 expected late 2026. CVD investigation parallel-track.

EU IUU yellow card — the persistent overhang

Vietnam has carried the EU's IUU "yellow card" warning since 2017; lifting it requires deeper port-state-control, vessel-monitoring (VMS), and catch-documentation compliance [Virtus 2025]. The yellow card raises pre-clearance friction on wild-catch product and indirectly slows aquaculture-EU paperwork. EUDR's December 2025 enforcement adds geo-located due-diligence requirements on raw-material origin.

Vietnam strategy & marine plan

MARD's Decision 339/QD-TTg targets aquaculture at 7.0 mt and total seafood at 9.8 mt by 2030 [MARD Strategy 2021]; Decision 1664/QD-TTg targets marine cage at 300 k ha and 1.45 mt with USD 1.8–2.0 bn export value by 2030 [MARD Marine 2021]. Sustainability axis is explicit: ASC certification, mangrove-shrimp programmes, and reduced antibiotic use.

Section 10 · AI Impact
020 / 022

AI in Vietnam aquaculture — six use cases that move 2027 economics

AIoT (AI + IoT) platforms can automate up to 95% of pond operating tasks [ShrimpTech 2025] — pH, DO, salinity, temperature telemetry; computer-vision shrimp counting; automated feed dosing; disease anomaly detection. Most use cases sit in research-to-pilot stage today; commercial deployment is the 2026–2027 inflection.

USE CASE · 01
Real-time water-quality telemetry
IoT sensor network reads pH, DO, salinity, temperature, alkalinity in real time [VietfishMag 2025]. AI anomaly detection triggers UFB oxygen generators automatically. Compresses pond-attendant labour and shrinks adverse-event windows from days to hours.
Pilot: Green AIoT MK Delta
USE CASE · 02
Computer-vision disease early detection
Pond-mounted cameras capture high-resolution shrimp images; AI classifies WSSV, EHP, AHPND symptoms days before visible mortality [Researchgate 2025]. Acts before disease propagates pond-cluster wide — the highest-impact 2027 use case for Mekong Delta clusters.
Anchor: USD 50–500/pond/loss avoided
USE CASE · 03
AI-driven feed dosing & conversion
eFishery-style automated feeder + AI dose-optimiser reduces feed waste 10–20% [Kira estimates based on eFishery 2024]. With feed at 50–60% of pond cost [Hoan Duong 2025], a 10% feed-conversion lift translates to a 5–6% pond-margin lift.
Pilot: eFishery, ShrimpTech
USE CASE · 04
Predictive yield & harvest forecasting
Combines telemetry + biomass-vision + weather data to project pond-level harvest tonnage 2-3 weeks ahead. Processors can pre-book trucks, processing slots, and buyer commitments; reduces cold-chain waste and forward-sales risk.
Stage: Late pilot
USE CASE · 05
Salinity-intrusion early warning
Provincial hydromet data + pond salinity telemetry feeds an AI model that flags Mekong saltwater pulse arrival 24-72 hours ahead [Kira estimates]. Enables pond owners to close intake gates, top up freshwater, or accelerate harvest before brackish-line crossing.
Anchor: Feb–Apr 2025 event
USE CASE · 06
EUDR traceability & geo-tagging
Geo-located pond records + supply-chain blockchain anchors satisfy EUDR plot-level due diligence [Virtus 2025]. Vietnamese processors with integrated traceability stacks (Vinh Hoan, Sao Ta) clear EU contracts faster than aggregator-pooled rivals.
Deadline: Dec 2025 EUDR enforcement

Section 11 · Outlook & Forecast
021 / 022

5-year outlook: USD 12–15 bn by 2031, on three converging tracks

KIRA's base case lands Vietnam seafood exports at USD 13.8 bn in 2031 [Kira estimates], between the MARD strategy line (USD 14+ bn) and a feed-cost-stressed bear case (USD 12.0 bn). Bull case stretches to USD 15.5 bn if US POR21 normalises and China demand holds.

Base case · USD 13.8 bn 2031

~4.0% blended CAGR 2025–2031, decelerating from 14%-YoY 2025 as the law-of-large-numbers kicks in. Mix evolves: China/HK share holds ~25%, US recovers to 17–19%, EU edges to 11–12%, CPTPP and Brazil sustain double-digit growth. Aquaculture output reaches 6.8 mt by 2031.

Bull case · USD 15.5 bn 2031

~5.5% CAGR sustains if POR21 normalises shrimp AD rates below 5%, China seafood demand keeps double-digit growth, soybean benchmark holds <USD 380/t average, and EUDR enforcement turns into a Vietnamese-processor competitive lever vs Ecuador. Marine cage scales materially toward the MARD 1.45 mt 2030 target.

Bear case · USD 12.0 bn 2031

~1.2% CAGR if severe Mekong salinity year(s) compress pond output, soybean benchmark spikes >USD 450/t, US AD posture worsens (POR21 reverts to mandatory-respondent >15%), and EU IUU yellow card escalates to a red. Disease outbreak (next-cycle WSSV/EHP) cluster-event would compound.
