The setup

Vietnam's data center market reached USD 1.04 bn in 2025 [Arizton 2025] with installed capacity near 525 MW [DC Outlook 2025], on a path toward ~950 MW by 2030 at a 12.6% CAGR [DC Outlook 2025]. Hyperscale ambition is real: announced and under-construction projects in Ho Chi Minh City alone exceed 500 MW [DCD 2026], anchored by a USD 2 bn G42–Microsoft–FPT campus and Viettel IDC's 140 MW Tan Phu Trung build [Cafef 2025].

The constraint

The bottleneck is physical. Operational load across Hanoi and HCMC was only about 60 MW in 2025 against a national live base near 104 MW [VnEconomy 2025]. Closing the gap to the announced pipeline depends on grid transmission and clean-power access — national demand is set to grow 10.3–12.5% a year through 2030 [MOIT 2025], and high-tech loads already report intermittent supply stress.

Five strategic implications for market participants

Five forces govern who captures Vietnam's data center build-out through 2027. Each is anchored to documented data and KIRA synthesis.

01 · POWER
Grid headroom is the binding constraint
National power demand grows 10.3–12.5% a year to 2030 [MOIT 2025]. Hyperscale campuses need firm, continuous load; siting now follows substation and 500 kV transmission access, not land price. Power readiness, not demand, sets the 2027 ceiling.
Anchor: 505 TWh 2030 demand
02 · CLEAN POWER
DPPA unlocks the hyperscaler mandate
Decree 57/2025 reopened direct power purchase agreements, including a private-wire model attractive to campuses near generation [IEEFA 2025]. Clean-energy access is now a landing prerequisite for global cloud tenants with renewable targets.
Anchor: Decree 57 Mar 2025
03 · CONCENTRATION
Telco incumbents own the base
Four domestic operators — Viettel, VNPT, FPT, CMC — hold ~97% of capacity [Cafef 2025]. Foreign hyperscalers enter via local JVs and colocation, not greenfield-alone. Incumbents convert connectivity and land into the landing rail.
Anchor: ~97% domestic share
04 · REGULATION
Localization manufactures demand
The Personal Data Protection Law (effective Jan 2026) plus Decree 53 data-residency rules force in-country storage [FPF 2025]. Compliance demand is structural, not cyclical — a demand floor beneath colocation through 2027.
Anchor: 24-mo retention rule
05 · CONNECTIVITY
Subsea capacity de-risks the hub bid
The 50 Tbps Asia Direct Cable went live in 2025, exceeding Vietnam's five prior systems combined [VietnamPlus 2025]. With 10 more cables targeted by 2030, the latency and resilience case for a regional hub firms up.
Anchor: 50 Tbps ADC capacity

Section 04 · Market Sizing
007 / 017

Capacity nearly doubles, revenue roughly triples by 2030

Two clocks run at different speeds. Installed capacity climbs from ~525 MW toward ~950 MW by 2030; market value compounds faster as AI-grade racks and colocation premiums lift revenue per MW.

Capacity curve

Installed capacity was about 525 MW in 2025 [DC Outlook 2025], projected near 950 MW by 2030 at a 12.6% CAGR [DC Outlook 2025]. An independent read puts total power capacity rising from ~580 MW to ~980 MW over the same window [Trade.gov 2025] — the two converge on a near-doubling.

Revenue curve

Market value stood at USD 1.04 bn in 2025, on track for USD 3.19 bn by 2031 (20.5% CAGR) [Arizton 2025]. Colocation, ~82% of the market, compounds fastest — from USD 148 mn in 2024 toward USD 673 mn by 2030 [Arizton 2025].

Cost edge

Build cost runs USD 6–8 mn per MW, the lowest among regional hubs [Arizton 2025]. The economic case to build is settled; the question is throughput. [Kira estimates] put realized 2027 capacity well below the headline pipeline.

Section 04 · Demand Drivers
008 / 017

Three demand engines: AI compute, cloud, and localization

Demand is the structurally easy side. A national digital-economy target, an AI-investment wave, and a hard data-residency regime each pull capacity in — and they reinforce each other.

AI compute

The marquee projects are explicitly AI-grade: a USD 2 bn G42–Microsoft–FPT “AI factory” in HCMC and a USD 2 bn KBC–AIC–VietinBank AI complex targeting 200 MW IT load [Cafef 2025]. AI racks draw far more power per cabinet, amplifying the grid question on page 010.

Cloud migration

Vietnam targets a digital economy at 20% of GDP in 2025, rising to 30% by 2030 [Trade.gov 2025], with full government cloud adoption by 2030. Colocation already holds ~82% of the market [Arizton 2025], reflecting enterprises renting rather than building.

Localization mandate

The Personal Data Protection Law (effective Jan 2026) and Decree 53 require in-country storage of Vietnamese user data, with a 24-month minimum retention [FPF 2025]. This converts compliance into durable colocation demand independent of the AI cycle.

Section 05 · Power & the Grid
010 / 017

Power supply is the gate, transmission is the lock

PDP8 (adjusted) commits to a near-tripling of generation capacity, but the binding question for campuses is where and when firm load reaches the meter — especially in the demand-heavy south.

The plan

The adjusted Power Development Plan VIII (approved Apr 2025) targets 183–236 GW of capacity by 2030, up from ~80 GW in 2023 [MOIT 2025]. It funds ~12,944 km of new 500 kV lines and major 220 kV build-out to fix inter-regional imbalance [MOIT 2025].

The stress

Demand grows 10.3–12.5% a year to 2030, reaching ~505 TWh [MOIT 2025]. The 2023 shortage cost an estimated USD 1.4 bn [Vietnam Briefing 2025]; high-tech loads, including data centers, already report intermittent supply stress.

The local fix

HCMC modeled a 5–10% supply-shortfall contingency for 2026 and is routing transmission from Ninh Thuan, Binh Thuan and the Mekong Delta to feed planned campuses [Thanh Nien 2025]. Clean-power siting is now a campus-design input, not an afterthought.

Section 05 · The Operational Gap
011 / 017

The gap between announced and energized

The pipeline reads in gigawatts of intent; the meter reads in tens of megawatts of delivery. Closing that gap by 2027 is a power-and-construction problem, not a demand one.

What is live

Operational load across Hanoi and HCMC was about 60 MW in 2025, within a national live base near 104 MW [VnEconomy 2025]. Vietnam hosts roughly 28–42 operational facilities, the two clusters dominating [DCD 2026].

What is announced

HCMC's pipeline alone exceeds 500 MW [DCD 2026]: Viettel IDC's 140 MW Tan Phu Trung build (completion 2030), the USD 2 bn G42–Microsoft–FPT campus, Sembcorp's approved hyperscale campus, plus Evolution DC (52 MW) and Starmason (60 MW) [Sembcorp 2026].

What lands by 2027

Most flagship campuses target 2027–2030 energization, with first AI-factory phases (~50 MW) due around 2027 [VnExpress 2025]. [Kira estimates] realized 2027 incremental live capacity at a fraction of the headline pipeline, throttled by grid connection windows.

Section 05 · Connectivity & Regulation
012 / 017

Subsea capacity rises; data rules harden the demand floor

Two enablers complete the constraint picture: international bandwidth that makes a regional-hub bid credible, and a data-residency regime that guarantees baseline local demand.

Connectivity

The 50 Tbps Asia Direct Cable went live in April 2025, exceeding Vietnam's five prior systems combined [VietnamPlus 2025]. Vietnam targets at least 10 new cables by 2030 (to 15 total), including a self-managed link to Singapore [VietnamPlus 2025].

Resilience risk

Cable fragility remains real: three subsea systems failed concurrently in 2024 and the APG cable faulted three times in 2025 [DCD 2026]. Route diversity is now a procurement criterion for hub-seeking tenants.

Regulation

The Personal Data Protection Law (Jan 2026) and Decree 356 add revenue-based penalties — up to 5% of annual revenue for cross-border-transfer violations [FPF 2025]. Decree 53 keeps the 24-month local-storage rule, anchoring colocation demand.
