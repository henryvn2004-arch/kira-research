This report triangulates central-bank supervisory data, financial-inclusion surveys, regulatory issuances, and operator disclosures into a forward read on Philippine digital banking. Primary inputs

→ Analyst triangulation
Where published figures diverge, our team reconciles to the most defensible mid-point and tags it as a KIRA estimate.

Scope & limits

→ Unit of analysis
The six BSP-licensed digital banks and the adjacent e-money wallets that compete for the same retail deposit and lending relationship. Universal-bank digital arms sit at the edge of scope.

→ Forecast convention

Philippine digital banking — from land-grab to break-even

Six licensed digital banks built scale fast — millions of depositors, fast-growing deposits, and a foothold in unsecured retail credit. The growth-at-any-cost phase is closing. The 2027 contest is about unit economics, asset quality, and reaching the unbanked majority profitably.

The sector state

Since the first licences in 2021, six digital banks — Maya Bank, GoTyme, UnionDigital, Tonik, UNO Digital, and the state-owned Overseas Filipino Bank — served 20.4 million customers by September 2025 [BSP 2025]. Deposits reached PHP 119.5 bn [BSP 2025], having breached PHP 100 bn for the first time in 2025 — still about 0.7% of system deposits, but compounding faster than any other segment.

Why now: the economics catch up

The model that bought scale — high deposit rates and aggressive unsecured lending — produced a combined 2024 net loss of PHP 7.1 bn and a digital-bank NPL ratio near 21%, versus ~3.6% for the system [BSP 2025] [BSP 2024]. Two banks — Maya and OFBank — turned profitable in 2024 [BSP 2025]. The 2027 question is which others convert scale into durable returns.

Five strategic implications for market participants

Five forces reshape competitive dynamics through 2030. Each is anchored to documented supervisory data and our synthesis of the Philippines' digital-banking trajectory.

01 · ECONOMICS
Profit, not deposits, is the new scoreboard
Deposit and account growth is no longer scarce; durable net interest margin is. With Maya and OFBank profitable from 2024 [BSP 2025], the others must price deposits down and lift credit yield without breaking asset quality. Cost-of-funds discipline decides 2027 survivors.
Anchor: 2 of 6 profitable in 2024
02 · CREDIT
Asset quality is the binding constraint
Digital-bank NPLs ran near 21%, well above the ~3.6% system level [BSP 2024]. Loss rates on thin-file unsecured lending must fall faster than the book grows, or capital erodes. Underwriting, not acquisition, is the moat.
Anchor: ~21% digital-bank NPL
03 · LICENSING
The licence window reopens, raising intensity
With the cap lifted, the BSP will admit up to four more digital banks [BSP 2025]. New entrants — likely telco- and platform-backed — intensify deposit competition just as incumbents pivot to margin. Distribution scale becomes the entry gate.
Anchor: +4 new licences
04 · RURAL
The unbanked majority is the real prize
With 44% of adults still unbanked in 2021 [BSP FIS 2021], concentrated in rural and lower-income provinces, the addressable wedge is national. PhilSys e-KYC lowers onboarding cost; cash-in/out and connectivity remain the binding last-mile gaps.
Anchor: 44% unbanked 2021
05 · MODELS
Alternative-data underwriting is the unlock
Thin-file borrowers have no bureau history; telco, wallet, and platform data is the only route to price risk. Model-driven scoring decides whether digital banks lend to the unbanked profitably or repeat the 21% loss cycle at larger scale.
Anchor: ~3.6% system NPL benchmark

Section 06 · The Sector at Scale
007 / 014

A fast-growing sliver, anchored to a payments-first base

Digital banks remain under 1% of system deposits, yet they sit on top of the largest e-money base in Southeast Asia. The wallet-to-bank conversion — turning payment users into depositors and borrowers — is the structural growth engine behind the headline numbers.

Scale, fast, off a small base

Combined deposits crossed PHP 100 bn for the first time in 2025 and reached PHP 119.5 bn by September [BSP 2025], up from roughly PHP 42 bn in 2022 [Kira estimates] — a near-tripling in three years against low-single-digit system growth. Customers hit 20.4 million, with 15.5 million depositors [BSP 2025].

Still a system sliver

Even after that run, digital banks hold about 0.7% of the system's ~PHP 22 trn deposits [BSP 2025]. The headroom is the story: incumbents own the balance sheet, but digital banks own the marginal new-to-bank customer.

Built on the wallet base

The Philippines runs one of the region's deepest e-money footprints; the leading wallet alone counts over 90 million registered users [Industry 2025]. Maya's bank sits inside that wallet — converting payment users into deposit and credit relationships is the cheapest acquisition channel in the market.

Section 07 · The Path to Profit
008 / 014

Profitability turns on credit yield, funding cost, and loss control

Digital banks earn a wide net interest margin on unsecured lending — but only if losses stay contained. With reported digital-bank NPLs as high as 21% [BSP 2024], the break-even path runs through underwriting discipline and a lower cost of funds, not faster book growth.

Wide margin, fragile to losses

The crossover, bank by bank

Maya Bank booked its first full-year profit of PHP 1.7 bn in 2025 on credit volume routed through its wallet [Maya 2024]; OFBank was also in the black. GoTyme leans on cost-light kiosk distribution and targets profit in 2027 [Industry 2025]. The rest remain in the investment phase — and the BSP itself flags a five-to-seven-year path to profit for the model [BSP 2025]. Parent-group capital patience, not technology, separates those that reach scale-profit from those that retrench.

The funding-cost lever

Early scale was bought with promotional deposit rates well above incumbents. As the rate cycle eases and the deposit land-grab cools, cost of funds should compress, widening net interest margin without any change in credit pricing [Kira estimates]. The banks with the stickiest payroll and wallet balances — low-cost CASA, not rate-chasing hot money — convert that compression into the most durable profit.

Section 11 · Rural Financial Inclusion
011 / 014

The unbanked wedge: national in scale, rural in concentration

Account ownership nearly doubled to 56% of adults by 2021 [BSP FIS 2021], yet 34.3 million adults — 44% — stayed outside the formal system, and cross-checks suggest the gain has since plateaued near 50% [World Bank 2025]. The remaining unbanked skew rural and informal; the national ID cuts onboarding cost, but cash access and connectivity remain the binding gaps.

A real gain, now plateauing

Banked adults rose from 29% in 2019 to 56% in 2021 [BSP FIS 2021], one of the fastest jumps in the region, driven by pandemic-era e-money accounts. Cross-checks since then show the share holding near 50% [World Bank 2025] — the easy gains are banked; the next leg is harder.

Where the unbanked are

The remaining unbanked skew rural, low-income, and informal-sector, where cash-in / cash-out access and reliable connectivity are the practical barriers — not lack of a smartphone [Kira estimates]. The constraint is distribution economics, not demand.

The PhilSys lever

National-ID coverage now spans most adults, with 56.5 million authentications logged by late 2025 [PhilSys 2025], and BSP rules let banks open accounts on PhilSys e-KYC [BSP 2025]. That collapses the cost of a compliant account opening — the largest fixed cost in serving a thin, low-balance rural customer.

Section 12 · Regulation & AI
013 / 014

Reopened licensing, tighter prudential gates, model-driven credit

The BSP lifted its three-year licence cap and will admit up to four more digital banks [BSP 2025], while holding capital, deposit-insurance, and consumer-protection lines. Alternative-data models are the route to lending profitably to borrowers no credit bureau can score.

The licence window reopens

After capping the sector at six since 2021, the BSP lifted its moratorium from January 2025, allowing up to four more licences (ten total), with applications closing December 2025 [BSP 2025]. New entrants are likely platform-, telco-, or conglomerate-backed, raising deposit competition just as incumbents pivot to margin.

The prudential gates

Digital banks face the same capital-adequacy and deposit-insurance regime as peers, with heightened supervisory focus on unsecured-credit concentration and consumer protection [BSP 2025]. Rising NPLs invite tighter provisioning, a direct drag on the break-even timeline.

Models, not branches

With no branches and thin-file customers, underwriting is the entire P&L. Alternative-data scoring on wallet, telco, and platform signals is the only way to price the unbanked — and the only defence against a 21% loss rate scaling with the book [BSP 2024].
