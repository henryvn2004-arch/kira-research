Supply tightness sets the floor

Global production reaches an estimated 15.32 Mt in 2026 against demand of 15.60 Mt [ANRPC 2026], the fifth consecutive deficit year. Thai output eased to 4.7 Mt in 2025 [RAOT 2026]. With ageing tree stock and slow replanting, the deficit caps downside even as auto demand recovers unevenly.

Section 04 · Macro Context
007 / 024

A slow-growth economy with a strong currency

Thailand extended its expansion streak but at a decelerating pace, while a firm baht pressures every export sector — rubber included.

Growth is easing, not stalling

GDP grew 2.4% in 2025, down from 2.9% in 2024, with 2026 forecast near 2.0% [NESDC 2025]. Q1 2026 surprised to the upside at 2.8% YoY on government spending and fixed investment [NESDC 2025]. Persistent headwinds — US tariffs, high household debt, a strong baht — keep the ceiling low.

The currency is the quiet tax

The baht gained roughly 9% against the dollar in 2025 and a further ~1.4% into 2026 [BOT 2026]. For a sector that sells in USD and pays smallholders in baht, appreciation compresses margin even when the SICOM benchmark holds firm.

Inflation stays contained

Core inflation averaged 0.8% in Q3 2025, inside the Bank of Thailand's 1-3% target band [BOT 2026], leaving room for accommodative policy but limiting any inflation-driven lift to nominal farm-gate prices.

Section 04 · Macro Context
008 / 024

Rubber as rural income floor and policy priority

Agriculture accelerated in 2025 on higher rubber, sugarcane and oil-palm yields, keeping farm-gate rubber prices a live political variable.

Rubber sits at the centre of farm policy

Agriculture accelerated in 2025, led by higher yields in rubber, sugarcane, oil palm and maize [NESDC 2025]. Rubber underpins income for the deep South and Northeast, so farm-gate weakness — the 2025 average fell to 54.7 baht/kg, down 5.1% [RAOT 2026] — translates directly into policy pressure.

State support is structural

The Rubber Authority of Thailand grants 10,000 baht per rai for eco-friendly replanting and runs integrated-system pilots in Surat Thani [RAOT 2026]. Support is now increasingly tied to traceability registration rather than price intervention alone.

Terms of trade favour tightness ahead

By April 2026, RSS3 farm-gate had recovered to 81.6 baht/kg [Daily News 2026] as global supply tightened, with the Authority projecting a 70-100 baht/kg band for 2026 [RAOT 2026] — a materially firmer income backdrop than 2025.

Section 05 · Sector Overview
010 / 024

Market size & growth: Thai natural rubber

A flat-volume, re-rating-price market. The value opportunity comes from the benchmark, the grade mix and the traceability premium — not from more tonnes.

Volume is flat; value is the lever

Output held near 4.7-4.8 Mt across 2023-2025 [RAOT 2026], about a third of the ~15 Mt global crop [ANRPC 2026]. With no material area expansion, Thailand's growth is entirely a price-and-mix story — the SICOM benchmark, not planted hectares, drives revenue.

Export value is climbing on price

Jan-Apr 2025 export value reached USD 2.0 bn, up 23.2% year on year even as volume growth was modest [RAOT 2026]. TSR block rubber alone re-priced +25.4% YoY to USD 1,775/t [Krungsri 2025], illustrating how the benchmark carries the top line.

Processing capacity outpaces raw supply

Three-quarters of Thai intermediate rubber output is exported [OEC 2024]; installed packing and TSR capacity now competes for a static raw-material pool, intensifying cup-lump procurement and pushing consolidation among processors [Kira estimates].

Section 06 · Grade Economics
011 / 024

Export grade mix: block rubber leads the tire pull

Thailand's export basket is block-rubber dominated, and the gap is widening as tire-grade demand grows while smoked-sheet volumes contract.

Block rubber is the tire workhorse

Technically-specified block rubber (STR/TSR) is 51.9% of export volume, ahead of concentrated latex at 26.1% and ribbed smoked sheet at 15.2% [RAOT 2026]. TSR20 is the tire-industry standard, laboratory-tested from cup lump and field coagulum — the grade radial and EV tires consume.

The mix is shifting, not static

In the year to November 2025, block rubber exports grew +8.6% YoY while smoked sheet fell 26.6% [RAOT 2026]. The RSS market — historically premium, medical- and consumer-facing — is ceding share to volume tire grades.

Latex is a distinct, glove-linked cycle

Concentrated latex tracks glove and dipped-goods demand, not tires; it re-priced +31.9% YoY to USD 1,363/t in 2025 [Krungsri 2025], moving on a health-consumption cycle that is largely decoupled from the tire-grade story.

Section 06 · Grade Economics
012 / 024

The value-add ladder from cup lump to specialty

Each processing rung and each certification layer steps up margin. The traceability premium is now a rung of its own — a documentation moat, not a physical one.

Physical value-add is well understood

Cup lump and field coagulum move up through TSR20 block, then compound and mixed grades: compound rubber priced USD 2,630/t and mixed rubber USD 1,644/t in 2025 [Krungsri 2025], versus TSR20 near USD 1,775/t. Downstream compounding adds 30-50% over base block [Kira estimates].

Certification is the newer premium rung

EUDR-documented rubber commanded a 3-5 baht/kg premium at peak demand [Thansettakij 2026]. Modelled through, farmer income rises an estimated 0.8% in 2025 and 1.4% in 2026 versus a no-EUDR baseline [KResearch 2026] as compliant volume attracts the premium.

Grade discipline protects margin

Processors that hold consistent TSR20 specification and blend to customer recipe defend price against Vietnamese and Ivorian volume [Kira estimates]; commodity undifferentiation is where the strong baht bites hardest.

Section 08 · Demand & Channels
020 / 024

China anchors demand; EVs lift intensity

The demand base is heavily concentrated on Chinese tire manufacturing, where the shift to electric vehicles is raising natural rubber per tire even as total vehicle counts plateau.

One destination dominates

China takes ~60.2% of Thai intermediate rubber exports, ahead of Malaysia (8.4%), the US (5.4%) and Japan (4.7%) [OEC 2024]. China imported ~6.2 Mt of rubber worth over USD 9 bn in 2024, its domestic output covering barely a third of demand [Industry press 2025].

EV tires are rubber-hungry

Electric-vehicle tires need thicker sidewalls and advanced tread compounds, raising natural rubber per tire by an estimated ~15% [Kira estimates]. With China NEV sales near 14.1 Mn units projected for 2026 [Industry press 2026], intensity growth partly offsets flat vehicle totals.

Synthetics cap, not replace

Synthetic rubber holds ~53% of global consumption [IRSG 2025] and covers ~25% of tire material, but cannot match natural rubber's fatigue resistance in high-load casings [Hamiico 2025]. Substitution trims marginal demand; it does not break the tire dependency.

Section 08 · Demand & Channels
021 / 024

The multi-tier chain from tapper to tire plant

Value moves through five distinct tiers. EUDR compliance and the traceability premium are won or lost at the collector-to-processor junction, where fragmented supply meets documentation demand.

Where margin and risk concentrate

Smallholder tappers sell cup lump to collectors and middlemen, who aggregate to packers running TSR and latex lines. Packers sell to exporters and traders, who ship — mostly to China — for tire compounding. The collector tier is where traceability data must first be captured.

Offtake versus spot shapes volatility

Long-term offtake agreements with tire majors hedge price for both sides; spot sales into China carry full benchmark exposure [Kira estimates]. The offtake-to-spot ratio is the single best predictor of an exporter's earnings volatility.

The compliance split is structural

The chain is bifurcating into EUDR-documented and conventional flows. Aggregators that fund geo-mapping at the collector tier can route compliant volume to premium buyers; those that cannot are pushed toward price-taking commodity sales [Kira estimates].

Section 09 · Regulatory & Policy
022 / 024

EUDR: the defining regulatory event

The EU Deforestation Regulation reshapes market access and pricing. Thailand's "Low Risk" status and 79% mapped area convert a compliance threat into a competitive edge — if smallholders can be brought inside the system.

The timeline is now firm

Large operators must comply by 30 December 2026; micro and small enterprises by 30 June 2027 [EU EUDR 2023]. Rubber, wood and palm oil are the Thai exports most exposed [Krungsri 2025]. Every EU-bound shipment needs plot-level, deforestation-free proof filed in TRACES.

Thailand is well positioned

Thailand earned "Low Risk" classification, easing due-diligence burden, and the Rubber Authority has mapped 3.1 Mn ha — about 79% of production area — with 1.6 Mn+ producers registered [RAOT 2026]. The Traceable Rubber Trading programme launched in April 2024.

The premium is real but contested

Documented rubber earned a 3-5 baht/kg premium at peak; an enforcement delay compressed it and some farmers were squeezed ~5.5 baht/kg on compliance costs [Thansettakij 2026]. Net, EUDR is modelled to lift farmer income 0.8% in 2025 and 1.4% in 2026 [KResearch 2026].

Section 10 · Technology & AI
023 / 024

AI as the traceability and yield layer

The commercial case for AI in Thai rubber runs through EUDR compliance and smallholder inclusion first, and plantation yield second — both defend the export franchise directly.

Compliance is the killer app

Satellite imagery from Sentinel-2, Landsat and Planet Labs screens plots for forest-cover change before goods leave origin [TraceX 2026]. AI-driven screening validated 100% of plots against the December 2020 cut-off in one tire-maker deployment covering 160,000 ha [TraceX 2026].

Inclusion keeps volume compliant

Mobile-first, multilingual platforms let smallholders geo-map plots and upload documents, keeping fragmented supply inside compliant flows [AgTech 2025] rather than sidelined by EUDR.

Yield is the second prize

Drone and satellite yield estimation, disease-risk modelling for leaf blight, and computer-vision cup-lump grading raise plantation productivity against ageing tree stock [Kira estimates].

Section 11 · Outlook
025 / 024

Five-year outlook & price scenarios

A structural deficit and firm tire-grade demand point up, but a strong baht and uneven auto recovery cap the top. The forecast is a price scenario, not a volume story.

Base case: firm, deficit-supported

With the market in a fifth deficit year — 15.32 Mt supply vs 15.60 Mt demand in 2026 [ANRPC 2026] — the base case sees TSR20 averaging near USD 1.85/kg in 2026 and prices rising ~2% in 2026 and ~3% in 2027 [World Bank 2025]. Thai farm-gate holds in a 70-100 baht/kg band [RAOT 2026].

Bull case: supply shock re-rate

La Niña, disease or logistics disruption on a deficit base could trigger a surge toward the 2024 peaks [Capmad 2026]; KResearch models Thai prices up ~4.8% in 2026 in a firm scenario [KResearch 2026].

Bear case: demand and FX drag

A stalled China auto cycle plus further baht appreciation would compress FOB margin even with the deficit intact — the downside is margin, not a price collapse, given the structural floor [Kira estimates].
