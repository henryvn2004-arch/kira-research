Market context

Vietnam's online retail market reached USD 31 bn in 2025 [MOIT 2025], up 25.5% year-on-year, and now accounts for roughly 11% of total retail [MOIT 2025]. The four-platform franchise (Shopee, TikTok Shop, Lazada, Tiki) recorded GMV of VND 429.7 trillion / USD 16.4 bn [Metric 2025], with Shopee plus TikTok Shop alone accounting for 97% of platform GMV [Kira estimates based on Metric 2025]. Internet base 79.8 million users at 78.8% penetration [DataReportal 2025]; mobile-payment users dominated by MoMo's 31 million [MoMo 2025] and ZaloPay's 21 million [ZaloPay 2025].

Why now: three convergent forces

First, the TikTok Shop overtake — H1 2025 GMV growth of 148% [Metric 2025] lifted share to 42% mid-year and to 50% by Q4 [Metric 2025], displacing Shopee at the top. Second, live commerce moved from novelty to mainline channel: 30% livestream conversion versus 3% on classic e-commerce [Metric 2025] and ~40% of platform sessions livestream-touched [Metric 2025]. Third, the new Law on E-Commerce (effective 1 July 2026) [MOIT 2025] plus PDP Law 91/2025 (effective 1 January 2026) [PDP Law 2025] reset cross-border compliance and seller traceability.

Section 03 · Executive Summary
005 / 022

Five strategic implications for market participants

The 2026 window decides whether participants ride the live-commerce wave with compliance-ready operations, or hand share to platforms and creators that adapted faster.

IMP · 01
Treat livestream as the primary discovery channel, not a bolt-on
Livestream conversion runs ~30% versus ~3% on classic shop pages [Metric 2025], and over 40% of platform sessions now touch livestream [Metric 2025]. Brands still budgeting livestream as 5–10% of digital spend will underperform peers operating at 25–35%.
~10x · livestream conversion uplift vs static
IMP · 02
Plan platform exposure for a two-horse race, not four
Shopee plus TikTok Shop hold ~97% of top-4 platform GMV in 2025 [Kira estimates based on Metric 2025]; Lazada and Tiki together fell to ~3%. Allocate sales-ops headcount, ad budget and inventory placement accordingly; multi-platform parity is no longer the efficient default.
97% · Shopee + TikTok Shop share of top-4 GMV
IMP · 03
Compliance work begins now for the 1 July 2026 Law deadline
The new Law on E-Commerce takes effect 1 July 2026 [MOIT 2025] and PDP Law 91/2025 from 1 January 2026 [PDP Law 2025]. Cross-border platforms need a local entity; sellers need VNeID identification; VAT exemption on small parcels is gone. Late movers face transaction holds and seller-account pauses.
1 Jul 2026 · Law on E-Commerce effective
IMP · 04
Wallet + livestream integration is the unit-economics layer
MoMo (~56% wallet share, 31M users) [MoMo 2025] plus ZaloPay (~23%, 21M) [ZaloPay 2025] front 80% of e-commerce checkout [DataReportal 2025]. Integration depth with platform livestream (one-tap checkout, instalment-on-stream) decides AOV and basket abandonment.
~80% · mobile-wallet preference share
IMP · 05
Build the AI personalisation stack or rent it from the platform
AI-driven personalisation lifts e-commerce conversion ~20% [Vietnamnet 2025]; only ~25% of regional sellers report full AI integration [CNBC 2025]. Sellers who delay buying personalisation, dynamic pricing and inventory-AI capacity hand operating margin to platforms that already monetise the layer.

Section 04 · Macro Context
007 / 022

Macro indicators driving e-commerce demand

Vietnam combines 8% GDP growth, 79% internet penetration and a 126% mobile-subscription base with the highest digital-engagement score in Southeast Asia. Each ratio compounds into addressable e-commerce demand faster than the headline retail figure suggests.

GDP outpacing the region

Vietnam's GDP grew 8.02% in 2025 [NSO 2025], second only to 2022's 8.12% over the past 15 years. Q4 2025 hit 8.46% [NSO 2025]; industry and construction +9.73%, services +8.82%. CPI held at 3.31% [NSO 2025], comfortably below the 4.5% target. The result is rising household nominal spending power without inflation eroding it — the favourable backdrop for discretionary online categories (fashion, beauty, electronics).

Digital base now structurally large

79.8 million internet users at 78.8% penetration [DataReportal 2025]; 127 million mobile subscriptions (126% of population) with 100% of connections on 3G/4G/5G [DataReportal 2025]; 76.2 million social media identities, 95.4% of internet users on at least one social platform. Mobile wallets are the preferred payment for 80% of consumers surveyed [DataReportal 2025] and 88% of Gen Z and Gen Y prefer cashless [SBS Software 2025].

Urbanisation + middle-class formation

Urban households cross the bar where online grocery becomes habitual: 60% of metropolitan households bought FMCG online in 2024 [TGM 2025], up from 29% in 2019. Smartphones now generate ~71% of all e-commerce transactions [TGM 2025], and Vietnam ranks first in SEA for AI engagement, with 81% of respondents using AI tools daily [e-Conomy SEA 2025].

Section 05 · Sector Sizing
009 / 022

Market size & growth: e-commerce Vietnam

MOIT's official 2025 figure of USD 31 bn at +25.5% YoY [MOIT 2025] anchors the headline. KIRA's blended 2030 outlook lands at USD 63–70 bn, between the government 70 bn target [MOIT Plan 2026-30] and the e-Conomy SEA 2030 view of USD 63 bn [e-Conomy SEA 2025].

Total GMV trajectory

E-commerce GMV trajectory: USD 15 bn (2023) → 22 bn (2024) → 31 bn (2025) [MOIT 2025] → KIRA blended ~38 bn (2026F) → 63–70 bn (2030F) [Kira estimates]. The internet-economy GMV including ride-hail, travel and food is USD 39 bn for 2025, up from 34 bn in 2024 [e-Conomy SEA 2025]; e-commerce alone of that internet economy was USD 25 bn [e-Conomy SEA 2025], rising 17% YoY.

Share of total retail

E-commerce is now ~11% of total Vietnam retail [MOIT 2025], up from ~7% in 2022; the MOIT Master Plan for 2026–2030 targets 20% by 2030 [MOIT Plan 2026-30]. That target embeds 20–30% annual growth and 70% of adults shopping online. KIRA's base case is closer to 18% retail share by 2030 — ambitious but credible given the 2024–2025 step-up.

Regional context

Vietnam holds #3 in ASEAN by GMV behind Indonesia and Thailand [MOIT 2025]. Per the regional e-Conomy SEA 2025 view, Vietnam's digital economy reaches USD 220 bn GMV by 2030 [e-Conomy SEA 2025], placing it second only to Indonesia regionally on growth trajectory.

Section 06 · Segment Economics
010 / 022

Segment economics: live, social, marketplaces and cross-border

The four sub-segments inside Vietnam's e-commerce stack have different growth rates, different margin structures and different regulatory exposures. Live commerce is the volume engine; classic marketplace remains the platform-economics backbone.

Live commerce — the volume engine

Live commerce GMV is projected at ~USD 11 bn by 2026 [Metric 2025], roughly 35–40% of platform GMV [Kira estimates]. Conversion runs ~30% versus 3% on classic pages [Metric 2025]; 78% of TikTok SEA users have purchased during a live session [TikTok 2025]. Limiting factor is host supply: 2024–2025 saw KOL/KOC consolidation after several "billion-VND livestream" hosts faced false-advertising scrutiny.

Social commerce — the discovery layer

Social commerce reaches USD 5 bn in 2025 [Decision Lab 2025], +25.4% YoY. Facebook leads (94% platform reach for social shopping), Zalo 49%, Instagram 31%, TikTok 23% [Decision Lab 2025]. 65% of online shoppers prefer social-led discovery [Decision Lab 2025]; the shift to micro- and nano-influencers continues as audiences grow distrustful of celebrity endorsement.

Marketplaces — the backbone

Top-4 platform GMV USD 16.4 bn in 2025 [Metric 2025], roughly half of total e-commerce. Shopee remains the marketplace logic (long-tail, search-led, ads-monetised); TikTok Shop overlays video discovery on the same fulfilment rails. Lazada and Tiki are ~3% combined [Metric 2025] — functionally niche.

Business model

TikTok Shop overlays a transactional layer on the TikTok feed. Discovery is video-first (For You feed, hashtag pages, creator profiles) and conversion happens via livestream rooms or shoppable short-form clips. The platform takes commission from sellers (typically 4–6% of GMV) plus ad revenue, plus a logistics-and-fulfilment margin via TikTok Shop Mall.

2025 trajectory

Q4 2025 saw TikTok Shop record VND 7.69 trillion in domestic revenue, +27% QoQ [Metric 2025], lifting share to 50% of platform GMV and overtaking Shopee for the first time. Fashion and accessories category alone delivered VND 23.4 trillion / USD 900 m in Q1 2025 [Metric 2025]. Livestream contributes 35–40% of TikTok SEA commerce GMV [TikTok 2025], with conversion 10x classic e-commerce.

Strengths

  • Video discovery flywheel — For You feed surfaces product to non-shopping intent.
  • Creator/KOL economy — affiliate commission funnels content investment in.
  • Livestream depth — "shoppertainment" format embedded in the app, not bolted on.
  • Gen Z and Gen Y reach — primary social platform for the highest-growth shopper cohort.

Risks & watch-list

  • Cross-border / data regulation — PDP Law 91/2025 plus Cross-Border Data Transfer rules raise compliance cost [PDP Law 2025].
  • KOL/KOC sanitisation — 2024–2025 enforcement against false-advertising creators thinned host supply.
  • Counterfeit and quality control — the new Law on E-Commerce raises platform liability from 1 July 2026 [MOIT 2025].

Section 08 · Demand & Channels
016 / 022

Channel & distribution structure: from session to doorstep

Demand splits across social discovery, livestream conversion, marketplace fulfilment and last-mile delivery. The platform-economics question is which layer captures margin; today, the answer leans heavily toward platforms and logistics-as-a-platform rather than brands.

Shopper mix and behaviour

62.8% of digital shoppers buy at least weekly [YouNet 2025]; smartphones drive 71% of all transactions [TGM 2025]. Millennials (29–44) shop most frequently, with average order value USD 125 vs Gen Z's lower basket [YouNet 2025]. 55% of Gen Z consult micro-influencers before buying fashion or beauty [YouNet 2025]; 51% buy products that go viral on social. Discovery is 65% social-led [Decision Lab 2025].

Payments stack

Mobile wallets are preferred by 80% of consumers [DataReportal 2025]. MoMo leads with ~56% share and 31M users [MoMo 2025]; ZaloPay holds ~23% / 21M users with deep Zalo-messaging integration [ZaloPay 2025]; VNPAY plus VietQR cover bank-led QR rails. QR-code transaction volume grew +107% in 2024 [SBV 2025]. The mobile-payment market hit USD 40.5 bn in 2026 [SBS Software 2025].

Logistics & last mile

Vietnam shipped ~2.2 billion parcels in 2024 [VLA 2025], rising with e-commerce growth above 25% annually. Express delivery market USD 1.36 bn in 2024 [Zion 2025]. Leading 3PLs: Viettel Post, Giao Hang Nhanh (GHN), J&T Express, Ninja Van, plus platform-owned Shopee Xpress and Lazada Logistics. Viettel Post opened a VND 3,300 bn Lang Son hub [Viettel Post 2025] with 200 AGV robots and 99% automation.

Section 09 · Regulatory Landscape
017 / 022

Regulatory landscape: the 2026 compliance reset

Vietnam moved from a thin Decree 52 regime (2013) to a full Law on E-Commerce within 18 months, while Personal Data Protection Law 91/2025 and cross-border tax tightening compound the reset. Effective dates cluster across H1 2026, so the compliance window is short.

Law on E-Commerce — effective 1 July 2026

The National Assembly promulgated Vietnam's first standalone Law on E-Commerce on 10 December 2025, with effect from 1 July 2026 [MOIT 2025]. Headline changes: cross-border platforms must establish a Vietnamese legal entity or appoint an authorised local representative with joint liability for compliance, tax and consumer protection; sellers must be VNeID-identified; counterfeit-goods enforcement strengthens; consumer cancellation rights expand [Baker McKenzie 2026].

Personal Data Protection Law 91/2025

PDP Law 91/2025/QH15 took effect 1 January 2026 [PDP Law 2025]. Vietnam-specific consent, cross-border data transfer impact assessments, data-subject access rights, and material penalties for breach. Platform livestream operations, ad-targeting and KOL-data sharing all need new consent flows.

Cross-border tightening

Temu and Shein operations suspended in late 2024 for non-registration [Reuters 2024]. VAT exemption on imported parcels under VND 1 million eliminated [National Assembly 2024]. Withholding tax mechanism introduced for e-commerce sellers, domestic and foreign. Cross-border data transfer regulation effective from 2025 [ITIF 2025].

Section 10.1 · AI Sizing
019 / 022

AI in Vietnam e-commerce: sizing + adoption shape

Vietnam indexes first in Southeast Asia for AI engagement, with 81% of respondents using AI tools daily [e-Conomy SEA 2025]. Platforms have monetised the layer for two years; sellers are split between those running full AI integration (~25%) and those still in early-stage pilots.

Platform-side AI

Section 10.2 · AI Use Cases
020 / 022

Six AI applications reshaping Vietnamese e-commerce operators

AI translation into seller and platform practice will distinguish 2026 winners from laggards. Use cases below span personalisation engines through livestream operations, with deployment patterns observable in Vietnam or the closest regional benchmarks today.

01 · PERSONALISATION
Recommendation & dynamic discovery
Real-time product recommendation engines lift conversion ~20% [Vietnamnet 2025]. Shopee and TikTok Shop already monetise the layer; sellers pay it back via ad spend. Brand stores that build first-party ML on customer-data platforms shave 4–6 pts off ad cost [Kira estimates].
Observable: Shopee Recommendation, TikTok FYP
02 · CONVERSATIONAL
Chat-led commerce & AI customer service
Conversational commerce GMV in SEA hits USD 23 bn by 2027 [BCG 2025]; 36% of Vietnamese buyers already purchase via chat. Lazada's LazzieChat shows the on-platform pattern; off-platform, Zalo OA and Facebook Messenger drive D2C conversations [BCG 2025].
Pilot: Lazada LazzieChat, Zalo OA
03 · PRICING
Dynamic pricing & promotion engines
Platforms run intra-day dynamic pricing based on competitive feed and inventory. Sellers using AI-powered repricing tools capture 8–12% incremental margin on competitive categories [Kira estimates]; manual repricing leaves margin on the table during livestream campaigns.
Trader-house workflows scale fastest
04 · LIVESTREAM
Livestream production & auto-clipping
AI tools auto-clip high-conversion moments from livestreams into short-form re-distribution; transcribe and translate hosts for cross-regional reach; recommend host-product matches. Cuts livestream content-cost per hour ~30–40% for mid-tier brands [Kira estimates].
Tie-in: TikTok Shop Affiliate, KOL agencies
05 · OPERATIONS
Demand forecasting & inventory
Vietnamese sellers face livestream-driven demand spikes that wreck classic inventory planning. ML models incorporating creator-calendar, hashtag-velocity and discount-stack data forecast peak-week demand 3–5x more accurately than rolling averages [Kira estimates].
Benchmark: Lazada Logistics Vietnam
06 · TRUST & FRAUD
Counterfeit detection & account integrity
Computer-vision counterfeit detection plus seller-behaviour anomaly detection prepare platforms for the July 2026 Law on E-Commerce, which raises platform liability for counterfeit goods [MOIT 2025]. Existing AI-fraud spend (~0.5–1% of GMV at top platforms) likely doubles by 2027 [Kira estimates].
Compliance: Law on E-Commerce 1 Jul 2026

Section 11 · Outlook & Forecast
021 / 022

5-year outlook: USD 63–70 bn by 2030, on three converging tracks

KIRA's base case lands Vietnam e-commerce GMV at USD 65 bn in 2030 [Kira estimates], between the government's USD 70 bn aspiration [MOIT Plan 2026-30] and the regional Google-Temasek-Bain USD 63 bn view [e-Conomy SEA 2025]. Bull and bear cases bracket that ±USD 12 bn.

Base case · USD 65 bn 2030

~20% blended CAGR 2025–2030, decelerating from 25.5% as the law-of-large-numbers kicks in. Mix shift continues: live commerce 35–40% of GMV, classic marketplace stable mid-50%, cross-border re-opening through legal channels as compliant operators register under the 2026 Law.

Bull case · USD 77 bn 2030

~25% CAGR sustains via deeper live-commerce penetration into FMCG and grocery (today underpenetrated vs fashion/beauty), faster credit-card and BNPL adoption, and a successful policy-execution path where the new Law on E-Commerce reduces friction and counterfeits without choking SME sellers.

Bear case · USD 53 bn 2030

~14% CAGR if compliance cost surges shake SME-seller participation, KOL/KOC enforcement reduces livestream host supply faster than new training scales, or a regional consumer slowdown (China spillover, FX shock) compresses discretionary online categories.
