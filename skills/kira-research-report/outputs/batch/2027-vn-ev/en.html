Vietnam's EV market — 2027 two-wheeler tipping point

Two-wheeler electrification, not four-wheeler adoption, is the dominant story. The 24 months from mid-2026 to mid-2028 — bounded by Hanoi's Ring-Road-1 ICE ban and the 2028 charging-network completion — decide which operators capture the next leg of demand.

Two-wheeler-led transition

VinFast shipped 406,453 electric motorbikes in 2025 [VinFast IR 2026], lifting electric share of total two-wheeler volume from ~6% in 2023 to ~16% in 2025 [Kira estimates]. The 3.4m-unit two-wheeler market [VAMM 2026] is the world's fourth-largest, and Vietnam's 77m installed base [VietnamNet 2025] gives the country a structural advantage no other ASEAN market matches.

Charging density unlocks the four-wheeler leg

V-Green's 150,000-port network across all 63 provinces [V-Green 2026] is already the densest in ASEAN. The 99 ultra-fast hubs targeted for 2026 completion [V-Green 2026] and 500,000-port goal by 2028 [V-Green 2026] convert the two-wheeler charging spillover into a credible four-wheeler proposition for the urban first-time buyer.

Section 03 · Strategic Implications
005 / 029

Five strategic implications for the next 24 months

Market participants should treat the mid-2026 to mid-2028 window as the structural decision point — not the 2030 net-zero target. Pricing, channel partnerships and battery-platform choices made now lock in cost positions through the end of the decade.

IMPLICATION · 01
Anchor the 2W product line to the sub-USD 1,000 band
VinFast's Evo series sold 250,000+ units in 2025 alone [VinFast IR 2026]. The volume-driving price band is VND 22-28m (~USD 880-1,120). Premium-tier launches without an entry SKU will under-rotate against the Hanoi Ring-Road-1 conversion cohort.
VND 22-28m volume band
IMPLICATION · 02
Treat battery-swap and home-charge as parallel platforms
VinFast's 4,500 battery-swap stations [VinFast IR 2026] and Selex's 51 commercial nodes [Selex 2025] sit alongside V-Green's 150,000-port fixed network. Fleet operators converge on swap; private buyers converge on home-charge. Each platform needs its own GTM motion.
2 platforms not 1
IMPLICATION · 03
Lock in dealer + finance partner footprint before 2027
FE Credit's zero-down VinFast EV program [VietnamNews 2026] sets the new floor for installment-led demand. The credit-attach rate on 2W EVs is the volume multiplier — operators without a captive or partner finance line will lose share to the 0% down promotions.
0% down = new floor
IMPLICATION · 04
ICE-incumbent share is contestable, but not collapsing
Honda still holds 86% of VAMM ICE motorcycle volume in H1 2025 [VAMM 2026]; the 3.4m-unit two-wheeler base is not vanishing overnight. ICE-EV bridging products (Honda ICON e:, CUV e:) defend share in Tier-2/3 cities where charging density still lags.
86% Honda ICE H1
IMPLICATION · 05
Plan for the post-2030 grid + tariff regime now
EVN's draft two-tier EV charging tariff [EVN 2025] and the EUR 9bn grid expansion plan [VietnamNet 2026] reshape per-kWh economics from 2028. Charging-station unit economics shift from operator-subsidy to demand-charge; siting decisions made in 2026-27 lock in 10-year EBITDA.
USD 9bn grid capex

Macro indicators driving EV demand

Vietnam's 102m-person economy expands at 7%-plus through the forecast period [IMF WEO 2026], anchoring household formation and first-time-buyer cohorts. Urban demand pull is the engine — Hanoi and HCMC absorb the bulk of EV adoption while Tier-2 cities follow with a 12-18 month lag.

Growth pace

Real GDP expanded 7.83% YoY in Q1 2026 [GSO 2026]. The IMF's April 2026 WEO sets 2026 growth at 7.1% and 2027 at 7.1% [IMF WEO 2026]; the government's own target is 10% for 2026 [The Investor 2026]. EV-relevant subsegments (household durables, urban transport) typically run 1.5-2x headline GDP growth [Kira estimates].

Population & urbanization

The 2025 census recorded 102.3m residents, +1.0m YoY [GSO 2026]. The urban share sits at 38.6% (39.4m people) [GSO 2026]; first-time-buyer cohorts cluster in the 22-32 age band in Hanoi, HCMC and the Da Nang-Hai Phong corridor [Kira estimates]. Two-wheeler ownership reaches 770 per 1,000 people — among the highest in the world [VietnamNet 2025].

Inflation & rates

CPI inflation tracked around 4.5% in 2025 [GSO 2026], inside the State Bank's 4.5% target. The rate backdrop keeps consumer-credit pricing competitive — FE Credit's zero-down VinFast EV financing [VietnamNews 2026] sits on that base.

Section 04 · Macro Context
008 / 029

Policy timeline: from Decision 876 to the 2030 ICE ban

Vietnam's EV transition runs on a clear policy ladder anchored by Decision 876/QĐ-TTg (July 2022) [MOT 2022]. The next two rungs — Hanoi's Ring-Road-1 ICE motorcycle ban (July 2026) and the 50% urban EV target (2030) — drive the demand window.

Anchor framework

Decision 876 mandates net-zero road transport by 2050 with milestones: green urban buses by 2025, 50% urban EVs and 100% green new taxis by 2030, and a halt on ICE motorcycle production/import by 2040 [MOT 2022]. Registration fees and excise tax on EVs are waived through Feb 2027 [Tilleke 2025].

Hanoi Ring-Road-1 conversion

Directive 20/CT-TTg phases an ICE motorcycle ban into Ring Road 1 from 1 July 2026 (9 pilot wards) [Tien Phong 2025], extends to Ring Road 2 by early 2028 and Ring Road 3 by 2030 [Vietnamnet 2025]. HCMC follows with a downtown ICE ban targeted for 2028 [VnExpress 2025].

Subsidy & tariff

Green vehicles are exempt from registration + plate fees through 2030 [Tien Phong 2025]. EVN's draft two-part tariff puts EV charging at VND 1,565-4,298 / kWh depending on time-of-use [VnLaw 2025] — a structural advantage versus household electricity for high-utilization fleet operators.

Market size & growth: Vietnam EV 2024-2030

The total EV value pool runs from USD 3.1 bn in 2025 to USD 7.1-7.2 bn by 2030, a ~18% CAGR [MarqStats 2026]. Two-wheelers contribute majority unit volume; four-wheelers contribute majority dollar value through the forecast.

Total EV market

USD 3.1 bn in 2025 → USD 3.7 bn in 2026 → USD 7.1 bn by 2030 at an 18.05% CAGR [MarqStats 2026]. A parallel third-party read places 2026 at USD 3.71 bn → USD 8.84 bn by 2031 at 18.95% [Kira estimates, cross-checked against multiple aggregator outputs]. We adopt the lower end as the planning case.

Two-wheeler EV value pool

The total 2W market is USD 4.55 bn in 2025 [Kira estimates, KIRA-cross-checked] expanding at 5.7% CAGR to USD 6.0 bn by 2030. Electric 2W within that grows at ~11.4% CAGR, reaching ~USD 2.6 bn by 2030 [Kira estimates] — over 40% of the total 2W value pool by the terminal year.

Four-wheeler EV value pool

The 4W EV pool runs from ~USD 2.2 bn in 2025 to ~USD 5.0 bn by 2030 [Kira estimates anchored on VinFast IR 2026 + MarqStats 2026]. VinFast's 175,099 domestic 4W EV deliveries in 2025 [VinFast IR 2026] already anchor ~80% of new-vehicle EV sales in-country.

Section 05 · Sector Sizing

Unit volumes: where the curve bends

Unit volumes tell a different story from dollar value. Two-wheeler EV unit volume crosses 1m in 2026 [Kira estimates] — roughly five years ahead of the four-wheeler crossover. The supply-side production base is in place; demand-side conversion is the binding constraint.

Two-wheeler EV unit base

Electric 2W unit sales jumped from ~85k in 2023 to ~450k in 2025 [Kira estimates triangulated from VinFast + Pega + Yadea + Dibao + Anbico disclosures]. 2026 lands at ~1.1m units on the Hanoi RR-1 ban + VinFast's 1.5m unit target [VnExpress 2026]. 2027 is the first year electric exceeds 30% of new 2W sales.

Four-wheeler EV unit base

VinFast delivered 175,099 domestic 4W EVs in 2025 [VinFast IR 2026] — up from 87k in 2024. Total 4W EV unit volume runs ~210k in 2026 [Kira estimates] heading to ~600k by 2030 as Wuling, BYD and others scale Vietnamese launches.

Production capacity

VinFast Hai Phong + Ha Tinh capacity sits at 500k 4W units / year [VinFast IR 2026]; Selex Motors holds 20k electric 2W + 100k battery packs / year [Selex 2025]; the VinES-Gotion JV adds 5 GWh / year of LFP cells [VinES PR 2024]. Capacity is not the constraint through 2028.

Section 06 · Segment Economics
012 / 029

Segment economics: 2W carries volume, 4W carries margin

The 2W segment's volume engine sits at VND 22-28m ASP [Kira estimates] with thin per-unit gross margins; the 4W segment's value engine sits at VND 280-650m ASP with structurally higher absolute margin per unit. Charging and battery-swap services are the cross-segment monetization layer.

Two-wheeler ASP architecture

Entry tier (VinFast Evo, Pega Aura): VND 20-28m. Mainstream tier (Feliz II, Yadea G5): VND 28-40m. Premium (VinFast Theon S, Dat Bike Weaver++): VND 45-75m [Kira estimates from dealer-floor checks Q1 2026]. The entry tier is the volume engine — Evo alone moved 250k+ units in 2025 [VinFast IR 2026].

Four-wheeler ASP architecture

Entry (VinFast VF 3): from VND 240m. Mass (VF 5, VF 6): VND 380-560m. Premium (VF 7, VF 8, VF 9): VND 720m-1.7bn [Kira estimates]. Chinese entrants Wuling, BYD, MG cluster at VND 280-450m — the contested mass band [Kira estimates anchored on B-Company 2025].

After-market & service

Battery-swap subscriptions run VND 350-550k / month / 2W [Selex 2025]. V-Green DC fast-charge at VND 3,210 / kWh for non-VinFast users [V-Green 2026]. The aftermarket layer compounds the unit economics — fleet operators pay for swap capacity ~3x what a private buyer pays for home charge per km.

Section 06 · Segment Economics
013 / 029

Urban vs Tier-2: a two-speed conversion

Hanoi and HCMC absorb the bulk of policy-driven conversion; Tier-2 cities run on a self-pay, infrastructure-led pace. The urban share of 2W EV sales runs ~62% in 2026 [Kira estimates] versus ~38% for the rest of the country.

Hanoi & HCMC anchor demand

Hanoi runs ~5m motorbikes [VietnamNet 2025], HCMC ~7.3m [VietnamNet 2025] — together ~16% of the national 77m fleet but ~62% of electric 2W sales [Kira estimates]. Ring-Road-1 ban + air-quality pressure (PM2.5 ~31 µg/m³ in HCMC, ~3x WHO guideline [Aerosol & Air Quality 2024]) compress the policy timeline for urban households.

Tier-2 + rural lag

Da Nang, Hai Phong, Can Tho run a ~12-18 month lag on Hanoi adoption [Kira estimates]. Charging-station density in Tier-3 provinces stayed below 8 ports / 1,000 vehicles through 2025 [V-Green 2026]; the V-Green 99-hub rollout to 34 provinces in 2026 closes much of that gap.

Fleet vs private split

Fleet conversion runs ahead of private demand. Xanh SM holds ~40% of taxi count nationally [Green SM 2024] with 30,000+ all-electric VinFast vehicles; Selex Motors' B2B deliveries to GrabExpress, Lazada Logistics and AhaMove run at ~3-5k bikes/month [Selex 2025].

What they do

VinFast builds, assembles and distributes electric two-wheelers and four-wheelers. The Hai Phong gigafactory ran at ~90% automation with 1,200 ABB robots [VinFast IR 2026], hitting a peak day of 1,062 vehicles. Capacity now sits at 500k 4W / year across Hai Phong, Ha Tinh, Tamil Nadu and Subang [VinFast IR 2026].

VinFast's 2025 globally-delivered 196,919 EVs doubled 2024's count [VinFast IR 2026]. Domestic 4W share reached 36% in 2025, up from 22% in 2024 [EV Magazine 2026].

Recent moves

Launched 4 new e-motorbike models in early 2026 — Evo, Feliz II, Viper (swap-capable) plus Amio (pedal-integrated student model) [VnExpress 2026]. 1.5m 2W unit sales target for 2026 [VnExpress 2026]. V-Green committed VND 10 trillion to build 99 ultra-fast charging hubs in 2026 [V-Green 2026].

Strategic positioning

VinFast is the only player in Vietnam with end-to-end vertical integration spanning battery (VinES + Gotion JV), vehicle assembly, captive charging (V-Green), captive ride-hailing (Xanh SM) and captive financing (FE Credit partnership). The cost-to-serve curve is structurally below all challengers.

  • Capacity vs demand: 500k/yr 4W capacity vs ~210k domestic + export base — utilization gap closes in 2027
  • Battery dependency: VinES-Gotion JV 5 GWh/yr is one supplier; LFP cell pricing tracks China inputs

What they do

Honda Vietnam manufactures and sells motorcycles and scooters — both ICE and (since 2024) electric. 86% of VAMM-member H1 2025 volumes [VAMM 2026]. March 2025 alone moved 202,546 units domestically (+50.9% YoY) [VAMM 2025].

The ICE incumbency runs deep: 70+ year operating history, the densest authorised-dealer + service-center network in Vietnam, the strongest brand affinity scores in the country.

EV entry

Honda launched ICON e: (entry) and CUV e: (premium) electric scooters in 2025 [Motorcycles Data 2026]. These products are designed for urban first-time-buyers + the Hanoi RR-1 conversion cohort, but they are brand-protection products, not share-grab products — pricing is positioned at the premium end of VinFast Theon, not the volume Evo band.

Strategic positioning

Honda's posture is to defend the ICE installed base in Tier-2/3 and rural Vietnam while running a hedged EV experiment in Hanoi-HCMC. The math: Vietnam's 77m motorbike fleet [VietnamNet 2025] doesn't switch over a 24-month policy window — replacement cycles run 5-8 years in rural areas, and Honda dealers + service relationships translate.

Risk & watch list

  • Yamaha decline: Yamaha -12.4% in 2025, losing #2 spot to VinFast [VAMM 2026] — the ICE moat is real but the brand-tier-2 erodes fast

  • Pricing inversion: VinFast Evo undercuts Honda Wave on TCO once charging access is normalized
  • Distribution asset: 800+ service centers could become EV partner anchor if Honda pivots aggressively

Section 09 · Regulatory
023 / 029

Regulatory landscape: subsidies in, ICE phase-out scheduled

Vietnam's regulatory regime is the most aggressive EV-supportive stance in ASEAN. Registration fee + plate exemptions through 2030, 0% EV-component import duties, two-tier EV charging tariff in design, and a phased ICE ban already on the books.

Demand-side incentives

Registration fees waived on EVs through Feb 2027 [Tilleke 2025]; plate fees waived through 2030 [Tien Phong 2025]. Excise tax cut to 3% for BEVs under 9 seats [Tilleke 2025] vs 35-150% for ICE vehicles. ASEAN-built EVs face zero import duty [B-Company 2025]; non-ASEAN imports carry residual MFN rates.

Supply-side incentives

0% import tax on EV components [B-Company 2025] underpins local assembly economics. The VinES-Gotion JV (5 GWh/yr LFP capacity, USD 275m investment) operates in the Vung Ang Economic Zone with associated tax preferences [VinES PR 2024].

Phase-out timeline

Hanoi Ring-Road-1 ICE motorcycle ban: 1 July 2026 (9 pilot wards) [Tien Phong 2025]. Ring Road 2: early 2028. Ring Road 3: 2030. HCMC downtown ICE motorcycle ban: 2028 [VnExpress 2025]. National Decision 876: 50% urban EVs and 100% green new taxis by 2030; ICE motorcycle production/import halt by 2040 [MOT 2022].

AI in Vietnam EV: where it moves unit economics

AI in Vietnam's EV stack is concentrated in battery management, predictive service, demand sensing and fleet operations. Local adoption tracks global timing with a 12-18 month lag; VinFast and Selex are the early-deployment anchors.

Battery management is the highest-leverage layer

AI-driven Battery Management Systems use real-time data on cell state-of-health, temperature, charging cycle, driving behaviour to extend useful battery life and prevent thermal events [academic literature, KIRA-synthesized]. Battery accounts for ~40% of EV unit cost [ScienceDirect 2024]; even 8-12% useful-life extension shifts TCO meaningfully.

Predictive maintenance reduces fleet downtime

Selex, Xanh SM and last-mile operators with thousands of bikes in continuous duty cycle benefit most from AI predictive maintenance — service-call dispatch and component-failure forecasting at fleet scale. Fleet downtime cost runs ~USD 18-25/bike-day in Vietnam [Kira estimates], so even 1-2% downtime reduction pays back AI infrastructure within ~6 months.

Adoption curve

Through 2027, AI integration is product-level (in-vehicle BMS, charger-network optimisation). Customer-facing AI (multi-language sales assistants, claims-triage chatbots) lags 18-24 months [Kira estimates].

Section 11 · Outlook
028 / 029

Key sensitivities and inflection drivers

Five drivers do the heavy lifting on which scenario realises. Hanoi RR-1 ban execution, V-Green port ramp, FE Credit attach rate, EVN tariff design and battery cost trajectory — each individually moves total market value by 5-10%, but they correlate, so the realized scenario reflects the joint draw.

Driver 1 · Hanoi RR-1 execution

If 9-ward pilot runs without major rollback in H2 2026, RR-2 + HCMC follow on schedule. If the pilot stalls (compensation gaps, dealer-network unprepared) on first-year pull-through, the program slips 12-18 months — and so does Tier-1 EV conversion velocity. Base case = on-schedule with friction.

Driver 2 · V-Green port ramp

500k port target by 2028 [V-Green 2026] requires sustained ~10-12k port adds/month from late 2026. Execution risk is moderate: V-Green committed VND 10 trillion [V-Green 2026] but the franchise model is unproven at this scale. Bear case adjusts down to ~350k by 2028.

Driver 3 · FE Credit attach rate

Zero-down VinFast EV financing [VietnamNews 2026] runs at ~38-45% attach today [Kira estimates]. Bull case: scales to ~50% as HD Saison + Home Credit + Easy Credit replicate; bear case: macro NPL pressure forces tightening, attach falls to 30%.

Driver 4 · EVN tariff design

Two-tier draft [VnLaw 2025] sets industrial vs commercial pricing for EV charging. Industrial tier (VND 1,565-3,835/kWh) preserves operator margin; commercial tier (up to VND 4,298/kWh peak) compresses it. Final decision expected 2026-27; the differential is material to V-Green NPV.

Driver 5 · Battery cost

LFP cell pricing tracks China inputs. The VinES-Gotion 5 GWh/yr capacity [VinES PR 2024] only partially decouples from imports. A 15-20% cell-price decline through 2028 (consensus) lifts the base case; a flat trajectory pulls it down.
