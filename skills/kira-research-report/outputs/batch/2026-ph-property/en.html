The recovery side

Metro Manila's office market beat expectations in 2025: Q3 net take-up reached 215,000 sqm, with the IT-BPM sector the key demand driver [Colliers 2026]. Prime CBDs lead the turn — Makati CBD vacancy is set to fall from ~7.2% to ~5.5% in 2026 and toward 2.6% by 2028, while BGC has held single-digit vacancy since early 2024 [Colliers 2026]. The 2028 IT-BPM roadmap targets a USD 59 bn industry and 2.5 million workers [IBPAP 2026].

The oversupply side

Residential is the mirror image. Metro Manila secondary-market condominium vacancy ended 2025 at a record ~24.7% and is seen peaking near 25.6% in 2026 [Colliers 2026], weighed by the offshore-gaming exit and pandemic-era completions. Yet absorption is improving: remaining inventory life fell to 6.8 years in Q1 2026 from a 13.4-year peak [LPC 2026], led by affordable units.

01 · OFFICE
Prime CBDs flip to a landlord's market
Makati CBD vacancy is set to drop from ~7.2% to ~5.5% in 2026 and BGC rents to rise as supply tightens [Colliers 2026]. Only ~350,000 sqm of new office is due 2026–28 [Colliers 2026] — pricing power returns to prime landlords first.

02 · DEMAND
BPO and GCCs, not POGO, drive take-up

Section 04 · Macro & Policy
007 / 017

Steady growth and an easing cycle underwrite the recovery

The macro backdrop is supportive but not euphoric. Moderate growth and benign inflation let the central bank ease toward a three-year-low rate, lowering mortgage costs — while a structurally growing services-export base keeps office demand intact.

Growth & inflation

The economy is seen growing about 5.1% in 2025, with the IMF since trimming its 2026 path toward the 4–5% range amid external headwinds [IMF 2026]. Inflation stayed benign — near 1.7% in 2025, rising toward ~2.8% in 2026 as base effects fade [IMF 2026] — giving policymakers room to support demand.

The easing cycle

The central bank cut its key rate to a three-year low of 4.5% after five consecutive 25bp moves, signalling the easing cycle is near its end [BSP 2026]. Lower policy rates feed gradually into mortgage costs — a tailwind for the rate-sensitive affordable and mid-market condominium tiers where rejection has been highest.

Services exports

The structural support is the IT-BPM and GCC base. Industry revenue reached ~USD 40 bn in 2025 on a 1.9 million-strong workforce, with a 2028 target of USD 59 bn and 2.5 million workers [IBPAP 2026]. That pipeline of high-value services jobs is the demand floor under prime office and, indirectly, under residential.

Section 05 · Office & BPO
009 / 017

Outsourcing pulls prime CBD vacancy back from the peak

The office recovery is real but uneven. Prime CBDs tighten while secondary markets still offer incentives — and demand is led by IT-BPM occupiers and a growing share of global capability centres taking higher-value space.

The vacancy turn

Metro Manila office vacancy peaked near 19.8% in 2024 after the offshore-gaming exit, and Colliers holds its 2026 year-end forecast at ~19% with net take-up of ~400,000 sqm [Colliers 2026]. The recovery is concentrated: Makati CBD is set to fall from ~7.2% to ~5.5% in 2026, then toward 4% in 2027 and 2.6% in 2028, becoming a landlord's market [Colliers 2026].

BPO & GCC demand

The active leasing pipeline is 227,000 sqm, split almost evenly between IT-BPM (114,000 sqm) and traditional tenants (113,000 sqm) [Colliers 2026]. Within IT-BPM, third-party outsourcers lead at 61% but global capability centres now take ~39% [Outsource Accelerator 2026] — in-house captives that sign longer leases and occupy higher-spec space.

Supply & rents

Only about 350,000 sqm of new office is due to complete across 2026–28, well below pre-pandemic delivery [Colliers 2026]. With supply thin and prime demand firming, BGC rents are expected to rise in 2026 while Quezon City, Ortigas and Cebu still offer flexible terms [Colliers 2026].

Section 06 · Market Sizing
011 / 017

Inventory still heavy, but absorption is turning

Two signals diverge. The unsold stock remains large and vacancy is still rising, yet remaining inventory life is falling sharply as preselling demand returns — the market is destocking from a high base.

The unsold overhang

Metro Manila holds about 79,200 unsold condominium units, with nearly 30,000 unsold ready-for-occupancy units concentrated in Cubao–New Manila, the Bay Area, Pasig and Alabang–Las Piñas [LPC 2026]. Secondary-market vacancy ended 2025 at a record ~24.7% and is projected to peak near 25.6% in 2026 as ~13,000 new units complete [Colliers 2026].

Absorption turning

The destocking is visible. Remaining inventory life fell to 6.8 years in Q1 2026 from a 13.4-year peak in mid-2025 — the lowest in six quarters [LPC 2026]. Net preselling take-up surged ~765% year-on-year to roughly 2,000 units, from just ~200 a year earlier [LPC 2026], concentrated in the rate-sensitive affordable tier.

Supply discipline

New supply for full-year 2026 is set near 13,000 units — up from a depressed 2025 but well below the launch peaks that built the overhang [Colliers 2026]. Developers are deliberately restraining the high-rise pipeline and re-weighting toward core and economic housing to match where demand actually clears.

Section 06 · Segment Economics
012 / 017

A bifurcated market: affordable absorbs, Bay Area stalls

The glut is concentrated, not general. Economic and affordable units clear fastest, while the offshore-gaming exit left the Bay Area with vacancy approaching 60% and rents far below their pre-2020 peak.

The affordable rebound

Demand has shifted down-market. In Q1 2026, units priced PHP 1.8–3.6m drove the bulk of preselling activity, with the economic and affordable segments accounting for ~74% of net take-up [LPC 2026]. Lower policy rates and end-user demand — not investor speculation — underpin this tier, making it the engine of the recovery.

The Bay Area overhang

The pressure point is the POGO-exposed Bay Area. Vacancy there sits near ~60%, and Manila Bay-zone rents have fallen roughly 52% versus early 2020 — studios now lease near PHP 700/sqm against ~PHP 1,200 before [Colliers 2026]. This is a localised, structural overhang that will take years to re-tenant.

The upscale cooldown

The top has softened rather than collapsed. Upscale demand cooled through 2025 even as core housing picked up [Ayala Land 2026]; developers still launch ultra-prime towers in Makati for cash-rich and expatriate buyers, but volume now sits in the affordable band where the credit cycle is turning most favourably.

Section 10 · Technology in the Channel
015 / 017

Digital channels and AI tools reshape how units get sold

In an oversupplied residential market, distribution efficiency decides absorption. Proptech platforms and AI-assisted sales tools let developers qualify thinner end-user and overseas-Filipino demand at lower cost.

The channel shift

The Philippine real-estate and proptech platform market is valued near USD 7.5 bn, anchored in Metro Manila, Cebu and Davao [KenResearch 2025]. Online search, virtual tours and remote payment have moved from novelty to default — critical when overseas-Filipino buyers transact remotely and developers compete to clear a large unsold base.

AI in the sales funnel

Adoption clusters around four uses: AI-driven lead scoring and buyer matching, automated multilingual enquiry handling for OFW buyers, virtual tours and 3D walkthroughs for remote inspection, and data-led pricing and absorption analytics. Each compresses acquisition cost where every qualified buyer is harder won.

Where it pays

The return is sharpest in the oversupplied affordable and mid tiers, where marketing efficiency sets absorption speed. Analytics help developers price and sequence releases against real demand rather than carry stock; virtual tools extend reach into the OFW pool. The technology does not create demand — it lowers the cost of finding and converting it.

Methodology & sources

How the numbers were derived, and the full citation for every named source used in this view.

Methodology

This view triangulates two evidence streams. Where a figure carries a named source tag (e.g. [Colliers 2026], [LPC 2026]), the underlying data comes from that source; the full citation appears at right. Where a figure carries [Kira estimates], KIRA analysts derived it from a blend of disclosed inputs — brokerage vacancy and take-up series, listed-developer disclosure, and industry datasets — using the method disclosed in the relevant section.

Submarket-level vacancy paths, the price-band take-up split, and the 2028 scenario fan are KIRA constructions reconciled against published market data. Approximately 25% of quantitative claims are KIRA-derived; the remainder are externally cited. Research drew principally on English-language Philippine sources, which dominate the country's property and business press.
