Indonesia digital banking — the profitability turn

Per March 2026, the average digital-bank ROA reached 2.87% — above the industry's 2.50% — with NIM at 13.90% versus the 4.49% sector average [OJK 2026]. Nine of the licensed neobanks posted positive FY-2025 net income. The next 24 months decide whether ecosystem-backed scale players can stretch that profitability into the 97 million unbanked Indonesians still concentrated in rural Java, Sumatra and the eastern archipelago [World Bank 2024].

From acquisition to monetization

Six of the largest neobanks — SeaBank, Bank Jago, Bank Neo Commerce, Allo, Aladin and Superbank — collectively reported FY-2025 net income near IDR 2.3 trillion [Kira estimates from operator filings]. SeaBank alone delivered IDR 678 bn, +79% YoY [SeaBank 2026]; Bank Jago doubled to IDR 276 bn [ARTO 2026]; Bank Neo Commerce posted IDR 565 bn, 27x prior-year [BBYB 2026]. The growth-at-all-cost phase is over.

Rural remains the unmet wedge

Rural financial inclusion sits at 70% vs 78% urban [OJK 2024]. APJII puts internet penetration at 80.5% but fixed broadband at just 21% of households [APJII 2025]. Mobile-first is the only viable channel, and CAC outside Java is 2-3x the Jakarta benchmark [Kira estimates]. Operators with agent-banking distribution (BRI, BNI parents) hold structural advantage over digital-only challengers.

Five strategic implications

Where capital and product effort belong over the next 24 months — ranked by asymmetry of return given how the rural acquisition wedge is now scoring.

01
Ecosystem distribution is the moat
SeaBank's 28M users [SeaBank 2026], Bank Jago's GoTo embedding [ARTO 2026], Allo's CT-Corp retail tie-in [BBHI 2026] all turn the parent's customer base into a free CAC funnel. Standalone neobanks without ecosystem anchors will compress in 2026-2027 as cost-of-funding rises.
ANCHOR · ~85M top-6 combined accounts
02
Rural unlocks need agent-bank pairs
BRILink runs 81,000+ villages [BRI 2024]; BNI Agen46 covers 35,000 villages with 207k agents [BNI 2024]. Pure-digital neobanks cannot match this physical reach. Distribution JVs with BRI/BNI/Mandiri agent networks are the only viable rural play short of greenfield buildout.
ANCHOR · 207k Agen46 nodes
03
CIR compression is the new game
Bank Jago cut CIR from 74% to 59% in Q3-2025 [Bank Jago Q3 2025]. Aladin compressed BOPO from 119% to 85% [BANK 2026]. The winners build a sub-50% CIR by 2028 — the rest get acquired or wind down. Operational discipline now beats deposit promo.
ANCHOR · 59% Jago Q3-25 CIR
04
POJK 3/2024 sandbox is the wedge
OJK's expanded ITSK sandbox covers alt-credit scoring, eKYC, robo-advisory and crypto-asset-related models [POJK 3/2024]. One-year test windows. Operators that pass through sandbox set the de-facto compliance benchmark for the segment. Lenders without sandbox roadmaps cede regulatory voice.
ANCHOR · 12 mo max sandbox tenure
05
AI is unit economics, not narrative
Device-intel underwriting lifts approval rates up to +40% while cutting fraud loss [Kira estimates from industry benchmarks]. Bank Jago publicly attributes part of its CIR compression to AI ops [Bank Jago Q3 2025]. The frame is opex payback, not consumer-facing copilots. Buyers picking AI vendors should require disclosed delta on cost-to-serve.
ANCHOR · +40% approval lift potential

SECTION 04 · CHAPTER ONE

Macro context: Indonesia 2026

A 5.3% growth economy with 80% internet penetration, the world's fourth-largest unbanked population, and a central bank now tightening on rupiah defense creates an asymmetric demand floor: digital banking volume holds, but rural acquisition becomes more capital-intensive. Macro is the multiplier and the friction this cycle.

Section 04 · Macro Context
007 / 026

Growth holds, but funding cost is the headwind

Bank Indonesia projects 5.33% GDP growth in 2026, possibly 5.4% if fiscal spend accelerates [BI 2026]. The IMF marks growth at a more conservative 5.1% [IMF Nov 2025]. Funding-cost sensitivity is the variable to watch: BI raised the policy rate by 50 bps to 5.25% in May 2026 [BI 2026], reversing the prior cutting cycle.

Growth carries the volume floor

Indonesia's nominal GDP per capita is approaching USD 5,300 by 2026 [Kira estimates from BPS + BI projections]. Services and consumption hold ~57% of value-added growth [BPS 2025]. Digital-finance addressable market widens with each percentage point of urban middle-class spend; the rural floor is policy-anchored via KUR subsidized lending.

Rate cycle compresses cheap-funding plays

BI-Rate at 5.25% (May-2026) vs the 4.75% Nov-2025 floor [BI 2026]. Deposit beta rises through 2026-2027. Neobanks paying 8-9% promo rates on flexible deposits [Kira estimates from product disclosures] face spread compression first. Operators with cheap-funded parent banks (Cake-VPBank analog, Aladin-BSI sister linkage) hold structural advantage.

Inflation manageable, not benign

BI projects 2.62% headline inflation for 2026, inside the 2.5%±1% target band [BI 2026]. IMF flags rupiah depreciation pressure as the primary risk vector [IMF Nov 2025]. Imported-input cost-push remains the watch item for the next 12 months.

Section 04 · Macro Context (cont.)
008 / 026

Penetration is high, rural fixed-line is not

Indonesia hit 230 million internet users (80.5% penetration) in October 2025 [APJII 2025]. Mobile broadband subscriptions exceed 121 per 100 inhabitants [BPS 2025]. Fixed broadband sits at just ~21% of households, with the urban-rural delta over 40 percentage points [BPS 2025]. Mobile-first is not a strategy choice for digital banks — it is the only path.

Mobile leapfrogging is mature

Smartphone penetration sits in the high-70s nationally with strong rural skew via prepaid telco bundles [DataReportal 2026]. The expansion vector is not "more phones" but app-store ARPU and data quality. Telkomsel, Indosat and XL Axiata 4G coverage exceeds 95% population reach [Kira estimates from operator filings].

Rural infrastructure is the gap

SATRIA-1 satellite (mid-2023) targets 150,000 rural public-service points [BPS 2025]. Indonesia's 60/40 urban-rural split [BPS 2025] obscures provincial variance: NTT, Maluku and Papua remain < 60% internet-penetrated [Kira estimates]. Last-mile latency is the underwriter's real enemy here, not user appetite.

Payments rails are world-class

QRIS hit 13.66 bn transactions in 2025 (vs 6.5 bn target), with 59M users and 42M merchants [BI Stats 2026]. BI-FAST handled 414.6M transactions in July 2025 alone, +38% YoY, IDR 1 quadrillion total [BI Stats 2026]. Neobanks consume these rails as a public good — differentiation must come elsewhere.

SECTION 05 · CHAPTER TWO

Sizing & segment economics

The neobank revenue pool is small in absolute terms relative to incumbent assets but compounds at 25-30% annually. The interesting story is in the unit-economics dispersion: NIMs from 6% to 14%, CIRs from 50% to 90%, NPLs from 0.4% to 4%+. Aggregation hides the convergence pattern.

Section 05 · Sector Sizing
010 / 026

A USD 3-4 bn revenue pool, compounding at 25%

Indonesia hosts 17 licensed digital banks — the largest count in Southeast Asia [Nikkei 2025]. Aggregate customer deposits across the top seven players grew from USD 2.2 bn in 2021 to USD 6.2 bn in 2025 at a 30% CAGR [Redseer 2025]. Neobanking transaction value is projected to expand at a 9.6-14.3% CAGR depending on definition [Kira estimates]; revenue pool reaches USD 7-9 bn by 2031.

Three tiers of operator

Tier-1 (SeaBank, Bank Jago, Allo, Bank Neo Commerce) hold > 14M customers each [Kira estimates from operator filings]. Tier-2 (Superbank, Aladin, Krom) reaching 4-15M with strong ecosystem anchors. Tier-3 (Jenius BTPN, Blu BCA Digital, LINE Bank, TMRW) remain captive to parent-bank distribution. The middle compresses fastest — Tier-2 either accelerates via ecosystem deals or gets absorbed.

Deposit growth outruns lending

SeaBank DPK +20.6% YoY to IDR 28.32 trn H1-2025 [SeaBank 2026]. Bank Jago DPK +51% to IDR 22.4 trn [ARTO 2026]. Allo DPK +78% to IDR 8.78 trn Q3-2025 [BBHI 2026]. Loan books trail (LDR averaging 60-80%) which keeps treasury-yield as a meaningful contributor to current profit lines.

Section 06 · Segment Economics
011 / 026

NIM 14% looks rich, but credit risk is the offset

Per March 2026 OJK data, the digital-bank segment runs at 13.9% NIM versus the 4.5% industry average [OJK 2026]. This is structurally consumer-finance economics, not banking economics: high yield but elevated NPLs at Tier-2/Tier-3 players. The cohort splits in two: prime-data-rich (Jago, Allo) at 0.4-2% NPL; thin-file aggressive (BNC, Superbank) at 3-5%+ but with NIM > 10%.

NIM dispersion is wide

Bank Jago runs at ~10% NIM with 0.4% gross NPL [ARTO 2026], deploying GoTo ecosystem data for credit selection. Bank Neo Commerce sustains a higher headline yield but credit cost runs structurally above the segment [Kira estimates]. Allo and Aladin sit between — conservative books leaning on parent-conglomerate distribution.

CIR is the convergence metric

Bank Jago: 59% Q3-2025, target sub-30% in 4 years [Bank Jago Q3 2025]. Aladin BOPO from 119% to 85.16%; CIR from 137% to 73% [BANK 2026]. Sub-50% CIR is the structural winner threshold — matches Nubank's ~30% ratio observable at scale. Operators above 80% remain unit-economics fragile.

Cost-to-serve is the AI frontier

Nubank's USD 0.80/month/active cost-to-serve sits 85% below LatAm incumbents [Redseer 2025]. Indonesia equivalents range USD 1.50-3.00 [Kira estimates]. AI-driven KYC + customer-service automation is the realistic lever to close this delta over 2026-2028.

The thesis

SeaBank is the cleanest example of ecosystem-as-CAC in SEA digital banking. Direct integration with Shopee converts Indonesia's largest e-commerce installed base into bank-account opens at near-zero marginal acquisition cost. The bank's profitability for four consecutive years [SeaBank 2026] validates the model.

The numbers

FY-2025 net income IDR 678.4 bn, +79% YoY [SeaBank 2026]. Customer count from 13M (mid-2024) to 28M (YE-2025) — a near-doubling driven by Shopee peak-period promotions. Total assets IDR 44.4 trn, +28.5% YoY. Third-party funds IDR 34.8 trn — the largest deposit base among Indonesian digital banks.

Strategic position

  • CAC moat: Shopee installed base converts at app-store yields no rival matches.
  • Cross-sell vector: ShopeePay payments → SeaBank deposits → lending via Shopee SPayLater.
  • Geographic skew: Tier-2/3 cities over-indexed via Shopee logistics network.
  • Risk: 4y profitability built when ShopeePay growth was free funding — cycle is normalizing.

What to watch in 2026

The trajectory from 28M to 35M customers [Kira estimates] is the natural pause point: Java tier-1 saturation is near; rural acquisition becomes the marginal user. Cost-to-serve must compress further or net-add quality drops.

The CIR story

BOPO compressed from 118.75% to 85.16%; CIR from 136.71% to 73.27% [BANK 2026] — the most dramatic efficiency turnaround in segment. Partnership with Alfamart, Alfamidi, Lawson and DAN+DAN gives cash withdrawal at 20,000+ modern-retail outlets [BANK 2026].

Superbank (SUPA)

Backed by Grab, Emtek, Singtel, KakaoBank and GXS Bank. FY-2025 PBT IDR 143.3 bn, reversed from prior-year loss [Superbank 2026]. 6M customers, IDR 24 trn assets (+72% YoY), and the "Pinjaman Atur Sendiri" digital lending product showed 130%+ growth Dec-2024 to Jun-2025 [Superbank 2026]. Grab consolidates Superbank in 2026 after Singtel stake transfer.

Krom Bank (KCBI)

Subsidiary of Kredivo Group. Targets 20M accounts by 2030 [Mambu 2025]. Q3-2024 third-party funds reached IDR 2.22 trn, +541% YTD from IDR 347.5 bn the prior December [Krom 2024]. Offers up to 8.75% time-deposit rates — among the most aggressive in segment. Mambu cloud-banking stack renewal signals continued tech investment.

What to watch in 2026

Aladin's sharia angle differentiates in halal-conscious deposit segments; Superbank's Grab consolidation may unlock OVO × Superbank cross-sell at scale; Krom needs to convert its yield-led acquisition into stickier balances. All three are sub-scale to Tier-1 — ecosystem JVs or partnership deepening will define 2026-2027.

Rural acquisition: the unmet wedge

97 million unbanked Indonesians remain — the fourth-largest pool globally [World Bank 2024]. Mobile-first works for Java tier-1; tier-2/3 and the rural archipelago require physical agent overlays. BRI, BNI and Mandiri agent networks are the structural advantage no neobank can match without a JV.

Section 08 · Rural Acquisition

Why agent overlays beat pure-digital in rural Indonesia

Rural financial inclusion sits at 70% vs 78% in urban areas [OJK 2024]. The top two reasons Indonesians don't have bank accounts: "too far" (33%) and "too expensive" (32%) [OJK Inklusi 2024]. Pure-digital onboarding cannot resolve either reason in NTT, Maluku, Kalimantan Tengah or West Papua. Agent banking is the only proven bridge.

The agent-network reality

BRILink covers 81,616 villages across 34 provinces [BRI 2024]. BNI Agen46 reports 207,027 agents across 6,000+ sub-districts and 35,000 villages, including 15,000 in 3T (Frontier-Remote-Underdeveloped) zones [BNI 2024]. Mandiri Agen and BSI Laku Pandai add depth in Sumatra and East Java. Cumulative reach exceeds 300,000 physical touchpoints [Kira estimates].

What pure-digital cannot do

Cash-in/cash-out for daily-wage rural earners. KYC for users without smartphones (still ~15% of rural adults [Kira estimates from BPS 2025]). Trust-building for first-time bank-account holders. Bill-payment plus account-opening in one visit. The friction stack is physical, not digital.

The viable rural plays

Three patterns emerge. One: ecosystem-borrowed reach (Allo via Indomaret/Alfamart-style retail). Two: BRI/BNI/Mandiri agent JV with revenue share. Three: super-app physical extension via Grab/Gojek driver-as-agent. Standalone digital expansion into rural is uneconomic at current cost-to-serve.

Channel CAC tells the same story

Estimated CAC for a digital-only neobank ranges USD 8-12 in tier-1 Jakarta and USD 22-35 in rural eastern Indonesia [Kira estimates from operator channel mix]. Agent-banking partnerships drop the rural figure to USD 6-9 via revenue-share economics. The arithmetic determines who wins the next 30 million accounts.

CAC dispersion by channel

Super-app cross-sell (Shopee → SeaBank; GoTo → Bank Jago): USD 3-6 per acquired account, near zero marginal. Performance-marketing acquisition: USD 10-18 in tier-1 cities, USD 18-30 in tier-2/3. Pure-digital rural acquisition: USD 22-35 due to higher app uninstall rates and need for support-call follow-up [Kira estimates from industry channel benchmarks].

Agent JV economics

BRILink agents earn IDR 2,500-5,000 per transaction commission [BRI 2024]. A neobank entering a JV typically pays IDR 25,000-50,000 per fully-onboarded account [Kira estimates from industry deal practice]. At USD 6-9 fully-loaded that's competitive with tier-1 digital acquisition and dramatically below pure-digital rural.

The KYC overlay

OJK requires Dukcapil NIK + biometric liveness for new accounts [OJK 2024]. Pure-digital flows fail at 8-15% rate on rural connectivity [Kira estimates]. Agent-assisted KYC succeeds at 95%+. The economics multiply: agent JV not only acquires cheaper but converts more reliably.

Section 09 · Regulatory Landscape
021 / 026

POJK 12 raised the bar, POJK 3/2024 opens the sandbox

OJK Regulation 12/POJK.03/2021 sets the IDR 10 trillion minimum core capital for new digital banks (IDR 3 trn at in-principle stage; IDR 1 trn for bank-subsidiary digital) [POJK 12/2021]. POJK 3/2024 then expanded the ITSK regulatory sandbox covering alt-credit scoring, eKYC, robo-advisory and crypto-asset business models [POJK 3/2024]. OJK also stood up a dedicated Digital Banking Supervision Directorate effective 1 January 2026 [OJK 2026].

The capital floor matters

POJK 12 effectively closed the door on under-capitalized fintech-to-bank conversions. The IDR 10 trn (~USD 640M) requirement filters new entrants to either deep-pocketed parents (Sea, GoTo, CT Corp, Akulaku, Grab/Emtek, Kredivo) or M&A of small banks. No greenfield digital bank can clear this bar in 2026-2027 [Kira estimates] without significant external backing.

Sandbox is the regulatory wedge

Sandbox tenure is one year, extendable [POJK 3/2024]. Use cases approved include alt-data credit scoring, AI-based fraud detection, automated underwriting and embedded insurance. Cryptoasset oversight transferred from Bappebti to OJK in January 2025 [POJK 3/2024]. Sandbox graduates set the de-facto compliance standard for the segment.

Supervision tightens in 2026

The new OJK Digital Banking Directorate signals separate-track scrutiny: capital adequacy, AI governance, eKYC standards, NPL classification for thin-file consumer credit [OJK 2026]. Expect tighter disclosure rules and stress-testing requirements through 2026-2027. The regulator is professionalizing alongside the operators.

Forget the consumer-facing copilot narrative. AI's near-term ROI in Indonesian digital banking sits in three places: alternative-data credit scoring for thin-file users, real-time fraud and device-intel risk management, and cost-to-serve compression. Bank Jago publicly credits AI for part of its 15-pp CIR drop. The bar is opex payback, not vision.

AI is unit-economics infrastructure, not a feature

Indonesian neobanks deploy AI primarily for alt-data underwriting, fraud detection, and process automation. Estimated AI-attributable opex savings reached USD 80-120 million in 2025 across the top-6 [Kira estimates from operator filings + industry benchmarks]. The lift is in cost-to-serve and approval rates — not in headline customer features.

Underwriting is the highest-ROI surface

Tokopedia's SDB credit-scoring platform uses e-commerce behavior; Blibli's Vici Score combines credit and behavior; Amartha layers social-reputation and group-lending signals [GBG 2025]. Device-intel platforms (CredoLab pattern) lift credit + fraud risk protection up to +40% [Kira estimates from industry benchmarks].

Fraud is the second-most actionable

QRIS fraud rises with payment volume. Real-time scoring + device intelligence + behavioral biometrics layer the response. Sub-100ms decisioning is now table stakes for the segment.

Customer support is the third

Vernacular-LLM chat ops at scale — including Bahasa Indonesia, Javanese, Sundanese — cut support-cost per active by 30-50% at deployed peers [Kira estimates]. The translation problem is largely solved; the deployment-discipline problem is not.

Six AI applications reshaping Indonesian neobanks

Each use case is anchored to a real deployment or near-deployment pattern in the Indonesian market. Ordered by current ROI, not novelty — underwriting and fraud lead; chat and personalization trail.

01 · UNDERWRITE
Alt-data thin-file credit scoring
Telco records, e-commerce behavior, device metadata, social reputation. Lifts approval rates while holding NPL in the 0.4-2% band when paired with ecosystem data [Bank Jago Q3 2025].
Observable: Bank Jago + GoTo ecosystem data
02 · FRAUD
Real-time device-intel + behavioral biometrics
Sub-100ms scoring for QRIS, BI-FAST, and intra-bank transfers. Up to +40% combined credit + fraud uplift at deployed peers [Kira estimates from industry benchmarks].
Pattern: CredoLab-style device scorecards
03 · KYC
Liveness + Dukcapil NIK match
Mandatory under OJK eKYC framework [OJK 2024]. AI liveness blocks deepfake injection. The bottleneck in 2025-2026 is rural-connectivity success rates, not model accuracy.
Mandate: OJK eKYC under POJK 3/2024
04 · OPS
Automated back-office and reconciliation
RPA + LLM-assisted exception handling cuts ops headcount per transaction. Bank Jago publicly attributes part of CIR compression to AI ops integration [Bank Jago Q3 2025].
Disclosure: ARTO Q3-2025 CIR commentary
05 · CHAT
Vernacular customer-support LLM
Bahasa Indonesia primary; regional language secondary. Cuts support cost-per-active 30-50% when deployed with handoff discipline [Kira estimates]. The accuracy threshold is set by OJK consumer-protection rules.
Constraint: OJK explainability standard
06 · OFFER
Personalized product offers
Next-best-action targeting for deposit cross-sell, BNPL attach, insurance bolt-on. Lower ROI than the first four at current scale — revisit when active-user base exceeds 10M per bank [Kira estimates].
Watch: Allo cross-sell to CT Corp footprint

Section 11 · Outlook & Forecast
025 / 026

Five-year outlook: USD 7-9 bn revenue pool, three winners

Base case — revenue pool of digital banking in Indonesia grows from ~USD 3.5 bn in 2025 to USD 7.5 bn by 2031 at 13-14% CAGR [Kira estimates triangulated with Statista neobanking 9.62% CAGR + IFTS Outlook 2031]. Three operators capture 60%+ of incremental pool: SeaBank (Shopee), Bank Jago (GoTo), Allo (CT Corp). Mid-tier consolidates via M&A.

Three scenarios

Base case (60% prob.): 13-14% CAGR, top-3 reach 70% share. Tier-2 consolidates via M&A. Upside (25% prob.): rural agent JVs unlock the 30M+ unbanked layer faster than expected; revenue pool reaches USD 9 bn. Downside (15% prob.): credit cycle bites; NPL drift to 3-5% sector-wide; profitability compresses, pool flat at USD 5 bn by 2031.

Five strategic implications for 2026-2027

(1) Build or buy an agent-banking JV by end-2026. (2) Compress CIR below 50% within 36 months. (3) Pass through the ITSK sandbox at least once. (4) Anchor AI investment to disclosed opex savings, not features. (5) Build vernacular ops capacity for tier-2/3 expansion. Operators that miss two or more of these checkpoints become acquisition candidates by 2028.
