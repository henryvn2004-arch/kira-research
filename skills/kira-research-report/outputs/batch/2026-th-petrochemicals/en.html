A trough that forced the merger

Global ethylene capacity added ~40 Mt over 2020–2025 against ~27 Mt of demand, roughly 70% of it in China [S&P 2026]. NE Asia cracker spreads sit near USD 219/t, under the ~USD 250/t integrated breakeven [S&P 2026]. Thai sector sales value compressed to an estimated USD 15–16 bn in 2025 from ~USD 20 bn in 2023 [Kira estimates]. Scale, not survival, is now the strategic question — hence the April 2026 SCGC–GC olefins MoU.

From commodity tonnes to specialty value

PTT Global Chemical and SCGC are deliberately shifting from commodity olefins toward high-value-added lines — MMA, propylene oxide, acrylonitrile, performance polyolefins and recycled resin [GC-SCGC 2026]. GC's allnex integration lifted performance-chemicals EBITDA margin ~15 pp by 2025 [Kira estimates]. The specialty pivot is the margin story; consolidation is the cost story.

Section 04 · Macro Context
007 / 022

A steady demand backdrop against a global supply shock

Thai growth has held in the low-2% range while the binding constraint on the sector sits offshore — Chinese capacity, not Thai demand, sets the price.

Domestic demand: modest but positive

Real GDP grew 2.4% in 2025 with Q4 investment up 8.1% [NESDC 2026]. The planning council lifted its 2026 range to 1.5–2.5%, carried by consumption, tourism recovery toward ~35 m arrivals and a step-up in public capex [NESDC 2026]. Construction, automotive and packaging — the three demand layers for resin — all sit in slow expansion [Kira estimates].

The supply shock is the story

China's ethylene capacity reached ~66 Mt in 2025 from ~40 Mt in 2021, with another ~32 Mt slated through 2028 [S&P 2026]. A flood of Chinese resin into ASEAN has compressed utilisation and spreads region-wide [CEN 2026]. East Asia is now planning >13 Mt of cracker closures by 2027 to rebalance — the supply-side gate on any recovery [S&P 2026].

Feedstock and FX exposure

Thai crackers run mostly on naphtha; spreads track the naphtha–ethylene gap, and imported feedstock carries USD/THB exposure [Krungsri 2025]. The strategic counter is US ethane import optionality, now under active study by the majors [Krungsri 2025].

Section 05 · Sector Overview
009 / 022

World-scale capacity, a structural utilisation gap

Map Ta Phut clusters ~60 plants and ~37 Mt/yr of capacity [GTCC 2025]. The constraint is not nameplate — it is the gap between installed tonnes and through-cycle effective demand.

Installed base

The Map Ta Phut estate hosts ~60 major producers totalling ~37 Mt/yr, ranking among the world's top-tier petrochemical clusters [GTCC 2025]. GC alone runs ~8.2 Mt/yr of olefins and aromatics plus ~228 kbd of refining; SCGC's Rayong and Map Ta Phut olefins complexes add ~2.7–3.0 Mt/yr [GC TH 2025; SCGC 2025].

Output value compressed

KIRA sizes 2025 sector sales value at roughly USD 15–16 bn, down from ~USD 20 bn in 2023, as price — not volume — collapsed [Kira estimates]. Thai production still grew ~3% in volume in 2024 before a flat-to-soft 2025 [GTCC 2025].

The utilisation gate

With Chinese oversupply depressing export netbacks, effective run-rates sit well below nameplate — the visual story is the idle wedge, not the headline capacity [Kira estimates]. Closing that gap is what consolidation and the specialty pivot are for.

Section 08 · Demand & Channels
016 / 022

Resin flows shift as the value mix climbs

Roughly 55% of downstream output exports as resin or pellets [GTCC 2025]. The strategic move is to lift the high-value share of that flow — not to chase commodity volume into an oversupplied region.

Channel & export structure

Thailand is ASEAN's largest producer and the world's third-largest bioplastics maker [OECD 2025]. About 55% of downstream goes to export, much as PE/PP resin and pellets to regional converters [GTCC 2025]. ASEAN frameworks anchor packaging-resin supply to Cambodia and Vietnam; US-China tariff arbitrage lifted early-2025 US shipments ~62% [Argus 2025].

End-use demand pull

Packaging, automotive and construction anchor PE/PP demand; traditional resins held ~71% of the domestic plastics mix in 2025 on entrenched scale and end-use breadth [Argus 2025]. The demand layers are steady — the threat is import substitution by cheap Chinese resin, not weak local appetite [CEN 2026].

The specialty migration

HVA and specialty grades are an estimated ~29% of value today and rising as MMA, PO, acrylonitrile, bio-PE and recycled resin scale [Kira estimates]. ENVICCO (GC×ALPLA) runs Thailand's largest food-grade recycling plant at ~45 kt/yr [GC Sustain 2025].

Section 09 · Regulatory & Corridor
017 / 022

The corridor underwrites scale; competition policy gates the merger

EEC incentives and Map Ta Phut Port Phase 3 lower the cost of building at scale, while the Trade Competition Commission becomes the decisive approval gate for the proposed olefins JV.

EEC & investment incentives

The Eastern Economic Corridor (Chonburi, Rayong, Chachoengsao) has drawn ~USD 56 bn of FDI in five years, targeting ~USD 65 bn by 2028 [BOI 2025]. Petrochemical investors can access up to 15-year corporate-income-tax exemption, duty-free raw-material imports and long land leases [BOI 2025]. H1 2025 alone saw 172 chemical/petrochemical FDI projects worth ~USD 844 m, up ~12% YoY [GTCC 2025].

Map Ta Phut Port Phase 3

Under construction since 2023, the port expansion adds capacity to handle ~31 Mt/yr of gas and liquid feedstock, due operational in 2027 — logistics backbone for the next feedstock wave, including US ethane optionality [GTCC 2025; Krungsri 2025].

Capacity & competition policy

Government sought voluntary capacity-rationalisation plans from producers by end-2025 in exchange for state financial support — an unusual managed-restructuring lever [Krungsri 2025]. Any SCGC–GC tie-up is explicitly subject to Trade Competition Commission review [GC-SCGC 2026].

Section 10 · AI Impact
019 / 022

AI in Thai petrochemicals: margin in the operating layer

Digital twins and AI-driven reliability are the highest-immediate-impact moves for asset-heavy producers — basis-points of yield and reliability that compound at trough spreads.

Where AI pays first

Predictive maintenance and digital twins are the near-term leaders — reducing unplanned downtime by a benchmarked 30–50% and lifting throughput ~15% in deployed plants [HC Eng 2025].

Adoption posture

Digital twins matured in 2025 into integrated, AI-driven plant replicas covering maintenance, inspections and shift handovers [HC Eng 2025]. For Thai majors the logic is sharper at trough margins: every point of yield or reliability is the difference between clearing and missing breakeven [Kira estimates].

Boundary

AI is an operating-margin lever, not a demand or feedstock fix — it complements consolidation and the specialty pivot rather than substituting for them [Kira estimates].

020 / 022

Six AI applications reshaping Thai petrochemical operations

Operational, asset-level use cases ranked by near-term impact for integrated producers running at trough margins.

01 · RELIABILITY
Predictive maintenance on crackers
ML on furnace, compressor and reactor sensor streams flags failure precursors before they trip the line — the single biggest reliability lever in a continuous-process plant.
Benchmark: 30–50% unplanned downtime cut
02 · YIELD
Furnace & reactor yield optimisation
Real-time set-point optimisation on cracking severity and separation lifts olefin yield and cuts energy per tonne in an energy-intensive operation.
Benchmark: 2–5% efficiency on the table
03 · ENERGY
Energy & emissions management
AI balances steam, power and heat across the integrated site, trimming the energy bill that is 2–5 pp of margin — and feeding the net-zero roadmap.
Pilot: site-wide utility optimisation
04 · QUALITY
Computer-vision QC on grades
Vision models inspect pellet and film quality inline, replacing sampling — critical as portfolios tilt to spec-sensitive HVA grades.
Use case: medical & food-grade resin QC
05 · SUPPLY
Demand sensing & feedstock
AI matches order-book signals to distributor stock and optimises naphtha-vs-ethane feedstock choice against spreads and FX.
Use case: feedstock-flex optimisation
06 · NETWORK
Distributor & logistics optimisation
Route, allocation and inventory optimisation across the resin export network — lowering working capital as ~55% of output ships abroad.
Use case: regional resin allocation

Section 11 · 5-Year Outlook
021 / 022

A demand-led recovery, gated by supply discipline

The base case sees Thai sector value recover toward ~USD 22 bn by 2030 as regional closures, consolidation and the specialty mix reset margins — but profitability normalisation likely extends into the early 2030s.

Base case

Capacity build slows post-2026 and the sector pivots to demand-led recovery [S&P 2026]. KIRA models sector sales value recovering from ~USD 15 bn (2025) toward ~USD 22 bn by 2030 on volume growth, a richer HVA mix and gradual spread repair [Kira estimates].

Bull

Faster East-Asian closures (>13 Mt by 2027) plus a completed SCGC–GC JV tighten supply and lift utilisation — value toward ~USD 24.5 bn with the JV capturing feedstock and scale synergies [Kira estimates].

Bear

Persistent Chinese exports, a stalled JV (TCCT or due-diligence) and weak spreads cap recovery near ~USD 17.5 bn — a prolonged trough that strands merchant converters [Kira estimates].
