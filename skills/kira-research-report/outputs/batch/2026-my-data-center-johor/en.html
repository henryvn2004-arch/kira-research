Johor's data centre market at the cross-border inflection

A land-and-power-constrained Singapore is exporting demand across the strait, and the January 2025 JS-SEZ pact has lowered the cost of co-locating there. The build is now power-gated, not demand-gated — capacity is the scarce asset.

A demand spillover, not a domestic boom

Johor's surge is fundamentally Singapore's overflow. A 2019 moratorium and tight green rules capped the island; even the December 2025 DC-CFA2 call releases only ~200 MW there [IMDA 2025]. Johor, 16 km away with sub-5 ms latency and 20+ subsea cables, absorbs the workloads Singapore cannot site [Lightreading 2025]. Live Johor supply grew ~145% a year over 2019-2024 [Arizton 2025].

The pact reset the cost of co-location

The January 2025 JS-SEZ pact pairs Singapore connectivity with Malaysian land, power and a 5% corporate tax rate for up to 15 years plus a 100% investment tax allowance [MIDA 2025]. In 9M 2025, JS-SEZ projects were 74.6% of Johor's RM91.1 bn approved investment [MIDA 2025].

Section 05 · The Demand Engine
007 / 016

Singapore's ceiling is Johor's demand floor

A multi-year moratorium and a green-DC regime hold Singapore's net new supply to a trickle. The workloads still need to land within milliseconds of the island's connectivity — and Johor is the only adjacent site that qualifies.

A capped island

Singapore froze new data centre projects in 2019 on energy and water grounds [DCD 2025]. The Green DC Roadmap reopened growth only conditionally, targeting at least 300 MW of additional near-term capacity through efficiency and green energy [IMDA 2025]. The December 2025 DC-CFA2 call allocates ~200 MW more, but only to operators meeting strict PUE and 50%-green thresholds [IMDA 2025].

The overflow valve

Land scarcity and planning controls pushed hyperscalers to alternatives — Johor and Batam first among them [DCD 2025]. By end-2026, Johor's projected power draw of 2,770-3,000 MW is more than twice Singapore's data centre load [Fulcrum 2025].

Why 16 km matters

Johor holds sub-5 ms latency to Singapore and access to 20+ international subsea cables [Lightreading 2025]. For latency-tolerant training and storage, the strait is a rounding error; the connectivity premium stays in Singapore while the megawatts move north.

Section 06 · Capacity Build-out
008 / 016

Reading the Johor capacity ladder

Headline pipeline figures conflate live, contracted and speculative megawatts. The disciplined read separates what is energised today from what is merely announced — the gap is where execution risk lives.

Live versus announced

Malaysia's operational capacity doubles to 2,055 MW by end-2026 [JLL 2026], and Johor is the dominant share — more than 60% of national power capacity [Lightreading 2025]. But the November 2025 Johor stack reads ~4.0 GW of upcoming capacity with only 700 MW under construction and 3.3 GW still planned or announced [DCD 2025].

The cluster geography

Capacity concentrates in four parks — Nusajaya Tech Park, Sedenak Tech Park, the YTL green campus and Nusa Cemerlang [DCD 2025]. Sedenak alone, a revitalised 1,400-acre former plantation, anchors the new hyperscale wave [DCD 2025].

Growth base effect

Johor live supply compounded at roughly 145% a year over 2019-2024 [Arizton 2025]. That base effect flatters headline growth; the durable signal is contracted MW with secured grid connection, which we estimate near 1.5-1.8 GW [Kira estimates].

Section 07 · Where Capital Lands
010 / 016

The JS-SEZ co-investment mechanics

Signed January 2025, the zone pairs Singapore's capital and connectivity with Malaysian land and power under a shared incentive frame. The result: data centres became the flagship use case for cross-border capital, and the numbers followed fast.

The incentive stack

Eligible companies access a 5% corporate tax rate for up to 15 years, a flat 15% personal rate on chargeable employment income for 10 years, a 100% investment tax allowance on qualifying capex, and stamp-duty exemptions [MIDA 2025]. Applications run to MIDA from January 2025 through 31 December 2034 [MIDA 2025].

Frictionless siting

The Invest Malaysia Facilitation Centre-Johor has run since February 2025 as a one-stop permit desk, with manufacturing licences inside the zone obtainable in seven days [MIDA 2025]. The zone spans 3,288 km² across nine flagship areas — nearly five times Singapore's footprint [JLL 2025].

Capital responded

In 9M 2025 Johor drew RM91.1 bn of approved investment, of which JS-SEZ projects were RM68 bn — 74.6% of the total [MIDA 2025]. Data centres and electronics are the named beneficiary sectors [PwC 2025].

Section 09 · The Binding Constraint
013 / 016

Power, water and the new gating rules

Every megawatt of compute is a megawatt the grid must deliver and a flow the water table must sustain. The state has moved from courting volume to rationing it on efficiency — reshaping which projects clear.

The grid is the queue

The utility has received data centre applications exceeding 11,000 MW — over 40% of Peninsular Malaysia's ~27,000 MW installed generation [TNB 2025]. It plans capex of RM42.8 bn for 2025-2027, more than double the prior period, and targets 5,000 MW of data centre supply by 2035 [TNB 2025]. The Green Lane cuts energisation to ~12 months [TNB 2025].

Energy is the swing cost

The Peninsular base tariff stepped up from 39.96 to 45.62 sen/kWh for the RP4 window (Jul 2025-Dec 2026) [TNB 2025]. CRESS, live since September 2024, lets operators draw renewable power directly across the grid — the practical route to the green mandate [Reccessary 2025].

Water as the second gate

The regulator flags national water shortage risk within five years [Foe-Malaysia 2025]. Johor now requires green energy for new data centres and screens water-use efficiency [Reccessary 2025]; incentives since December 2024 are gated on PUE, CUE and WUE [Reccessary 2025].

Section 10 · The AI Workload Pivot
014 / 016

From colocation overflow to sovereign compute

Johor's first wave was latency-tolerant Singapore overflow. The second wave is GPU-dense AI training and sovereign-compute campuses — denser, hotter, and harder to power, but structurally higher-value.

The density shift

A live 600 MW accelerated cluster in Kulai, built with a leading GPU vendor and YTL Power, deploys rack-scale NVL72-class systems for large-model training and inference [Converge 2026]. A second developer is building a 510 MW all-GPU campus phased across successive accelerator generations, a RM26.6 bn (~USD 6.7 bn) programme [w.media 2026].

The connectivity dividend

AI-ready campuses in Iskandar Puteri sit ~16 km from Singapore with sub-5 ms latency and direct subsea-cable access [Lightreading 2026]. The TM Nxera campus targets first commercial operations in 2H 2026 [Lightreading 2026].

The value re-rating

AI workloads command higher revenue per MW than vanilla colocation [Kira estimates]. The competitive axis moves to power density and cooling, where new builds out-design legacy stock.
