How we built this view

This view is built from two evidence streams: primary inputs — seller conversations, marketplace category reads, regional fulfilment checks — and secondary anchors — BPS, Kementerian Perdagangan, Bank Indonesia, listed-platform filings and regulator releases. Every quantitative claim carries an explicit source tag.

Primary inputs

Seller and category reads
SKU-level rate checks across F&B, fashion, electronics and beauty categories on Shopee, Tokopedia, Lazada and TikTok Shop; surfaces real take-rate ladders, ad-budget share and live-commerce conversion bands.
Operator conversations
Discussions with platform commercial teams, third-party logistics (J&T, SPX, Anteraja, Ninja Van) and Tier-2 city fulfilment hubs in Surabaya, Medan, Bandung and Makassar; cross-read against BPS regional GDP releases.
Analyst synthesis
KIRA in-house triangulation across BPS national accounts, Bank Indonesia retail surveys, Sea Group / GoTo / Lazada parent disclosures, and Kementerian Perdagangan regulatory bulletins. Tagged inline as [Kira estimates].

Secondary anchors

Statistical authorities

Listed-operator filings

Triangulated estimates

Permendag 31 reshaped the stack

Kemendag's Permendag 31/2023 [Kemendag 2023] forced a structural separation of social media and electronic transactions, shutting down TikTok Shop's in-app checkout in October 2023. Bytedance's December-2023 acquisition of a controlling stake in Tokopedia [GoTo IR 2024] put the live-commerce engine back online inside a licensed e-commerce shell. Shopee, TikTok-Tokopedia and Lazada together now hold >80% of GMV [Kira estimates from platform disclosures].

Tier-2 cities carry the next leg of growth

Jabodetabek's e-commerce penetration is approaching saturation; Tier-2 city GMV runs ~USD 46bn in 2025 [Kira estimates] and heads to ~USD 77bn by 2030 [Kira estimates from Tech Collective + warehouse demand reads]. Surabaya, Bandung, Medan and Makassar each grow their middle-class base at 7-8% annually [Hadirr 2025], with Makassar leading at 8.5% [Hadirr 2025].

Five strategic implications for the next 24 months

Operators should treat 2026 through 2027 as the structural decision window, not the 2030 master-plan target. Platform commitments, Tier-2 fulfilment positions and creator-affiliate share secured now lock in unit economics through the end of the decade.

IMPLICATION · 01
Treat the TikTok-Tokopedia stack as one demand surface
Live commerce now contributes ~20% of SEA e-commerce GMV [e-Conomy SEA 2025], up from <5% in 2022. Bytedance routed TikTok demand into the licensed Tokopedia shell post-Permendag 31; treat the two front-ends as one P&L for ad spend, inventory and creator contracts.
~20% live-commerce share
IMPLICATION · 02
Build Tier-2 fulfilment ahead of the cost-share window
Tier-2 cities like Semarang, Palembang and Balikpapan are forecast to grow at 11.45% CAGR [Tri Mulia BP 2025], with warehouse rents 45-60% below Jakarta [Tri Mulia BP 2025]. Surabaya, Bandung, Medan already absorb 58.8% of e-commerce logistics volumes [Kira estimates from logistics-market reads]; locking sites pre-2027 fixes the cost base.
11.45% Tier-2 logistics CAGR
IMPLICATION · 03
Defend take-rate through affiliate creator depth
TikTok Shop has engaged ~8m affiliate creators [Bytedance 2025] in Indonesia, and 980k of 1.1m influencers are nano-tier (1k-25k) [Marketing LTB 2026]. Affiliate-influenced GMV is now 10-20% of leading-marketplace GMV [Cube Asia 2025]. The depth of the creator graph — not headline ad spend — sets the take-rate ceiling.
~8m affiliate creators
IMPLICATION · 04
Anticipate Permendag 31 enforcement creep
Permendag 31/2023 bans foreign-merchant sales below USD 100 [Kemendag 2023] and forbids social platforms from running in-app transactions [Kemendag 2023]. Enforcement has been uneven; a 2026-27 tightening cycle would re-shock the TikTok-Tokopedia structure. Plan for stricter KYC, local-sourcing screens and rupiah-only settlement rails.
USD 100 foreign-merchant floor
IMPLICATION · 05
Yield-per-customer beats raw user growth
Indonesia's 278m population [Indocham 2025] is largely already on a smartphone, with penetration >70% [Indocham 2025]. The next leg of value is ARPU expansion, not net new users. Live commerce conversion of 4-7% [Indonesianlantern 2024] beats regular e-commerce's 1-2% — channel mix beats audience growth.

Macro indicators driving e-commerce demand

Indonesia's 278m-person economy expands at ~5% through the forecast period [IMF WEO 2026], anchoring household formation and digital-spend capacity. The digital economy reaches USD 99bn GMV in 2025 [e-Conomy SEA 2025], with e-commerce contributing ~72% [Databoks 2025].

Growth pace

Real GDP growth printed 5.0% in 2024 and is forecast at 5.1-5.2% through 2027 [IMF WEO 2026]. Household final consumption sits at ~55% of GDP [BPS 2026], with discretionary spending lifting fastest in households earning IDR 5-15m/month — the e-commerce sweet spot. Digital-economy GMV growth of ~10-12% YoY [e-Conomy SEA 2025] outpaces nominal GDP.

Currency & payment rails

IDR/USD sits near 16,200 in Q1 2026 with CPI tracking 2.8% [BPS 2026]. Bank Indonesia's QRIS rails crossed 50m users in 2025 [BI 2026], with daily transaction value above IDR 2tn. QRIS-on-checkout has compressed cash-on-delivery from ~40% of e-commerce orders in 2022 to ~22% in 2025 [Kira estimates].

Smartphone & internet base

Internet penetration reached ~79% in 2025 [Kominfo 2025] with smartphone penetration above 70% [Indocham 2025]. The Gen-Z and Millennial cohort — ~55% of total population [BPS 2025] — carries the highest e-commerce per-capita spend and the fastest live-commerce adoption.

Section 04 · Macro Context
008 / 028

Policy timeline: from PP 80/2019 to the social-commerce reset

Indonesia's e-commerce policy ladder runs on three rungs: PP 80/2019 as the overarching e-commerce law [Trade.gov 2025], Permendag 31/2023 as the social-commerce reset [Kemendag 2023], and the digital-economy roadmap targeting USD 130bn by 2030 [Kominfo 2024]. The 2027 enforcement cycle is the next material policy event.

Foundational law

Government Regulation No. 80/2019 (PP 80/2019) is the overarching e-commerce law [Trade.gov 2025], covering consumer protection, electronic contracts, liability and tax compliance. It defines the licensed marketplace operator regime — the structural enabler of the post-2019 GMV climb.

Social-commerce reset

Permendag 31/2023, issued September 2023 [Kemendag 2023], banned in-app transactions on social platforms and imposed a USD 100 floor on foreign-merchant cross-border sales. TikTok Shop's in-app checkout closed October 4, 2023 [KR Asia 2023]. Bytedance's December-2023 majority stake in Tokopedia [GoTo IR 2024] re-routed the live-commerce engine through a licensed marketplace.

2027 enforcement watch

The Prabowo administration signalled tighter enforcement in 2025-26 on cross-border imports below USD 100, predatory pricing screens and tax-compliance audits. Operators should plan for stricter KYC, local-sourcing screens and rupiah-only settlement rails through 2027 [Kira estimates from Kemendag press cycle].

Market size & growth: Indonesia e-commerce 2022-2030

Indonesia e-commerce GMV runs from USD 59bn in 2022 to USD 120bn by 2030, a ~9-10% CAGR base case [Kira estimates anchored on e-Conomy SEA 2025 + BPS 2026]. The 2030 upper-band range of USD 220-360bn [e-Conomy SEA 2025] is achievable only if Tier-2/3 penetration matches Jabodetabek's current depth.

Total GMV trajectory

GMV ran from USD 59bn in 2022 [e-Conomy SEA 2025] to USD 71bn in 2025 [e-Conomy SEA 2025] — ~10% CAGR. Sellercraft's industry read puts 2025 GMV at USD 73-75bn [Sellercraft 2025], with the wider Indonesia e-commerce revenue base nearer USD 68bn [Statista 2025]. Our base case lands USD 102bn GMV by 2028 and USD 120bn by 2030 [Kira estimates].

Sub-segment composition

Marketplace e-commerce (Shopee, Tokopedia, Lazada): ~75% of GMV [Kira estimates]. Social commerce post-reset (TikTok Shop under Tokopedia, Instagram-led DM commerce): ~15% [Kira estimates]. Direct-to-consumer (D2C) brand sites + Blibli + Bukalapak: ~10% [Kira estimates]. The marketplace share is structurally rising as social-commerce flows route through licensed platforms.

Live commerce sub-segment

Live commerce now represents ~20% of SEA e-commerce GMV [e-Conomy SEA 2025], up from <5% in 2022. Indonesia is the structural anchor — 93% of Indonesian online shoppers have participated in live shopping [Indonesianlantern 2024], with 56% having purchased through it.

Section 05 · Sector Sizing
011 / 028

Category mix: fashion leads, beauty & F&B accelerate

Category mix tells a different story from total GMV. Fashion is the largest category at ~28% of GMV [Kira estimates from platform category reads], but beauty & personal care and F&B / groceries grow fastest as live commerce penetrates the lower-AOV categories.

Top categories 2025

Fashion + apparel: ~USD 20bn GMV, ~28% share [Kira estimates]. Electronics + appliances: ~USD 14bn, 20%. Beauty + personal care: ~USD 8bn, 11%. F&B + groceries: ~USD 7bn, 10%. Home + living: ~USD 6bn, 8%. The fashion category leads on volume; beauty + F&B lead on growth rate, both compounding above 15% YoY [Kira estimates].

AOV trajectory

Average order value (AOV) sits at ~USD 18 across marketplaces in 2025 [Kira estimates from category-rate reads], rising to ~USD 22 by 2027. Live-commerce AOV runs lower (~USD 12) but session frequency offsets — the channel mix decides per-customer monthly contribution, not single-basket size.

Live-commerce penetration

Live commerce has gone from a marketing channel to a primary commerce surface. 56% of Indonesian shoppers report having purchased during live streams [Indonesianlantern 2024]; conversion runs 4.3-7.2% depending on platform [Indonesianlantern 2024] versus 1-2% on regular product pages. The fastest-growing category surface in 2027 is beauty + F&B live shows.

Section 06 · Segment Economics
012 / 028

Channel economics: marketplace anchors, social runs hotter

The four-channel architecture — marketplace, social commerce, D2C and quick-commerce — converges on a single logistics spine but diverges sharply on take-rate, ad-spend ratio and seller economics. Take-rates run 6-9% across marketplaces, 8-15% on social commerce [Kira estimates].

Marketplace economics

Shopee, Tokopedia and Lazada operate take-rates in the 6-9% band [Kira estimates from Sea Limited + GoTo disclosures] including transaction fees, ad-product spend and shipping commissions. Sea Limited has guided Shopee toward sustained profitability since 2023, with Indonesia the highest-contribution market [Sea Limited IR 2025]. Lazada's parent Alibaba absorbs SEA losses inside a broader portfolio [Alibaba 2025].

Social-commerce economics

TikTok Shop's commission + ad-spend stack runs materially higher than pure-play marketplaces — sellers using live shopping reported ~7x sales growth vs static listings [Bytedance 2025], driving willingness to absorb 8-15% effective take-rate. Affiliate creators take a further 5-20% of attributed sales [Cube Asia 2025].

D2C & brand sites

D2C brand sites (Erigo, Mahoni, Wardah's own channel) capture <5% of category GMV but at structurally higher margins — no marketplace take-rate, lower ad-spend ratio, and full customer-data ownership. Brand independence is rising as marketplace take-rates compress.

Section 06 · Segment Economics
013 / 028

Tier-2 unit economics: lower AOV, lower CAC, higher repeat

Tier-2 buyer economics diverge from Jabodetabek at every layer. AOV runs ~25% below national average [Kira estimates from category-rate reads], but customer-acquisition cost runs ~40% lower thanks to lower paid-search competition. Repeat-purchase rates run materially higher once first-order delivery is on time.

AOV & basket composition

Tier-2 AOV: ~USD 14 vs national USD 18 [Kira estimates] — lower-tier discretionary basket weighted toward fashion (lower ASP), beauty essentials, and FMCG re-orders. Electronics share is materially lower at Tier-2 vs Jabodetabek — the high-ticket discretionary spend stays in Tier-1.

Acquisition economics

Paid-acquisition costs run ~40% lower in Tier-2 cities [Kira estimates] thanks to thinner ad-platform competition. Organic-share is structurally higher at Tier-2 — live-commerce video-first acquisition substitutes for paid search where shoppers anchor on familiar creators rather than search queries.

Fulfilment cost line

Tier-2 fulfilment unit-cost runs ~25-35% below Tier-1 in-region [Kira estimates from warehouse-rent + last-mile reads] thanks to warehouse rents 45-60% below Jakarta [Tri Mulia BP 2025], lower labour cost, and shorter last-mile loops in cities like Yogyakarta, Solo and Malang. Cross-island delivery to Sumatra and Sulawesi reverses the math — logistics is the structural unit-economics decider.

What they do

Shopee operates Indonesia's largest e-commerce marketplace alongside SPX Express (last-mile + warehousing), ShopeePay (e-money), ShopeeFood (quick-commerce) and ShopeeLive (live commerce). The vertical-integration thesis is the defining structural advantage in SEA — Shopee captures rail share, not just transaction share.

Indonesia is Shopee's largest single market by GMV and the strongest profitability contributor in Sea's portfolio [Sea Limited IR 2025]. The Indonesia P&L crossed sustained EBITDA-positive in 2024 and held through 2025.

Recent moves

ShopeeLive aggressive scale-up to challenge TikTok-Tokopedia on creator graph + live-commerce conversion. SPX Express network expansion into Tier-2 city fulfilment centres through 2025-26 [Kira estimates from warehouse-demand reads]. Cross-border merchant filtering tightened post-Permendag 31 to comply with the USD 100 foreign-merchant floor.

Strategic positioning

Shopee's competitive moat is logistics integration + payment rail. SPX Express controls cost on the last-mile leg where competitors rely on third-party 3PLs (J&T, Anteraja, Ninja Van); ShopeePay closes the wallet loop. The structural unit-cost advantage compounds as Tier-2 fulfilment density rises.

  • Singapore-parent perception: Permendag enforcement creep on "foreign merchant" definitions could touch the parent structure

Section 09 · Regulatory
024 / 028

Regulatory landscape: foundation, reset, enforcement

Indonesia's e-commerce stack runs on three regulatory pillars: PP 80/2019 sets the marketplace operator licensing regime [Trade.gov 2025], Permendag 31/2023 separates social media from electronic transactions and imposes a USD 100 foreign-merchant floor [Kemendag 2023], and the 2024-26 enforcement cycle tightens tax-compliance audits and cross-border KYC.

PP 80/2019 — the foundation

Government Regulation 80/2019 is the overarching e-commerce law [Trade.gov 2025], covering consumer protection, electronic contracts, operator liability and tax compliance. It defines the licensed marketplace operator regime — structurally why post-2019 GMV climbed via Shopee, Tokopedia, Lazada and Blibli rather than diffuse social-commerce pages.

Permendag 31/2023 — the reset

Permendag 31/2023 (September 2023) banned in-app transactions on social-media platforms [Kemendag 2023] and imposed a USD 100 minimum on foreign-merchant cross-border products [Trade.gov 2025]. TikTok Shop's in-app checkout closed October 4, 2023 [KR Asia 2023]; the partnership with Tokopedia (December 2023) re-routed the live-commerce engine through a licensed marketplace.

Enforcement creep

2024-26 enforcement themes: predatory-pricing screens, cross-border KYC, tax-compliance audits and local-sourcing requirements. The Prabowo administration has signalled tighter enforcement on cross-border imports below USD 100, audits on platform-VAT collection and stricter merchant-licensing for foreign-owned platforms. Plan for 2027 cycle to materially shape platform economics.

5-year outlook: base, bull and bear

Our base case puts Indonesia e-commerce GMV at USD 120bn by 2030 [Kira estimates] from USD 71bn in 2025 [e-Conomy SEA 2025]. The bull case (+15%) hinges on Tier-2/3 penetration matching Jabodetabek's current depth; the bear case (-12%) on Permendag enforcement creep and a TikTok-Tokopedia structural re-shaping.

Base case · USD 120bn GMV by 2030

GMV: USD 71bn (2025) → USD 120bn by 2030 [Kira estimates] at ~11% CAGR. Marketplace mix holds ~70%; live commerce climbs to ~30% of total. Tier-2/3 GMV rises USD 46bn → USD 77bn [Kira estimates]. The base case requires Permendag enforcement to remain status-quo and platform unit economics to stay constructive.

Bull case · USD 138bn GMV (+15%)
