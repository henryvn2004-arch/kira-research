Two diverging signals for 2027

Vietnam's coffee sector delivered a record export year in 2025 — USD 8.92 bn in revenue on roughly 1.59 million tonnes shipped [MARD 2026], driven by robusta prices that surged to multi-decade highs as global supply tightened. That tailwind is unwinding. Vietnamese export prices are forecast to retrace 10–15% in 2026 and ease a further 5% by 2027 [Kira estimates based on ICO and USDA forward curves], as Vietnam's own supply expansion and a Brazilian recovery in arabica both ease the global deficit.

The durable structural shift

Volume growth is real and sustained: 32.5 million 60-kg bags are forecast for 2026/27 [USDA GAIN 2026], an area anchored by 730,000 hectares of planted coffee across the Central Highlands [GSO 2025]. More significant is the mix shift — processed and value-added exports crossed USD 1 bn for the first time in 2025 [Kira estimates]. Specialty arabica from Lam Dong is attracting GI recognition and micro-lot premiums. The instant coffee segment is forecast at USD 703.8 million by 2027 [Kira estimates based on sector CAGR].

Section 01 — Market sizing
05 / 16
Volume is the anchor; unit value is the variable.
Vietnam produced 31.7 million bags in 2025/26 [USDA GAIN 2026] and is forecast at 32.5 million bags in 2026/27 — driven by area expansion rather than yield improvement, with El Niño weather risk the principal downside.

Total market trajectory

The Vietnam coffee market reached USD 4.3 bn in 2025 and is forecast to compound at 6.99% CAGR through 2035 to reach USD 8.5 bn [Kira estimates based on MARD and trade data]. The 2027 mid-point sits near USD 5.0–5.3 bn [Kira estimates] — export value declines partially offset by domestic market expansion and processed-product premiums. The instant coffee sub-segment, at USD 545 m in 2024 [Kira estimates], targets USD 703.8 m by 2027 at a 12.9% CAGR as urban convenience demand intensifies.

Production volume: area-led expansion

USDA revised 2025/26 production to 31.7 million bags [USDA GAIN 2026], up 9.3% from 29 million bags in 2024/25, and forecasts a further rise to 32.5 million bags in 2026/27. This reflects 730,000 hectares of planted area [GSO 2025], with 92% harvestable. However, the replanting programme is running behind target — only 74,500 of a planned 91,000 hectares have been renewed — meaning aging trees in marginal growing zones temper yield upside.

Domestic consumption: structural, not cyclical

Domestic consumption reached 4.9 million bags in 2025/26 [USDA GAIN 2026], with our analysts forecasting 5.0–5.2 million bags by 2027 [Kira estimates]. Per capita consumption has approximately doubled since 2015 to ~3 kg annually [MARD 2026], driven by café proliferation, rising household incomes, and a young median demographic. On-trade channels (cafés, hotels) are the fastest-growing sub-channel, expanding at 6.75% CAGR [Kira estimates].

Weather risk: El Niño caveat

The USDA flags El Niño probability between June and August 2026, with below-normal rainfall already recorded in Dak Lak, Gia Lai, and Lam Dong in Q1 2026 [USDA GAIN 2026]. Below-trend rains during critical flowering and cherry-development phases could compress actual 2026/27 harvest by 5–8% against the base forecast [Kira estimates] — a material swing given Vietnam's role as the world's top robusta supplier.

Section 03 — Export market dynamics
09 / 16
Record revenue in 2025 was price-led; 2027 is a volume story with tighter margins.
Vietnam exported 1.59 million tonnes for USD 8.92 bn in 2025 [MARD 2026] — a 58.8% revenue surge driven by historically high robusta prices. USDA forecasts 27.3 million bags of exports in 2025/26 (+8% YoY). The 2027 trajectory moderates in value as supply normalizes.

Germany: re-export gateway to Europe

Germany is Vietnam's largest single export destination, functioning not primarily as a consuming market but as Europe's primary roasting and re-export hub [VICOFA 2025]. EU buyers operating through German logistics use Vietnam's robusta as a foundational blend component for espresso and RTD segments. EUDR compliance is bifurcating the supplier landscape — exporters with plot-level GPS certification are receiving premium contract extensions; those without face renegotiation pressure from 2026.

United States: robusta as a blend anchor

Rising arabica prices have pushed US roasters to increase robusta incorporation in blends, and Vietnam has been the primary beneficiary of that substitution dynamic [USDA FAS 2025]. The RTD and instant sub-segments are the dominant volume drivers, with major US beverage companies relying on Vietnamese robusta for flavor stability at scale. Our analysts expect US demand to remain structurally elevated even as arabica supply recovers, given established blend reformulations.

Japan: processed coffee and specialty upside

Japan was Vietnam's second-largest coffee supplier in January 2026, taking 9,100 tonnes worth USD 42.7 m in that month alone [MOF Japan 2026]. Robusta accounted for 63% of export value to Japan [Vietnam.vn 2026], but the processed segment is accelerating. Japanese buyers are actively seeking higher-value freeze-dried and specialty arabica lots from Lam Dong, where Geographical Indication status awarded in October 2025 strengthens origin marketing.

Price outlook: normalising, not collapsing

Export unit prices are forecast to fall 10–15% in 2026 versus 2025 peaks, and a further ~5% in 2027 as Colombian arabica recovery eases global supply pressure and Vietnam's own expanded robusta crop reaches market [Kira estimates based on ICO and USDA forward data]. The base scenario yields a 2027 export revenue of approximately USD 7.7 bn [Kira estimates] — still historically elevated, roughly 73% above the 2023 level.

Section 04 — Domestic consumption & premiumization
11 / 16
Domestic demand is structural: café culture, young demographics, rising incomes.
Domestic consumption has reached 4.9 million bags in 2025/26 [USDA GAIN 2026] and is growing at ~2.1% per annum in volume, with value growth materially higher as premiumization shifts spending upward.

Per capita consumption trajectory

Per capita coffee consumption in Vietnam has approximately doubled since 2015, reaching ~3 kg annually [MARD 2026]. The country's median age of ~32 and urbanization rate of roughly 38–40% [GSO 2025] continue to pull new consumers into café and home-brew occasions. This demographic base is structurally different from mature markets — consumption is additive, not substitutional.

The café chain expansion

On-trade channels are the fastest-growing sub-channel in domestic coffee at an estimated 6.75% CAGR [Kira estimates]. Highlights Coffee operates 855 stores with 13% revenue growth in 2024 [company filings]. Challengers including Phuc Long (~150+ stores) and The Coffee House are competing on menu differentiation and mobile ordering. Trung Nguyên Legend's café format sits at the premium end, positioning alongside international specialty chains entering Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City.

Specialty arabica: from highland curiosity to shelf product

Vietnam's Ministry of Agriculture and Environment has targeted 19,000 hectares of certified specialty production by 2030 [MARD 2025], predominantly arabica from Lam Dong and Kon Tum. The Cau Dat plateau (1,400–1,600m elevation), Langbiang Mountain K'Ho micro-lots, and Di Linh Bourbon lots are achieving cup scores competitive with East African origins. GI certification for Lam Dong arabica and robusta was awarded by the IP Office in October 2025 — a credential that commands 15–25% premiums in specialty import markets [Kira estimates].

Premiumization catalysts

Three structural forces are pulling the domestic market upmarket: rising disposable incomes in urban centres (Hanoi, HCMC, Da Nang corridors); social media influence on café experience — Vietnam ranks among the highest globally for café visits per social media influencer post; and international specialty awareness from Vietnamese diaspora communities returning and bringing third-wave taste preferences. The specialty segment is projected to expand at a 6.95% CAGR [Kira estimates] to 2031, outpacing all other sub-segments.

Section 05 — EUDR & regulatory environment
13 / 16
EUDR compliance: a traceability gap that rewards early movers.
The EU Deforestation Regulation entered force for large operators in December 2025. Vietnam's EU exposure of ~40% of exports (~USD 3.2 bn at 2025 values) [MARD 2026] makes compliance a material commercial issue, not a niche sustainability question.

The compliance gap at origination

EUDR requires farm-level proof that coffee was not grown on land deforested after December 2020, with GPS plot-level data as the primary verification mechanism. Vietnam's production landscape is dominated by smallholder farms of 0.5–2 hectares — a fragmented origination base where approximately 60% of smallholders do not maintain reliable harvest records, and only ~10% keep detailed plot-level data [Forest Trends / Tavina survey 2025]. This structural gap at origination creates compliance risk that aggregates up through the supply chain to exporting companies.

Government and industry mobilization

Vietnam's Ministry of Agriculture and Rural Development issued an EUDR Action Plan Framework in 2025, providing guidance to provincial institutions and operators. Vietnam partnered with IDH (Sustainable Trade Initiative) and JDE Peet's to develop a deforestation risk database mapping coffee farms [IDH 2025]. These are serious interventions — Vietnam is among the most proactive producing nations on EUDR preparation — but the gap between policy and smallholder implementation remains wide heading into 2026–27.

Commercial bifurcation emerging

EU buyers are bifurcating their sourcing: compliant exporters with GPS-verified supply chains are receiving premium pricing and longer-term contracts, while non-compliant exporters face renegotiation pressure and some displacement toward non-EU buyers (notably China, which absorbs ~10% of Vietnam's exports and has no equivalent traceability mandate). Our analysts estimate the premium for fully EUDR-compliant Vietnamese robusta will reach USD 80–120 per tonne above benchmark by 2027 [Kira estimates].

Non-EU market diversification hedge

Beyond Europe, Vietnam is actively expanding market presence in China, South Korea, Australia, and the Middle East — markets without EUDR requirements and with growing RTD and specialty café cultures. South Korea accounted for ~4% of export value in 2025 [VICOFA 2025] and is growing at a double-digit pace [Kira estimates], supported by Vietnamese diaspora networks and K-café culture's adoption of Vietnamese-style drip and egg coffee formats.

Section 06 — Key risks & opportunities 2027
14 / 16
Five risks, five opportunities: the 2027 risk register.
The macro tailwind from 2024–2025 is unwinding, but structural opportunity — processed exports, domestic premiumization, EUDR compliance differentiation — is larger than the cyclical headwind.

Key risks

El Niño crop stress in Central Highlands
Below-normal Q1 2026 rainfall in Dak Lak and Lam Dong [USDA GAIN 2026]; ENSO probability elevated Jun–Aug 2026. A 5–8% yield shortfall would reduce 2026/27 output by 1.6–2.6 million bags below baseline [Kira estimates].
Export price correction deeper than forecast
If Colombian and Brazilian recoveries proceed faster than USDA base, robusta premiums versus arabica narrow more sharply. Downside scenario: 2027 export revenues at USD 6.9–7.1 bn vs. base USD 7.7 bn [Kira estimates].
EUDR enforcement displacing non-compliant exporters
EU buyers tightening supplier vetting from H2 2026. Exporters without GPS-verified supply chains risk losing contracts worth an estimated USD 200–400 m in aggregate [Kira estimates].
Input cost inflation eroding farm economics
Fertilizer and labor costs have risen 15–20% since 2022 [MARD 2025]. If Robusta prices decline while input costs remain elevated, smallholder margin compression could trigger replanting stagnation or area reduction.
Trade policy disruption (US tariff exposure)
US accounts for ~12% of export value. Any increase in Section 301-style tariffs on Vietnamese goods would add cost friction for US roasters sourcing Vietnamese robusta, moderating demand at the margin.

Key opportunities

Processed exports: the USD 1 bn threshold crossed
Processed coffee exports hit USD 1 bn for the first time in 2025 [Kira estimates]. Value-per-kg in instant and freeze-dried is 3–4× raw bean. Government incentives for processing investment align with the industry's direction [MARD 2025].
EUDR compliance as a commercial differentiator
Exporters investing in GPS traceability now will command USD 80–120/tonne premium by 2027 [Kira estimates] and access top-tier EU roaster contracts that less-prepared competitors are losing.
Specialty arabica GI premium in Japan and South Korea
Lam Dong GI certification (Oct 2025) opens GI-premiums in Japan and South Korea, markets where origin authentication commands 15–25% value uplift [Kira estimates]. Early off-take deals with Japanese specialty importers are already in negotiation.
Domestic café chain unit economics at scale
Highlands Coffee's 13% revenue growth on 855 stores [company filings] demonstrates the on-trade economics. Challengers in tier-2 cities (Danang, Can Tho, Hai Phong) have materially lower rental costs and underserved consumer demand [Kira estimates].
Non-EU market diversification building resilience
China (~10%), South Korea (~4%), and Middle East (~3%) are all growing import segments without EUDR friction [VICOFA 2025]. Structural demand from Vietnamese-style coffee adoption internationally provides a demand floor outside the EU compliance perimeter.

Methodology & data sources
15 / 16
Research methodology and source transparency.
This report draws on primary government statistics, trade association data, listed-company filings, and KIRA's proprietary triangulation model. All quantitative claims are tagged inline. Forecasts marked [Kira estimates] are derived from the triangulation approach documented below.

Triangulation approach

KIRA's market sizing methodology uses three independent anchors and averages the results, with outliers flagged. For Vietnam coffee 2027: (1) bottom-up from USDA production volume × average export unit price trajectory derived from ICO and USDA forward curves; (2) top-down from GDP-adjusted household consumption expenditure growth applied to the 2023 base market; (3) cross-check against MARD export revenue data adjusted for price correction scenarios. Where the three anchors produce ranges wider than ±15%, we report a range rather than a midpoint.

Forecast confidence levels

Production volume forecasts (32.5 million bags 2026/27): high confidence — USDA GAIN provides two independent revisions per year; area data from GSO is reliable. Export unit price forecasts: medium confidence — dependent on Brazilian arabica recovery pace and global supply balance, both carrying meaningful uncertainty. Domestic consumption: high confidence — driven by structural demographic forces less subject to short-term commodity price swings. Specialty/processed premium estimates: medium confidence — early market development with limited price discovery data.

Data currency

Primary data in this report is current to May–June 2026. USDA GAIN references are VM2025-0018 (Annual) and VM2025-0051 (Semi-Annual). MARD export data is through December 2025. Competitive positioning data reflects publicly available company filings and trade data through Q1 2026.
