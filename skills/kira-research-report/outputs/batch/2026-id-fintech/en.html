From light-touch to prudential perimeter

POJK 32/2025 (gazetted 15 December 2025) closes a four-year window in which paylater scaled to USD 8.6 bn under multi-instrument oversight. From mid-June 2026, BNPL may only be offered by commercial banks (Bank Umum) and licensed multifinance companies (Perusahaan Pembiayaan) — with multifinance providers requiring prior OJK approval [POJK 32/2025]. Standalone fintech BNPL is no longer a permitted business model. Penalties run up to IDR 15 bn plus management removal and licence revocation [Lexology 2025].

Credit-scoring stack is the second front

POJK 29/2024 created a formal Alternative Credit Scoring (ACS) regime [Pefindo 2025]: licensed providers may use telco, e-commerce, utility and device data — consent-based and explainable. Combined with mandatory paylater reporting to SLIK from July 2025 [POJK 11/2024], the underwriting baseline shifts from app-only behavioural data to a regulated, bureau-anchored stack within 18 months.

Five strategic implications for operators

Where market participants should focus capital and product effort over the next 24 months — ranked by the asymmetry of return given how the regime is now scoring.

01
Pick a licence path, fast
Standalone fintech BNPL is no longer permitted. Operators choose between bank charter, multifinance approval, or partner-sponsored distribution. Multifinance approval requires prior OJK consent under POJK 32/2025 — queue management starts now. 15 June 2026 is the hard transition cut-off.
ANCHOR · 6 mo compliance window from 15 Dec 2025
02
Reprice to the 0.1%/day cap
The cumulative cap on principal-plus-charges of 2x principal and 0.1%/day consumer interest from 1 Jan 2026 will compress unit margin on thin-file segments. Operators that historically blended late-fee revenue into APR must rebuild pricing — or exit the under-IDR 1 m ticket band entirely.
ANCHOR · 2x total-cost cap on principal [SEOJK 19/2023]
03
Wire SLIK into the funnel
All licensed paylater providers report borrower data to SLIK from July 2025 [POJK 11/2024]. Delinquency is now visible to every other lender. Funnel design must assume any default scars all future credit applications — including mortgage. Underwriting can tighten without losing acceptable users.
ANCHOR · Jul-2025 SLIK reporting mandatory
04
Wire ACS into the stack
POJK 29/2024 legitimises telco + e-commerce + utility + device data for credit decisioning, through licensed ACS providers [Pefindo 2025]. The fastest acquirers will be those who plug a licensed ACS bureau into origination during the same six months as the POJK 32/2025 retrofit — not after.
ANCHOR · USD 15 bn alt-data scoring market addressable
05
Treat the unbanked wedge as a 5-year asset
~98 m unbanked Indonesian adults remain the structural growth pull [World Bank 2021]. Compliance is up; cost-to-serve will follow. But unit economics improve as bureau-driven underwriting replaces blind-test cohorts. The right play is multi-year cohort building, not Q-by-Q growth chasing.

Section 05 · Policy context & history
007 / 015

Policy context & history

The regime traces to POJK 77/2016 — OJK's first dedicated peer-to-peer lending registration framework — and tightened through five subsequent statutes as fintech volumes outran rule-making. POJK 32/2025 is the closing chapter, not the opening one.

From registration to perimeter

OJK began with light-touch registration. POJK 77/2016 created the first fintech-lending framework. As deliberate default groups, fraud rings and NPL pockets surfaced — 21 P2P platforms recorded NPL above the 5% threshold by late 2024 [OJK 2024] — the rules tightened: POJK 10/2022 raised governance, POJK 40/2024 superseded it with stricter standards, POJK 22/2023 introduced consumer-protection principles, and POJK 11/2024 mandated SLIK reporting for paylater.

POJK 32/2025 closes the carve-out

Before December 2025, BNPL operated under multifinance and e-money rules without a dedicated framework. POJK 32/2025 codifies BNPL as a discrete product, restricts issuance to banks and multifinance, mandates credit-scoring assessment for every borrower, regulates interest, fees and collection, and aligns reporting with SLIK. Compliance window: six months from 15 December 2025 [POJK 32/2025].

Recent changes in detail — POJK 32/2025

Two material changes in the last 12 months reset the operating environment: POJK 32/2025 (BNPL framework) and POJK 29/2024 (alternative credit scoring). This page details the first; the next covers the second.

15 Dec 2025
POJK 32 Tahun 2025 · Penyelenggaraan BNPL
Permitted-operator perimeter narrows to banks & multifinance

BNPL is codified as a discrete product class. Only commercial banks (Bank Umum) and finance companies (Perusahaan Pembiayaan) may issue. Multifinance providers must obtain prior OJK approval; bank issuance follows banking-law conduct rules. Mandatory credit-scoring assessment per borrower; SLIK reporting obligation aligned.

  • Penalties: written warnings, product restrictions, fines up to IDR 15 bn, management removal, licence revocation [Lexology 2025]
  • Transition: existing BNPL operators have 6 months from 15 Dec 2025 to align — cut-off 15 Jun 2026
  • Pre-existing contracts: remain valid until expiry; amendments must comply with POJK 32/2025
SOURCE: OJK POJK 32/2025 OFFICIAL TEXT · LEXOLOGY 2025 ALERT
Effect on the operator field
Approx. ~16 active BNPL brands [Kira estimates]
Industry response & immediate market signals

Akulaku Finance, Kredivo (FinAccel via multifinance subsidiary), Atome Indonesia (PT Atome Finance), and bank-issued products (BCA, Mandiri, BRI) are structurally well-placed. Shopee PayLater (Sea Group), GoPayLater (GoTo) and Lazada PayLater require sponsor-bank or multifinance pairing or a charter migration.

  • ~16 active BNPL brands at end-2025 [Kira estimates, OJK directory]
  • P2P NPL TWP90: 2.75% Jul 2025, 2.85% Jun 2025 [OJK Press Release Mar-2025]
  • 21 platforms with NPL above 5% threshold flagged late 2024 [OJK 2024]
  • Capital-adequacy uplift creates structural advantage for bank-issued BNPL — deposit funding cost

Recent changes in detail — ACS + SLIK

The credit-scoring stack reset under POJK 29/2024 and the mandatory SLIK reporting under POJK 11/2024 are the operational counterparts to the perimeter tightening — they make underwriting compliance auditable.

Dec 2024
POJK 29 Tahun 2024 · Alternative Credit Scoring
Formal ACS regime legitimises non-traditional data underwriting

Licensed Alternative Credit Scoring providers may use telco, utilities, e-commerce, and device-metadata for risk assessment — explainable, consent-based, privacy-compliant. Traditional credit/financing data is prohibited in ACS models; bureau data flows through Pefindo / Credit Bureau Indonesia / regulated channels instead.

  • ~98 m unbanked adults structurally addressable through ACS [WB 2021]
  • Pefindo IdAlternativeScore draws BPJS Ketenagakerjaan, DGT, APPI [Pefindo 2025]
  • UU PDP 2022 consent + purpose-limitation rules apply [Kominfo]
  • Tokopedia SDB, Investree AIForesee, Finantier Score — existing alt-data engines now formalised under licence
SOURCE: OJK POJK 29/2024, PEFINDO BIRO KREDIT MATERIALS 2025
31 Jul 2025
POJK 11 Tahun 2024 · SLIK reporting
All licensed paylater providers report borrower data to SLIK

SLIK (Sistem Layanan Informasi Keuangan) is OJK's debtor database, successor to BI Checking. From 31 Jul 2025, every licensed paylater provider must report borrower credit data. Delinquency is now visible to banks, multifinance, and other paylater operators on the next loan application — including mortgage and credit-card decisions.

  • Mandatory reporters: banks, multifinance, BNPL providers
  • Optional reporters: P2P, cooperatives, microfinance — still a coverage gap
  • 96-97 licensed P2P platforms tracked by OJK [OJK Directory Jan-2025]
  • End-state: SLIK becomes the single shared credit-history layer; deliberate-default groups visible across the system
SOURCE: OJK POJK 11/2024, BTN MATERIALS 2025, KIRA SYNTHESIS

Pending changes & lobby positions

Three implementing-circular tracks are pending issuance through 2026: credit-scoring method SEOJK, complaint-handling mechanics, and collection-conduct rules. Outcome shapes the operational bar on top of POJK 32/2025.

AS-DRAFTED
Q2 2026
SEOJK on credit-scoring method issued as drafted: standardised scorecard variables, mandatory ACS-only or hybrid models for thin-file, consent-renewal cycle every 12 months. Compliance build — not commercial drag.
INDUSTRY-AMENDED
Q3 2026
After AFPI + APPI comment cycle: scorecard variables left flexible; safe harbour for licensed ACS bureau use; consent renewal moved to 24 months. Operator burden lower; OJK reserves right to audit.
FURTHER-TIGHTENED
2H 2026

Compliance cost & winner/loser map

One-time compliance burden estimated at USD 0.6-1.2 m per existing operator; steady-state opex uplift USD 0.3-0.6 m / year. Winner-loser map separates structurally-licensed incumbents from forced exits.

Compliance-cost analysis · per existing operator

One-time setup: USD 0.6-1.2 m [Kira estimates]. Buckets: charter / approval filing (legal counsel + capital uplift), SLIK reporting infrastructure build, credit-scoring stack integration with licensed ACS bureau, internal-conduct policy + complaints handling overhaul. Bank-issued products absorb most through existing compliance machinery.

Steady-state: USD 0.3-0.6 m / year [Kira estimates]. Triangulation method: anchor on Malaysia Consumer Credit Bill RIA estimates × 16 active Indonesia operators; adjust for IDR labour cost. Anchored, not surveyed — surface as estimate, not as primary.

Fixed vs variable split

Roughly 60% fixed (legal, capital, infrastructure) and 40% variable (FTE: compliance, audit, collection-conduct review). Variable component scales with portfolio size; large operators absorb on existing teams. Sub-scale exit threshold: operators with under USD 30 m disbursed annually face uneconomic compliance ratio.

Industry-RIA evidence is thin — no published OJK RIA on POJK 32/2025 at time of writing. Estimate methodology disclosed in endnote.

Methodology summary

Where a figure carries a named source tag (e.g. [POJK 32/2025], [OJK 2024], [Pefindo 2025]), the underlying data comes from that source — full citation is given in the sources panel right.
