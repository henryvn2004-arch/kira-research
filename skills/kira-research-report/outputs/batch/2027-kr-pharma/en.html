The market is small; the export lever is not

Domestic pharma is a slow-growing base near USD 23 bn in 2025 on a ~2.4% trajectory [Korea Pharma Outlook 2025]. The story sits in exports: medicine exports reached USD 10.4 bn in 2025, up 11.8%, with biologics at 62.6% of the total [KPBMA 2026]. Korea sells capacity and molecules, not its home market.

Two engines, compounding

Biosimilars give Korea a pipeline edge — 5 of 18 US FDA biosimilar approvals in 2025, the most of any country, led by Celltrion and Samsung Bioepis [MDToday 2026]. CDMO gives it scale: Samsung Biologics cleared KRW 4.56 trn revenue and KRW 6 trn in new orders in 2025 [SBL FY25]. The BIOSECURE Act, signed into the FY26 NDAA, hands Korean plants the demand Chinese CDMOs vacate [Lawtimes 2026].

Five strategic implications for the next 24 months

Korea's pharma franchise is two linked bets — a biosimilar pipeline lead and a contract-manufacturing scale lead — landing into a patent-cliff window and a once-in-a-cycle supply-chain realignment. The moves below sequence where to lean in.

01
Treat capacity as the moat, fill it ahead of the cliff
The 2025-2029 biologic patent cliff is of a scale prior cycles never matched. Operators with idle, validated bioreactors win the bid; those still building lose the first wave. Pre-book capacity against named launches, not forecasts.

02
Bank the BIOSECURE tailwind while it is live

Section 01 · Market & exports

Market size & the export mix: where the growth actually sits

The domestic market is a stable, demographically-driven base. The expansion vector is the bio-export lever — biosimilars plus contract manufacturing — which is where capacity and pipeline decisions earn their return.

Domestic: slow, aging, defensive

Korea's domestic pharma market sits near USD 23 bn in 2025, growing about 2.4% a year toward USD 26 bn by 2030 [Korea Pharma Outlook 2025]. An aging population and broad national insurance support volume, but price control and tendering cap value growth. Oncology and large-molecule biologics are the fastest domestic segments.

Exports: the real engine

Medicine exports hit USD 10.4 bn in 2025, up 11.8%, with biologics at 62.6% of the total [KPBMA 2026]. The mix has shifted hard toward biosimilars and CDMO output. First-half 2025 health exports — pharma, biosimilars, diagnostics — passed USD 13 bn, a fresh record [Korea Health Export 2025].

Innovation, not just volume

In 2025 Korean firms signed technology-transfer deals worth over USD 4.8 bn [Korea Health Export 2025]. Korea increasingly exports the right to make molecules, not only the molecules — a higher-margin layer on top of product shipments.

Section 01 · Biosimilar position
008 / 015

Korea's global biosimilar position: an approval lead, two anchors

Korea has been the world's most prolific source of new US biosimilar approvals two years running. The lead rests on two firms with opposite go-to-market models — and a portfolio now moving from first-wave monoclonals to higher-margin formats.

The approval lead is real and repeating

Of 18 US FDA biosimilar approvals in 2025, 5 came from Korean firms — the most of any country, a second consecutive year at the top [MDToday 2026]. Korea held the second-largest cumulative count of FDA-approved biosimilars behind the US as far back as 2022 [GaBI 2025].

Two anchors, two models

Celltrion runs its own commercial network in the US and EU, controlling pricing, tenders, and brand; it posted ~KRW 4.1 trn revenue and KRW 1.2 trn operating profit in 2025, both records [Celltrion FY25]. Samsung Bioepis sells largely through partners; FY25 revenue reached KRW 1.67 trn, up 28% ex-milestones [SBE FY25].

The margin is migrating

At Celltrion, next-generation products — Zymfentra, Yuflyma, Vegzelma, Steqeyma — now exceed 60% of sales [Celltrion FY25]. Zymfentra, a novel subcutaneous infliximab approved as a US new drug, is the template: format IP earns drug economics where me-too monoclonals see 85%+ price erosion [Kira estimates].

Section 02 · Demand drivers
011 / 015

What fills the new capacity: three demand currents

Pouring liters is the easy part; filling them is the test. Three currents — the supply-chain realignment under BIOSECURE, the GLP-1 peptide boom, and the ADC modality shift — together decide whether Korea's 2026-2027 capacity runs hot or idle.

BIOSECURE: the demand China vacates

The BIOSECURE Act was folded into the FY26 NDAA and signed in December 2025, restricting US federal-linked work with named Chinese bio firms [Lawtimes 2026]. Roughly 47% of one targeted Chinese CDMO's 2023 revenue was US-sourced — demand now seeking validated, non-China capacity [Kira estimates]. Korean operators report rising RFP flow.

GLP-1: a peptide supercycle

SK pharmteco signed a long-term GLP-1 API supply deal reported at up to KRW 2 trn, widely linked to a US obesity-drug leader, with revenue feeding 2027+ [Hankyung 2024]. A KRW 310 bn Sejong peptide plant targets 2026 start [Edaily 2026].

ADC: the next modality

Lotte Biologics and SK pharmteco signed an LOI in October 2025 for one-stop ADC CDMO — SK on linker-payload, Lotte on bioconjugation and drug substance [Lotte-SK 2025]. ADC and oligonucleotide lines diversify away from crowding mAb fill-finish.

Six patent-cliff windows opening into 2030

The biologic patent cliff through 2030 is, by revenue at risk, of a different magnitude than prior cycles. Each window below is a discrete launch race; Korean developers contest most of them, but capture depends on format, timing, and litigation resolution.

WINDOW 01
Ustekinumab (Stelara)
US exclusivity lapsed 2025; biosimilars launched with WAC discounts past 80%. Korean entrants are live — the first test of second-wave commercial discipline. [Patent Cliff 2025]
STATUS · LIVE 2025 · PRICE WAR ON
WINDOW 02
Adalimumab tail (Humira)
Humira's biosimilar field is crowded, but subcutaneous and high-concentration formats still differentiate. Yuflyma grew 44% in the US in 2025 off format positioning. [Celltrion FY25]
STATUS · LIVE · FORMAT-LED
WINDOW 03
Aflibercept (Eylea)
Ophthalmology biosimilars open a high-value, lower-competition lane. Korean ranibizumab experience (Byooviz) seeds credibility for the aflibercept follow-on. [SBE FY25]

Denosumab (Prolia/Xgeva)
Celltrion's Stoboclo/Osenvelt are already in market; bone-health volume is steady and tender-driven, a defensive cash lane against pricier launches. [Celltrion FY25]

Trastuzumab deruxtecan / ADC

Section 03 · Regulatory & policy
014 / 015

Regulatory & policy ladder: harmonized at home, leveraged abroad

Korea's biosimilar advantage is partly regulatory design — an MFDS pathway built to align with EMA, WHO and Japan from the start, so home approval shortens the road to export markets. National policy now leans harder into bio as a strategic export.

A pathway built for export

Korea's biosimilar framework, legislated from 2009 and revised since, was harmonized with EMA, WHO and Japanese standards by design [GaBI 2025]. A product cleared by the MFDS carries a credible head start into EU and US filings — a structural reason Korea over-indexes on FDA approvals.

The 3rd 5-year bio plan

Government targets through 2030 include doubling bio-pharma exports, three blockbuster-class new drugs, and a top-3 clinical-trial market [KHIDI 2025]. The national new-drug fund (KDDF) plans roughly USD 1.5 bn across 1,200+ projects over a decade [KPA News 2025].

The watch-item: trial momentum

Execution risk is visible. Active clinical trials slipped to 2,175 in 2025 from 2,307 in 2024 [KPA News 2025]. Capacity and pipeline-breadth are strong; early-stage trial depth is the variable that decides whether the post-2030 pipeline stays full.

2025-2030 forecast: base / bull / bear

Korea's bio-export trajectory is set by three variables: how much new Songdo capacity fills, how fast biosimilar launches land into the patent-cliff windows, and how durable the BIOSECURE re-sourcing tailwind proves. Scenarios anchor to 2025 exports of USD 10.4 bn.

Three scenarios

Base — USD 17 bn by 2030. Capacity fills at a steady cadence; biosimilars take the live windows; BIOSECURE adds a moderate, durable lift. [Kira estimates]

Bull — USD 21 bn. Keytruda-class windows land on schedule, GLP-1/ADC contracts ramp, and re-sourcing accelerates. [Kira estimates]

Bear — USD 13 bn. Trial slowdown thins the late-decade pipeline and capacity outpaces fillable demand. [Kira estimates]
