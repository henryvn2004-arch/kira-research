How we built this view

This view is built from two evidence streams: primary inputs — pharmacy-channel checks, hospital-tender walks, listed-issuer calls — and secondary anchors — statistical authorities, regulator releases, listed-company filings. Every quantitative claim carries an explicit source tag.

Primary inputs

Issuer conversations

Analyst synthesis

Secondary anchors

Statistical authorities

Triangulated estimates

Vietnam pharma 2027 — generics localize, biosimilars open the next door

Vietnam's drug market sits at ~USD 8 bn in 2025 [Fortune BI 2025], with the Decision 1165 strategy steering domestic share toward 70% of market value by 2030 [Decision 1165 2023]. The hospital-tender system was rewritten in 2025 to favour EU-GMP-certified local supply, and the first biosimilar wave is landing into oncology and chronic-disease therapy windows.

Localization is the structural story

Decision 1165 targets domestic drug supply at 80% of demand and 70% of market value by 2030 [Decision 1165 2023]; locally produced API reaches a more modest 20% share. Circular 40/2025/TT-BYT explicitly rewards EU-GMP-certified domestic supply in hospital tenders [MoH 2025], converting the certification race into pricing power. The 2025-27 window decides which domestic majors lock in tender share before Tier-1 lot competition stabilises.

Biosimilars open the second leg

Celltrion launched Remsima (infliximab) and Herzuma (trastuzumab) into Vietnam in 2024-25 [Celltrion 2025]. Nanogen's Stimus (darbepoetin alfa biosimilar) is in late-stage hospital trials [Nanogen 2025]. With biologics at ~USD 7.3 bn within the broader pharma value chain [Medpath 2025] and ASP discounts of 50-70% versus reference biologics [InsightAce 2025], biosimilar penetration becomes the 2027-30 margin lever for both ETC tender wins and BHYT cost containment.

Section 03 · Strategic Implications
005 / 026

Five strategic implications for the next 24 months

Market participants should treat 2025-27 as the localization lock-in window, not the 2030 strategy target. EU-GMP commitments, tender lot positioning, and biosimilar dossier filings made now lock in cost positions and channel access through the end of the decade.

IMPLICATION · 01
Treat EU-GMP certification as the tender-eligibility moat
Circular 40/2025/TT-BYT gives EU-GMP-certified local production a Tier-1 tender lot preference [MoH 2025]. Today only 17 plants nationally hold the credential [InvestVietnam 2025]; the gap between EU-GMP holders and WHO-GMP only-holders widens through 2027 as tender lots reprice. Plants on the certification path before 2026 capture the next absorption wave.
17 / 238 EU-GMP plants
IMPLICATION · 02
Anchor channel strategy on pharmacy chains, not single counters
The pharmacy channel drives 61% of market value and grew 11% in H1 2025, ahead of the 6% hospital channel growth [VIRAC 2025]. Long Chau (FRT), Pharmacity, An Khang and Trung Son are consolidating the OTC retail layer. Brand listings and detailing budgets should rebalance toward the chain GMs, not loose-counter wholesalers.
61% · +11% pharmacy share & growth
IMPLICATION · 03
Use foreign-strategic partnerships as a localization shortcut
SK Group's ~48% stake in Imexpharm [VIR 2024], Taisho's 51%+ in DHG [VIR 2024], Abbott's 52% in Domesco [VietnamNews 2024] underwrite EU-GMP capex and dossier filings. Pure organic build-outs face 3-5 year EU-GMP commissioning cycles [Kira estimates]; technology-transfer JVs compress that to ~18-24 months.
~48% / 51% / 52% top-3 stakes
IMPLICATION · 04
Position biosimilar dossiers ahead of the tender repricing cycle
Biosimilars trade at 50-70% ASP discounts to reference biologics [InsightAce 2025]. Vietnam Social Security's price-negotiation list for biologics expands into 2026; trastuzumab, infliximab, rituximab, denosumab and insulin analogues are the highest-value reprice windows. Filing dossiers now positions for 2027-28 tender entries.

IMPLICATION · 05
De-risk API exposure before the 2027-28 supply-shock window
Vietnam imports the dominant share of finished-dose APIs; local production satisfies a fraction of the 80%+ import dependency [Kira estimates based on Davipharm + EuroCham 2024]. Decision 1165 targets 20% domestic API by 2030 [Decision 1165 2023]. Operators with multi-sourced API supply chains survive the next China-supply shock; single-sourced lines face margin compression.

Section 04 · Macro Context
007 / 026

Demand backbone: demographics, coverage, spend

Vietnam's 102m-person economy expands at 7%-plus through the forecast window [IMF WEO 2026], with health spending and BHYT coverage compounding on top. Pharma spend per capita sits near USD 80 in 2025 [Kira estimates] — well below Thailand and Malaysia peers, and the convergence path is the underlying demand pull.

Population & ageing

Vietnam's population reached ~102m in 2025 [GSO 2025], with 14% over 60 and rising to ~20% by 2035 [GSO 2025]. The ageing curve translates directly into chronic-disease prescription volumes — diabetes, cardiovascular, oncology — the same therapy windows where biosimilar entry is now economically meaningful.

BHYT coverage

BHYT (compulsory health insurance) covers ~93% of the population [VSS 2024], anchoring the ETC channel. The 2025 abolition of hospital-rank distinction in drug-use eligibility [Circular 37/2024 2024] expanded access to higher-tier therapies at lower-tier facilities — the demand pull for biosimilars and high-end generics widens correspondingly.

Spend trajectory

Total health spend tracked 6%+ of GDP in 2025 [Kira estimates from GSO + MoH bulletins]. Pharma spend per capita rises to ~USD 110 by 2030 in our base case [Kira estimates], driven by ETC formulary expansion plus OTC retail consolidation. The relative-price tailwind versus brand-imports compresses; volume becomes the dominant growth lever.

Section 04 · Policy Strategy
008 / 026

Decision 1165 sets the 2030 finish line

PM Decision 1165/QĐ-TTg (October 2023) codified the national strategy through 2030 with vision to 2045 [Decision 1165 2023]. The headline target — domestic supply at 80% of demand and 70% of value by 2030 — sets the structural goalposts that downstream regulation (Law 44/2024, Circular 40/2025, Decree 163/2025) is now implementing.

Localization targets

By 2030: 80% domestic supply by demand, 70% by market value [Decision 1165 2023]. 20% of API used in domestic production produced locally; 50% of raw materials for functional foods + pharma cosmetics [Decision 1165 2023]. 100% of expanded-program vaccination and 30% of service vaccination demand met by domestic vaccines. Generic bioequivalence target: 30% of generic drugs (excluding topical and bioequivalence-established) [Decision 1165 2023].

Vision 2045

Beyond 2030, Vietnam targets specialty drug, biological and original-brand production capacity [Decision 1165 2023] with an aspirational USD 20 bn-plus contribution to GDP. The 2045 horizon makes 2027 the mid-point checkpoint where execution credibility is assessed; capex commitments made now feed the 2030 KPI snapshot.

Where execution stands

Domestic drugs captured 26.2% of the VND 34.7 trillion hospital-tender pool in 2023 [VSS 2024] — well short of the 2030 70% target. Closing that ~44-pt gap inside 7 years requires both supply-side capacity (EU-GMP lines) and demand-side levers (tender preference, BHYT formulary inclusion). Both are being pulled simultaneously through 2025-27.

Market size & growth: Vietnam pharma 2023-2030

The total pharma value pool runs from USD 6.8 bn in 2023 to USD 12.0 bn by 2030 in our base case — a ~8% CAGR [Kira estimates anchored on Fortune BI 2025 + Stellapharm 2025]. Statista's narrower prescription-only definition tracks at USD 2.4 bn 2025 [Statista 2025]; the spread between definitions is the OTC + functional-food layer that other estimators include.

Value pool trajectory

Vietnam pharma sat at USD 7.6-8.0 bn in 2024-25 across reconciled estimators [Fortune BI 2025; Stellapharm 2025]. The headline trajectory points to USD 12.0-16.0 bn by 2030-32, with the spread driven by API and biologics inclusion. Our base case targets USD 12.0 bn by 2030 [Kira estimates] — conservatively below Fortune's 9.33% CAGR (USD 16.0 bn by 2032) but ahead of Statista's narrower 5.14% prescription-only path.

H1 2025 acceleration

H1 2025 spend reached VND 231 trillion (~USD 9 bn annualised), +9% YoY [VnEconomy 2025]. The pharmacy channel led with 11% growth while hospital channel grew 6% [VIRAC 2025]. The channel-split divergence is the central structural signal — chain pharmacies are absorbing share from both loose-counter retail and from hospital outpatient dispensing.

Per-capita context

Pharma spend per capita rises from ~USD 80 in 2025 toward USD 110 by 2030 [Kira estimates] — still ~half Thailand's level and ~one-third Malaysia's [Kira estimates]. The convergence math underwrites a structural growth premium versus mature peers; volume — not price — is the dominant lever through 2030.

Section 05 · Channel Split
011 / 026

ETC vs OTC: where the margin sits

The ETC channel — hospital tenders, BHYT-reimbursed therapies — anchors ~75-76% of revenue [Stellapharm 2025], leaving ~25% for OTC. But the OTC layer is the consolidation story: Long Chau (FRT) operates 1,900+ stores [FRT 2025], Pharmacity ~1,000+, and An Khang + Trung Son expanding into tier-2 cities. Channel power is shifting toward chain GMs.

ETC channel architecture

Hospital tender pool: VND 34.7 trillion (~USD 1.4 bn) in 2023 [VSS 2024], with domestic supply at 26.2% share. Tender lot tiers under Circular 40/2025 reward EU-GMP certified production with Tier-1 eligibility, GMP-only with Tier-2-4 placement [MoH 2025]. The 70% domestic-value target by 2030 requires both domestic share gains in existing lots and overall tender pool expansion.

OTC channel consolidation

Long Chau (FRT subsidiary) reached 1,900+ stores end-2025 [FRT 2025], targeting 3,000 by 2027. Pharmacity at ~1,000 stores, An Khang ~500, Trung Son ~300 [Kira estimates]. The top-4 chains hold ~25% of the OTC layer by value [Kira estimates] — up from <5% in 2020. The loose-counter independent layer is being absorbed by chain expansion and BHYT-card acceptance at chain locations.

Implication for brand strategy

Brand listings now compete for chain-GM attention rather than wholesaler-rep coverage. Detailing budgets re-weight from doctor-detailing toward chain category management [Kira estimates from operator conversations]. For domestic majors, the chain channel is a defensible OTC moat; for foreign-brand imports, chain category position decides volume.

Section 06 · Localization
013 / 026

The EU-GMP race: 17 plants set the Tier-1 pool

Vietnam has 238 WHO-GMP plants and 17 EU-GMP plants as of late 2025 [InvestVietnam 2025]. Circular 40/2025 reshaped the tender hierarchy so EU-GMP certified domestic production qualifies for Tier-1 lots — the highest-margin tender segment historically held by foreign-brand imports.

Certification stack

WHO-GMP is the floor — required for any domestic manufacturer. EU-GMP, PIC/S-GMP and US-FDA inspections are the differentiators. Imexpharm holds the largest set of EU-GMP-compliant production lines in Vietnam with 11 lines across three plant clusters [VIR 2025]. UIP earned EU-GMP at its Binh Duong VSIP-2 facility in early 2025 [Vietnam.vn 2025]. Bidiphar, DHG Pharma and Domesco are deep into EU-GMP commissioning.

Tender preference economics

Circular 40/2025/TT-BYT splits tender lots into 5 tiers. Tier-1 — reserved for "high-quality drugs" — accepts EU-GMP-certified manufacturers and SRA-approved drugs [MoH 2025]. Domestic operators in Tier-1 effectively compete head-to-head with foreign-brand imports at 20-40% price discounts [Kira estimates]. The tier preference compresses foreign-brand pricing power and re-rates EU-GMP plants commercially.

Capex commitment window

EU-GMP commissioning runs 18-36 months from greenfield depending on technology partner and line type [Kira estimates from operator conversations]. Plants that haven't broken ground by mid-2026 won't be certified by 2028; they cede the 2027-30 absorption wave to incumbents. Tech-transfer JVs with foreign-strategic partners compress that timeline materially.

Section 06 · API Constraint
014 / 026

The API gap is the localization story's blind spot

Vietnam's domestic API base is thin. Vietnam API market value sits at ~USD 430 m in 2023, rising to ~USD 707 m by 2030 at 7.4% CAGR [Grand View 2024]. Against finished-dose production needs, domestic API satisfies an estimated ~5% of consumption [Kira estimates] — the 80%+ residual is imported, mostly from China and India.

Import dependence

Local production of API is constrained by an underdeveloped industrial chemistry base [Davipharm 2024]. Key starting materials (KSMs), intermediates and most active substances are imported. China and India dominate the supplier mix [Kira estimates from MOIT trade data]; the same single-country concentration that India is publicly trying to unwind structurally exposes Vietnam to upstream-shock risk.

Decision 1165's 20% API target

Decision 1165 targets 20% of API for domestic drug production produced locally by 2030 [Decision 1165 2023]. From the ~5% baseline, that's a 4x scale-up in 7 years. The pathway requires (a) industrial-chemistry capacity build, (b) fermentation/biologics infrastructure, (c) regulatory pathway adjustments to favour local API in tender scoring. Of the three, only (c) is fully on track.

Tech-transfer optionality

Foreign-strategic partnerships are the most pragmatic API capacity shortcut. SK Group's Imexpharm position [VIR 2024], Taisho-DHG [VIR 2024], Abbott-Domesco [VietnamNews 2024], plus newer Chinese strategic investors in CDS-pharma [The Investor 2025] route technology and feedstock access to local plants. Pure-domestic build-outs remain capital-intensive and slow.

Section 08 · Biosimilar Landscape

Biosimilars: the Korean pipeline meets Vietnamese BHYT tender

Vietnam's biopharmaceutical pool reached ~USD 7.3 bn in 2023 with 7%+ annual growth [Medpath 2025]. Biosimilar penetration sits at single-digit % share of the biologics layer [Kira estimates], with Celltrion as the dominant foreign entrant and Nanogen as the leading domestic developer. The 2025-28 tender repricing cycle is the absorption window.

The Celltrion entry

Celltrion launched Remsima (infliximab biosimilar) and Herzuma (trastuzumab biosimilar) in Vietnam in 2024-25, targeting autoimmune disease and breast/stomach cancer [Celltrion 2025; Medpath 2025]. Truxima (rituximab biosimilar) and Remsima SC are next in the Vietnam regulatory queue. Celltrion's portfolio gives Vietnam access to first-wave-mature monoclonal-antibody biosimilars with proven ex-Vietnam reference data.

Domestic developers

Nanogen's Stimus (darbepoetin alfa biosimilar) is in late-stage clinical trials at multiple Vietnamese hospitals for anemia in chronic kidney disease and chemotherapy patients [Nanogen 2025]. Domestic biosimilar capacity remains thin compared to South Korea (Celltrion, Samsung Bioepis) or India (Dr. Reddy's, Biocon); the realistic 2027-30 picture is import-dominant supply with selective local manufacturing partnerships.

BHYT tender as the demand pump

The 2025 price-negotiation list expands biologics coverage [Binh Phuoc DOH 2025]. Trastuzumab biosimilars price at ~80% of Herceptin's reference brand [InsightAce 2025]; trastuzumab + bevacizumab + rituximab biosimilars typically achieve 50-70% ASP discounts versus reference biologics [InsightAce 2025]. For BHYT, the math compels switching as biosimilar reference standards reach equivalence parity.

Section 09 · Regulatory Timeline
023 / 026

Regulatory ladder: from Decision 1165 to e-registration

The 2023-26 regulatory stack converges on one direction: simpler registration, faster approvals, preference for EU-GMP-certified local supply. Foreign-invested enterprise restrictions eased; e-commerce drug sales formalised; price transparency tightened [Vietnam Briefing 2025; Tilleke 2025].

Law 44/2024/QH15

Passed November 2024, effective July 1 2025 [Lexology 2025]. Simplifies drug registration, renewal, and amendment procedures. MoH can omit dossier appraisals for drugs meeting defined safety / efficacy / quality criteria [Lexology 2025] — a structural compression on time-to-market. Expands e-commerce drug sales authorisation and increases market access for foreign-invested enterprises [Pharmaceutical Tech 2025].

Implementing guidance

Decree 163/2025/ND-CP and Circular 31/2025/TT-BYT both effective July 1 2025 [Tilleke 2025] flesh out registration mechanics. Electronic dossier submission system now live — registration timelines compress materially. The 2026 regulatory framework includes specific provisions for biosimilars and high-tech generics [Freyr 2025], setting the procedural baseline for the 2027-30 biosimilar wave.

Circular 40/2025 tender preference

Circular 40/2025/TT-BYT (drug bidding at public health facilities) gives locally produced, EU-GMP-certified drugs a competitive advantage in hospital tenders [LNT Partners 2025]. Combined with Circular 37/2024 abolishing hospital-rank distinction in BHYT drug eligibility [Luatvietnam 2025], the demand-side levers favour local high-tech supply at every BHYT-facility tier.
