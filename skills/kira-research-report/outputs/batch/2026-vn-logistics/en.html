Macro indicators driving demand

Vietnam closed 2025 at 8.02% GDP growth [NSO Vietnam 2026] — the fastest pace since 2007. AMRO projects 7.6% in 2026 [VIR 2025], the highest among ASEAN+3 economies. Industry and services contribute most to logistics-relevant demand.

Growth composition

Industry & construction grew 9.73% in 2025, services 8.82%, agriculture 3.70% [NSO Vietnam 2026]. Q4 2025 hit 8.46% YoY — the fastest quarterly print since Q4 2007. Q1 2026 came in at 7.83% [NSO Vietnam 2026], confirming the trend hasn't been a one-quarter spike.

Demographic tailwind

102.2 m population, median age 33.4 [NSO Vietnam 2026]. Urban share at 42.2% in 2026 and rising ~3% annually [NSO Vietnam 2026]. UN projects 50% urban by 2039. Urban household income hit VND 6.9 m/month (~USD 261) in 2024, up 10.1% YoY [NSO Vietnam 2026].

Middle-class expansion

Middle class reaches 26% of population by 2026 [VnPlus 2025], up from 13% in 2023 — a doubling in three years. Real earnings grow ~4.8% annually through 2029 [Kira estimates based on BMI baseline].

Policy timeline anchoring the next decade

Decision 2229/QD-TTg in October 2025 reframed logistics from a downstream cost line into a recognized economic pillar with quantitative 2035 targets. The 2026-2028 window is the operational ramp.

Headline targets to 2035

Decision 2229 targets logistics value-added at 5-7% of GDP [Decision 2229 2025], with annual sector growth of 12-15% and logistics cost reduced to 12-15% of GDP from ~16% today [MoIT 2025]. Vietnam aims for a top-40 LPI rank by 2035 [Decision 2229 2025] — up from 43rd in 2023 [WB LPI 2023].

Infrastructure pipeline

The eastern North-South Expressway now runs 2,063 km uninterrupted from Lang Son to Ca Mau [VIR 2025]. Long Thanh International Airport completed its first technical flight in Dec 2025 [VIR 2025], with phase 1 operations slated for mid-to-late 2026. ACV has proposed a USD 450 m air logistics + duty-free center at Long Thanh [VnExpress 2025].

Risk: fuel + driver inputs

Diesel hit VND 39,660/L in March 2026 amid the Strait of Hormuz disruption [VIR 2025] — RON95 surged 60%+ from February. Logistics operators introduced fuel surcharges; national rail freight rates rose 15% [VIR 2025]. This is the variance to watch.

Section 05 · Sector Overview & Sizing
010 / 024

Market size & growth: Vietnam logistics

Vietnam's freight & logistics market is sized at USD 52.06 bn in 2025 [Kira estimates], reaching USD 55.5 bn in 2026 and USD 71.9 bn by 2030 at 6.67% CAGR. The Ministry of Industry & Trade's domestic estimate exceeds USD 60 bn including 40,000+ active operators [MoIT 2025].

The headline anchor

Estimates vary by methodology: the broader logistics services envelope reads USD 60+ bn in 2025 [MoIT 2025], while a narrower freight-only definition lands at USD 52 bn [Kira estimates]. We use the narrower frame for sub-segment comparability but flag the headline-vs-services gap.

Where the growth concentrates

The aggregate 6.6% CAGR masks segment heterogeneity. Cold chain compounds at 11-13% [FiinResearch 2025], e-commerce logistics at 12.4% [GTI 2025 / Kira estimates], 3PL/4PL at ~10% [Kira estimates]. Road freight — 70% of the volume base — compounds at 6-7% in line with the headline [Kira estimates].

Operator count signals fragmentation

More than 40,000 logistics operators are registered [MoIT 2025], with the top 10 controlling 80%+ of parcel revenue [Vietdata 2026] but a far smaller share of freight. The long tail of road freight is where structural fragmentation persists.

Cold chain — the highest-CAGR pocket

Vietnam's cold-chain market sits at USD 230 m in 2025 [TheReportCubes 2025] on a capacity base of 1.3 m pallet positions across 117 facilities and 1,499 reefer trucks [GCCA 2024]. Capacity heads toward 1.7 m pallets by 2028 [GCCA 2024] driven by five major projects.

Investment pipeline 2024-2027

Five named projects anchor the next-leg capacity: NECS Tay Ninh (110,000 pallets, USD 80 m) commissioned Dec 2025 [Tuoi Tre 2025]; Nichirei Long Hau (20,000 pallets, Jun 2024) [GCCA 2024]; Yokorei Long An (USD 52 m, 4.5 ha) [TheInvestor 2025]; LOTTE Global Logistics Nhon Trach (USD 34 m, 5.5 ha, May 2026 ops) [Cushman Wakefield 2025]; Lineage Logistics Vietnam expansion [Vnindustry 2025].

Demand concentration

Occupancy reaches 90% in HCMC and 81% in Hanoi [FiinResearch 2025]. The South dominates due to seafood and agricultural export concentration in the Mekong Delta and Long An. Pharma cold chain runs USD 1.2 bn separately [Ken Research 2024], with ~90% of vaccines requiring cold storage [MoH Vietnam 2024].

Rural gap is the structural drag

Only ~30% of rural healthcare facilities have adequate cold storage [Ken Research 2024] while 65% of Vietnam's population is rural-distributed. The cold-chain rural penetration deficit is both a constraint and a 5-7 year opportunity.

Section 06 · Segment Economics

Segment economics — growth, margin, capital intensity

Sub-segment economics diverge sharply. Cold chain and e-commerce logistics are the only pockets where double-digit revenue growth meets defendable unit economics. Road freight grows volume but compresses margin under fuel and labour pressure.

Cold chain — highest margin, highest capex

Cold-chain operators run 20-25% EBITDA margins [Kira estimates based on ABA Cooltrans channel reads, Mekong Capital 2024]. The catch: per-pallet capex sits at USD 1,500-2,500 [Kira estimates]. NECS Tay Ninh's USD 80 m / 110,000 pallet build = ~USD 727 / pallet, helped by automation density [Tuoi Tre 2025].

Last-mile parcel — scale or die

Industry-wide parcel revenue grew 9.3% but the sector is described as "high revenue, slim profits" [VnPlus 2025]. Cost per parcel ~VND 17,000-22,000 [Kira estimates]; price per parcel ~VND 22,000-28,000. Last-mile alone is 28% of total shipment cost [Kenh14 2024]. Operating leverage decides who survives.

3PL — quietly compounding

3PL market sits at USD 6.3 bn [Ken Research 2024], compounding ~10% annually, with FDI-driven contract logistics the most defensible pocket. ESR, Logos, Frasers Property all expanding land banks [Freshfields 2025].

What they're doing

Viettel Post claimed the #1 position in 2025 with 22.3% market share and ~VND 20.8 tn delivery revenue [VTP AR 2024]. The flagship play is the USD 132 m Lang Son Logistics Park opened February 2025 with 200 AGV robots reaching 99% automation [VnPlus 2025] — a deliberate move to lock in unit-economics leadership against e-commerce-captive competitors.

From 26 April 2026, VTP takes over operational stewardship of FedEx's Vietnam network — collection, last-mile, warehousing, customs coordination [Elibook 2026]. This shifts VTP from domestic parcel into international express on rented brand equity.

Strategic posture

The two-anchor structure with SPX creates explicit "avoid the platform fight" strategy: VTP positions logistics infrastructure as its weapon rather than chasing platform-captive volume [Thoibao Nganh Hang 2025].

Strengths

  • Capital depth via Viettel Group parent — defence-sector cross-financing
  • Automated mega-hub at Lang Son sets unit-cost benchmark
  • FedEx mandate opens international express revenue

  • SPX continues to scale within Shopee — could pressure share
  • Driver shortage and 18% wage inflation [VIR 2025] erode margin

What they're doing

SPX Express has scaled from rounding error to #2 by revenue in five years, compounding at 283% CAGR 2020-2023 [Vietdata 2026]. The play is structurally simple: capture nearly all Shopee Vietnam volume (Shopee = 56%+ of national e-commerce GMV [Metric 2026]) on owned infrastructure.

Strategic posture

Vertically integrated with Shopee, SPX is structurally insulated from price wars in non-captive channels. The risk is concentration: Shopee + TikTok Shop together = 97% of GMV [Metric 2026], and TikTok Shop runs its own logistics.

NECS — the new capacity anchor

NECS Tay Ninh opened December 2025 with 110,000 pallets — the largest automated cold storage in Southeast Asia [Tuoi Tre 2025]. Total investment VND 2,000 bn (~USD 80 m) including robotic racking, smart shelving, IoT temperature control, and AI optimization layers [VnExpress 2025]. The facility is bonded ("ngoại quan") — buyer can use stored goods as collateral.

This single project adds ~8% to national pallet capacity. The location at Tay Ninh anchors the HCMC-Cambodia corridor — a deliberate hedge against southern coastal concentration.

ABA Cooltrans — the reefer specialist

ABA Cooltrans runs 205 self-owned refrigerated trucks serving Bach Hoa Xanh, WinMart, Masan [Mekong Capital 2024]. A Mekong Capital portfolio company, ABA is the most operationally specialized cold-chain reefer player — built around "right temperature, right time, right goods."

Strengths (NECS + ABA together)

  • Domestic ownership — fast permitting, local rate flexibility
  • NECS: largest single pallet capacity in country

  • Bonded warehouse status = collateral monetization for owners

  • Capex weight — VND 2 tn at NECS is a payback-period bet on 11-13% CAGR holding

Channel & distribution structure

Two demand engines drive freight + cold growth: e-commerce GMV at USD 16.4 bn [Metric 2026] and modern grocery retail (Bach Hoa Xanh ~2,000 stores, WinCommerce ~4,500 stores) [MWG 2025, Masan 2025]. The channel mix is shifting from traditional GT toward MT + e-com at a pace that pulls cold and parcel infrastructure with it.

E-commerce: the parcel pull

2025 GMV across Shopee/TikTok/Lazada/Tiki reached VND 429.7 tn (~USD 16.4 bn) [Metric 2026], +35% YoY. H1 2025 alone hit USD 8.49 bn [VIR 2025]. Shopee + TikTok Shop = 97% of platform GMV [Metric 2026]. Parcel volumes reached 2.2 bn units in 2024 [Vietdata 2026] and likely 2.7-2.9 bn in 2025 [Kira estimates].

Modern grocery: the cold pull

Bach Hoa Xanh (MWG) crossed ~2,000 stores in 2025 with VND 46,900 bn revenue (+14% YoY) and Northern expansion launched Nov 2025 [MWG 2025]. WinCommerce reached ~4,500 stores with WinMart+ driving 47% rural revenue growth [Masan 2025]. AEON: 8 malls + 180 convenience stores [AEON 2025]. Every new store demands reefer service.

Geographic split

Southern Vietnam = 48.9% of e-comm logistics share [Kira estimates], driven by HCMC concentration (~70% of regional online shopping) and port proximity. The North-South balance shifts as BHX rolls out northern stores.

Section 09 · Regulatory & Policy
020 / 024

Regulatory & policy landscape

The 2025 strategy reset (Decision 2229/QD-TTg) is the most consequential policy event for the sector in a decade. Implementation through provincial action plans, customs digitization, and infrastructure programs runs 2026-2030 — operators with read-throughs on timing have a planning advantage.

Headline policy: Decision 2229

Promulgated 9 Oct 2025 [Decision 2229 2025]: logistics value-added 5-7% of GDP, 12-15% annual sector growth, logistics cost down to 12-15% of GDP from ~16% today [MoIT 2025]. Top-40 LPI target by 2035 (vs 43rd in 2023 [WB LPI 2023]). MoIT issued action plan with specific provincial deliverables.

AI in Vietnam logistics — market sizing & use cases

Vietnam's logistics robots and warehouse-automation market sat at USD 89.6 m in 2024, on track to USD 420 m by 2033 at 18.75% CAGR [Kira estimates]. The bigger story: Viettel Post, VNPost, and Gemadept have all deployed AI in warehouse management, route optimization, and demand forecasting [TMA 2025].

Adoption trajectory

Industrial-robot installation grew ~27% in 2025 [TMA 2025] — one of the fastest in the region. National Robotics Strategy (2023) deploys USD 500 m R&D funding [TMA 2025].

The orchestration shift

The 2025 unlock was orchestration platforms that bind AGV/AMR, AS/RS, conveyors, and human labor into a single WMS execution layer [Logistics Viewpoints 2026]. By 2026, AI becomes a standard decision layer inside WMS — particularly for high-SKU-variability environments like e-commerce fulfillment.

The skills gap

RMIT 2026 frames Vietnam as positioned to climb the robotics value chain — assembly today, sub-systems by 2030 [RMIT 2026]. But the 180k driver-shortage backdrop [VIR 2025] makes automation strategic, not optional.
