How we built this view
This view is built from two evidence streams: primary inputs — analyst synthesis, program tracking across Poland, the Gulf and Southeast Asia, and prime-contractor and offset-partner reads — and secondary anchors — procurement-agency export tallies, listed-company disclosures, and named defense-industry reporting. Every quantitative claim carries an explicit source tag.

Primary inputs

Program and contract tracking
Deal-level tracking of framework and executive contracts — Poland K2 and K9, Chunmoo, Gulf air-defense, and the FA-50 and naval pipeline — surfacing signed value, delivery cadence, and localization terms not visible in headline export totals.
Analyst synthesis
KIRA in-house triangulation across disclosed inputs: procurement-agency export series, prime-contractor backlog disclosures, and program contract values. Tagged inline as [Kira estimates].
Offset and operator reads
Input from offset partners, localization-program participants, and regional procurement specialists closer to the buyer economics than aggregate export tallies.

Secondary anchors

Procurement and trade authorities
Defense Acquisition Program Administration (DAPA) annual export series; SIPRI arms-transfer rankings; government export-ambition statements — cited inline by named source and period.
Listed-company disclosures
Results, backlog and contract disclosures of Hanwha Aerospace, Hyundai Rotem, LIG Nex1 and Korea Aerospace Industries; named defense-industry trade press, cited via English aliases.
Triangulated estimates
Where no single source covers a figure, we combine 2–3 inputs and disclose the method. Tagged [Kira estimates]. Approximately 24% of quantitative claims are KIRA-derived; 76% are externally cited.

Rebound, not yet a new peak

Exports fell from a record USD 17.3 bn in 2022 to USD 9.5 bn in 2024 before recovering to USD 15.4 bn in 2025 [DAPA 2025]. The 2025 figure missed the government's USD 20 bn target but reversed two years of decline, lifted by a USD 4.1 bn third Chunmoo contract with Poland signed in December [Seoul Economic 2026].

A platform-and-pipeline model

Growth concentrates in a few exportable platforms. The K9 holds roughly 52% of the global 155mm tracked-howitzer market [KoreaBizwire 2026]; the K2 is scaling through a second Poland contract with local assembly [KED 2025]. Destinations widened from 14 to 17 countries [DAPA 2025].

Export sizing
07 / 14
A lumpy rebound, carried by a few large European and Gulf contracts.
Exports reached USD 15.4 bn in 2025, +60.4% YoY [DAPA 2025], recovering most of the drop from the 2022 peak. The book is concentrated: a single USD 4.1 bn Chunmoo contract with Poland in December was the decisive swing factor [Seoul Economic 2026], underscoring how program timing — not steady flow — drives the annual line.

Europe is the anchor region

Poland remains the single largest customer, layering K2, K9 and Chunmoo executive contracts on its 2022 framework [KED 2025]. Korea's NATO-member arms share reached ~8.6%, second only to the United States [Herald 2025], as Romania, Finland and Norway added K9 orders [UPI 2026].

Breadth is widening

Destinations rose from 14 to 17 countries and projects from 21 to 28 in 2025 [DAPA 2025]. The Gulf and Southeast Asia entered the mix alongside Europe, easing the dependence on a single Poland cycle that has made the annual figure so volatile [Kira estimates].

Still below the 2022 peak

At USD 15.4 bn, 2025 sits below the USD 17.3 bn 2022 record and the government's USD 20 bn target [DAPA 2025]. SIPRI ranked Korea the world's 10th-largest major-arms exporter over 2020–24 at a 2.2% global share [SIPRI 2025], the base from which the top-four ambition is launched.

Platform engine
08 / 14
Two land platforms — the K9 and K2 — carry most of the export book.
The K9 self-propelled howitzer holds roughly 52% of the global 155mm tracked-howitzer market, with more than 1,500 units exported to over ten countries [KoreaBizwire 2026]. The K2 tank scaled through a USD 6.5 bn second Poland contract for 180 units in 2025 [KED 2025], with hull, turret and gun localization built into the terms.

The K9 is the export workhorse

Cumulative K9 export value passed KRW 14 trn [Seoul Economic 2026], on sales to Poland, India, Norway, Turkey, Finland, Estonia, Australia, Egypt, Romania and Vietnam [KoreaBizwire 2026]. Romania joined as the tenth user-club member, and Norway and Finland added follow-on batches in 2025 [UPI 2026].

The K2 scales on localization

Hyundai Rotem's second Poland deal covers 180 K2 tanks worth ~USD 6.5 bn [KED 2025]; 117 are built in Korea and 63 assembled in Poland by PGZ, with technology transfer for hull, turret, gun and suspension [Notes Poland 2026]. Localization is now the template for European land wins [Kira estimates].

Poland anchors the framework

Poland's 2022 framework covered 672 K9 howitzers and a large Chunmoo tranche [KED 2025]; by 2025 it had received 364 K9 units and inked a third Chunmoo execution contract worth USD 4.1 bn [Seoul Economic 2026]. The framework still has multi-year delivery and follow-on volume to convert.

Regional pipeline
10 / 14
The Gulf turns into a high-value market; Southeast Asia opens deliberately.
Korea's Middle East exports tripled from USD 241 m in 2019 to ~USD 748 m in 2024 [Herald ME 2025], led by a USD 3.2 bn Saudi Cheongung-II air-defense deal [GDC 2025] and repeat UAE KM-SAM purchases. Southeast Asia opened with Vietnam's first K9 order and a 12-unit Philippine FA-50 follow-on [KED VN 2025] [KED FA50 2025].

The Gulf buys air defense at scale

Saudi Arabia signed a USD 3.2 bn deal for the Cheongung-II (KM-SAM II) system with LIG Nex1 [GDC 2025], and the UAE became a repeat KM-SAM buyer [WashInst 2025]. Gulf air-defense and MRO demand now anchors a multi-trillion-won regional market, with localization partnerships emerging [Kira estimates].

Southeast Asia is the deliberate opening

Hanwha Aerospace's USD 276 m, 20-unit K9 sale to Vietnam is the platform's first Southeast Asian win [KED VN 2025]. The Philippines added 12 FA-50 light combat aircraft worth ~USD 700 m [KED FA50 2025] and is pursuing KF-21 delivery later this decade [ArmyRec 2026].

FA-50 is the regional air anchor

KAI has exported 140-plus FA-50 units to six countries — the Philippines, Indonesia, Malaysia, Thailand, Iraq and Poland [Flight Global 2025]. Malaysia's 18-unit order and follow-on options give Korea an installed base for upgrades, support and KF-21 conversion across ASEAN [Kira estimates].

AI and autonomy
12 / 14
AI lands first where it raises hit-rate and compresses the production line.
For an export book built on land and air-defense platforms, AI's near-term payoff is operational: guided-munition accuracy, uncrewed-system pairing, and production throughput on a backlog that must be delivered over years. Korean primes are embedding autonomy into next-generation platforms rather than selling AI as a standalone line [Kira estimates].

Precision and air defense

The Cheongung-II air-defense system is credited with a ~96% intercept-test success rate, the kind of AI-assisted fire-control performance that anchors Gulf demand [Data Economy 2026]. Guided artillery and loitering munitions extend the same precision advantage across the land portfolio.

Uncrewed and manned-unmanned teaming

Primes are pairing crewed platforms with drones and robotic vehicles — the K2 and next-gen ground systems with uncrewed scouts, the KF-21 with loyal-wingman concepts — to keep the export portfolio competitive against US and European autonomy roadmaps [Kira estimates].

5-year outlook
13 / 14
Toward USD 30 bn in orders by 2030 — execution sets the path.
Industry officials forecast overseas orders climbing toward USD 30 bn by 2030 from an estimated USD 10 bn base [Modern Diplomacy 2025], in line with the top-four ambition. The variance is set by three levers: European follow-on conversion, Gulf and SEA pipeline maturation, and how fast localization commitments can be delivered without eroding margin.

Methodology endnote and source mix

How to read the numbers

This view triangulates two evidence streams. Where a figure carries a named source tag — for example [DAPA 2025], [KED 2025], [SIPRI 2025] — the underlying data comes from that source, with the full citation listed opposite. Where a figure carries [Kira estimates], our analysts derived it from a blend of disclosed inputs — procurement-agency export series, prime-contractor backlog disclosures, program contract values, and offset-partner reads — using the method noted in the relevant section.

Approximately 24% of quantitative claims are KIRA-derived; 76% are externally cited. Annual export figures are on a DAPA basis in USD; company backlog and profit figures are in KRW as disclosed, on a quarter-end or full-year basis as labeled. Contract values reflect disclosed signed value and may span multi-year delivery.

Company 2025 figures reflect disclosed results and consensus estimates as of early 2026; some full-year profit figures are estimates pending audited accounts. The Middle East export series and the AI-leverage scoring are directional reads, not audited series. Forecast scenarios are KIRA constructions anchored to the 2025 export base and the official USD 30 bn / 2030 ambition. Korean-language sources are cited via English aliases for readability.
