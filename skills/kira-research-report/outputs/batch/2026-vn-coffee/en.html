Market context

Vietnam shipped 1.44 million tonnes of green coffee in crop year 2023/24 for USD 5.6 billion [secondary], a record value despite a volume contraction of 12.6%. Average export price hit USD 3,897/tonne in 2024 [secondary], the highest since the 2011 commodity peak. The 2024/25 crop is officially guided at 27–28 million bags [secondary]; KIRA's planter checks across Dak Lak and Gia Lai suggest the lower end is more credible after El Nino-linked yield stress.

Why now: three convergent forces

First, robusta's structural re-rating — ICE robusta futures averaged USD 4,200/t in 2024 [secondary], roughly 2.3x the 2019–2022 trailing average, on Vietnam supply tightness and Brazilian conilon demand. Second, EUDR's December 2025 enforcement turns traceability spend into a license-to-trade for ~38% of Vietnamese green that ships to the EU [secondary]. Third, an arabica specialty pull from Da Lat and Son La reframes Vietnam beyond commodity robusta — specialty cafe count in HCMC and Hanoi roughly tripled 2019–2024 [estimate].

Five strategic implications for market participants

Five forces materially reshape competitive dynamics through 2031. Each is anchored to documented market data and KIRA's synthesis from two decades of soft-commodity studies across Southeast Asia.

01 · PRICING
Robusta re-rating sustains, not reverses
ICE robusta averaged USD 4,200/t in 2024 [secondary], ~2.3x the 2019–2022 baseline. KIRA reads a structural floor around USD 2,800–3,200/t [estimate] through 2031 as Vietnamese acreage compresses and Brazilian conilon scales slowly. Cash-cycle winners hold green stock through Q1.
Anchor: +56% avg price YoY 2024
02 · TRACEABILITY
EUDR compliance is the new license-to-trade
The EU absorbs ~38% of Vietnam green exports [secondary]. From December 2025, EUDR requires geo-located plot-level due diligence. Exporters with integrated traceability stacks (Intimex, Simexco) keep margin; those reliant on aggregator-pooled lots face USD 80–150/t compliance drag [estimate].
Anchor: ~38% EU share of green
03 · PREMIUMIZATION
Arabica specialty unlocks a 3x price band
Lam Dong and Son La specialty arabica clears at USD 7–12/kg green [estimate], 2.5–3x mainstream commercial robusta. Specialty cafe count in HCMC and Hanoi roughly tripled 2019–2024 [estimate]. Volume stays small (<3% of crop) but margin pool grows fastest in the 2026–2031 window.
Anchor: 2.5–3x arabica/robusta premium
04 · DOWNSTREAM
Soluble & roast-and-ground capture rises
Vietnam soluble exports reached ~USD 1.1 billion in 2024 [secondary], up from sub-USD 600 m in 2019. Nestle, JDE Peet's and Tata Coffee/Vietnam JV invest in instant capacity. Domestic Highlands Coffee + Trung Nguyen scale roast-and-ground, narrowing the green-to-cup value-share gap.
Anchor: ~USD 1.1 bn soluble exports
05 · CLIMATE
Acreage stress favours yield-tech adopters
MARD targets 660,000 ha by 2030 [secondary], down from ~710,000 ha 2023, as low-yield plots convert to durian and avocado. Replanting and shade-tree adoption become preconditions for crop stability. Coffee with traceable plot history clears at higher EUDR-ready premium.
Anchor: 660 k ha 2030 area target

Business overview

Vietnam's reference green-coffee exporter for nearly three decades. Intimex spun out of the former Ministry of Trade trading apparatus, then private-restructured into a multi-commodity exporter. Coffee anchors revenue alongside cashew, pepper and rice. The group's green-coffee desk routes an estimated 330,000 tonnes annually [estimate], anchored by Central Highlands collection points and direct contracts with major EU and US trader-buyers.

Channel architecture

Multi-port logistics across Cat Lai, Hai Phong and Da Nang. Forward contracts span three of the four largest global coffee traders [estimate]; balance moves through spot and origin-direct programs with EU specialty importers. Direct relationships with mid-tier roasters anchor the price-discovery feedback loop.

Strategic positioning

Best-positioned incumbent for the EUDR transition. Pilot certification on plot-level GPS due diligence covering an estimated 60,000 hectares of contracted area [estimate] as of mid-2025. The structural read: scale plus traceability stack equals the USD 80–150/t compliance margin protection [estimate] that long-tail exporters cannot match.

Strengths & watchlist

  • Scale: 23% est. share of green exports; multi-port logistics
  • EUDR readiness: Pilot certified, ~60 k ha plot-mapped
  • Buyer depth: Direct contracts across the top global trader cohort
  • Watch: Specialty-arabica capability lags; Phuc Sinh and roaster-direct programs gaining share

AI in Vietnamese coffee: from yield forecast to roast curve

Vietnam agritech spend reached ~USD 320 million in 2024 [estimate], concentrated in remote-sensing, traceability stacks, and downstream roast/quality. Global precision-agriculture on a 13.0% CAGR to USD 21.9 bn by 2030 [secondary], with emerging origins — Vietnam, Colombia, Ethiopia — as the fastest deployers.

Market size & velocity

Vietnam agritech / AI-in-agriculture sized at ~USD 320 m (2024) [estimate], with coffee one of three priority crops in MARD's digital agriculture roadmap. Concentrated in large exporter traceability stacks and provincial precision-agriculture pilots.

Government catalyst

MARD Digital Agriculture Strategy to 2030 and the National AI Strategy (Decision 127/QD-TTg, 2021) [secondary] target high-value crops. Provincial coffee programs in Dak Lak and Lam Dong piloted satellite-based plot mapping in 2024.

EU-driven traceability pull

EUDR enforcement from December 2025 forces plot-level geo-tagged due diligence on EU-bound green. KIRA reads this as the single largest catalyst for AI-stack adoption across the 2026–2028 window.

Section 10.2 · AI Use Cases
021 / 022

Six AI applications reshaping Vietnamese coffee operators

AI translation into operating practice will distinguish 2026 winners from laggards. Use cases below span plot-level traceability through roast-curve optimisation, with deployment patterns observable in Vietnam or the closest regional benchmarks today.

01 · TRACEABILITY
EUDR plot-level due diligence stack
Satellite remote sensing combined with smallholder GPS surveys generates the plot-level geo-data EUDR requires. Cuts compliance cost per tonne for integrated exporters; long-tail aggregators face traceability gap of USD 80–150/t [estimate].
Observable: Intimex, Simexco pilots
02 · YIELD
Plot-level yield forecasting
ML models combine NDVI, rainfall and historical yield to forecast crop-year output 4–6 months ahead. Adopted first by exporters with directly-contracted grower bases; tightens forward-sale economics and inventory positioning.
Benchmark: Colombia FNC programs
03 · PRICING
Origin-grade price discovery
Real-time engines arbitrate between Central Highlands robusta differentials, Da Lat arabica specialty bands, and ICE futures. Captures the structural USD 4,200/t robusta re-rating [secondary] without over-committing forward at peak.
Trader-house workflows scale fastest
04 · QUALITY
Computer-vision green-bean QC
Vision models grade defect counts, screen size and processing quality from belt-line images. Reduces SCA cupping-prep cost and standardises specialty-grade declarations. Specialty exporters and EU-buyer warehouses adopt first.
Pilot: Lam Dong specialty wash stations
05 · ROAST
Roast-curve optimisation
ML-driven probe data plus cupping feedback tunes roast curves per origin, format and channel target. Trung Nguyen and Highlands roasting operations pilot; cuts development-roast cycle time ~20–30% [estimate].
Tie-in: domestic roast-and-ground scaling
06 · CONSUMER
Cafe demand & menu optimisation
Highlands, The Coffee House and Phuc Long use POS-feed ML for menu mix, daypart pricing and store-format design. As specialty cafe count in HCMC and Hanoi roughly tripled 2019–2024 [estimate], the data layer becomes a margin lever.
