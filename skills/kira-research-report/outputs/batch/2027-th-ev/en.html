Chinese-OEM-led, not incumbent-led

EV sales reached roughly 120,300 units at 19.4% of the total market in 2025 [Nation 2026]. Chinese brands — led by BYD, MG and GWM — hold 70-80% of BEV volume, with seven of the top-ten EV brands Chinese [MarkLines 2026]. Chinese share of the total auto market rose from 3.2% in 2020 to 21.2% in 2025 [MarkLines 2026], a structural handover no incumbent has reversed.

The used market is the unhedged risk

A new-car price war cut Chinese BEV stickers an average -13.1% at Motor Expo 2024 [Krungsri 2025], with discounts reaching 38% by late 2025 [Bloomberg 2025]. That reset cascades into residuals: a one-year-old EV can shed ~30% on new-price cuts and another ~30% as a used unit [Bangkok Biz 2025]. The used-car market fell ~10% to 285,000 units in H1 2025 [Thansettakij 2025].

Section 03 · Strategic Implications
005 / 029

Five strategic implications for the next 24 months

Market participants should treat the mid-2026 to mid-2028 window as the structural decision point — not the 2030 30@30 target. Production-mandate compliance, residual-value engineering and channel choices made now lock in cost and trust positions through the end of the decade.

IMPLICATION · 01
Engineer residual value, not just sticker price
A one-year-old EV sheds ~30% on price cuts plus ~30% used, versus 5-10%/yr for ICE [Bangkok Biz 2025]. Battery-health certification and buy-back guarantees are now the demand lever — the buyer's real cost is total ownership minus residual, not the discounted entry price.
~30%+30% Y1 loss
IMPLICATION · 02
The EV3.5 mandate forces local output regardless of demand
EV3.5 ties subsidies to a 1:2 import-to-local ratio by 2026 and 1:3 by 2027 [BOI 2025]. Brands must build locally even into a soft market, so export capacity — not domestic absorption — becomes the survival hedge. The board now counts one export unit as 1.5 toward the obligation [BOI 2025].
1:3 ratio by 2027
IMPLICATION · 03
Win the EEC as an export base, not just a sales market
BYD's Rayong plant exported ~10,250 units in 2025, including to Europe to sidestep EU tariffs [CnEVPost 2025]. Thailand's value as a right-hand-drive ASEAN hub outlasts the domestic price war — BOI EV-chain investment topped THB 137bn [BOI 2025].
THB 137bn EV capex
IMPLICATION · 04
Incumbent share is contestable via hybrids, not BEVs
Toyota pledged THB 55bn to retool Thai lines for hybrids [Nation 2025]; BOI now offers an HEV incentive for ≥THB 3bn investments [Legal500 2025]. Japanese majors cede BEV volume but defend the mass market where charging density and residual anxiety still bite.
THB 55bn Toyota HEV
IMPLICATION · 05
Plan for the elimination round — not every brand survives
NETA's share fell from 12% in 2023 to 4% in 2025, dealers from 66 to 53 [Nation 2025]. The 2025-2027 window is an "elimination round" for the 100-plus EV models on sale [ThaiPBS 2025]. Distribution and after-sales capacity, not launch cadence, decide the cull.

Section 04 · Macro Context
007 / 029

Macro indicators shaping EV demand

Thailand's economy grows slowly — 2.1% in 2025 and 1.6% in 2026 [IMF 2026] — with high household debt constraining new-car demand. The EV surge is supply- and policy-driven, layered onto a 600,000-strong auto-manufacturing workforce, not onto strong consumer pull.

Growth pace

The IMF puts 2025 GDP growth at 2.1% and 2026 at 1.6% [IMF 2026], slowing from 2.5% in 2024 on weaker exports, tariff uncertainty and a soft tourism rebound. Auto demand is cyclically pressured — total vehicle sales remain below the 2018-19 peak even as EV share climbs [Kira estimates].

Household debt & credit

Household debt near 90% of GDP keeps auto-loan approval tight [Kira estimates]; rejection rates on pickup and passenger-car financing rose through 2024-25 [Bangkok Biz 2025]. This is why the price war is so destructive — discounts substitute for credit access, and that erodes residual values across the parc.

Inflation & rates

Headline inflation tracked near -0.1% in 2025 and an estimated 0.4% in 2026 [IMF 2026], below the Bank of Thailand's 1-3% target until 2027. Low inflation keeps nominal sticker cuts visible and sharp, intensifying the deflationary EV-pricing spiral.

Section 04 · Macro Context
008 / 029

Policy ladder: from EV3 to the 30@30 target

Thailand's EV transition runs on an incentive ladder anchored by the 30@30 production target [BOI 2025] and the EV3 / EV3.5 subsidy packages. The next rungs — the EV3.5 local-production deadline and the BOI's anti-oversupply refresh — set the 2026-2027 demand window.

Anchor framework

The 30@30 policy targets zero-emission vehicles at 30% of total production by 2030 — about 725,000 cars and 675,000 motorcycles [BOI 2025]. EV3 (2022-23) and EV3.5 (2024-27) layer purchase subsidies, excise cuts (8%→2%) and import-duty relief on top [EY 2024].

EV3.5 production mandate

EV3.5 ties subsidies to local production: imported CBUs must be offset 1:2 domestically by 2026 and 1:3 by 2027 [BOI 2025]. A 2025 refresh added export flexibility — one export unit counts as 1.5 — explicitly to prevent domestic oversupply [Autospinn 2025].

Subsidy & tariff

Passenger BEVs under THB 2m drew rebates of THB 100,000 (2024) and THB 75,000 (2025) at ≥50 kWh [EY 2024]. Cars under THB 7m get excise cut to 2% [AseanBriefing 2024]. The HEV incentive (≥THB 3bn investment) [Legal500 2025] now hedges the all-BEV bet.

Section 05 · Sector Sizing
010 / 029

Market size & growth: Thailand EV 2022-2030

EV sales hit ~120,300 units at 19.4% share in 2025 [Nation 2026], with BEV registrations near 100,000 [USABC 2026]. BEV share of new passenger cars crossed into the high-teens, while domestic BEV production rose to 70,914 units, up 632% YoY [FTI 2026].

Registrations & share

Total EV sales of ~120,300 in 2025 lifted EV share to 19.4% [Nation 2026]. BEV registrations alone reached a record ~100,000 [USABC 2026]. In September 2025, passenger-BEV sales grew 99% YoY, briefly outpacing ICE car growth [EV-Roads 2025].

The supply-demand gap

Domestic BEV output rose to 70,914 units in 2025, up 632% from 9,688 in 2024 [FTI 2026] as EV3.5 mandates bit. BYD's inventory ran 3-4 months — about the dealer ceiling [Nation 2025]. Supply now leads demand, which is the root of the price war.

Price convergence

Average BEV retail price fell to ~USD 35,000 (THB 1.19m) — near ICE parity at ~USD 34,000 [IEA 2025]. With the entry-price advantage gone, the contest moves to financing access, after-sales and residual value, not headline cost.

Section 06 · Segment Economics
011 / 029

Segment economics: BEV, PHEV and the hybrid hedge

The BEV segment carries the headline growth and the price war; HEV/PHEV is the incumbents' defensive lane. Export volume is the emerging third segment, turning local mandates into a regional supply position rather than dead inventory.

BEV — growth and bloodletting

BEV is where Chinese OEMs hold 70-80% share [MarkLines 2026] and where discounts run deepest — newly launched models cut -16% to -21% at Motor Expo 2024 [Krungsri 2025]. Volume growth is real but margins are negative for several challengers carrying 3-4 months of stock [Nation 2025].

HEV/PHEV — the defensive lane

Japanese majors lean into hybrids where range anxiety and residual risk still favor a fuel-backed drivetrain. Toyota's THB 55bn retool [Nation 2025] and the BOI HEV incentive [Legal500 2025] formalize this multi-pathway hedge against an all-BEV market that is oversupplied.

Export — the survival segment

BYD's Rayong plant shipped ~10,250 units abroad in 2025, including to Europe [CnEVPost 2025]; GWM exports HEVs across ASEAN [MarkLines 2026]. Export demand absorbs mandated local output, converting the EV3.5 burden into a regional hub advantage.

Section 08 · Used-Car Disruption
020 / 029

The used-car residual shock

The price war does not stop at the showroom. Each new-car discount resets the residual of every EV already on the road — and the used-car trade is buckling. The used-car association warned in late 2025 that the EV price war risks spreading damage market-wide [Autolife 2025].

Depreciation stacks

A new EV can shed ~30% on sticker cuts within its first year, then lose roughly another ~30% as a used unit — versus 5-10%/yr for a used ICE car [Bangkok Biz 2025]. The used-EV market stays small and "price-unstable" because the technology turns over fast and few units reach resale [Bangkok Biz 2025].

Battery-health anxiety

Sellers rush to offload EVs over battery-degradation fears, stripping the "premium" image from electric cars [Bangkok Biz 2025]. Without trusted state-of-health certification, buyers price in worst-case battery replacement, deepening the residual discount.

Trade flow contracts

The broader used-car market fell ~10% to 285,000 units in H1 2025 [Thansettakij 2025]. New-car discounting pulls buyers out of the used channel, while finance houses tighten on EV collateral whose value is hard to underwrite [Bangkok Biz 2025].

Section 08 · Channels & Charging
021 / 029

Charging density and the route to market

Charging supply has scaled fast — 3,720 stations and 11,622 chargers by March 2025 [Roland Berger 2025] — but DC fast-charging remains the gap against the 2030 target. Distribution is shifting from incumbent dealer networks toward Chinese-brand experience stores and one-price used platforms.

Charging build-out

Thailand had 3,720 charging stations and 11,622 chargers by March 2025, ahead of the original 2025 target [Roland Berger 2025]. EA Anywhere runs 430+ stations / ~1,800 heads [EA 2025]; PTT's EV Station PluZ exceeds 650 units and targets 7,000 charge points by 2030 [Green Energy TH 2025]; PEA VOLTA exceeds 420 stations [Motorist 2025].

DC fast-charging is the gap

The 12,000-DC-charger 2030 goal is still distant [Motorist 2025]. Highway and inter-city DC density — not urban AC points — is the binding constraint on four-wheeler confidence and on used-EV resale appeal outside Bangkok [Kira estimates].

Channel shift

Chinese brands lead with experience stores and aggressive digital retail, while incumbents lean on legacy dealer + service depth. One-price used platforms are positioning to certify EV battery health — the missing trust layer in the resale channel [Kira estimates].

Section 09 · Regulatory Landscape
023 / 029

Policy pivots from stimulus to supply discipline

Through 2025 the BOI shifted from pure demand stimulus toward managing oversupply and rewarding exports. EV-chain investment topped THB 137bn [BOI 2025], but the production mandate now risks forcing local output into a market that cannot absorb it.

The mandate tightens

EV3.5 requires CBU imports to be offset by local production 1:2 in 2026 and 1:3 in 2027 [BOI 2025]. Brands that took early subsidies must now build locally — even into soft demand — or repay incentives, which is why output rose 632% as sales growth slowed [FTI 2026].

Oversupply guardrails

A November 2025 EV-Board refresh added flexibility — counting one export unit as 1.5 toward the obligation — explicitly to prevent domestic oversupply [Autospinn 2025]. BOI investment in EVs, batteries and parts reached THB 137-140bn by late 2025 [BOI 2025].

The hybrid hedge

The BOI's HEV incentive (≥THB 3bn investment over four years) [Legal500 2025] formally backs the Japanese multi-pathway view. Policy is now diversifying away from an all-BEV bet toward a managed, export-led transition.

AI in Thailand's EV economics: where it moves the needle

AI's highest-value role here is not in the car — it is in certifying battery health and pricing residuals. Solving the used-EV trust gap is worth more to the 2027 market than any in-cabin feature, because it unlocks the resale channel that the price war broke.

The trust-gap thesis

Used-EV prices are unstable largely because battery condition is opaque [Bangkok Biz 2025]. AI-driven state-of-health (SoH) and remaining-useful-life (RUL) estimation can turn that opacity into a credible, certifiable number [EV Engineering 2025].

From passive to adaptive BMS

AI/ML battery management — using historical fleet data to estimate SoC, SoH and RUL in real time [Monolithic 2025] — supports predictive maintenance, anomaly detection and longer battery life [MDPI 2025], directly lifting residual confidence.

Inventory discipline

Demand sensing across the dealer network can cut the 3-4 months of stock that fuels the price war [Kira estimates], aligning the EV3.5-mandated output with where it can actually clear.
