How we built this view

This view draws on two evidence streams: primary inputs — producer filings, trade-association data, and analyst synthesis — and secondary anchors — statistical authorities, ministry plans, and triangulated estimates. Every quantitative claim carries an explicit source tag.

Primary inputs

Trade-association & customs data
Monthly cement and clinker export volumes, destination splits, and consumption tallies published by the national cement association and customs — the closest read of real tonnage and pricing pressure.
Producer filings & price notices
Listed and state-group financial disclosures plus dealer price circulars — surfaces real margins, real loss-making, real cost pass-through on coal and power.
Analyst synthesis
KIRA in-house triangulation across capacity, utilization, and the export-versus-domestic split. Tagged inline as [Kira estimates].

Secondary anchors

Government plans & decrees
The national carbon-market decree, the pilot emission-quota allocation, the clinker export-tax adjustment, and public-investment budgets — cited inline by named source.
Trade-remedy & CBAM texts
Philippine anti-dumping and safeguard rulings and EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism guidance — the policy frame around the export book.
Triangulated estimates
Where no single source covers a figure — e.g. realized 2027 utilization — we combine 2–3 inputs and disclose the method. Tagged [Kira estimates].

Vietnam's cement market — a 2027 decarbonization turn

A USD 4.1 bn market in 2025 carries roughly 122 Mt of installed capacity against under 70 Mt of domestic demand. Record exports relieve the surplus; a tightening of trade and carbon walls, not demand, now sets which producers thrive to 2027.

The setup

Vietnam's cement market reached USD 4.12 bn in 2025, growing 6.6% with a 6.3% CAGR projected to 2029 [Industry Databook 2025]. The country became the third-largest producer globally, overtaking the United States, and remained the largest cement exporter [Cemnet 2025]. Total consumption crossed 100 Mt, of which exports of cement and clinker reached about 37 Mt, near 32% of the total [VNCA 2025].

The constraint

The structural problem is supply, not demand. Installed capacity near 122 Mt across 92 lines dwarfs domestic demand below 70 Mt [MOC 2025]; utilization ran only 64% in 2024 [Industry Databook 2025]. Exports vent the surplus, but Philippine trade remedies, EU CBAM, and a domestic carbon market now reprice that escape valve toward low-clinker, lower-carbon product [Decree 29/2026].

Section 03 · Executive Summary
005 / 017

Five strategic implications for market participants

Five forces govern who captures value in Vietnam's cement build-out through 2027. Each is anchored to documented data and KIRA synthesis.

01 · DEMAND
Public investment carries the recovery
Domestic cement demand is set to grow 10.2% to 65.9 Mt in 2026 [Cemnet 2026], pulled by a public-investment budget near USD 41.5 bn, up 12% [MOF 2026]. Expressway and rail megaprojects, not housing alone, anchor the floor.
Anchor: +15%/yr demand to 2027
02 · EXPORT RELIEF
The export vent stays open, narrowly
A clinker export-tax cut from 10% to 5% (to end-2026) reopened the relief valve [VNCA 2025]; exports rose 17% YoY in 9M25 [VNCA 2025]. But the cut is temporary — its renewal is a 2027 policy variable, not a given.
Anchor: 10%→5% clinker tax
03 · TRADE WALLS
Destination markets are closing
The Philippines — a top destination — imposed anti-dumping duties of 4–28% on OPC and 3–55% on blended cement, plus a 2025–2028 safeguard [Antidumping VN 2025]. Destination diversification is now a survival mandate.
Anchor: 5-yr PH duty
04 · CARBON
Clinker intensity becomes a P&L line
From 2026, 51 cement plants sit inside the pilot ETS with a 268.4 Mt CO₂e cap [Decree 29/2026]; CBAM's definitive phase began 1 Jan 2026 [EC 2026]. Carbon per tonne now prices directly into export competitiveness.
Anchor: 268.4 Mt 2026 cap
05 · INVESTMENT
Low-clinker capex is the moat
Waste-heat recovery, alternative fuels, and LC3 / calcined-clay substitution are the documented levers; a supported pathway targets −46.7 Mt CO₂e over ten years [UNIDO 2024]. Early movers convert compliance into a cost and access edge.
Anchor: −46.7 Mt CO₂e potential

Section 04 · Market Sizing
007 / 017

Capacity outruns demand by more than 50 million tonnes

Two numbers define the market. Installed capacity near 122 Mt against domestic demand below 70 Mt — a structural surplus that fierce price competition and exports must absorb each year.

The surplus

Vietnam runs 92 production lines with capacity above 122 Mt/yr, with design capacity capped near 125 Mt by 2025 [MOC 2025]. Domestic demand sits under 70 Mt, leaving an excess of tens of millions of tonnes [Vietnam.vn 2025]. Utilization improved from 58% in 2023 to 64% in 2024 [Industry Databook 2025] — still well below the level that supports healthy pricing.

The value

Market value reached USD 4.12 bn in 2025 at 6.6% growth, with a 6.3% CAGR projected to 2029 [Industry Databook 2025]. Total cement-and-clinker consumption crossed 100 Mt, with domestic at ~60–65 Mt and exports ~37 Mt [VNCA 2025].

The read

The surplus is not cyclical — it is built into the asset base. [Kira estimates] place realized 2027 utilization in the mid-to-high 60s even on a firm recovery, keeping exports a permanent relief channel rather than an opportunistic one.

Section 04 · Demand Engines
008 / 017

Three engines pull domestic demand back up

Recovery rests on a documented spending wave. A public-investment surge, social-housing build-out, and a thawing property market each lift volume — and infrastructure leads the pull.

Public investment

The 2026 public-investment allocation could rise 12% to about VND 1.08 quadrillion (USD 41.5 bn) [MOF 2026]. Eighty priority projects worth USD 18.4 bn were green-lit in April 2025, with transportation taking ~48% of 2025 outlays behind expressway and high-speed-rail megaprojects [Hanoi Times 2025].

Housing

Social-housing supply could rise ~45% in 2025 and ~50% in 2026 [Hanoi Times 2025], adding durable finishing-material demand. Combined, infrastructure and housing lift cement demand an estimated 15% a year through 2027 [Hanoi Times 2025].

The trajectory

Domestic consumption is projected to grow 10.2% to 65.86 Mt in 2026 [Cemnet 2026], with 1Q26 production already up 22% YoY [Cemnet 2026]. [Kira estimates] see the recovery firming into 2027 but not closing the surplus alone.

Section 05 · The Export Book
010 / 017

Record tonnage, but a thinning per-tonne return

Exports are the pressure valve that keeps lines running. Clinker is leading the 2025 surge, but average prices are falling and the destination mix is shifting under trade defence.

The volumes

Total exports rose 17% YoY to 26.44 Mt in 9M25 — clinker up 39% to 10.63 Mt and cement up 6% to 15.81 Mt [VNCA 2025]. The full-year figure crossed 37 Mt [VNCA 2025]. Momentum carried into 2026: 1Q26 exports rose 21% to 10.09 Mt [VNCA 2025].

The destinations

In 1Q26, Côte d'Ivoire and Bangladesh led clinker offtake, while the United States, Singapore and the Philippines led cement [VNCA 2025]. The shift toward African and South-Asian clinker buyers reflects defence measures closing nearer markets.

The price

Tonnage growth masks margin erosion: average export prices are falling as competition intensifies [Ximang.vn 2025]. [Kira estimates] see export value lagging volume through 2027 — the book defends utilization, not profitability.

Section 05 · Trade Walls & Relief
011 / 017

Export relief is real but provisional

Two forces pull in opposite directions: a domestic tax cut that reopened the vent, and importing-market defences plus EU carbon rules that narrow it. The net is relief with a hard expiry.

The relief

The clinker export tax was cut from 10% to 5% from late May 2025, extended through end-2026 — a measure the cement association credits with materially supporting exports [VNCA 2025]. Producers also leaned on certificates of origin to widen market access [MOIT 2025].

The walls

The Philippines imposed five-year anti-dumping duties of 4–28% on OPC and 3–55% on blended cement, layered with a 2025–2028 safeguard after import surges since 2023 [Antidumping VN 2025]. Other Asian markets are tightening import restrictions in parallel [Ximang.vn 2025].

The carbon wall

CBAM's definitive phase began 1 January 2026, with Vietnam the EU's second-largest cement supplier [EC 2026]. Direct EU exposure is modest today, but CBAM sets the embedded-emissions standard that other buyers will adopt — making clinker intensity an export-access variable.

Section 05 · Cost & Margin
012 / 017

Energy and carbon costs squeeze the margin

Producers are caught between rising input costs and weak pricing power. Coal and electricity dominate the cost stack, and a carbon price now sits on top — pushing loss-making lines toward consolidation.

The cost stack

Coal and electricity prices have risen with no sign of easing; electricity alone is 15–20% of production cost depending on the plant [Bao Dau Tu 2025]. Producers pushed through a VND 50,000/tonne price rise in early 2025 to defend margins [Bao Dau Tu 2025], but most factories still ran at a loss.

The losses

The pressure is visible in filings: the largest state cement group reported a loss of about VND 1,402 bn in 2024, a second consecutive loss-making year [Vicem 2024]. Weak property demand, oversupply, and energy inflation compounded.

The carbon overlay

From 2026, plants exceeding free ETS allowances must buy them, and CBAM prices embedded emissions on EU-bound tonnes [Decree 29/2026]. [Kira estimates] see carbon adding a structural cost wedge that favours fuel-efficient, low-clinker operators by 2027.

Methodology & sources

How the numbers were derived, and the full citation for every named source used in this view.

Methodology

This view triangulates two evidence streams. Where a figure carries a named source tag (e.g. [VNCA 2025], [Decree 29/2026]), the underlying data comes from that source; the full citation appears at right. Where a figure carries [Kira estimates], KIRA analysts derived it from a blend of disclosed inputs — trade-association data, producer filings, ministry plans — using the method disclosed in the relevant section.

Capacity, consumption, and export figures reconcile association and customs data against producer disclosure; the 2027 scenario fan and realized-utilization reads are KIRA constructions. Approximately 25% of quantitative claims are KIRA-derived; the remainder are externally cited. Research drew on English and Vietnamese-language sources, cited under English aliases.
