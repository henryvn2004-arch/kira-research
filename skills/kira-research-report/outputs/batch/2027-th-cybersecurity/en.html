The market state

Thailand's cybersecurity market was worth roughly USD 440 mn in 2024 and is on a high-single-digit path toward USD 573 mn by 2029 [VMR 2025] [Statista 2025]. Network security still holds the largest share at 61% of spend [GVR 2024], but the growth is concentrated in cloud security and managed services as enterprises outsource defence they cannot staff.

Why now: enforcement as demand engine

The Personal Data Protection Committee issued its first administrative penalty — the maximum THB 7 mn — in August 2024, then layered on THB 14.5 mn across five further cases in August 2025 [PDPC 2025]. With organisations facing 3,180 attacks a week — 70% above the global norm [Check Point 2025] — compliance fear has become a budget line.

Five strategic implications for market participants

Five forces reshape competitive dynamics through 2032. Each is anchored to documented market data and our synthesis of Thailand's regulatory and threat trajectory.

01 · COMPLIANCE
Enforcement converts fear into recurring spend
With PDPA penalties now real — THB 21 mn imposed since August 2024 [PDPC 2025] — boards treat data protection as a budget line, not a policy memo. Providers that bundle compliance automation with monitoring capture the durable, renewable revenue.
Anchor: THB 7 mn maximum single fine
02 · MANAGED
The MSSP model is the growth pool
Managed security services lead the market as firms outsource defence they cannot staff. SMEs grow fastest at a ~16% CAGR [GVR 2024], skewing toward managed detection and subscription bundles. Selling product alone leaves the recurring margin on the table.
Anchor: ~16% SME segment CAGR
03 · TALENT
The skills gap is structural, not cyclical
Thailand lacks 20,000+ cybersecurity professionals [NCSA 2024], and a national plan to train 10,000 by 2026 only partly closes it. Scarcity entrenches the outsourcing logic and rewards providers that operate shared SOCs efficiently.
Anchor: 10k target trained by 2026
04 · CLOUD
Cloud security outpaces the perimeter
Network security still leads at 61% of spend, but cloud security grows fastest at a mid-teens CAGR as fintech, e-commerce, and government cloud scale [GVR 2024]. Budgets pivot from boxes to identity, posture, and workload protection.
Anchor: cloud security ~16% CAGR
05 · AI
AI arms both attacker and defender
AI-driven phishing and deepfake fraud are rampant, while AI detection becomes table stakes [Nation 2025]. An anti-scam decree already prevented ~THB 6 bn in losses in three months [Nation 2025]. Defenders who automate triage win the loss-ratio battle.
Anchor: ~THB 6 bn scam losses averted

Section 06 · The Threat Landscape
007 / 018

An attack surface running hotter than the world average

Thailand's national cyber-response team logged 1,800+ incidents in 2024, and more than 1,000 in the first five months of 2025 alone [NCSA 2025]. Each incident converts a latent compliance risk into a documented one — and a documented risk is a budget request.

Volume far above the norm

Over the six months to January 2025, Thai organisations faced an average of 3,180 attacks per week, against a global average of 1,843 [Check Point 2025]. Attacks here run roughly 70% hotter than the world, concentrated in phishing and financial fraud.

Phishing, scams, and AI

Banking scams and phishing dominate the volume, and criminals increasingly use AI — deepfake voices, generated content — to scale impersonation and bypass bank controls [Nation 2025]. More than 63% of organisations reported a breach, and 52% admitted paying a ransom [Bangkok Post 2025].

The breach cost

In one survey, 68% of Thai firms suffered a significant incident in twelve months, and 34% lost more than THB 3 mn each [Fortinet 2024]. Loss frequency, not just severity, is what moves the buying decision.

Section 07 · PDPA Enforcement
008 / 018

From grace period to fines: the compliance demand engine

Thailand's Personal Data Protection Act took full effect in 2022 but sat in an awareness phase until 2024. The regulator's pivot to real administrative penalties is the single clearest catalyst converting cybersecurity from a discretionary cost into a defensible investment.

The enforcement turn

The PDPC's first penalty — the maximum THB 7 mn on a major e-commerce platform that ran without a data-protection officer — landed in August 2024 [PDPC 2024]. A year later, eight fines across five cases added THB 14.5 mn [PDPC 2025].

Who got fined, and why

The 2025 round reached both sectors: a government agency and its system developer were each fined for a leak of 200,000+ citizens' records; a hospital paid THB 1.21 mn over leaked patient files; a cosmetics company THB 2.5 mn; and a retailer's data processor THB 3 mn [PDPC 2025]. The common thread is missing security measures and poor processor oversight — exactly what managed monitoring and governance services address.

The commercial read

Before 2024, PDPA compliance competed with every other discretionary project. After two enforcement rounds — and a regulator that proactively flagged 5,273 potential leak cases in a single quarter [PDPC 2024] — it competes with nothing: it is mandatory. Vendors that pair compliance automation, DPO-as-a-service, and continuous monitoring convert that mandate into renewable contracts rather than one-off audits.

Section 08 · Market Sizing & Segment Mix
009 / 018

Spend by segment: managed services and cloud lead the mix

Network security still dominates the installed base, but the growth is concentrated in cloud security and managed services. Perimeter spend is mature; the demographic of demand — regulated, under-staffed, cloud-migrating buyers — pulls the market toward subscription and outsourced models into 2027.

Solutions lead, services grow faster

Solutions held about 61% of 2024 spend [GVR 2024], reflecting the installed base of firewalls and endpoint controls. But services — managed detection, consulting, compliance — grow faster as the skills gap forces outsourcing, and cloud-delivered deployments now capture the majority of new buying.

Cloud and SMEs are the engine

Cloud security grows fastest at a mid-teens CAGR, propelled by government-cloud rollouts and fintech adoption [GVR 2024]. Large enterprises still account for ~65% of revenue, but SMEs — pushed by PDPA fines and supply-chain mandates — post a ~16% CAGR, the pivotal incremental engine [GVR 2024].

The headline frame

The total market is about USD 440 mn in 2024, on track for roughly USD 573 mn by 2029 at a ~9.3% CAGR [VMR 2025] [Statista 2025]; longer-horizon reads see it approaching USD 900 mn by 2032.

Section 11 · Regulation
014 / 018

A regime tuned for accountability, not just adoption

Thailand's regulatory stack — the PDPA, the Cybersecurity Act B.E. 2562, and the 2025 anti-scam Royal Decree — is explicitly about enforceable obligations as digital risk steepens. Organisations that align governance, monitoring, and reporting early will absorb the next cycle better than those that resist.

PDPA: the demand-side catalyst

With penalties up to THB 5 mn per offence plus criminal exposure, and over THB 21 mn imposed since August 2024 [PDPC 2025], the PDPA makes data governance, breach notification, and security controls non-negotiable. It is the clearest driver of compliance-led cybersecurity spend.

Cybersecurity Act & the NCSA

The Cybersecurity Act B.E. 2562 (2019) empowers the National Cyber Security Agency to set policy and mandate incident reporting, especially for critical information infrastructure [NCSA 2024]. Reporting duties and penalties push regulated operators toward continuous monitoring.

What it means for participants

The accountability turn favours scale, continuous monitoring, and Thai-language compliance depth. The 2025 anti-scam Royal Decree — which helped prevent ~THB 6 bn in losses and froze ~182,000 accounts in three months [Nation 2025] — shows the state will mandate active defence, not just disclosure.

  • Winners: MSSPs that bundle compliance, monitoring, and reporting
  • Pressured: product-only resellers without a services wrap
  • Pivotal: partners that explain obligations in Thai to SME boards
  • Emerging: cyber-insurance and breach-response packaged with cover

Section 12 · AI in Detection & the Skills Gap
015 / 018

AI moves from pilot to detection core — as talent stays scarce

AI now sits on both sides of the Thai cyber-conflict: criminals scale phishing and deepfake fraud, while defenders automate triage and detection [Nation 2025]. With a 20,000+ professional shortfall [NCSA 2024], AI-augmented managed services are the only way most Thai organisations can staff a credible defence.

AI on the attack side

Cybercriminals exploit AI models to scale impersonation, financial theft, and bank-control evasion; AI-generated phishing and deepfake voices are now rampant [Nation 2025]. The volume and sophistication outrun manual defence, especially for under-staffed SMEs.

AI on the defence side

Thai banks and MSSPs deploy AI for fraud detection, anomaly spotting, and faster blocking of malicious sites, backed by new law [Nation 2025]. By 2026 AI-assisted detection is baseline capability, not a differentiator — the question is who operationalises it at scale.

The skills-gap multiplier

With the talent gap structural, AI is less a cost-cutter than a force multiplier for scarce analysts. Providers that pair automation with a shared SOC stretch limited human expertise across many clients — the economic core of the MSSP model.

Section 13 · Outlook to 2032
017 / 018

Outlook to 2032: steady headline, decisive mix shift

We expect Thai cybersecurity spend to compound in the high single digits, with enforcement and the skills gap driving above-trend expansion in managed services, cloud security, and compliance. The providers that win are those that reposition from product resale toward recurring managed defence now.

Base case

Market spend compounds around 9% to 2029, reaching ~USD 573 mn [Statista 2025] [VMR 2025], with managed services and cloud security outpacing the market as enforcement and the talent gap persist. By 2032 the market approaches ~USD 700 mn on the same trajectory [Kira estimates].

Upside & downside

Upside: harder PDPA enforcement, mandatory critical-infrastructure reporting, and SME compliance uptake. Downside: a budget squeeze deferring discretionary upgrades, or a talent-training surge that slows the outsourcing tailwind near term.

The strategic imperative

Enforcement and scarcity are the most reliable variables in this market. Reposition toward recurring managed defence — compliance, MDR, cloud posture — and invest in AI-augmented shared SOCs. Product-only resellers face the steepest adjustment.
