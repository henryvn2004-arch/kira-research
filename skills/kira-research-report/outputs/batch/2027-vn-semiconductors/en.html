Section 03 · Executive summary

Vietnam climbs from ATP workhorse toward advanced packaging

A decade of low-cost assembly, test and packaging has built the base; the 2025–27 wave of advanced-packaging capital and an emerging IC-design layer mark the inflection. The constraint shifts from attracting capital to supplying engineers — and that gap defines who captures the next leg.

The back-end shift is real and capital-backed

Vietnam's position in the global chain has been back-end by design: assembly, test and packaging (ATP) is labour-intensive, capital-lighter than fabrication, and well-matched to the country's wage and FDI profile. That base is now thickening into higher value. Amkor's USD 1.6 bn Bac Ninh plant — its most advanced globally — began official operation in 2025 Amkor 2025; Samsung has signalled a chip-test plant and a multi-billion advanced-packaging facility in Thai Nguyen Reuters 2026. Cumulative chip FDI has reached USD 14.2 bn across 241 projects MPI 2025.

Talent, not money, is the gate

Vietnam counts roughly 15,000 semiconductor specialists against a 2030 target of 50,000, with packaging alone short an estimated 27,000 workers over five years VietnamPlus 2025. Demand is outrunning training capacity, and most domestic engineers sit in back-end roles rather than full-flow design Kira estimates. The 2027 outcome turns on whether the talent pipeline can keep pace with committed cleanroom capacity.

Section 03 · Strategic implications

Five implications for market participants

The value-chain climb rewards participants who control scarce inputs — cleanroom-ready talent, qualified suppliers, and reliable power — and penalizes those who assume capital alone secures position.

01 · ATP operators

Advanced packaging is the margin frontier

Commodity assembly and test margins compress as labour costs rise. The defensible position is high-value packaging — SiP, 2.5D/3D, high-bandwidth-memory integration — where AI-driven demand is tightest. Operators without an advanced-packaging roadmap will be repriced as commodity subcontractors.

↗ see page 007 — value chain
02 · Anchor MNCs

Lock talent before competitors scale

Intel and Samsung have first-mover advantage on the engineering pool. As Amkor, Hana Micron, and domestic entrants ramp, wage competition for cleanroom and test engineers intensifies. University partnerships and captive training pipelines are now a sourcing strategy, not corporate-social goodwill.

↗ see page 011 — talent constraint
03 · Domestic players

IC design is the sovereign wedge

FPT and Viettel are building a design layer — PMICs, edge-AI SoCs on 28–32nm — that captures IP value without fab capital. This is where Vietnamese firms can hold equity rather than rent labour to foreign principals. The 2027 question is commercial traction, not technical capability.

↗ see page 007 — value chain
04 · Investors

Power reliability gates the thesis

Cleanroom process flows are unforgiving of grid instability — a brief outage can cost millions in scrapped wafers. Sites with captive or priority power (Hoa Lac, SHTP, Bac Ninh zones) carry a structural premium. Diligence must weight grid resilience over headline incentives.

↗ see page 012 — policy & power
05 · Suppliers

The supplier ecosystem is under-built

Materials, gases, precision components, and test sockets remain heavily imported. As ATP volume scales, qualified local supply is the bottleneck and the opportunity. Early-qualified suppliers to Intel and Samsung lines capture durable, sticky demand at the cluster's core.

Section 04 · Market size & capacity

USD 10.2 bn market, 1% of global ATP — and a steep climb planned

Vietnam's semiconductor market reached an estimated USD 10.2 bn in 2025, but the more telling number is share of global packaging and test capacity — barely 1% today, targeted at 8–9% by 2030 as committed cleanrooms come online.

Market size and growth

The domestic semiconductor market is estimated at approximately USD 10.2 bn in 2025, growing toward USD 16.5 bn by 2030 at roughly a 10% CAGR Kira estimates. Most of this is export-driven back-end output: integrated-circuit exports were valued at USD 13.25 bn and semiconductor devices at USD 7.53 bn within Vietnam's USD 126.5 bn electronics export base in 2025 Customs 2025. Semiconductor exports rose from roughly USD 3 bn in 2019 to USD 8.1 bn in 2023, with the United States taking 41% of the flow GSO 2025.

Capacity-share is the real story

Vietnam holds an estimated 1% of global ATP capacity, projected to reach 8–9% by 2030 as Amkor, Hana Micron, Intel, and Samsung lines scale SEMI 2025. Realizing that requires roughly ten packaging-and-test facilities and a small fab — the explicit 2024–30 target under the national strategy Decision 1018 2024.

FDI concentration

Cumulative chip FDI reached USD 14.2 bn across 241 projects by 2025, with Bac Ninh alone drawing around 13.8% of newly registered manufacturing FDI MPI 2025. The northern cluster — Bac Ninh, Thai Nguyen, Bac Giang — concentrates the back-end build.

Section 05 · Value chain

Three layers: commodity ATP, advanced packaging, and an emerging design wedge

Vietnam's back-end is stratifying. Commodity assembly and test remains the volume base; advanced packaging is the value frontier pulled by AI demand; and a small but strategically vital IC-design layer is the only segment where domestic firms hold IP rather than rent labour.

Layer 1 · Volume base

Commodity assembly & test

The historical workhorse — final assembly, burn-in, and electrical test of memory, logic, and discrete devices. Labour-intensive, margin-thin, and the segment most exposed to wage inflation. Intel's SHTP plant and Hana Micron's Bac Giang lines anchor the volume.

~10,000 of 15,000 specialists sit here
Layer 2 · Value frontier

Advanced packaging (SiP, 2.5D/3D)

System-in-package, chiplet integration, and high-bandwidth-memory assembly — the segment AI workloads are pulling hardest. Amkor's Bac Ninh plant leads; Samsung's planned Thai Nguyen packaging facility would deepen it materially.

AI packaging demand ~14% CAGR
Layer 3 · IP wedge

IC design service emergence

60+ design firms and ~7,000 design engineers, led by FPT (PMICs, edge-AI SoC) and Viettel (5G DFE/RFIC). The only layer where Vietnamese firms capture IP value without fab capital — and the strategic priority of the national plan.

First commercial chip export to Japan, 2025
Adjacency · Materials

Supplier ecosystem (under-built)

Specialty gases, substrates, photonics, and test consumables remain largely imported. Coherent's USD 127 m Dong Nai facility for SiC and precision photonics signals the start of a local materials layer feeding AI and datacom chains.

Coherent Dong Nai · USD 127 m
Adjacency · Domestic fab

First small-scale manufacturing

Viettel's Hoa Lac fab on a 27-ha site targets trial production by end-2027; CT Semiconductor is advancing a phase-2 USD 100 m plant. Fabrication remains nascent and capital-heavy — a 2030s story, not a 2027 one.

Viettel Hoa Lac · trial by 2027
Vector · Sovereign

FPT–Viettel value-chain tie-up

A comprehensive partnership spanning training, design, fab, ATP, and commercialization — co-developing edge-AI SoCs on 28–32nm for cameras, drones, and smart devices. The clearest signal of a domestically-owned vertical ambition.

28–32nm edge-AI SoC roadmap

Market positioning

Intel's Saigon Hi-Tech Park facility is its single largest packaging and test base worldwide, accounting for over half of global capacity and more than 6,000 staff Intel AR 2025. Export value climbed from USD 10.31 bn in 2023 to USD 11.67 bn in 2025, with 2026 projected at USD 14.6 bn — a 25% step-up — as relocated lines ramp SHTP 2026.

2027 strategy signals

Intel is moving assembly, packaging and test from Costa Rica to SHTP and scaling EMIB advanced-packaging capacity, with production focused on data-center server chips including Panther Lake and Wildcat Lake on 18A DIGITIMES 2025. Section 07 · Ecosystem pull

Samsung's move turns an assembly base into an ecosystem

Samsung's planned chip-test and advanced-packaging build in Thai Nguyen is more than another factory — it converts a smartphone-assembly footprint into a back-end semiconductor cluster that pulls suppliers, talent, and second-tier investment into the northern provinces.

The Samsung commitment

Samsung — already Vietnam's largest foreign investor at over USD 23 bn committed across decades — plans a roughly USD 1.5 bn chip-test plant in Thai Nguyen validating legacy DRAM and NAND, with operations targeted for late 2027 and a possible USD 2.5 bn second phase Reuters 2026. Separately it has signalled a ~USD 4 bn advanced chip-packaging facility, a USD 2 bn first phase, aimed squarely at AI-driven demand for high-bandwidth memory integration Samsung 2026. The test plant targets annual capacity of 153.3 billion gigabits of DRAM and 255.6 billion gigabits of NAND.

Why test, why now

The test investment is a response to a quiet shortage of legacy memory chips created by the AI boom pulling capacity toward leading-edge nodes Reuters 2026. Locating final test in Vietnam — adjacent to Samsung's existing Thai Nguyen and Bac Ninh footprint — shortens the back-end loop and leverages an established workforce and supplier base Kira estimates.

The ecosystem multiplier

Each anchor expansion pulls a second tier. Bac Ninh leads the nation in newly registered manufacturing FDI at ~13.8% of the total, and electronics drew USD 1.5 bn across 148 projects in the first ten months of 2025 MPI 2025. The northern cluster — Bac Ninh, Thai Nguyen, Bac Giang — now hosts Amkor, Hana Micron, and Samsung lines within commuting distance, concentrating talent and supplier qualification Kira estimates.

Intel and Samsung as gravity wells

The two anchors deepen Vietnam's position in different directions: Intel pulls advanced logic packaging in the south at SHTP; Samsung pulls memory test and packaging in the north. Together they validate the country to the next wave of OSAT, materials, and equipment suppliers evaluating Southeast Asian sites Kira estimates.

The supplier gap

Specialty materials, gases, substrates, and test consumables remain largely imported. Coherent's USD 127 m Dong Nai plant for SiC and photonics is an early local-supply signal, but a qualified domestic supplier base is the cluster's most under-built layer — and its clearest 2027 opportunity Coherent 2025.

Section 08 · Talent constraint

Talent is the binding constraint: 15,000 today against 50,000 by 2030

Capital is committed; cleanrooms are rising. The variable that decides the 2027 outcome is engineers. Vietnam has roughly 15,000 semiconductor specialists against a 50,000 target, and packaging alone faces a 27,000-worker shortfall over five years.

The gap, quantified

Vietnam counts roughly 15,000 semiconductor specialists, of whom about 10,000 work directly in manufacturing, packaging and test — meeting an estimated 20% of real demand Bao Dau Tu 2025. In packaging alone there are ~8,000 workers against demand near 35,000, a 27,000-person shortfall within five years VietnamPlus 2025. The national target is at least 50,000 degree-level personnel by 2030 — ~15,000 in design, ~35,000 in manufacturing, packaging and test Decision 1018 2024.

Training is scaling but lagging demand

The 2025 admissions cycle enrolled over 137,000 students in semiconductor-related fields within 237,000 STEM admissions, with HUST, VNU-HCM and UET launching dedicated programmes MoET 2025. The constraint is speed and depth: most graduates can fill back-end roles, but few are full-flow design engineers, and lab capacity and qualified lecturers lag Kira estimates.

The US partnership lever

Vietnam is one of eight ITSI Fund partners under the US CHIPS Act, with ASU-led ATP teaching labs and curriculum support targeting the 50,000-engineer goal US State 2025.

Section 09 · Policy & power

Generous incentives, a sharpening legal base — and a power-supply risk

The policy stack is unusually favourable: a 2024 national strategy, Resolution 57, a new Corporate Income Tax regime, and US partnership. The countervailing risk is structural — grid reliability, where a brief outage can scrap a wafer lot worth millions.

The policy stack

Decision 1018/QĐ-TTg (September 2024) sets the national strategy to 2030 with a 2050 vision and an explicit 2024–30 build target: 100+ design firms, one small fab, and ten ATP facilities Decision 1018 2024. The Politburo's Resolution 57 frames science, technology and digital transformation as national priorities, and 2025 saw a wave of enabling laws including the Law on Science, Technology and Innovation effective October 2025 VietnamPlus 2025.

Tax and location incentives

Semiconductor R&D, design, production, packaging and test qualify for a preferential 10% CIT rate for 15 years, up to four years of exemption and nine years of 50% reduction PwC 2025. From October 2025 the new CIT law restricts these incentives to designated high-tech parks and centralized IT zones — industrial parks lose eligibility — concentrating value into Hoa Lac, SHTP and similar sites EY 2025. Hanoi and HCMC have stood up national semiconductor clusters with land-rent subsidies up to 50% Kira estimates.

The power-supply risk

Cleanroom process flows are unforgiving of grid instability: even a minor outage can cause losses worth hundreds of millions of dollars DIGITIMES 2025. Some investors have flagged electricity-shortage risk at Vietnam's current demand-growth rate as a reason to reconsider site decisions DIGITIMES 2025. The government response — priority power infrastructure, captive sources at high-tech parks, grid upgrades, and a revised Electricity Law — is real but unproven at semiconductor-scale reliability Kira estimates.

The 2027 self-reliance ambition

The Prime Minister has restated a target of self-reliance in semiconductor design, manufacturing and testing by 2027 — a deliberately aggressive signal of political priority more than a literal capability forecast VietnamPlus 2025. For investors, the durable read is policy stability and incentive depth, not a binary 2027 milestone Kira estimates.

Net assessment

The incentive and policy environment is among the most favourable in Southeast Asia for back-end investment. Power reliability and talent supply — not policy or capital — are the two variables that gate execution through 2027.

Section 12 · Methodology endnote

Methodology and sources

This report triangulates two evidence streams. Where a figure carries a named source tag (e.g. MPI 2025, SEMI 2025), the underlying data comes from that source; the full citation is given to the right. Where a figure carries Kira estimates, KIRA analysts derived the number from a blend of disclosed inputs — government strategy documents, FDI registers, customs trade data, listed-company disclosures, and channel conversations with operators, suppliers, and engineering-talent recruiters — using methodology disclosed in the relevant section.

Approximately 35% of quantitative claims in this report are KIRA-derived triangulations; 65% are attributable to named external sources. Estimates carry the uncertainty inherent in a fast-moving FDI-driven sector where committed capacity, ramp timing, and headcount disclosures vary. Ranges are provided where single-point estimates would overstate precision.

Market figures refer to semiconductor activity within Vietnam — assembly, test, packaging, design, and nascent fabrication — including the export-driven back-end output of foreign-invested operators. All monetary figures in US dollars unless otherwise noted. Local-language sources are cited via English aliases; original Vietnamese-language source names are available on request for traceability.
