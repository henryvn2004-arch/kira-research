Growth, inflation, and the demand floor

GDP grew 8.02% in 2025 [GSO 2026] — the fastest pace since 2007. The World Bank forecasts 6.3% in 2026 [WB 2025]. Inflation accelerated to 5.46% in April 2026 [GSO 2026] on oil-import pressure, but remains inside the SBV's 4.5% annual target. This is a growth economy with a rate-cycle headwind — not a recessionary one.

Growth holds the volume floor

Services contributed 49% of 2025 value-added growth [GSO 2026]; industry contributed 46%. Per-capita GDP reached USD 5,026 [GSO 2026]. For fintech, this matters because the addressable middle-class wallet is widening: nominal GDP per capita grew 8% in USD terms, lifting the spend-able-on-financial-services line.

Inflation pressure tightens funding

The SBV holds the 4.5% inflation target for 2026 despite oil-import pressure [Reuters/SBV 2026]. Deposit beta will rise. Unsecured-consumer-credit fintechs face funding-cost compression first — the operators with cheap parent-bank funding (Cake by VPBank, TPBank's LiveBank stack) hold structural advantage over thin-balance-sheet challengers.

Q1 2026 already softer

Q1 2026 GDP slowed to 7.83% from 8.46% in Q4 2025 [GSO 2026]. Not recession territory — but a reminder that the rate-cycle bite will hit consumer-credit demand before payments volume.

Penetration, urbanization, and policy

85.6 million internet users (84.2% penetration) [DataReportal 2026], 79+ million smartphone users (84.4%) [DataReportal 2026], 41% urbanization heading past 50% by 2030 [GSO 2025]. The pipes are laid — policy is now setting the monetization rules.

Digital plumbing complete

87% of adults hold a bank account [SBV 2025] — up from 31% in 2017. Cashless transaction value in 2025 reached ~28x GDP [VnEconomy/SBV 2026]. NAPAS clears 35-36 million transactions a day [NAPAS 2025]. By any infrastructure benchmark, Vietnam is no longer an early-stage payment market.

Policy floor: cashless mandate

Decree 52/2024 (effective July 2024) defined e-money in law for the first time and raised financial-switching capital requirements to VND 300 bn [SBV 2024]. Decree 94/2025 launched the sandbox in July 2025 [SBV 2025]. Crypto-asset trading pilots launched Jan-2026 under Resolution 05/2025 [MoF 2026].

Urbanization adds the next 10 million

Urbanization above 50% by 2030 [GSO 2025] adds an estimated 10-12 million net new urban adults — the highest-LTV cohort for digital lending and wealth products. Fintech operators with rural-pull capability (agent networks, super-app gig drivers as last-mile) capture this flow first.

Section 05 · Sector Overview
010 / 029

Market size & growth: Vietnam fintech

Multiple definitions in play. Our revenue-pool view (operator topline after interchange) sizes at USD 4.3 bn in 2026 growing to USD 8.9 bn by 2031 [Kira estimates]. The wider transaction-value funnel — what the public reads — is USD 50+ bn. Both numbers are right; they're measuring different things.

Three layers, three numbers

Transaction value (gross volume cleared) is the largest number: USD ~50 bn digital-payment value in 2026 [PwC 2021 + Kira estimates], reaching USD 60 bn+ by 2030. Revenue pool (operator topline after costs of clearing) is ~10% of that — USD 4.3 bn. Net contribution margin is ~25% of revenue. The press cites the first; investors price the third.

Growth split: payments stable, credit accelerating

Payments revenue grows ~12% CAGR through 2031 — volume up, take-rate down. Embedded credit + BNPL revenue grows ~27% CAGR off a smaller base [Kira estimates]. Lending overtakes payments in marginal-dollar contribution by 2029. Wealth/investing is the tail bet — high LTV but slow ramp.

Why CAGR estimates diverge

Public sources cite 15-21% CAGR depending on definition. We anchor at 15% CAGR for the revenue pool, ~18% for transaction value. Higher-CAGR numbers usually count gross flow growth — which double-counts as super-apps intermediate the same retail wallet.

Sub-segment trajectory — 2026

Five sub-segments matter for the strategic read. E-wallet payments dominate volume; BNPL and embedded lending dominate growth. Digital banking is the only sub-segment with profitable scale today.

E-wallet payments — USD 1.6 bn revenue pool

~37% of fintech revenue pool [Kira estimates]. Take-rate compressed from ~120 bps in 2020 to 60-80 bps in 2026 as super-apps and merchants gained leverage. Growth ~11% CAGR — volume offsets price.

BNPL & consumer credit — USD 1.1 bn

26% of pool. BNPL transaction value USD 2.6 bn in 2025 [Allied Mkt 2025], CAGR 26.7% to 2030. Take-rate 4-7% blended — the headline margin segment. Credit-card penetration only 5-6% [Vietnam Briefing 2025] leaves structural runway.

Digital banking — USD 0.9 bn

21% of pool. Cake by VPBank is the only operator at scale-and-profit (USD 12.8 bn TPV, 6.2M users) [VPBank 2025]. Timo, TNEX, Cake combined ~10M customers [Kira estimates]. NIM 3-4% on the lending book.

Wealth, insurance, remittance tail

Combined ~16% of pool. Wealth (Finhay, Tikop) and digital insurance (Igloo, Papaya) are sub-scale; growth high (30%+) but base small.

Section 06 · Segment Economics
012 / 029

Segment economics — take-rates & unit economics

Where the money actually accrues. Take-rate, customer-acquisition cost, and payback period vary by an order of magnitude across the five sub-segments — and the gap is widening as the channel consolidates.

Payments — thin margin, captive flywheel

Blended MDR ~60-80 bps in 2026 [Kira estimates] — from ~120 bps in 2020. CAC fell to USD 2-4 per active wallet user as the top three operators saturated. Payback ~9 months. The volume game is mature; profitability hinges on whether a wallet attaches credit, wealth, or insurance.

BNPL — structural take-rate gift

Merchant-discount-rate 4-7% blended [Kira estimates] — 5-10x payments. Net-loss rate 3-5% on under-USD-200 tickets. CAC USD 6-10 acquired through e-commerce checkout integration. Payback 4-7 months when underwriting holds.

Digital banking — NIM-led

Cake-class digital bank: 3-4% NIM on unsecured personal loans, fee income ~25% of revenue [VPBank 2025 + Kira estimates]. Cost-to-income ratio 35-45% — structurally below traditional banks (60-65%) due to no-branch model. Funding cost runs ~150 bps above incumbent CASA.

Where margin actually compounds

A wallet acquired for USD 3 of CAC pays back in nine months on payments alone. Attach a credit product, and lifetime contribution rises 6-9x with marginal incremental CAC. This is the core operator thesis for 2026-2031.

The attach math

Standalone wallet user: 3-year contribution ~USD 12 (payments only) [Kira estimates]. Wallet + BNPL attach: ~USD 70-90. Wallet + BNPL + digital bank deposit: ~USD 110-140. The capital is in the cross-sell, not the acquisition.

NPL is the bear case

Consumer-credit NPL ratio hit 9.6% in Q3 2024 [SBV/VIR 2024] — concentrated in unsecured consumer credit. Banking-system NPL was 5.3% in Mar-2025 [IMF Art IV 2025]. Fintechs with sub-prime exposure (BNPL bottom-tickets, P2P) face the first hit from any cycle turn.

Super-app channel tax

BNPL distributed through Shopee / Grab carries a ~30-40% revenue share back to the super-app [Kira estimates]. Independent BNPL operators (Fundiin, Kredivo) keep more economics but pay USD 12-18 CAC. The trade-off is structural — no operator wins both axes.

Strategic position

The dominant wallet operator with the deepest merchant network (50,000+ partners). MoMo's flywheel: bill-pay scale drives daily-active behaviour, daily-active drives wallet top-up, top-up funds the credit attach. The 4.2x weekly usage [Cimigo 2024] is the metric competitors can't yet replicate.

Recent moves

  • 2024: Launched "Vi Tra Sau" BNPL post-pay product integrated with Grab checkout.
  • 2025: Deepened AI credit scoring on alternative data (bill-pay history, transaction graph) — targeting thin-file approvals.
  • 2025-2026: Active in SBV regulatory sandbox (eKYC, credit scoring streams).

What we're watching

Three vectors. (1) Credit attach rate — if MoMo can move BNPL/loan attach above 25% of active wallets, it becomes a non-bank lender at scale. (2) IPO timeline — investors expect a 2026-2027 listing window. (3) Grab co-opetition — the 2023 payment-integration deal commoditized rides, but Grab's own super-app finserv push competes for the same wallet.

Strategic implication

For competitors: direct payment-volume competition is closed. The viable angles are (a) niche category depth (B2B payments, government), (b) bank-rails partnership (which VNPay holds), or (c) embedded distribution through super-apps that aren't already MoMo-locked.

Channel mix: QR-led, super-app intermediated

Cashless transactions reached 18 billion in the first 9 months of 2025 [SBV 2025] with total value 260 quadrillion VND (~USD 10 bn at clearing). QR alone grew +54% in volume and +141% in value [SBV 2026]. The channel mix has flipped: digital is no longer the supplement — it's the rail.

QR as default consumer surface

QR payments are now the dominant in-person cashless surface. Five-year average growth: +106% volume and +128% value annually 2021-2025 [SBV 2026]. NAPAS clears 35-36 million transactions per day on average [NAPAS 2025]. The mobile-money MoMo, ZaloPay, VNPay rails sit on top.

Super-app channel concentration

Grab, Shopee and (locally) MoMo's own super-app surface have collapsed acquisition channels into three intermediaries. Embedded finance — BNPL, micro-insurance, embedded lending — runs through these surfaces. Bank-direct acquisition for under-35 cohorts has fallen below 30% of digital-finance new opens [Kira estimates].

Internet + mobile channels still growing

Internet-channel transaction volume +52.9% [SBV 2026]; mobile +36.5%; QR +54.5%. The orthogonal growth signal: customers are using multiple channels concurrently, not switching from one to another. Operators with cross-channel reach (Cake, MoMo) benefit; single-channel apps (early-gen wallets) lose share.

Channel margin waterfall: who keeps the basis point

A USD 100 BNPL transaction. Who keeps the margin and who pays the channel tax. Super-apps and merchants together capture ~60% of operator-side economics on embedded BNPL today — the structural reason wallet operators are pushing direct-merchant attach.

The 100-dong dollar

On a USD 100 BNPL transaction with a 5% MDR: USD 5 in gross take. Net of net-loss provision (~3% on bottom-tickets), processing cost, and the super-app channel tax, the BNPL operator nets ~USD 1.5-1.8 [Kira estimates]. That's the contribution — before CAC amortization and opex.

Why direct merchant attach matters

BNPL operator going direct (merchant-integrated, no super-app intermediation) keeps an extra USD 1.5-2 of the same transaction. The CAC trade-off: direct CAC is 3-4x super-app channel CAC. The math only works for operators with a brand pull strong enough to drive merchant-side demand — today that's only MoMo and one or two challengers.

Cross-border remittance: thin but growing

Remittance to VN ~USD 14 bn annually [WB 2025]. Digital channels (vs traditional MTOs) now ~40% of flow [Kira estimates]. Take-rate compressed from 4-5% to 1.5-2% on digital rails. Margin segment for fintechs is on the receive-side conversion to wallet balance — not the corridor itself.

Section 09 · Regulatory Landscape
023 / 029

Regulatory & policy landscape

Two decrees define the regulatory inflection. Decree 52/2024 gave e-money its first legal definition in July 2024. Decree 94/2025 launched the fintech regulatory sandbox in July 2025. Crypto pilots began Jan-2026. The SBV is the first-class actor — every operator-side strategic move now passes through Hanoi.

Decree 52/2024 — e-money becomes a legal concept

Replacing Decree 101/2012 (effective July 2024) [SBV 2024]. Defined e-money as "VND-equivalent value stored electronically" via prepaid balances at banks or payment-service organizations. Required only banks and licensed entities to issue e-wallets and prepaid cards. Raised financial-switching capital to VND 300 bn — 6x prior — effectively setting a scale floor for new payment-clearing entrants.

Decree 94/2025 — sandbox unlocks experiments

Effective July 1, 2025 [SBV 2025]. Two-year regulator-supervised test window (extendable) for credit scoring, Open API, P2P lending, eKYC, digital lending. Eligibility: 100% Vietnamese ownership, IT systems in-country, executive background checks. The cohort of approved participants is small — selection itself is a competitive signal.

Crypto: from grey to pilot

Resolution 05/2025 + Decision 96/QD-BTC (Jan 2026) launched a pilot administrative framework for crypto-asset trading services [MoF 2026]. Pilot — not blanket legalization. Implications for fintech operators: partnerships with licensed crypto-trading entities will likely shape the next 18 months; direct crypto activity still requires pilot inclusion.

AI in Vietnam fintech — market sizing

AI-in-fintech spend in Vietnam is on a 21% CAGR trajectory through 2030 [Kira estimates], reaching ~USD 600M by 2033. Most of that is internal-operations spend — fraud, underwriting, KYC, claims — not consumer-facing AI. The vendor stack is split between in-house ML teams (MoMo, ZaloPay) and external partners (Trusting Social, regional infra providers).

Where the budget goes

Fraud + AML monitoring: ~35% of AI fintech spend. QR-payment fraud surged +60% YoY in Vietnam and Thailand [GBG 2025] — rule-based legacy systems run 90%+ false-positive rates. ML-driven scoring is now table stakes for sandbox participation.

Where it lands first

Credit decisioning: ~30% of spend. VIB cut credit-card approval to 15-30 minutes via Trusting Social partnership [VIB 2025]. MoMo runs internal ML on alt-data (bill-pay history, transaction graph). Approval-time compression is the headline ROI metric.

Document understanding (KYC, claims): ~20%. High-volume, structured, fast payback. Almost every digital-banking onboarding stack now uses OCR + LLM-assist for ID/utility-bill verification.

5-year outlook: base, bull, bear

Our base case sees the fintech revenue pool reach USD 8.9 bn by 2031 at 15% CAGR. Bull case (penetration converges to regional benchmarks): USD 11.5 bn. Bear case (rate-cycle hits consumer credit, super-app channel tightens): USD 6.8 bn. The wide range reflects credit-cycle and channel-power uncertainty, not headline-payments risk.

Base case — the central scenario

15% CAGR through 2031. Payments revenue grows 11%; BNPL+credit 27%; digital banking 19% [Kira estimates]. Banking-system penetration convergence toward regional benchmarks (Thailand ~95%, Malaysia ~92% bankable share) holds the volume floor.

Bull case — what would it take

+ Successful sandbox-to-licensing transition for P2P + open-API.
+ Crypto pilot expands from administrative to retail.
+ Super-app channel-tax compression as ZaloPay and a third pole counter Grab/Shopee leverage.
Result: 20% CAGR to USD 11.5 bn by 2031.

Bear case — the asymmetric risk

+ Consumer-credit NPL spikes above 12% (currently 9.6%) [SBV/VIR 2024].
+ Sub-scale BNPL operators fail; concentration accelerates but pool shrinks.
+ Rate-cycle compresses NIM at digital banks.
Result: 9% CAGR to USD 6.8 bn by 2031.
