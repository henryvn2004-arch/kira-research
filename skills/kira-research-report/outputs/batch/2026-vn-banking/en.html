Market context

Vietnam ended 2025 with system credit near VND 18.4 quadrillion (~USD 690 bn), up roughly 19% — the fastest expansion in years [SBV 2025]. That growth sits on a credit-to-GDP ratio above 140%, the highest in the region, prompting the SBV to steer 2026 system credit back toward ~15% [SBV 2026]. The headline question for participants is no longer access to growth — it is the quality and price of that growth.

Why now: the resolution reset

On-balance-sheet NPLs reached 4.3% in January 2025, with 24 of 27 listed banks reporting Q1 rises [SBV / VNDirect 2025]. The amended Law on Credit Institutions, effective 15 October 2025, codifies Resolution 42's collateral-seizure mechanism into permanent law and extends it to all NPLs regardless of origination date [VNA 2025]. Faster recovery recycles capital, lowers provisioning, and reprices balance-sheet risk — the central de-rating-to-re-rating story of 2026.

Section 04 · Macro Context
006 / 019

Macro indicators driving credit demand

A high-growth economy running a credit-led model now confronts its own leverage ceiling. The 2026 setup is one of moderating volume, reviving funding costs, and a policy tilt toward selective, productive lending.

Credit outgrew the economy

System credit reached ~USD 690 bn by end-2025, +19% YoY [SBV 2025], lifting credit-to-GDP above 140% [SBV 2026] — among the highest globally. The SBV's 2026 guidance of ~15% system growth is a deliberate brake to contain systemic leverage while still funding the government's GDP-growth ambition.

Funding cost is the swing variable

Banks pulled in record deposits in 2025, yet credit ran ahead of deposit growth, tightening liquidity into year-end. Funding costs are set to rise more sharply from mid-2026 as demand recovers faster than deposits — the mechanism behind sector NIM staying below 3%.

Policy steers the mix

Beyond the headline quota, the SBV is layering stricter quarterly lending limits and real-estate exposure caps, redirecting credit toward production, SMEs and retail. The 2026 ratio reform from annual to quarterly quotas tightens the feedback loop on bank behaviour.

Section 05 · Bad-Debt Resolution
008 / 019

Sizing the bad-debt problem — and its turn

On-balance-sheet NPLs hit 4.3% in January 2025, concentrated in weak and special-control banks. The legalised resolution toolkit is the lever expected to bend the ratio back below 3% within the first year of enforcement.

The stock and its concentration

The headline 4.3% on-balance-sheet NPL [SBV / VNDirect 2025] understates the gross overhang once restructured and VAMC-parked debt are counted, but overstates risk for top-tier banks — the stock clusters in weak and special-control institutions. In Q1 2025, 24 of 27 listed banks reported rising NPLs as the post-stimulus credit vintage seasoned.

The expected turn

Sell-side consensus is that legalising the resolution framework pulls the sector ratio from 4.3% toward sub-3% in the first year [VNDirect 2025]. The mechanism is enforcement speed, not forgiveness: faster seizure and disposal shrink the stock and deter strategic default.

Why it reprices equity

Capital recycled out of dead collateral re-enters lending, provisioning normalises, and credit costs fall — feeding directly into re-rating for banks with large secured-NPL books and active asset-management arms.

Business Overview

Vietnam's most profitable bank, with FY2025 pre-tax profit around VND 45 tn (~USD 1.71 bn) and total assets up nearly 20% to ~USD 94 bn [VCB FY2025]. Its foreign-trade heritage underpins a low-cost deposit base and a dominant position in trade finance and FX.

Asset Quality Edge

VCB carries the cleanest book among large lenders, with the sector's lowest funding cost translating into a NIM near 3.04% even as the system compresses below 3% [VCB FY2025]. That gap is the franchise's defining moat in a margin-squeezed cycle.

Strategic Positioning

The benchmark for asset quality and affluent-customer share, VCB anchors system stability. It is a primary beneficiary of legalised NPL resolution given its scale, but its already-low NPL means the re-rating is gentler than for higher-NPL peers [Kira estimates].

Strengths & Watchlist

  • Funding moat: lowest cost of funds among large banks
  • Asset quality: cleanest large-bank book, strong provisioning
  • Affluent franchise: top bank for affluent customers

Section 08 · Digital Lending
016 / 019

The digital lending contest: acquisition, then underwriting

Three digital-bank models — Cake, Timo and TNEX — and recovering consumer-finance leaders are rerouting how mass-market credit is sold and priced. eKYC compresses onboarding; AI scoring compresses risk cost.

Three challenger models

Cake (VPBank × Be) embeds banking in a ride-hailing app — Vietnam's first such tie-up. Timo pioneered eKYC and retail "hangouts". TNEX targets Gen Z as a tech-first neobank, scaling from 3.4 m users (end-2024) to ~4.5 m by end-2025 [Mambu / Asian Banker 2025].

Consumer finance recovers

FE Credit (VPBank) remains the consumer-finance leader and returned to recovery in 2025, targeting ~15% credit growth on the back of FE Online 2.0 and AI-driven approval [VPBank 2025]. Small loan sizes and high fixed costs make AI underwriting the decisive unit-economics lever.

Where the edge accrues

Banks with low-cost digital funding and proprietary AI scoring win the short-tenor segment; branch-led incumbents cede the mass and Gen Z tiers unless they embed credit into partner ecosystems [Kira estimates].

Section 10 · AI Impact on Banking
017 / 019

AI in Vietnamese banking: from pilots to production

AI has moved from experiment to revenue line. Underwriting, fraud detection and service automation are live at scale — and the 2026 Digital Technology Law sets the risk-based guardrails for how far they can go.

At production scale

Techcombank ran 55+ AI models by end-2025 with a ~VND 4.1 tn uplift; VPBank and BIDV deploy fraud, risk-scoring and service automation via transfer learning [GFM / Springer 2025].

Fraud as the proof point

Technology blocked 390,000 suspected scam transactions worth ~USD 61 m in 9M 2025 — the clearest near-term ROI on AI investment for the sector [VietnamNet 2025].

Regulation catches up

The Digital Technology Law (effective Jan 2026) adds risk-based rules and AI labelling; Decree 94's sandbox lets banks trial AI lending under supervision for up to two years [Gov VN 2025].
