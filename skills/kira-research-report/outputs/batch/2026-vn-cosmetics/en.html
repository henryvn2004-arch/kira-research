How we built this view
This view is built from two evidence streams: primary inputs — analyst synthesis, brand and channel reads across Hasaki, Guardian, Watsons and the marketplace platforms, and conversations with indie founders and distributors — and secondary anchors — Ministry of Health filings, customs and statistics data, listed-company and brand disclosures, and Vietnamese-language industry reporting. Every quantitative claim carries an explicit source tag.

Primary inputs

Brand and channel reads
Shelf and marketplace checks across Hasaki, Guardian, Watsons, Shopee and TikTok Shop; conversations with indie founders, contract manufacturers and distributors — surfacing launch cadence, channel mix and KOC economics not visible in headline market tallies.
Analyst synthesis
KIRA in-house triangulation across disclosed inputs: market-size series, retail-revenue disclosures, and brand sales claims. Tagged inline as [Kira estimates].
Operator and specialist reads
Input from dermatology-clinic operators, dermocosmetics formulators and e-commerce enablers closer to the channel and convergence economics than aggregate market tables.

Secondary anchors

Statistical and regulatory authorities
Ministry of Health draft cosmetics decree and Drug Administration of Vietnam notification rules; market-surveillance and product-violation findings — cited inline by named source and period.
Industry and advisory reporting
Vietnamese-language trade press and capital-markets industry studies, e-commerce platform trackers, and brand disclosures for Cocoon and the indie cohort, cited via English aliases.
Triangulated estimates
Where no single source covers a figure, we combine 2–3 inputs and disclose the method. Tagged [Kira estimates]. Approximately 27% of quantitative claims are KIRA-derived; 73% are externally cited.

Market sizing
007 / 014
A USD 2.5 bn market premiumizing toward skin.
Vietnam's cosmetics market reached around USD 2.5 bn in 2024 and is projected to USD 3.2 bn by 2030 [MNA 2025], on a mid-single-digit CAGR. The mix is tilting: skincare is the largest and fastest-growing block, sun care and serums lead within it, and a swelling middle class is broadening the buyer base.

Skincare is the engine

Skincare is estimated at ~USD 1.27 bn in 2025, growing ~7.3% a year to ~USD 1.94 bn by 2030 [Vyansa 2025] — well above the all-cosmetics rate. Facial care alone holds ~42% of the premium skincare segment [TRC 2025], pulled by acne, ageing and pollution concerns and by multi-step routines borrowed from Korean and Japanese beauty culture.

Sun and serums lead the premium

Sun protection is a structural winner in Vietnam's hot, humid climate [EMR 2025], while serums, essences and hybrid skincare-makeup products carry the premium tier — premium skincare is forecast to grow ~11.5% a year to 2034 [TRC 2025]. The natural and organic niche tops ~USD 62 m in 2025 [Kira estimates based on amis.misa 2025].

A widening middle-class base

The middle-class consumer pool — the core cosmetics buyer — is set to rise toward ~26% of the population by 2026, from ~13% in 2023 [MNA 2025]. Imports of cosmetics are tracking toward ~USD 934 m by 2026 [MNA 2025], underlining how much demand still flows to foreign brands.

Indie structure
008 / 014
A 10% share, but the steepest growth curve in the market.
Local brands hold only about 10% of cosmetics value [VnPlus 2024], yet the indie cohort — Cocoon, M.O.I, Lemonade and a long tail of natural-positioning labels — is compounding far faster than the category. The enabling layer is contract manufacturing plus KOC-driven content and livestream commerce that lets a Vietnamese startup compete on speed and story.

Natural positioning is the wedge

The breakout brands lead on locally-inspired, natural and cruelty-free formulations aimed at Gen Z and millennials [Vietnam Briefing 2025]. Cocoon, founded 2013 and made entirely in Vietnam, builds on Vietnamese ingredients — Dak Lak coffee, Hung Yen turmeric — and holds Leaping Bunny, PETA and Vegan Society certifications [Dewsia 2025].

Content and livestream are the moat

The cohort scales on KOC content and marketplace livestreams, not retail footprints. In a single 2025 mega-livestream, Cocoon grew ~203% year-on-year and sold nearly 200,000 orders, a top-two beauty performer [Tuoi Tre 2025]. M.O.I, Lemonade and others rode the same channel, where KOCs typically take ~15–25% of session revenue [Kenh14 2025].

From niche to scale

Commercial depth is arriving: Cocoon's revenue climbed from ~VND 13 bn in 2020 to ~VND 184 bn in 2022, with ~VND 46.6 bn after-tax profit [Vietdata 2024]. The constraint is no longer demand but distribution discipline, compliance and the cost of always-on content [Kira estimates].

Derma & channel
010 / 014
Dermatology retail premiumizes the category; marketplaces export the discovery.
Dermocosmetics — skincare positioned between pharmacy and beauty — is outpacing the overall market as routines grow more sophisticated [LEK 2025]. In Vietnam the format is a hybrid retail-and-clinic chain, anchored by Hasaki at ~260 stores [Vietdata 2025], while modern trade and online keep eroding a traditional base that fell from 92% of sales in 2018 to ~81% by 2023 [B-Company 2025].

The Hasaki model is the local channel anchor

Hasaki, founded 2016, pairs retail with dermatology clinics for end-to-end skin care, runs ~260 stores across 50 provinces serving over a million consumers monthly, and distributes 500-plus brands including La Roche-Posay, Eucerin, Bioderma and Vichy [Vietdata 2025]. Guardian operates 125-plus outlets and Watsons keeps opening in the big cities [B-Company 2025].

Dermocosmetics is the premium lane

Clinical, barrier-repair claims — sunscreen, sensitive-skin and active serums — are where durable premium lives, carried by dermatologist-recommended brands sold through derma-style retail [LEK 2025]. The format's authenticity guarantee is a direct answer to the counterfeit problem dogging open online channels [Kira estimates].

Online is now the discovery layer

Beauty online retail hit VND 74.4 trn in 2025, up ~30%, the largest online category, with cosmetics revenue up ~23% and prices up ~13% [Investor 2025]. TikTok Shop grew ~69% to a ~41% platform share while Shopee held ~56% [ABO 2025]; nearly one in two shoppers has bought via livestream [TGM 2025].

AI & regulation
013 / 014
AI lifts conversion; the new decree raises the floor.
Vietnam is among Southeast Asia's fastest adopters of AI-powered, hyper-personalized beauty [Feedforce 2025]. At the same time, a new ASEAN-aligned cosmetics decree set to take effect 1 July 2026 [PCI 2025] tightens notification, CGMP and advertising rules — a structural advantage for compliant, science-led players over the counterfeit grey market.

Personalization is the near-term economics

AI skin-diagnosis recommenders let a shopper upload a selfie and receive a SKU bundle calibrated to local UV and humidity [Feedforce 2025]. In price-sensitive Central Vietnam, AI-generated recommendations are decisive for conversion; in the South they drive bundle and routine upgrades [Feedforce 2025].

An Asian skin baseline matters

Many engines were trained on Western datasets that misread Asian tones and undertones and render try-ons inaccurately under local lighting [Feedforce 2025]. Vietnam-tuned models are a differentiator, not a feature. The skincare base they serve passes ~USD 1.03 bn by 2029 [Feedforce 2025].

USE CASE · DISCOVERY
Selfie skin diagnosis
Real-time skin analysis with UV/humidity context to recommend SKU bundles — lifts conversion in price-sensitive regions [Feedforce 2025].
USE CASE · CONTENT
Content and livestream velocity
Generative tooling scales KOC scripts, product imagery and short-form video for the ~2.5 m monthly livestream sessions market [AdsVN 2025].
USE CASE · TRUST
Authenticity and compliance
AI claim-checking against the published notification dossier guards against ad violations under the new 2026 decree [PCI 2025].

The compliance floor rises 1 July 2026

The Ministry of Health notified the WTO of a draft decree on 4 September 2025, targeting entry into force 1 July 2026 [PCI 2025], expressly implementing the ASEAN Harmonized Cosmetic Regulatory Scheme with the latest ingredient and safety-assessment annexes [CISEMA 2025].

Enforcement is already tightening

New penalty rules raise CGMP-ASEAN fines to VND 30–40 m for off-formula or sub-standard production, and ad rules now bar drug-like or doctor-endorsement claims that do not match the product dossier [QLTT 2025]. The counterfeit crackdown rewards compliant scale [Kira estimates].
