# Internal interview notes — Vietnam tea market, Q1 2026

Compiled by KIRA team. Two informants, anonymised. Used as primary research input for the Vietnam tea 2026 report.

---

## Informant A — Café operator, multi-outlet, HCMC

> "Premium green at retail USD 14-18/kg gives us about 3x revenue per cup compared with commodity tea. The driver is the middle-class consumer wanting something they can call 'authentic Vietnamese' — origin matters, brand matters less."

> "We switched from imported Taiwan oolong to Thai Nguyen-origin premium green in 2024. Volume same, margin up roughly 22%."

> "Cafe chain expansion in Hanoi and Da Nang is the biggest channel growth for premium. The wet markets stay flat for premium; modern trade is where the volume shifts."

---

## Informant B — Export agent, Hanoi

> "Bulk black tea export to Middle East and Russia is up around 8% year-over-year. The EU side is shrinking — pesticide residue standards are tightening faster than the smallholder side can adapt."

> "Pakistan stays our biggest single export market by volume. Price-sensitive, brown-tea preference, low-grade leaf. Strategic moat is the FOB-to-Karachi logistics relationship, not the product."

> "The Vinatea state-linked supply network is consolidating. Smaller exporters are getting squeezed out of the China cross-border trade by post-2024 anti-grey-market enforcement."

---

## Synthesis (KIRA analyst)

Key signals to incorporate:
1. Premium domestic shift is real and accelerating — 15-18% growth band
2. Channel re-mix favours modern trade and café for premium tier
3. Export market diverging: ME/Russia growing, EU shrinking
4. Vinatea consolidation dynamic is consistent with broader SOE rationalisation in Vietnam ag/food
5. Pesticide compliance is a structural moat for the few exporters who can meet EU standards
